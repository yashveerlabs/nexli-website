# Privacy Policy — Nexli School Management Platform

This Privacy Policy explains how personal data is collected, used, stored, shared and protected when a school ("**School**") uses Nexli, a multi-tenant school-management platform ("**Nexli**", "**we**", "**us**"), and how the data principals whose data is processed (students, parents/guardians, and staff) can exercise their rights.

This Policy is written to align with the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act")**, the **Information Technology Act, 2000** and the **Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011 ("SPDI Rules")**, and the child-protection expectations of the **POCSO Act, 2012**, the **Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 ("RTE Act")** and the **CBSE Affiliation Bye-Laws**.

> **Roles under the DPDP Act.** For student, parent and staff personal data entered into the platform, the **School is the Data Fiduciary** (it determines the purpose and means of processing) and **Nexli acts as a Data Processor** processing such data only on the School's documented instructions. The relationship is governed by the separate **Data Processing Agreement (DPA)**. Where this Policy describes School-level decisions (e.g. which purposes data is collected for), those decisions are made and controlled by the School.

## 1. Scope

This Policy covers personal data processed through the Nexli web application and associated services on behalf of a School. It does not cover a School's own offline records or third-party systems the School independently operates.

## 2. Personal data we collect

Personal data is collected by the School and processed by Nexli for the categories below. Nexli does **not** require, and the platform does **not** collect, **biometric data** (e.g. fingerprints, facial-recognition templates or iris scans). Attendance is recorded by manual or OTP-based methods, not biometrics.

**a. Student personal data**
- Identifying data: name, date of birth, gender, photograph, admission/roll number, class/section, address.
- Government identifiers where the School chooses to record them (e.g. Aadhaar number, where applicable for RTE/UDISE+ reporting). These are sensitive and access is restricted.
- Academic data: marks, report cards, assessments, attendance, progress and behavioural notes recorded by staff.
- Health and emergency data: medical/allergy notes, immunisation records, emergency contacts (a special category requiring particular care).
- Operational data: transport route/stop and bus-location association (only for opted-in transport), hostel allocation, library and canteen records.
- Welfare data: counselling, special-education (IEP) and child-protection (POCSO) records — held in the most restricted manner (see Section 7 and 11).

**b. Parent / guardian personal data**
- Name, relationship to student, phone number, email, address, occupation/income (only where recorded for RTE/EWS eligibility), and consent decisions.

**c. Staff personal data**
- Name, contact details, role/designation, employment and payroll-related data, leave records, and authentication identifiers.

**d. Technical data**
- Authentication identifiers, audit logs of actions taken in the platform, and limited diagnostic data necessary to operate and secure the service.

We practise **data minimisation**: only data necessary for a stated purpose is collected, and identifiers in sensitive records (e.g. POCSO) are kept to the essential minimum.

## 3. Purposes of processing

Personal data is processed for the following purposes, as configured by the School:
- Student enrolment, admission and records management.
- Delivery of education: timetables, academics, assessments, report cards, attendance.
- Health, safety and emergency response.
- Transport, hostel, library, canteen and other operational services the family opts into.
- Communication with parents/guardians and staff.
- Fee invoicing, payment recording and financial administration.
- Statutory and regulatory compliance (e.g. RTE, UDISE+, CBSE, SMC governance).
- Safeguarding and grievance redressal.
- Securing, auditing and maintaining the platform.

Personal data is **not** used to serve **targeted/behavioural advertising**, and the platform does **not** undertake **behavioural monitoring or tracking of children** for such purposes (see Section 11).

## 4. Legal basis for processing

Under the DPDP Act, processing relies on:
- **Consent** of the data principal (or, for a child, of the parent/guardian — see Section 11), obtained for clearly stated purposes; and/or
- **Certain legitimate uses** recognised under the DPDP Act, including where processing is necessary for compliance with law, and for the performance of functions the School is legally entitled to perform in the interest of the student.

Where consent is the basis, it is collected purpose-by-purpose and may be **withdrawn** at any time (Section 6). Sensitive personal data (including health information) under the SPDI Rules is processed with particular care and restricted access.

## 5. Rights of data principals (DPDP Act)

Subject to the DPDP Act, every data principal (or the parent/guardian of a child) has the right to:
- **Access** a summary of their personal data being processed and the processing activities;
- **Correction, completion and updating** of inaccurate or incomplete data;
- **Erasure** of personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purpose or where consent is withdrawn (subject to legal retention obligations) — see the platform's **erasure-request** workflow;
- **Grievance redressal** through the School and Nexli (Section 12); and
- **Nominate** another individual to exercise these rights in the event of death or incapacity.

Requests are ordinarily made to the **School (Data Fiduciary)**. Nexli, as Processor, provides the tools (consent register, erasure-request register) to help the School action these requests and will assist the School as required under the DPA.

## 6. Consent and withdrawal

Consent is recorded against specific purposes in the platform's consent register, with the channel (in-app/paper/verbal) and date. A guardian may **withdraw** consent for an optional purpose at any time; the consequence of withdrawal (e.g. a service can no longer be provided) will be explained. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal. Some purposes are necessary for enrolment or legal compliance and cannot be declined without affecting the service.

## 7. Children's data — DPDP Act s.9

Because the platform primarily concerns minors, special protections apply (DPDP Act **section 9**):
- **Verifiable parental consent** is obtained before processing a child's personal data for consent-based purposes (see the separate **Parent Consent** document).
- The platform does **not** undertake **tracking or behavioural monitoring of children**, and does **not** use children's data for **targeted advertising**.
- Processing of children's data is kept to what is necessary and is **not** undertaken in a manner likely to cause a detrimental effect on the well-being of the child.

## 8. Retention

Personal data is retained only as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by law (e.g. academic and statutory records that schools are obliged to retain). On expiry of the retention period, or on a valid erasure request where no legal obligation requires retention, data is deleted or anonymised. Specific retention periods are set by the School as Data Fiduciary; Nexli will delete or return data on termination per the DPA.

## 9. Security

We implement reasonable security practices appropriate to the sensitivity of the data, including:
- Tenant isolation (each School's data is structurally separated under its own scope);
- Role-based access control, so users see only the data their role permits, with the most sensitive records (POCSO, counselling, health) restricted to a small set of authorised roles;
- Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and encryption at rest provided by the underlying cloud infrastructure;
- Authentication, audit logging of significant actions, and least-privilege administration.

No system is perfectly secure; we continuously work to improve our safeguards.

## 10. Sharing and sub-processors

Personal data is **not sold**. It is shared only:
- With the School and its authorised users, per their roles;
- With sub-processors who provide infrastructure necessary to run the platform — principally **Google Firebase / Google Cloud** (authentication and database). Where feasible, data is hosted in a **Google Cloud India region**; and
- Where required by law or to protect the safety of a child (e.g. mandatory POCSO reporting to the appropriate authorities).

Our sub-processors are listed and bound by data-processing terms under the DPA (Annexure on sub-processors).

## 11. Cross-border transfer

Personal data is intended to be stored and processed in **India** using Google Cloud's India region(s) where available. To the extent any processing occurs outside India (e.g. for limited operational or support functions), it will be carried out consistently with the DPDP Act and any restrictions notified by the Central Government, and subject to appropriate safeguards.

## 13. Breach notification

In the event of a personal-data breach, Nexli (as Processor) will notify the affected School(s) without undue delay so that the School (as Data Fiduciary) can notify the **Data Protection Board of India** and affected data principals as required by the DPDP Act. Nexli maintains an internal breach-notification register to track detection, assessment and notification.
