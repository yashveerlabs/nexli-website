## Managing Principal Stress

- **Recognize early signs:** Exhaustion, cynicism, reduced effectiveness
- **Build support:** Mentors, peers, family, professionals
- **Set boundaries:** Can't work 70 hours/week indefinitely
- **Focus on impact:** Remember why you do this
- **Delegate:** You don't have to do everything
- **Rest:** Regular breaks and vacations are essential

---

## Career Transitions

**When to stay:** School is improving, you're learning, culture is good

**When to consider moving:**
- No growth opportunity
- Fundamental values misalignment
- Leadership doesn't trust you
- Better opportunity elsewhere

**Before moving:**
- Ensure you're not running from a problem
- Have clarity on what you want next
- Secure new role before leaving
- Plan smooth transition

## FAQ

**Q: How much time should I spend on personal development?**
A: At least 5% of your time (2-3 hours/week). Invest in courses, reading, mentoring.

**Q: Is burnout normal for principals?**
A: It happens, but it's not inevitable. Prevention (boundaries, support, delegation) is key.

**Q: When should a principal seek external support?**
A: If you're overwhelmed, stressed, or struggling with decisions, coaches and mentors are valuable.
