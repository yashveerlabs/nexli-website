## Executive Summary

A 5-year plan outlines your school's trajectory: Where are we today? Where do we want to be in 5 years? What are the stepping stones? Schools with clear 5-year plans report 70% better resource allocation, stronger stakeholder alignment, and more sustainable growth. This article provides a template and methodology.

---

## The Planning Process

### Year 1: Assess & Plan
- Current state analysis (academics, finance, infrastructure, staff)
- Vision and goals for year 5
- Year 1 priorities and investments
- Budget and resource allocation

### Year 2-3: Build Capacity
- Strengthen existing programs
- Invest in staff development
- Upgrade infrastructure
- Stabilize finances

### Year 4-5: Scale & Innovate
- Launch new programs
- Expand enrollment
- Achieve targets
- Build for next cycle

### Framework by Area

**Academic Excellence:**
- Year 1: Diagnostic systems, teacher training begins
- Year 2-3: Curriculum modernization, results improve
- Year 4-5: Innovation, competitive advantage

**Infrastructure:**
- Year 1: Planning, design approval, fundraising
- Year 2-3: Construction, installation
- Year 4-5: Full operationalization, expansion

**Financial Sustainability:**
- Year 1: Cost control, revenue optimization
- Year 2-3: Revenue growth (enrollment, programs)
- Year 4-5: Profit, reserves build, investment

**Staff Development:**
- Year 1: Hiring, training infrastructure
- Year 2-3: Continuous professional development
- Year 4-5: Leadership pipeline, retention

## Key Milestones by Year

| Year | Academic | Infrastructure | Finance | People |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | Diagnostic; coaching starts | Lab planned/approved | Fees optimized; costs cut 10% | Teachers trained; retention focus |
| Year 2 | Results improve 5–10%; innovation pilot | Lab built | Revenue +15%; margins improve | Attrition <10%; key hires made |
| Year 3 | Results improve another 5%; new program launches | Lab operational; expansion planned | Revenue +20%; breaks even on growth | Staff fully developed; culture strong |
| Year 4 | New program scales; academic gaps close | New building/section operational | Revenue +25%; healthy margins | Leadership pipeline visible |
| Year 5 | Excellence achieved; competitive advantage | New section open; capacity +20% | Revenue +30%; strong reserves | Next cycle planned |

## FAQ

**Q: How detailed should a 5-year plan be?**
A: Detailed for year 1, moderate for years 2–3, flexible for years 4–5 (adjust as you go).

**Q: What if things change?**
A: Review annually. Adjust if needed. Plans should guide, not constrain.
