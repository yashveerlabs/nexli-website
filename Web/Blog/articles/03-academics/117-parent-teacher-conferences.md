## Executive Summary

Structured parent-teacher meetings improve school-home communication and student outcomes. Schools conducting data-driven conferences report 35% higher parent satisfaction and better student accountability.

---

## Conference Preparation

**Gather:**
- Formative and summative assessments
- Attendance records
- Homework completion rates
- Behavioral observations
- Work samples (good and most recent)

**Invite student to prepare:** "What's one strength? One area to improve?"

**Organize space:** Round table, student work displayed, comfortable, private.

---

## Conference Flow (30 minutes)

### Opening (5 min)
"Thanks for coming. Let's discuss [Student]'s progress and how we can partner to support learning."

### Celebrate (5 min)
Share 2-3 specific strengths with examples.

### Discuss Data (10 min)
Share assessment data, attendance, participation patterns. Identify 1-2 growth areas.

### Set Goals (5 min)
"By [date], [Student] will [specific, measurable goal]."

### Action Plan (5 min)
Who does what? When? How do we track progress?

### Close
"Let's check in by [date]."

---

## Tips for Difficult Conversations

- Stay calm and collaborative
- Describe behavior, not judge person
- Focus on solutions
- Pause if emotions escalate
- Ensure student voices included
