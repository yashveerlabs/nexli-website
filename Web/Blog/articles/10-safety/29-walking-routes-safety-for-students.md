# Walking Routes: Safety for Students

Students often walk to school. Ensure routes are safe.

**Route Identification** - Safe walking routes identified, shortest reasonable path, hazard assessment, lighting adequate, foot traffic normal.

**Hazard Assessment** - Traffic volume, crossing points, visibility, street condition, construction, crime indicators, route safety evaluation.

**Pedestrian Infrastructure** - Sidewalks available, crosswalks marked, signals functioning, curbs proper, drainage adequate, well-maintained.

**Traffic Control** - Crossing guards if needed, traffic signals, speed limits enforced, driver behavior, safety monitoring, incident prevention.

**Student Training** - Walking safety taught, traffic rules, crossing procedures, stranger danger, awareness, personal safety, responsibility.

**Supervision** - Adult supervision if young, safety buddy system, designated routes, accountability, communication with parents.

**Family Communication** - Routes explained, safety procedures, expectations clear, communication regular, parent monitoring encouraged.

**Weather Considerations** - Safe in weather, visibility in poor conditions, winter/summer adjustments, appropriate footwear, preparation.

**Special Needs** - Accommodations for disabilities, crossing assistance, speed matching, individual plans, inclusivity, support.

**Technology** - GPS tracking possible, emergency communication, safety apps, tracking permitted, privacy respected.

**Community Engagement** - Partner with community, traffic safety education, enforcement support, crossing guard volunteers, collective responsibility.

**Data** - Incident tracking, route safety records, near-miss documentation, patterns identified, improvement opportunities.

Safe walking routes enable independent student mobility while maintaining security.
