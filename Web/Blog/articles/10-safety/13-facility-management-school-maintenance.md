## Facility Management & School Maintenance: Preventing Breakdown and Safety Issues

**A school building is a complex asset. Electricity, plumbing, HVAC, classrooms, labs, sports grounds, parking, all need regular maintenance. Most schools run in reactive mode: something breaks, then it gets fixed. This costs more, creates downtime, and creates safety risks. Schools using preventive maintenance (regular inspections, scheduled repairs, asset tracking) report 40% reduction in breakdowns, 25% cost savings, and significantly higher safety compliance. This article reveals how principals can implement facility management systems that keep schools functional, safe, and cost-effective.**

---

## The Problem: Reactive vs. Preventive Facility Management

### The Reactive Scenario (Most Schools)

Monday morning: The AC in the principal's office stops working.
- Technician called; arrives Wednesday (delayed response)
- Cost: ₹8,000 for emergency repair
- Downtime: 3 days
- By next month, 2 more ACs break due to lack of preventive maintenance
- By year-end: Multiple breakdowns; total cost ₹50,000+
- Staff and students uncomfortable; complaints pile up

### The Preventive Scenario (Forward-Thinking Schools)

Every 6 months: Scheduled AC maintenance
- Technician comes; cleans filters, checks gas, services unit
- Cost: ₹2,000 per unit per service
- Time: 1 hour, no disruption (scheduled during lunch)
- Result: ACs run smoothly all year; no emergency breakdowns
- Annual cost: ₹4,000 × 10 units = ₹40,000 (vs. ₹50,000+ in reactive mode)

**Preventive is cheaper AND safer.**

---

## Why Schools Don't Do Preventive Maintenance

### 1. **No Tracking System**
"When was the AC serviced last?" No one knows. No record. Maintenance happens ad-hoc.

### 2. **Insufficient Budget**
"We don't have money for preventive maintenance." Reality: You'll spend more on emergency repairs than preventive maintenance.

### 3. **No Accountability**
Who's responsible for maintenance? Facilities manager? Principal? No clear owner means nothing happens.

### 4. **Lack of Planning**
No maintenance schedule. No list of what needs to be serviced when. Decision-making is reactive.

### 5. **Safety Complacency**
"The building looks fine." But electrical hazards, structural stress, safety systems failing, you can't see them until it's too late.

---

## The Cost of Reactive Maintenance

### For the School
- **Higher costs:** Emergency repairs cost 3–5x more than preventive maintenance
- **Downtime:** Broken AC/computers/equipment means disrupted classes
- **Safety risk:** Unmaintained buildings develop electrical, structural, or water hazards
- **Reputation:** Parents see broken equipment; school looks neglected
- **Staff morale:** Working in poor-condition building affects morale

### For Students
- **Learning disruption:** Class halted due to broken projector or AC
- **Discomfort:** Studying in malfunctioning classroom
- **Safety risk:** Broken equipment or facilities pose safety hazards

## Solutions: Implementing Preventive Facility Management

### Step 1: Create an Asset Register

**What:** Document every major asset in the school.

**Assets to Track:**

| Category | Examples | Replacement Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Electrical | Main panel, sub-panels, AC units, fans, lighting | ₹50,000–5 lakhs |
| Plumbing | Water tanks, pipes, taps, water purifiers | ₹50,000–3 lakhs |
| IT | Computers, servers, printers, WiFi | ₹20,000–10 lakhs |
| Sports/Labs | Equipment, apparatus, lab instruments | ₹10,000–1 lakh each |
| Vehicles (if any) | Buses, vans | ₹10–30 lakhs each |
| Building | Roof, paint, walls, windows | ₹1–5 lakhs repair |
| Furniture | Desks, chairs, lockers | ₹5,000–20,000 each |

**Register Format:**

| Asset | Location | Purchase Date | Last Service | Next Service | Cost | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| AC Unit 1 | Principal's Office | Jan 2020 | Jun 2026 | Dec 2026 | ₹45,000 | Working |
| Server | IT Room | Aug 2021 | Aug 2025 | Aug 2026 | ₹2,00,000 | Working |
| Water Tank | Terrace | Mar 2018 | Mar 2026 | Sep 2026 | ₹80,000 | Working |

**Why:** Register gives you visibility into what you own, when it was last maintained, and when it needs servicing next.

### Step 2: Create a Maintenance Schedule

**What:** Define when each asset gets serviced.

**Common Maintenance Schedules:**

| Asset | Frequency | What's Checked |
|---|---|---|
| AC Unit | Every 6 months | Filter cleaning, gas refill, cooling check |
| Plumbing (water system) | Every 3 months | Pressure check, tank cleaning, pipe inspection |
| Electrical | Every 6 months (yearly full audit) | Panel inspection, grounding, overload protection |
| Computers/Servers | Every 6 months | Cooling, backup verification, antivirus updates |
| Fire Safety Systems | Every 6 months | Extinguisher charge date, alarm test, exit path check |
| Building Inspection | Every year | Roof, walls, foundation, moisture, structural issues |
| Sports Equipment | Every 3 months | Safety inspection, wear and tear check |
| Vehicles | Every month | Oil change, tire pressure, brakes, lights |

**Calendar Template:**

| Month | Maintenance Tasks |
|---|---|
| January | AC units, Electrical audit (winter recap) |
| February | Building inspection (pre-monsoon) |
| March | Plumbing system, Water tank |
| April | Fire safety systems |
| May | IT equipment |
| June | AC units (mid-year), Sports equipment |
| July | (Pre-monsoon prep) Roof inspection, gutters |
| August | Plumbing, Water quality check |
| September | Fire safety systems, Emergency lights |
| October | Building inspection (post-monsoon), Electrical |
| November | IT equipment, Computers |
| December | AC units, Year-end audit |

**Why:** Scheduled maintenance means nothing is forgotten. Tasks are predictable and budgeted.

### Step 3: Assign Ownership & Accountability

**What:** Designate a Facilities Manager or coordinator responsible for maintenance.

**Role Responsibilities:**
- Maintain asset register (update last service dates)
- Schedule maintenance appointments (weeks in advance)
- Oversee maintenance work (verify it's done properly)
- Track invoices and expenses
- Report to principal: "What was done, cost, next service date"
- Flag issues that arise during maintenance (escalate to principal)

**Accountability:**
- Monthly report to principal: "Maintenance completed, schedule for next month"
- Quarterly review with principal: "What broke? Why? What did we learn?"

**Cost:** If you don't have full-time facilities manager, assign this to office manager or HR coordinator (add to their duties).

### Step 4: Budget for Preventive Maintenance

**What:** Allocate annual budget for regular maintenance (don't treat it as emergency expense).

**Budget Calculation:**

Estimate annual preventive maintenance cost:
- AC servicing: ₹2,000 × 10 units × 2 services/year = ₹40,000
- Plumbing system: ₹3,000 × 4 services/year = ₹12,000
- Electrical: ₹5,000 × 2 services/year = ₹10,000
- IT equipment: ₹2,000 × 10 computers × 2 services/year = ₹40,000
- Fire safety: ₹1,000 × 6 services/year = ₹6,000
- Building inspection: ₹15,000 (annual full audit)
- Sports equipment: ₹5,000 (inspection & minor repairs)

**Total Annual Preventive Maintenance:** ₹1,28,000

**Plus: Emergency Reserve (10–15% of total):** ₹15,000–20,000

**Total Facility Budget:** ₹1,43,000–1,48,000/year (for medium school, 400 students)

**Why:** Budget upfront means maintenance happens. No competing for emergency funds mid-year.

### Step 5: Implement Safety Inspections

**What:** Regular safety audits ensure school meets safety standards.

**Safety Audit Checklist:**

**Electrical Safety:**
- ☐ Main panel has proper grounding
- ☐ No exposed wires
- ☐ Overload protection (circuit breakers working)
- ☐ Lightning arrestor installed
- ☐ All switches & outlets in good condition

**Structural Safety:**
- ☐ No water seepage in walls/ceiling
- ☐ Roof is sound (no leaks, cracks)
- ☐ Windows and doors lock properly
- ☐ Stairs and railings are secure
- ☐ No visible structural cracks

**Fire Safety:**
- ☐ Fire extinguishers are charged and accessible (1 per 500 sq ft)
- ☐ Emergency exits are clear (no obstacles)
- ☐ Emergency lights work (tested monthly)
- ☐ Fire alarm system functional (tested quarterly)
- ☐ Assembly point marked and accessible

**Water & Sanitation:**
- ☐ Water storage tanks are clean (cleaned annually)
- ☐ Taps have filters; water quality is safe
- ☐ Bathrooms are clean and functional
- ☐ Drainage system works (no blockages)

**Conduct Safety Audit:**
- Annually (full audit) by qualified inspector
- Quarterly (internal self-check) by facilities manager
- After any incident, repair, or major change

**Cost:** ₹5,000–15,000 for annual professional audit (depending on school size)

---

### Step 6: Document & Track Work Orders

**What:** Every maintenance request is documented and tracked.

**Work Order Process:**

1. **Request Submitted**
   - Staff member submits maintenance request (broken AC, water leak, electrical issue)
   - Request includes: What's broken, where, when did it break, priority level

2. **Work Order Created**
   - Facilities manager assigns a work order number
   - Technician assigned; timeline given (urgent same-day, routine within 5 days)
   - Estimated cost provided

3. **Work Completed**
   - Technician completes work; provides invoice
   - Facilities manager verifies work quality
   - Work order closed; details logged (date, cost, what was fixed)

4. **Record Kept**
   - All work orders archived (digital or paper)
   - Helps track: What breaks often? Why? What's the pattern?
   - Informs future maintenance strategy

**Digital System:** Nexli can track work orders, or use simple Google Form / Excel.

## FAQ

**Q: We don't have a facilities manager. Who should own maintenance?**
A: Assign to office manager or admin coordinator (add to their duties). Or, hire part-time facilities coordinator (₹15,000–20,000/month). ROI is quick: preventive maintenance saves more than it costs.

**Q: How do we justify preventive maintenance budget to the board?**
A: Show the math: "Reactive emergency repairs cost ₹50,000/year. Preventive maintenance costs ₹35,000/year. We save ₹15,000 and eliminate downtime." Numbers convince boards.

**Q: What if a major repair is needed (roof, electrical panel replacement)?**
A: Set aside 5% of annual school budget as "capital reserve" for major repairs. Plan ahead. If you keep facilities well-maintained, major repairs are less frequent.

**Q: How do we track if maintenance actually happened?**
A: Require technician to provide proof (photos before/after, signed receipt, parts replaced). Facilities manager verifies before payment. Digital system (Nexli) logs all details.

**Q: Should we do maintenance during school hours or after?**
A: Schedule non-disruptive maintenance (AC, plumbing, electrical) during lunch or weekends. Emergency repairs may require class adjustments. Plan to minimize disruption.
