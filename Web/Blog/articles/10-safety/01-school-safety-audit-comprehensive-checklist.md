## School Safety Audit: Comprehensive Checklist

**A school safety audit is not optional, it's a legal requirement and a moral imperative. Every principal must know: Are our buildings physically safe? Is our campus secure? Do we have emergency procedures in place? Are staff trained? This comprehensive checklist walks you through every category of safety: physical infrastructure, security protocols, emergency preparedness, medical readiness, and compliance. Use this checklist annually (at minimum) to audit your school, document findings, and close gaps.**

---

## The Core Problem: Many Schools Have No Idea If They're Safe

Most principals assume their school is safe because no incident has happened. But absence of incident doesn't mean safety, it means you got lucky. A real audit reveals:
- Broken fire extinguishers (nobody noticed)
- Exits blocked by stored materials (nobody checks)
- Staff untrained in first aid (never budgeted for training)
- No evacuation procedure documented (never tested)
- Visitor management is chaotic (anyone walks in)

Without an audit, you're flying blind.

### Why This Matters

**For students:** An unsafe school is a traumatizing environment. Students can't focus on learning if they're worried about safety.

**For parents:** Parents trust schools with their children. A safety incident (or worse, a tragedy) destroys that trust and the school's reputation.

**For staff:** Staff need to feel safe and competent. Proper safety training and procedures build that confidence.

**For you:** If a safety incident occurs and you're found negligent (didn't audit, didn't maintain, didn't train), you face legal liability.

## The Safety Audit: 5 Domains to Inspect

### Domain 1: Physical Infrastructure & Building Safety

#### Structural Integrity
- [ ] Roof: Is it in good condition? Any leaks? (Inspect after monsoon)
- [ ] Walls: Any cracks or damage? (Significant cracks indicate structural issues)
- [ ] Floors: Any gaps, holes, or uneven surfaces? (Trip hazard)
- [ ] Ceilings: Any sagging, water damage, or loose tiles? (Falling hazard)
- [ ] Stairs/ramps: Are railings secure? Any broken steps? (Fall hazard)

#### Electrical Safety
- [ ] Wiring: Is all wiring concealed (not exposed)? Any visible fraying?
- [ ] Outlets: Are electrical outlets grounded (3-pin)?
- [ ] Load: Is the electrical load within building capacity? (Check with electrician)
- [ ] Maintenance: When was last electrical inspection? (Annually required by code)
- [ ] Overload: No multiple extension cords daisy-chained (fire hazard)

#### Fire Safety
- [ ] Fire extinguishers: Present in every 500–600 sq ft? (Check)
- [ ] Fire extinguisher maintenance: Last serviced within 12 months? (Check tags)
- [ ] Fire alarm system: Functional? Tested monthly? (Check logs)
- [ ] Emergency lighting: Backup power works? (Test monthly)
- [ ] Fire exits: Clear, marked, unlocked from inside? (Inspect)
- [ ] Fire hose: Present, accessible, functional? (Check)

#### Plumbing & Water Safety
- [ ] Water supply: Clean, tested regularly? (Quarterly water quality testing)
- [ ] Toilets: Functional, clean, adequate number for student population?
- [ ] Drainage: No backups or water leaks? (Check basement/underground areas)
- [ ] Hand-washing facilities: Near toilets and kitchen? (Hygiene)
- [ ] Drinking water: Filtered, labeled, clean? (Visible cleanliness)

#### Sanitation & Hygiene
- [ ] Waste management: Segregated (wet/dry)? Disposed properly? (No open waste)
- [ ] Cleanliness: Floors, walls, surfaces clean and disinfected? (Weekly inspection)
- [ ] Pest control: Any evidence of rodents, insects? (Regular spraying)
- [ ] Hand sanitizers: Available in classrooms, hallways, bathrooms? (Compliance)
- [ ] Masks/PPE: Available if needed? (Health preparedness)

#### Lab & Art Room Safety
- [ ] Fume hood: Functional, vented properly? (Science lab)
- [ ] Chemical storage: Locked, labeled, MSDS sheets available? (Toxic hazard)
- [ ] First aid kit: Stocked and accessible? (Every lab)
- [ ] Safety goggles, gloves, aprons: Available, enforced? (Student protection)
- [ ] Sharp objects: Properly stored and handled? (Cut hazard)

**Inspection timeline:** Quarterly (at minimum). More frequently for labs and high-risk areas.

### Domain 2: Security & Access Control

#### Entry & Exit Points
- [ ] Main gate: Locked during school hours? (After entry closes, before exit)
- [ ] Perimeter fencing: Intact, no gaps? (Student containment)
- [ ] Side exits: Locked from outside, accessible from inside? (Emergency compliance)
- [ ] Roof access: Locked? Secured? (Prevent unauthorized access)
- [ ] Basement/storage areas: Locked? Restricted access? (Prevent theft, injury)

#### Visitor Management
- [ ] Visitor log: Maintained at gate? (Name, time in, time out, purpose)
- [ ] Visitor ID badges: Issued and worn? (Identification)
- [ ] Restricted access: Visitors not left unsupervised near students? (Safety)
- [ ] Expected visitor list: Pre-registered? (Reduce uncertainty)
- [ ] Blacklist: Is there a list of persons not allowed on campus? (Safety protocol)

#### Surveillance
- [ ] CCTV cameras: Present at entry, main halls, parking, high-risk areas? (Coverage)
- [ ] CCTV recording: Continuous 24/7? Storage backup? (Incident investigation)
- [ ] Monitoring: Live monitoring during school hours? (Threat response)
- [ ] Maintenance: Cameras functional, lenses clean, no blind spots? (Working)

#### Staff & Key Management
- [ ] Master key: Held by principal and VP only? (Limited access)
- [ ] Key log: Who has which keys? Updated annually? (Accountability)
- [ ] Key security: Keys not left in locks or lying around? (Prevent theft, unauthorized access)
- [ ] Lock replacement: Locks changed when staff departs? (Security)

#### Digital Security
- [ ] Network: Firewalled, password-protected? (IT security)
- [ ] Student data: Encrypted, access-controlled, regularly backed up? (DPDP compliance)
- [ ] Visitor WiFi: Separate from school network? (Network security)
- [ ] Password policy: Regular changes, strong passwords required? (System security)

**Security inspection:** Monthly. Visitor management checked daily.

### Domain 3: Emergency Preparedness

#### Emergency Plans
- [ ] Fire evacuation plan: Written, posted, practiced? (Minimum 2x per year)
- [ ] Earthquake evacuation plan: Written, practiced? (If seismic zone)
- [ ] Medical emergency: Written, staff trained? (First aid, 911 protocol)
- [ ] Lockdown procedure: Written, practiced? (Active threat response)
- [ ] Weather emergency: Procedures for flooding, extreme heat, etc.? (Relevant to region)
- [ ] Missing student: Protocol for immediate response? (Kidnapping risk)

#### Emergency Drills
- [ ] Fire drill: Practiced, documented (date, time, notes)? (Last: _____)
- [ ] Earthquake drill: Practiced, documented? (Last: _____)
- [ ] Evacuation time: Documented for each building? (Target: <5 min for all students)
- [ ] Students with disabilities: Specific evacuation plans? (Wheelchairs, etc.)
- [ ] Staff debriefs: After drill, issues noted and fixed? (Continuous improvement)

#### Emergency Supplies & Equipment
- [ ] First aid kits: Multiple locations, well-stocked, unexpired items? (Every 6 months check)
- [ ] AED (Automated External Defibrillator): Present, functional, staff trained? (Cardiac response)
- [ ] Emergency contact list: Updated, accessible? (Parent emergency numbers)
- [ ] Emergency meeting point: Designated, communicated to all? (Post-evacuation assembly)
- [ ] Communication system: Backup communication (horn, whistle) if electric fails? (Evacuation signal)
- [ ] Emergency supplies: Water, blankets, basic food, medicines? (Shelter-in-place)

#### Staff Training
- [ ] First aid certification: Current? Refresher every 2 years? (Medical response)
- [ ] CPR training: Minimum 2 staff trained? (Cardiac emergency)
- [ ] Emergency response: All staff know evacuation procedure? (Tested annually)
- [ ] Mental health crisis: Staff trained on student mental health emergencies? (Suicide/self-harm)
- [ ] Intruder response: Staff know lockdown procedure? (Active shooter training)

**Drill frequency:** Fire drill at least 2x per year. Other drills at least 1x per year. Document all.

### Domain 4: Medical & Health Safety

#### Medical Facilities & Equipment
- [ ] Clinic room: Clean, equipped with basic medical supplies? (Bandages, antiseptic, pain relief)
- [ ] Trained staff: School nurse or trained first aider present during school hours? (Medical oversight)
- [ ] Medical equipment: Blood pressure monitor, thermometer, pulse oximeter? (Health monitoring)
- [ ] Emergency medicines: EpiPen (for allergies), asthma inhalers (if needed by students)? (Allergy/asthma safety)
- [ ] AED: Accessible, functional, staff trained? (Cardiac emergency)

#### Student Medical Records
- [ ] Medical history: On file for every student? (Allergies, chronic conditions, medications)
- [ ] Immunizations: Up-to-date? Verified at admission? (Communicable disease prevention)
- [ ] Emergency contacts: Current? Tested (can you reach parents immediately)? (Emergency communication)
- [ ] Medication administration: Protocol in place for prescription meds at school? (Diabetes, asthma, ADHD)
- [ ] Confidentiality: Medical records locked, access restricted? (DPDP compliance)

#### Communicable Disease Control
- [ ] Handwashing: Soap and water available, students trained? (Hygiene)
- [ ] Respiratory hygiene: Tissues, trash cans, "cover your cough" posters? (Disease control)
- [ ] Sick leave policy: Clear policy on when students/staff should stay home? (Outbreak prevention)
- [ ] Vaccination: Flu shots encouraged? Polio boosters up-to-date? (Disease prevention)
- [ ] Isolation: Protocol for student/staff who shows symptoms? (Quarantine/testing)

#### Nutrition & Food Safety
- [ ] Canteen hygiene: Certified by FSSAI? Regular inspections? (Food safety)
- [ ] Vendor verification: Vendor credentials checked annually? (Food safety)
- [ ] Allergen management: List of common allergens posted? Staff trained on nut, shellfish, etc. allergies? (Allergy safety)
- [ ] Food storage: Refrigeration working? Expiry dates checked? (Foodborne illness prevention)
- [ ] Meal planning: Balanced nutrition, dietary accommodations for students? (Health)

**Medical inspection:** Monthly. After any health incident, conduct immediate review.

---

### Domain 5: Compliance & Documentation

#### Legal & Regulatory Compliance
- [ ] Fire NOC: Current? (Annual renewal, if required)
- [ ] Building occupancy certificate: Current?
- [ ] Electrical safety certificate: Current?
- [ ] FSSAI (canteen): Current?
- [ ] DPDP Act: Parental consent collected? Data protection policy in place?
- [ ] POCSO Act: Safeguarding policy in place? Staff trained?

#### Incident & Accident Documentation
- [ ] Incident log: Maintained (all incidents documented with date, time, details, response)?
- [ ] Accident records: Any injuries? Documented? First aid applied? Parent notified?
- [ ] Serious incidents: Escalated to authorities if required? (POCSO, theft, violence)
- [ ] Insurance: School liability insurance current? Covers injuries and incidents?
- [ ] Postmortem: After any incident, was a review conducted? Lessons documented?

#### Safety Policies & Communication
- [ ] Student safety handbook: Published, distributed to all students/parents? (Rules, expectations)
- [ ] Staff safety procedures: Written, distributed to all staff? (Response protocols)
- [ ] Parent communication: Parents informed of safety measures, emergency procedures? (Transparency)
- [ ] Annual safety audit: Conducted yearly? Findings documented and acted upon?
- [ ] Safety committee: Exists? Meets quarterly to review and plan? (Governance)

**Compliance checklist:** Annual review before school year starts. Any gaps → action plan → monthly follow-up.

## How to Conduct a Safety Audit

### Step 1: Assign a Safety Audit Team
- Principal (overall responsibility)
- Vice Principal or Admin Officer (detailed inspection)
- One HOD (academic facilities)
- One support staff (maintenance perspective)

**Outside expert (optional):** Bring in a fire safety auditor or building engineer if concerns exist.

### Step 2: Schedule & Notify
- Set specific date (e.g., last Friday of March before new academic year)
- Notify staff, caretakers, maintenance team
- Allocate 2–3 hours for thorough inspection

### Step 3: Inspect Using Checklist
- Walk the entire campus systematically (building by building, room by room)
- Document each item: ✓ (OK), ✗ (Problem), ? (Needs clarification)
- Take photos of concerns (helps when requesting repairs)
- Note priority (critical, high, medium, low)

### Step 4: Document Findings
- List all gaps/concerns
- Categorize by domain (physical, security, emergency, medical, compliance)
- Assign priority level

### Step 5: Action Plan
- For each concern: Who will fix? By when? Budget needed?
- Critical issues: Fix within 2 weeks
- High issues: Fix within 1–2 months
- Medium issues: Fix within 6 months
- Low issues: Address as resources allow

### Step 6: Communicate & Follow-Up
- Share findings with Managing Committee
- Request budget approval for repairs
- Monthly follow-up: What's been fixed? What's pending?
- Reassess in 3 months (is action plan on track?)

## FAQ

**Q1: How much does a professional safety audit cost?**  
A: ₹10,000–50,000 depending on school size and auditor expertise. Worth it for detailed review. For routine annual audits, internal team can do most work.

**Q2: What if we can't afford to fix all safety issues immediately?**  
A: Prioritize critical issues (fire safety, electrical hazards, medical emergencies) first. Budget other issues quarterly. Document your action plan and timeline for parents and board.

**Q3: Who should be trained in first aid?**  
A: Minimum 3–5 staff (including school nurse if available). Teachers and office staff who might be first responders.

**Q4: How often should we conduct fire drills?**  
A: Minimum 2x per year. Once per term is good practice. More for high-risk buildings.

**Q5: Should we conduct mock intruder drills?**  
A: It's good practice (especially high-risk schools), but handle sensitively. Brief parents beforehand. Don't traumatize students. Train staff on appropriate response.

**Q6: What if we find a serious safety violation during audit?**  
A: Stop that activity immediately. Inform parents. File incident report. Document action taken. If required by law, escalate to authorities.
