# Boundary Walls and Fencing: Safety

Secure boundaries protect students. Maintain fencing and walls properly.

**Purpose** - Keep unauthorized people out, contain students on campus, security perimeter, clear boundaries, designated entry/exit.

**Inspection** - Regular fencing checks, damage repair quick, holes and gaps sealed, climbing prevention, structural integrity, security maintained.

**Height** - Minimum height standards, hard to climb over, appropriate for grade level (higher for older students), balancing security and openness.

**Materials** - Chain-link, solid panels, brick walls, security fencing, maintenance requirements understood, durability, weather resistance.

**Entry Points** - Gates locked, keypad or key systems, monitoring of entry, visitor entry procedures, staff exits secured, controlled access.

**Climbing Prevention** - No footholds, no handholds, anti-climb features, landscaping management, visibility into campus, safety not paranoia.

**Maintenance** - Regular inspection, repairs completed, vegetation trimmed, rust prevention, durability extended, safety standards maintained.

**Emergency Access** - Fire exits available, emergency vehicle access, evacuation routes, lockdown provisions, balance security with emergency needs.

**Community Visibility** - Not fortress-like appearance, balance security with welcoming, visibility into campus, open community feeling, appropriate security.

**Signage** - Clear entry/exit signage, visitor expectations, no trespassing signs, emergency information, directional clarity.

**Special Needs** - ADA accessible gates, automatic openers, emergency access for wheelchairs, accommodations made, inclusive design.

Strong perimeter security prevents unauthorized access while maintaining a welcoming school environment.
