## Emergency Response Plan for Schools: Comprehensive Procedures

**Every school must have a written emergency response plan for fires, medical emergencies, violence, natural disasters, and missing students. This plan is not just a document—it's a practiced discipline. This guide shows you how to build, document, and execute an emergency response plan.**

---

## Core Emergency Types & Response Protocols

### Medical Emergency
**Immediate (0–2 min):**
- Call emergency services (911 or local ambulance)
- Alert school nurse/first aider to proceed to location
- If unconscious: Start CPR if trained

**Short-term (2–30 min):**
- Stabilize patient (first aid, do not move unless necessary)
- Alert Principal and parents
- Document: time, location, patient name, symptoms, response

**Follow-up:**
- Hospital coordination (who accompanies? who informs family?)
- Incident report filed within 24 hours
- Medical review: could this have been prevented?

---

### Fire
**Immediate (0–5 min):**
- Evacuate per fire evacuation plan
- Assembly at designated point
- Account for all students/staff
- Do NOT re-enter building

**5–30 min:**
- Wait for fire department all-clear
- Communicate with parents

**Follow-up:**
- Debrief (what worked, what didn't)
- Document lessons learned

---

### Violence/Intruder
**Immediate:**
- Lockdown (shelter in place)
- Call police (911)
- If safe, hide students; arm doors with barricades
- Silent (no noise to alert intruder)

**During:**
- Wait for police all-clear
- Do not open doors

**After:**
- Students to medical/counseling
- Parents informed
- Full investigation

---

### Missing Student
**Immediate (0–15 min):**
- Verify student is missing (check bathrooms, nurse, office)
- Contact parents/guardian
- Notify police (if child safety is at risk)
- Search campus with staff (safe perimeter only)

**15–60 min:**
- Police take over search
- Coordinate with police (student photo, clothing, identifying marks)

**Follow-up:**
- Incident report
- Review security (how did student leave without notice?)

---

## Communication Protocol

**Immediate Notification (first 30 minutes):**
- Principal notifies parents of affected children
- "Your child was involved in [incident]. Here's what happened. Here's what we're doing."

**School-wide communication (1–2 hours):**
- If incident is major, notify all parents/staff
- "All students are safe. Here's what occurred. Here's how we're supporting affected students."

**Media management:**
- Principal is sole spokesperson
- No details shared without police/medical clearance
- "We are cooperating with authorities and prioritizing student safety."

**Follow-up communication (24–48 hours):**
- Detailed incident summary
- Support resources (counseling, medical)
- Lessons learned, prevention measures

---

## Documentation

**Every emergency requires:**
- Incident report (time, location, what happened, response, outcome)
- Medical records (if injury/illness involved)
- Police report number (if applicable)
- Witness statements (if applicable)
- Parent notifications log (who was called, when, what was said)
- Follow-up actions and timeline

## FAQ

**Q1: Who should write the emergency response plan?**  
A: Principal + VP + senior staff + safety committee. External expert (fire chief, police) can review.

**Q2: How often should we practice emergencies?**  
A: Fire drill 2x/year. Other emergencies 1x/year. More frequent for high-risk scenarios.

**Q3: Should students be involved in emergency training?**  
A: Yes. Age-appropriate training on evacuation, lockdown, staying calm. Builds confidence and compliance.

**Q4: What if we can't reach parents immediately?**  
A: Document attempt. Keep trying. In meantime, care for student at school.

**Q5: Should we have a crisis counselor on call?**  
A: Strongly recommended for schools. Major incidents (injury, death, violence) require professional mental health support.
