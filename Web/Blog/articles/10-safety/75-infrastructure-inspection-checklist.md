# Infrastructure Inspection Checklist: Comprehensive Guide

Infrastructure inspection checklists ensure systematic review of facility conditions. Without checklists, inspections become inconsistent, hazards slip through, and compliance audits reveal missed problems. This guide provides actionable infrastructure inspection frameworks.

## Why Systematic Inspections Matter

**Hazard Detection**
Cracks in walls, loose ceiling tiles, rust in electrical panels—these become catastrophic if missed. Checklists ensure nothing is overlooked.

**Compliance**
Schools must pass accreditation, fire safety, and structural audits. Inspection records prove due diligence.

**Insurance**
Claims are denied if school failed basic maintenance. Documentation protects insurance claims.

**Cost Avoidance**
Minor crack → water damage → structural failure. Catching small issues prevents major repairs.

## Building Inspection Checklist

**Exterior Inspection**

- **Foundation & Walls:** Cracks in foundation, efflorescence (salt deposits), water seepage, tilting
- **Roof:** Missing shingles, moss/algae growth, sagging, water stains on underside
- **Gutters:** Blockages, rust, loose fasteners, proper drainage slope
- **Windows & Doors:** Broken glass, rusted frames, gaps, weatherstripping condition
- **Paint & Siding:** Peeling paint, rust, rotting wood, algae growth

**Interior Inspection**

- **Ceiling:** Water stains, sagging, loose tiles, cracks, paint peeling
- **Walls:** Water stains, cracks, paint condition, graffiti/damage
- **Flooring:** Cracked tiles, loose boards, slippery surfaces, subsidence
- **Stairs:** Loose handrails, broken steps, worn treads, adequate lighting

**Electrical Systems**

- **Main Panel:** Rust, loose connections, burning smell, tripping breakers
- **Outlets & Switches:** Non-functional, exposed wiring, water damage
- **Lighting:** Flickering, dead bulbs, inadequate brightness
- **Emergency Lighting:** Functionality, battery status, operational during outages

**Plumbing Inspection**

- **Water Supply:** Low pressure, discolored water, leaks, proper insulation
- **Fixtures:** Dripping taps, slow drains, broken valves
- **Pipes:** Visible corrosion, leaks, proper support, no burst risk
- **Septic/Drainage:** Overflow, foul smell, proper maintenance records

**HVAC Systems**

- **Air Conditioners:** Unusual noise, refrigerant leaks, filter condition, proper airflow
- **Heaters:** Functionality, fuel supply, proper venting
- **Ventilation:** Duct cleanliness, air circulation, humidity control
- **Thermostats:** Accuracy, responsiveness

**Fire Safety Inspection**

- **Fire Extinguishers:** Pressure gauge in green zone, pin intact, inspection tag current
- **Emergency Lights:** Functionality, battery status, placement
- **Exit Signs:** Illuminated, unobstructed, visible from all points
- **Sprinkler Systems:** Proper coverage, activation mechanisms, water pressure
- **Alarms:** Functionality test, batteries working, proper placement

**Safety & Accessibility**

- **Handrails:** Secure, proper height (34-38 inches), complete runs
- **Ramps:** Proper slope (1:12 ratio), handrails, non-slip surfaces
- **Doors:** Proper width (32+ inches), operational locks, emergency exit markers
- **Signage:** Exit signs, hazard warnings, direction markers
- **Storage:** Proper shelving, hazardous materials storage, no blocking exits

**Cleanliness & Sanitation**

- **Floors:** Clean, no hazards, proper drainage
- **Toilets:** Functionality, cleanliness, supply levels
- **Water Coolers:** Cleanliness, proper maintenance
- **Waste Disposal:** Bins adequate, daily emptying, no overflows

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should full building inspections happen?**
Minimum: annually. Better: semi-annually (beginning and end of academic year). Critical systems (electrical, plumbing, fire safety): quarterly.

**What if inspection finds major defects requiring major repair?**
Document in priority order. Secure emergency funding (insurance claims, parent fundraising, government grants). Develop phased repair plan (critical first, cosmetic last).

**Should we hire external inspectors or do internal audits?**
Mix both. In-house inspectors (cheaper, frequent). External auditors annually (objective, compliance verification). External specialists (electrical, structural) for technical assessment.

**What's the liability if we document a problem but don't fix it immediately?**
High. Document incident, take mitigation measures (barrier off area, post warnings), create repair timeline. Insurance may deny claims if hazard was known but ignored.
