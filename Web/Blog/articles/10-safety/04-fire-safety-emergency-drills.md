## Fire Safety and Emergency Drills: Preparation and Response

**Fire is one of the most immediate threats to a school. Every principal must have a fire evacuation plan, conduct regular drills, maintain equipment, and train staff. This guide covers fire prevention, detection, evacuation procedures, emergency drills, and post-incident response. Implementation of these practices significantly reduces fire risk and ensures rapid, safe evacuation if fire does occur.**

---

## Core Infrastructure & Prevention

**Fire Safety Checklist:**
- [ ] Fire extinguishers: Every 500–600 sq ft area, properly mounted, easily accessible
- [ ] Extinguisher maintenance: Annual servicing (check tag for date)
- [ ] Fire alarm system: Installed, tested monthly, functional
- [ ] Emergency lighting: Battery-backed, illuminates exit routes
- [ ] Fire hose: Accessible, tested quarterly
- [ ] Sprinkler system (if building size warrants): Functional, tested annually
- [ ] Fire exits: Clearly marked, unlocked from inside, unobstructed
- [ ] Fire NOC: Current (government certification of fire safety compliance)
- [ ] No flammable materials: Stored safely, away from heat sources

## Evacuation Plan & Procedures

**Evacuation Route Planning:**
- Identify primary exit route from each classroom, lab, office
- Identify secondary exit route (for if primary is blocked)
- Mark exits clearly with signage
- Ensure exits are clear (no stored materials blocking)
- Test evacuation time (target: <5 min for entire building)

**Assembly Point:**
- Designate outdoor assembly area (away from building, traffic, utilities)
- Mark with sign or flag
- Brief all staff and students on location
- Practice reaching assembly point during drills

**Evacuation Procedures:**
- **Alert:** Upon discovery of fire, activate fire alarm (or verbal alert if alarm fails)
- **First responder:** Teacher/staff nearest fire attempts to extinguish (only if small, confident)
- **Evacuation:** Teachers evacuate students immediately via primary route
  - Take class roster (to account for all students at assembly)
  - Close doors behind you (reduces smoke spread)
  - Walk quickly, don't run (avoid panic/falls)
  - Leave belongings (don't delay for backpacks)
- **Assembly:** Report to assembly point, staff account for students via roster
- **Communication:** Report status to Principal (all students accounted for? any missing? any injuries?)

## Emergency Drills: Best Practices

**Fire Drill Schedule:**
- Minimum 2 drills per year (one per term)
- Unannounced (surprise drills more realistic than scheduled)
- Vary time (morning, afternoon, different days of week)
- Include lockdown drill (1x per year, if applicable to region)

**Fire Drill Protocol:**
1. **Announce:** "Fire drill, evacuate building now"
2. **Evacuate:** All teachers evacuate classrooms via planned routes
3. **Assemble:** Students gather at assembly point
4. **Roster:** Teachers account for all students
5. **Report:** Status reported to Principal (✓ all students accounted for)
6. **Debrief:** Note evacuation time, any issues, improvements needed
7. **Document:** Record date, time, participants, evacuation time, issues

**Drill Evaluation:**
- Did evacuation complete within 5 min? (If not, optimize routes)
- Any students missing? (If yes, why? how to prevent next time?)
- Any exits blocked? (Clear them)
- Any confusion about procedures? (Retrain staff/students)

## Staff Training & Responsibilities

**All Staff Should Know:**
- Fire alarm location and how to activate
- Evacuation route from their classroom/office
- Assembly point location
- How to account for students
- Whom to report to (Principal)

**Designated Roles:**
- **Fire Warden (usually Principal or VP):** Oversees evacuation, accounts for all staff/students, communicates with fire services
- **Class Teachers:** Evacuate their class, account for students, report to warden
- **Support Staff:** Assist evacuation, check bathrooms/unused classrooms for stragglers, direct traffic at assembly point
- **First Aider:** Available if injuries occur

**Training Schedule:**
- New staff: Trained on evacuation on day 1
- Refresher: Annual drill participates for all staff
- Advanced: First aid/CPR certification for 2–3 staff

## Equipment Maintenance & Compliance

**Fire Extinguisher:**
- Visible location (not hidden behind furniture)
- Inspection tag visible (last serviced date)
- Annual servicing by certified vendor (document each service)
- Training: Staff know where they are, how to use them (P.A.S.S: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep)

**Fire Alarm:**
- Tested monthly (push test button, confirms alarm sounds)
- Visual and audio alert functional
- All staff know location and how to use

**Fire Hose:**
- Visible, accessible
- Monthly pressure check
- Quarterly full test by vendor

**Emergency Lighting:**
- Battery backup functional (test monthly)
- Illuminates all exits, hallways, stairs

**Fire NOC Renewal:**
- Obtained initially when school opens
- Renewed every 1–3 years (check local requirement)
- If expired: School can be temporarily shut down by fire department
- Mark renewal date in compliance calendar; renew 1–2 months before expiry

## Incident Response (If Fire Occurs)

**Immediate:**
1. Activate alarm
2. Call fire service (911 or local emergency number)
3. Evacuate building
4. Account for all persons at assembly point
5. Never re-enter building for any reason

**After Evacuation:**
- Communicate with parents: "Evacuation completed safely. All students accounted for. Awaiting all-clear from fire department."
- Wait for fire department to clear building
- Account for any injuries, provide first aid
- File incident report with fire department

**Post-Incident:**
- Debrief staff: What happened? What did we do well? What needs improvement?
- Review evacuation video (if recorded) with staff
- Adjust procedures if needed
- Communicate to parents: full account of incident, actions taken, no risk to school

## Special Considerations

**Students with Disabilities:**
- Mobility challenges: Plan evacuation assistance (buddy system, accessible exit routes)
- Hearing impaired: Visual alarm, staff escort
- Sight impaired: Verbal guidance, familiar escort
- Practice their evacuation route monthly

**Preschool/Younger Students:**
- Simpler explanations ("We practice leaving quickly")
- More frequent practice (monthly vs. bi-annual)
- Shorter assembly point distance (younger kids tire)
- Extra staff support during evacuation

**Hostel/Boarding Schools:**
- Night evacuation drill (students may be sleeping)
- Clear procedures for hostel warden
- Assembly point accessible at night

## FAQ

**Q1: How often should we conduct fire drills?**  
A: Minimum 2x per year (one per term). More frequent (monthly) for younger grades.

**Q2: What if we don't have a fire extinguisher in every area?**  
A: Violates fire code. Budget for installation. Not having them puts liability on you.

**Q3: Should we practice with students how to use fire extinguishers?**  
A: No. Students shouldn't fight fires (safety risk). Only trained staff use extinguishers. Students focus on evacuation.

**Q4: What if a fire drill is scheduled but a critical meeting is in progress?**  
A: Stop the meeting. Evacuate. Fire safety is always prioritized.

**Q5: Should we conduct fire drills unannounced or scheduled?**  
A: Both. Scheduled drills train, unannounced drills test readiness. Vary times to keep staff/students ready.

**Q6: What if we don't have a fire NOC yet?**  
A: Apply immediately. Until obtained, you're not compliant. Risk of suspension by education department.
