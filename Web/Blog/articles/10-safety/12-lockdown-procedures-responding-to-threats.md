# Lockdown Procedures: Responding to Threats

Lockdown procedures protect students and staff from threats. Clear procedures and training are essential.

**Threat Assessment** - Identify potential threats, monitor warning signs, threat assessment team, intervention protocols, prevention focused.

**Lockdown Signal** - Clear signal for lockdown, staff recognize immediately, announcement clear and precise, consistent procedure, reliable system.

**Procedures** - Close and lock doors, turn off lights, silence phones, move away from doors/windows, accounting for all present, wait for all-clear.

**Communication** - Alert appropriate authorities (police), staff-to-staff communication, parent notification procedures, ongoing updates, transparent communication.

**Special Considerations** - Students in hallways, bathrooms, cafeteria, library, outdoor areas, field trips, all locations covered, alternatives understood.

**Staff Roles** - Door locks secured, communication liaison, first aid responders, student supervision, special needs support, clear assignments.

**Training** - Drills practiced regularly (at least annually), staff know procedures cold, students understand and comply, muscle memory developed.

**Mental Health** - Trauma from lockdowns addressed, counseling available, psychological impact recognized, support provided, normalcy restored gradually.

**Communication with Parents** - Explain procedures, practice drills, why they matter, reassurance of safety, contact procedures, emergency information.

**Equipment** - Door barricades, communication devices, first aid supplies, emergency contact lists, readiness maintained, regular checks.

**Recovery** - After incident, counseling, debriefing, procedure improvements, community support, healing process, moving forward.

Lockdown procedures save lives. Prepare thoroughly and maintain readiness.
