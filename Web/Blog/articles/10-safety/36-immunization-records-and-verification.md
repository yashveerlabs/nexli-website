# Immunization Records and Verification

Immunization records are mandated by law. Verification ensures school health and compliance.

**Required Vaccines** - State requirements vary, common: polio, measles, diphtheria, tetanus, hepatitis, varicella, meningococcal, documentation.

**Verification** - Records reviewed, documentation complete, from healthcare provider, physician stamp, dates noted, accuracy verified.

**Exemptions** - Medical exemptions for allergies/conditions, religious exemptions where allowed, state requirements vary, documentation needed.

**Deadlines** - Registration deadlines, vaccination completion dates, compliance before attendance, extensions in circumstances, rules enforced.

**Communication** - Parents notified of requirements, documentation requested, deadline reminders, resources for vaccines, assistance if needed.

**Record Storage** - Secure storage, confidential, organized, accessible, updated, backup copies, electronic systems, compliance with regulations.

**Non-Compliance** - Identification of incomplete records, parent notification, opportunities for compliance, exclusion if necessary, legal compliance.

**Special Circumstances** - Homeschooled students, new enrollees, transfer students, records transfer procedures, documentation requirements, verification.

**Public Health** - Disease outbreak response, vaccination clinics, community partnerships, prevention education, collaboration with health departments.

**Data Tracking** - Vaccination rates monitored, trends identified, compliance tracked, reporting to health department, population health analysis.

**Staff Education** - Requirements understood, communication consistent, importance emphasized, resource availability, compliance culture.

**Review** - Annual audit of records, compliance assessment, improvements needed, documentation systems, accuracy verification.

Immunization verification protects student health and ensures legal compliance.
