# Earthquake and Natural Disaster Preparedness

Natural disasters threaten school safety. Comprehensive preparedness saves lives.

**Hazard Assessment** - What disasters likely in region? Earthquakes, hurricanes, flooding, tornadoes, wildfires. Understand specific risks. Plan accordingly.

**Emergency Action Plan** - Comprehensive disaster plan, procedures documented, staff trained, equipment and supplies, communication systems.

**Evacuation Procedures** - Routes clear, assembly areas designated, accountability process, special needs accommodated, practiced regularly.

**Shelter-in-Place** - Lockdown procedures, safe locations, supplies available, communication maintained, emergency contacts reached.

**Earthquake Preparedness** - Building earthquake-safe, secured heavy items, drop-cover-hold drills, staff trained, supplies for aftermath.

**Emergency Supplies** - First aid kits, water, blankets, food, batteries, flashlights, medications, sufficient for hours, regularly checked.

**Communication Plan** - How to reach families, staff communication, public information, media relations, regular updates, reunification procedures.

**Staff Training** - Regular drills and practice, everyone knows procedures, special roles assigned, confidence built, competency ensured.

**Special Needs** - Students with disabilities accommodated, mobility issues addressed, medical equipment available, individual plans developed, inclusive procedures.

**After-Disaster** - Immediate response procedures, injured care, missing persons, recovery operations, counseling, rebuilding.

**Regular Review** - Annual review and update, new staff trained, procedures refined, communication tested, readiness maintained.

Preparedness prevents panic and saves lives. Invest in thorough disaster planning.
