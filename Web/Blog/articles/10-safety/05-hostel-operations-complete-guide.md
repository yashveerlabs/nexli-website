## Hostel Operations: Complete Management Guide for Boarding Schools

**Hostel management is a 24/7 responsibility. Unlike day schools, hostels are responsible for student wellbeing round-the-clock: accommodation, meals, health, discipline, and pastoral care. This guide covers room allocation, roll-call procedures, medical protocols, safety, exeat (weekend leave) tracking, and best practices for a well-run hostel that prioritizes student safety and development.**

---

## Core Hostel Operations

### Room Allocation & Block Management

**Room Assignment Process:**
- Map hostels by block (Boys, Girls, staff, etc.)
- Assign rooms considering: academic year, behavioral fit, medical needs
- Create rooming assignment list (permanent for academic year)
- Document special requests (medical conditions requiring specific room)
- Communicate assignments to students and parents before move-in

**Capacity Planning:**
- Calculate total beds available per block
- Project enrollment by gender
- Plan new blocks if growing beyond 80% capacity
- Budget for regular bed/furniture replacement

---

### Daily Roll-Call & Attendance

**Morning Roll-Call:**
- Time: 7:00–7:30 AM (before breakfast)
- Warden/staff checks each room
- Mark: Present, Absent (with reason), Sick (needs medical attention)
- Absent without leave: Escalate to Principal immediately
- Document in roll-call register

**Evening Roll-Call:**
- Time: 8:00–9:00 PM (before lights out)
- Confirm all students in hostel (not out without permission)
- Check on sick students
- Staff briefing: any issues

**Weekly Reporting:**
- Summary to Principal: attendance rate, absences, medical cases, behavioral issues
- Parent update: "Your child attended 6/7 roll-calls; 1 day medical leave"

---

### Exeat (Weekend Leave) Tracking

**Request Process:**
- Submit 1 week prior (time out/in, destination, with whom)
- Warden verifies parent approval and timeline
- Approval documented with date and time
- Parent confirmation call before student leaves

**Tracking:**
- Exit record: Time, who picked up, contact
- Return record: Time, any incidents
- Late return: Immediate parent contact (safety check)
- Violation: Unapproved absence = discipline + parent meeting

---

### Medical Care & Health Management

**Medical Staff:**
- 24/7 nurse or first aider on duty
- Medical clinic with basic equipment
- After-hours emergency protocol

**Health Protocols:**
- Immunization verification at admission
- Weekly health inspection
- Hygiene training (handwashing, cleanliness)
- Quarantine protocol for infectious disease

**Medication Management:**
- Locked clinic cabinet storage
- Nurse administers prescriptions
- Emergency medications (EpiPen, inhalers) accessible
- Documentation of all doses

**Mental Health Support:**
- Weekly counselor check-ins for at-risk students
- Homesickness support
- Crisis protocol for mental health emergencies

---

### Facilities & Comfort

**Accommodation:**
- Adequate ventilation and lighting
- Quality beds and mattresses
- Storage for belongings
- Bathroom: 1 toilet per 5 students
- Common areas: study, recreation, TV rooms

**Maintenance:**
- Weekly cleaning (all areas)
- Monthly deep cleaning
- Repair requests: fix within 24 hours

**Nutrition:**
- Balanced meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks)
- Dietary accommodations
- Weekly menu published
- Daily quality monitoring

---

### Discipline & Safety

**House Rules:**
- Clear, posted visibly
- Known consequences
- Consistent enforcement

**Common Rules:**
- Lights out at designated time
- No phones during study hours
- Mandatory communal meals
- No unauthorized guests
- Respect for shared spaces

**Violation Response:**
- Minor: warning or room restriction
- Major: formal inquiry, parent meeting, possible suspension
- All violations documented

**Security:**
- Access restricted (card/PIN/keys)
- Visitor policy (pre-approved, supervised)
- CCTV in common areas (not private spaces)
- 24/7 security presence

## FAQ

**Q1: What staff-to-student ratio should hostels maintain?**  
A: Minimum 1 staff per 15 students. For younger students (under 14): 1 per 10. For special needs: 1:5 or 1:1.

**Q2: How often should hostel inspections occur?**  
A: Weekly (cleanliness and safety). Monthly (deeper inspection for maintenance).

**Q3: Can students receive packages from home?**  
A: Yes, with approval. Check for contraband (alcohol, drugs, weapons). Limit quantity.

**Q4: What do we do about homesick younger students?**  
A: Frequent calls home. Video calls with family. Buddy system. Counselor support. Most adjust within 2–3 weeks.

**Q5: Should hostels be co-ed?**  
A: Typically gender-separated for safety and comfort. Mixed common areas (dining, study) okay with separate sleeping blocks.

**Q6: What if a student refuses to return from exeat?**  
A: Escalate immediately. Contact parents. May need counselor involvement. Document and plan resolution.
