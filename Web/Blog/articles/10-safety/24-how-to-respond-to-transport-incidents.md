# How to Respond to Transport Incidents

Incident response is critical. Clear procedures minimize harm and ensure proper investigation.

**Emergency Response** - First aid provided, emergency services called if needed, student safety priority, driver stays calm, direction clear.

**Communication** - School notified immediately, parents contacted, information accurate, updates regular, transparent communication.

**Scene Control** - Safe location found, other traffic diverted if necessary, hazards contained, witnesses identified, scene documented.

**Injury Management** - First aid administered, ambulance called if needed, injuries documented, medical evaluation completed, follow-up care arranged.

**Investigation** - What happened, contributing factors, documentation thorough, photos/video if appropriate, witness statements, incident report completed.

**Documentation** - Detailed record of incident, times, locations, people involved, injuries, actions taken, police report if applicable.

**Support** - Counseling for affected students, staff support, family notifications, grief counseling if needed, trauma-informed approach.

**Policy Review** - After serious incident, procedures reviewed, improvements identified, preventive measures implemented.

**Learning** - Lessons extracted, staff training updated, communication improved, prevention focused, continuous improvement.

**Accountability** - Responsibility assigned if fault found, consequences appropriate, documentation maintained, learning emphasized over blame.

**Closure** - Family communication, recovery planning, return to normalcy, appreciation for responders, closure process.

**Prevention** - Similar incidents prevented, procedures refined, equipment updated, training enhanced, proactive measures.

Incident response procedures protect students and guide proper response.
