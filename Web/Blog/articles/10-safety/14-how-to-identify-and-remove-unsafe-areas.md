# How to Identify and Remove Unsafe Areas

Physical environment must be safe. Regular inspection identifies and removes hazards.

**Safety Inspection** - Regular walking audit, note hazards, photograph problems, prioritize issues, track corrections, systematic approach.

**Common Hazards** - Broken equipment, sharp edges, trip hazards, loose railings, faulty stairs, unstable shelves, blocked exits, poor lighting, slippery surfaces.

**Specific Areas** - Science labs, art rooms, PE facilities, playground equipment, parking lots, grounds, cafeteria, storage areas, all spaces reviewed.

**Outdoor Spaces** - Playground maintenance, equipment safety, fall zones, ground impact, drainage, lighting, fencing integrity, natural hazards.

**Indoor Spaces** - Hallway clutter, exit signs visible, lighting adequate, floor conditions, handrails secure, pipes/wires protected, temperature control.

**Preventive Maintenance** - Equipment serviced regularly, repairs completed, preventive schedule, contracts with vendors, quality assurance, safety standards.

**Documentation** - Photos of problems, written descriptions, dates noted, corrections tracked, records maintained, accountability system.

**Staff Training** - Hazard recognition, reporting procedures, quick fixes for minor issues, who to contact, ownership of safety.

**Student Involvement** - Student safety patrols, hazard reporting, ownership in safety, student suggestions, responsibility building.

**Liability Management** - Hazards documented and corrected, incidents reported, insurance updated, legal protection, duty of care fulfilled.

**Budget** - Safety improvements prioritized, funding adequate, preventive better than reactive, cost-benefit analysis, long-term savings.

Regular hazard removal prevents injuries and demonstrates duty of care.
