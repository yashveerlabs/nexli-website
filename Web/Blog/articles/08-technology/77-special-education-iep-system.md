## What IEP Management Technology Does for Schools

An Individualized Education Program is a legally recognized document that specifies the educational goals, support services, and accommodations a student with a disability needs in order to access appropriate education. Managing IEPs on paper in binders creates serious problems: files get lost, updates are undocumented, therapy logs are separated from the education plan, and when the child moves to a new class or school the history is incomplete.

A digital IEP management system keeps the entire record in one place: the initial assessment, the goals set, the accommodations provided, the therapy schedule, progress notes from each session, and the annual review outcomes.

### The Components of a Complete IEP Record

**Student profile with disability documentation:** Diagnosis, assessment reports, the specific functional limitations that affect learning, and the date of the most recent professional evaluation. This is the foundation on which the IEP is built.

**Educational goals:** Goals should be specific and measurable. "Will improve reading fluency to 60 words per minute by March 2027" is a goal. "Will improve reading" is not. The system should store the baseline measurement, the target, the timeline, and the method of measurement.

**Accommodations and modifications:** What the school will provide in the classroom. Extended time on tests, preferential seating, oral examination options, modified question formats, a scribe, assistive technology. Each accommodation should be documented with the date it was put in place.

**Therapy schedule:** If the student receives speech therapy, occupational therapy, physiotherapy, or any other specialist support, the schedule and therapist assignment should be recorded in the system. This allows the class teacher to know when the student will be out of class and for how long.

**Therapy session log:** Each session the student attends with a specialist should be logged: date, duration, activities covered, the student's response, and any notes for the next session. Over time this builds a picture of progress or areas where the approach needs to change.

**CwSN register:** Schools in India are required to maintain a register of Children with Special Needs under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016 (RPWD Act). The register documents each CwSN student's name, disability type, current class, support provided, and status. A digital system can generate this register automatically from the existing IEP data.

**Annual review record:** The IEP is reviewed at least once per year. The review meeting should be documented: who attended (parents, teachers, specialists, the student if appropriate), what was discussed, and what changes were made to goals or accommodations.

### RPWD Act 2016 Compliance

The Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016 mandates that all government and aided schools provide inclusive education for students with disabilities. The Act covers 21 categories of disability including visual impairment, hearing impairment, locomotor disability, intellectual disability, autism spectrum disorder, specific learning disabilities, and others.

Compliance requires the school to maintain records demonstrating that each CwSN student has an individualized plan, receives the accommodations specified in that plan, and that the plan is reviewed regularly. Inspections by state education authorities can request these records. Paper systems make compliance demonstrations slow and error-prone. Digital systems make it straightforward.

### Why Integration With the Broader School System Matters

The IEP does not exist in isolation. The student attends classes. Their attendance is tracked. They sit for examinations with accommodations. Their progress is reported to parents. A standalone IEP tool that does not connect to attendance, academics, and parent communication creates duplicate work and gaps.

When the IEP system is part of the school ERP, the class teacher sees the student's accommodation requirements when entering marks. The attendance system flags when the student has missed therapy sessions. The parent portal shows parents what goals are set and whether progress is being made. The examination system applies documented accommodations such as extra time or a scribe automatically when the exam schedule is generated.

### What Schools Get Wrong with SPED Management

The most common failure is treating the IEP as a document produced once a year at a review meeting and filed away until the next review. The IEP is a living plan. It should be consulted by every teacher who works with that student, every week.

The second failure is collecting data for the CwSN register but not using it. The register should inform resource planning. If there are twelve students with hearing impairment across the school, the school should have an FM system or sign language support proportionate to that need, and the principal should be able to see that number in a dashboard.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is an IEP legally required in Indian schools?**
A: Under the RPWD Act 2016, government and government-aided schools are required to provide individualized support for CwSN students. While the Act does not mandate the specific format, an Individualized Education Program is the standard framework for documenting and delivering that support. Private unaided schools are also expected to meet the spirit of inclusive education requirements.

**Q: Who should be involved in creating an IEP?**
A: At minimum, the IEP should involve the student's parents, the class teacher, the SPED coordinator or resource teacher, and any specialist therapists involved with the student. For older students, the student themselves should be included in the conversation about their goals. A representative from school administration is useful to ensure resource commitments are realistic.

**Q: How often should an IEP be reviewed?**
A: At least once per year. In practice, informal reviews should happen every term to assess whether goals are on track and whether accommodations are working. Formal reviews, with documentation and parent sign-off, are annual minimum.

**Q: What is the difference between an accommodation and a modification?**
A: An accommodation changes how a student accesses learning without changing what they are expected to learn. Extended time is an accommodation. A modification changes the content expectations. A modified curriculum with fewer learning objectives is a modification. Both can be appropriate depending on the student's needs, but they have different implications for assessment and reporting.

**Q: How does a digital IEP system support parent communication?**
A: A digital system with a parent portal allows parents to review their child's goals, see when therapy sessions occurred, and read progress notes between formal review meetings. This keeps parents informed and reduces the chance of disagreements at the annual review because parents have been part of the ongoing process.
