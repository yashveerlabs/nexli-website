## School Data Analytics: Starting With What Matters

School data analytics begins with a simple question: what decisions do you need to make, and what data would help you make them better? Schools generate enormous amounts of data daily, but most of it sits unused in registers, spreadsheets, and disconnected software. The schools that benefit from analytics are not the ones collecting the most data; they are the ones consistently reading a small set of meaningful indicators.

### Attendance Trends

Attendance is the most actionable dataset in a school because it connects to almost everything else: academic performance, fee collection, student welfare, and government compliance.

**What to track:**

- Overall attendance rate by class and section (daily, weekly, rolling 4-week average)
- Individual students with attendance below 75% (the threshold for most state boards)
- Days of the week with consistently lower attendance (often Monday and Friday)
- Attendance by subject teacher (are students skipping specific teachers' classes?)

**How to read it:**

An overall attendance rate of 85% sounds acceptable until you break it down. If three students are absent every single day, they are dragging the average down while the other 37 students are at 95%. Conversely, an average of 90% can hide a class where 20 students are at 75% attendance and barely eligible for exams.

Look at trends, not just snapshots. A class that was at 92% in July and dropped to 76% by October has a problem developing over time. A class that has been at 76% since June has a chronic problem that needs a different response.

### Fee Collection Patterns

Fee data tells you about cash flow and collection efficiency. The key metrics:

- **Collection rate by due date:** What percentage of fees due in a given month are collected within the same month?
- **Outstanding by days overdue:** How much is owed at 0-30 days, 30-60 days, 60-90 days, 90+ days?
- **Outstanding by class:** Are defaults concentrated in certain sections or fee categories?
- **Defaulter trend:** Is the number of students with outstanding dues growing or shrinking month over month?

**How to read it:**

A collection rate of 90% in the first week after a due date is healthy. A collection rate of 60% with 30% still outstanding after 30 days indicates that reminders are not reaching parents effectively, or that families are genuinely struggling and need payment plan options.

Fee outstanding broken down by class sometimes reveals that higher-class parents pay later (often an income timing pattern) while lower-class defaults are more likely to be persistent. This matters because the recovery strategy should differ.

### Academic Performance Distributions

Marks data is the most politically sensitive data in a school. A few principles for reading it honestly:

**Look at distributions, not averages.** A class average of 68% tells you almost nothing. A distribution showing 5 students below 40%, 25 students between 40-75%, and 10 students above 75% tells you the class has a small group at serious risk and a large middle group to support.

**Compare year-over-year, not just class-to-class.** Class 8B's average being lower than Class 8A's could reflect student composition, not teaching quality. The same section's marks dropping from one term to the next is a cleaner signal.

**Correlation, not causation.** If attendance is below 75% and marks are below 40%, the student needs support. But attendance is not necessarily causing low marks; both may have a common cause (health issue, family problem). The data flags the student; the counsellor investigates the cause.

### Basic Analytics Every School Should Run

If you are starting from zero, prioritize these five reports:

1. **Weekly attendance summary:** By class, flagging any class below 85% or any student below 75% cumulative.
2. **Fee outstanding report:** Sorted by days overdue, with contact details for follow-up.
3. **Term marks distribution:** By subject, showing the count of students in each grade band.
4. **At-risk student list:** Students with attendance below 75% AND marks below 40% in any subject. This is your priority counselling list.
5. **Month-on-month fee collection rate:** Are you collecting more or less than the same month last year?

These five reports, read consistently every week, will surface more actionable information than a complex analytics dashboard read once a term.
