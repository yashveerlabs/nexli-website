## How Do You Evaluate an ERP Vendor for an Indian School?

Evaluating a school ERP vendor requires more than watching a demo. Indian schools need to assess compliance knowledge (CBSE, DPDP Act, state board requirements), support responsiveness, data ownership terms, and realistic implementation timelines. This scorecard covers the seven dimensions that separate vendors who deliver from vendors who overpromise.

## Why Vendor Evaluation Matters More Than Feature Lists

Most ERP vendors claim to have the same features. The real differences show up in: how well they understand Indian school operations, how quickly support responds when something breaks at 7 AM on a Monday, and whether the contract gives you your own data if you want to leave.

Shortlist 3-4 vendors and run each through the same evaluation process. Do not sign with the first vendor whose demo impressed you.

## Dimension 1: Indian School Compliance Knowledge

Ask each vendor directly: "Does your system support CBSE affiliation reporting requirements?" and "How does your system handle DPDP Act 2023 obligations for student data?"

A vendor who knows Indian school compliance will answer these confidently with specific features. A vendor who says "we can customize that" or "we support all compliance requirements" without specifics is telling you they don't have it built and will charge you for custom development later.

**Specific compliance questions to ask:**
- CBSE LOC (List of Candidates) generation: Can the system export in CBSE-required format?
- RTE Act: Does the system track EWS seats and generate reimbursement documentation?
- DPDP Act 2023: How is parental consent collected and stored? Can you generate a consent audit trail?
- State board format: If your school is state board, does the report card generation match your board's format exactly?

**Score:** 0 (no knowledge) to 5 (specific feature demonstrations for each requirement).

## Dimension 2: Demo Quality and Data

A good demo uses realistic school data. It shows a complete workflow, not just screens.

**What a strong demo includes:**
- Creating a student admission record from scratch.
- Generating a fee invoice and recording a partial payment via UPI.
- Marking attendance for a class and showing how it appears in the parent portal.
- Generating a monthly fee collection report.
- Showing how a parent accesses their child's information.

**Red flags in a demo:**
- The vendor uses "sample data" that doesn't resemble an Indian school (e.g., names are "John Smith" and "Jane Doe").
- The vendor skips fee management and goes straight to analytics.
- The vendor cannot show a real report card format during the demo.
- The demo requires a stable internet connection with no discussion of what happens when the connection drops.

**Score:** 0 (rehearsed slide deck only) to 5 (complete workflow demo with your school's own scenario data).

## Dimension 3: Support Quality

This is where most school ERP relationships break down. Support during sales is always fast. Support 6 months after go-live is the real test.

**Questions to ask before signing:**
- What are your support hours? (Standard business hours vs. extended hours vs. 24/7.)
- What is your average first-response time for critical issues?
- Is support available in Hindi and regional languages?
- Do you have a dedicated account manager or does support go to a shared ticket queue?
- How are critical issues defined in your SLA? (A fee collection module that is down during school hours is critical.)

Ask for your support channel in writing. "We'll take care of it" is not a contractual commitment.

**Score:** 0 (no SLA, email only) to 5 (documented SLA with named contact, response times, and escalation path).

## Dimension 4: Reference Checks

Every vendor will give you reference customers. These references have been pre-selected and will give you a good review. The way to get real information is to ask references the right questions.

**Questions to ask reference schools:**
- How long did your implementation take compared to what the vendor promised?
- What problems came up during data migration and how were they resolved?
- How responsive is support 6-12 months after go-live?
- If you were signing the contract again today, what would you negotiate differently?
- Are there features you were shown in the demo that do not work as demonstrated?

Ask the vendor to introduce you to a school of similar size and type (same board, similar enrollment, similar city tier). A large CBSE school in Bengaluru is not a good reference for a small ICSE school in a tier-2 city.

**Score:** 0 (no references provided) to 5 (direct reference contact with schools matching your profile and honest answers to hard questions).

## Dimension 5: Data Ownership and Portability

This is the dimension schools most often ignore until they try to leave a vendor and discover they cannot export their own data.

**Contract terms to verify:**
- Who owns the data in the system? (The answer must be: the school owns all data.)
- Can you export a complete data backup at any time, on demand?
- What format is data exported in? (CSV or standard format is portable; proprietary format is a trap.)
- What happens to your data after you terminate the contract? (Standard: 30-90 days access to export, then deletion.)
- Is there a fee to export your data? (There should not be.)

**Score:** 0 (no export capability, data owned by vendor) to 5 (on-demand export in standard format, school owns all data, clear termination clause).

## Dimension 6: Contract Terms

**Key terms to review:**
- **Lock-in period:** Many vendors have 3-year minimum contracts. Understand the penalty for early termination.
- **Price escalation:** Is the price fixed or can it increase annually? By how much? (10% cap per year is typical; uncapped is a risk.)
- **Customization costs:** Modifications to standard forms or reports. Are they included or billed separately?
- **Implementation fees:** Separate from software licensing? What does implementation include?
- **Support tiers:** Is basic support included or is it a paid add-on?

Involve your school trust's legal or finance representative in contract review. ERP contracts are multi-year financial commitments and contain clauses that are easy to miss.

**Score:** 0 (unfavorable terms, high lock-in, uncapped pricing) to 5 (reasonable lock-in, capped price escalation, clear customization scope).

## Dimension 7: Technical Infrastructure

**Questions for cloud-based ERPs:**
- Where is your data hosted? (AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure are standard cloud providers.)
- Do you have data residency in India? (Required for DPDP Act compliance; data must be stored in India if you process Indian citizen personal data for certain categories.)
- What is your uptime SLA? (99.5% or above is acceptable; below 99% is a concern for a school that runs fee collection daily.)
- How often is data backed up and what is the recovery procedure?

**Score:** 0 (unclear hosting, no uptime SLA) to 5 (India data residency, 99.5%+ SLA, documented backup and recovery).

## Sample Vendor Scorecard

| Dimension | Weight | Vendor A | Vendor B | Vendor C |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Indian compliance knowledge | 20% | /5 | /5 | /5 |
| Demo quality | 15% | /5 | /5 | /5 |
| Support quality | 20% | /5 | /5 | /5 |

Multiply each score by weight and sum for a weighted total. The vendor with the highest weighted score is not automatically the right choice; use the scorecard as a structured conversation, not a formula.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many vendors should we evaluate before choosing?**
A: Shortlist 3-4 vendors and run all through the same scorecard. Fewer than 3 limits comparison; more than 4 creates decision fatigue without meaningful new information.

**Q: How long should we allow for the evaluation process?**
A: 4-6 weeks from initial contact to contract signing is a reasonable timeline. Allow at least 1 week for reference checks and 1 week for contract review.

**Q: Can we negotiate ERP contracts or are they fixed?**
A: Most school ERP contracts are negotiable, especially for multi-year terms. Lock-in periods, price escalation caps, and customization scope are the most commonly negotiated terms.

**Q: What if our preferred vendor can't show the CBSE LOC export during the demo?**
A: Ask for it within 2 weeks as a follow-up. If they cannot demonstrate it before contract signing, do not assume it will be ready after you pay.

**Q: Is a free trial better than a demo?**
A: A free trial with your own data is more valuable than a controlled demo. Ask if the vendor will set up a sandbox environment with your school's student list for a 2-week trial period. Most vendors who are confident in their product will agree.
