## Executive Summary

Most schools lose 50% of admissions inquiries within 2 weeks. Why? No tracking system, no follow-up protocol, single point of failure. This article reveals the root causes and fixes.

---

## Root Causes of Lost Inquiries

### Cause 1: No Tracking System

**Scenario:** Receptionist takes a call. Writes name on sticky note. Sticky note gets thrown away.

**Impact:** Inquiry is lost. No one knows it existed.

**Fix:** Track in system (ERP, Google Sheet, dedicated CRM). Every inquiry is logged.

### Cause 2: No Follow-Up Protocol

**Scenario:** Parent calls; receptionist says "Someone will call you back." No one does.

**Impact:** Parent calls a different school. They're enrolling elsewhere in 2 weeks.

**Fix:** Define follow-up cadence. Day 0: Confirmation. Day 1: Call. Day 5: Email. Day 10: Final call.

### Cause 3: Single Point of Failure

**Scenario:** Admissions officer takes leave. No one knows who handles inquiries. 10 inquiries sit unattended.

**Impact:** By the time officer returns, families have enrolled elsewhere.

**Fix:** Cross-train backup. Document handover.

### Cause 4: Unclear Responsibility

**Scenario:** Is the receptionist responsible for follow-up? Or admissions officer?

**Impact:** No one follows up. Each person assumes someone else will.

**Fix:** Clear responsibility matrix. "Receptionist logs; admissions officer follows up."

### Cause 5: No Visibility into Backlog

**Scenario:** Admissions officer doesn't know they have 20 pending follow-ups.

**Impact:** Leads age silently. By day 14, they're lost.

**Fix:** Daily dashboard. "You have 3 calls to make today to reach SLA."

## Solution: Lost Inquiry Prevention System

### 1. Centralized Tracking
- Every inquiry logged in one system (not multiple spreadsheets)
- Auto-SMS confirmation sent
- Auto-routing to admissions officer inbox

### 2. SLA-Based Follow-Up
| Status | Timeline | Next Action |
|---|---|---|
| Inquiry received | Day 0 | Send SMS confirmation |
| No application yet | Day 2 | Call parent; offer help |
| Application submitted | Day 5 | Confirm document receipt |
| Documents incomplete | Day 7 | Email missing docs; remind |
| Ready for interview | Day 10 | Schedule interview date |
| Interview done | Day 15 | Call with decision |
| Offer made | Day 18 | Send enrollment form |
| No response to offer | Day 21 | Final call; offer closes |

### 3. At-Risk Alerts
- Dashboard highlights: "Inquiry from Sharma family (Feb 5) not contacted since Feb 6. At risk!"
- Alert triggers: Call or email today
- Status updates: Admissions officer marks "Called" to clear alert

### 4. Daily Standup
- Every morning: "Today's admissions priorities"
- Review yesterday's follow-ups: Were they done?
- Identify at-risk inquiries
- Assign today's calls/emails

### 5. Backup Coverage
- When primary person (admissions officer) is unavailable, backup takes over
- Handoff document: "20 pending inquiries, priority order, due dates"
- No inquiry falls through during absence

## FAQ

**Q: How many inquiries should we track simultaneously?**
A: All of them. At any time, 50–100 inquiries in various stages.

**Q: What if an inquiry is old (2 months) with no progress?**
A: Close it politely. "Thank you for your interest. It's been a while and we're assuming you've made other plans. Please reapply next year."

**Q: How do we balance aggressiveness with respect?**
A: Follow-up, not harassment. If family doesn't respond after 3 contact attempts, stop and mark as "low interest."

**Q: Should we have different follow-up for different sources?**
A: Slightly. Referrals convert faster. Website inquiries need more nurturing.

**Q: Can we automate follow-up emails?**
A: Yes, but personalize them. "Hi Sharma family, your interview is confirmed for Feb 15 at 2 PM" is personal. Mass emails get ignored.
