## Executive Summary

A 300-student school with 6% annual turnover needs 18 new students yearly. Most schools get 50–100 inquiries annually. Leading schools get 500+. The difference: systematic lead tracking, follow-up, and conversion. This article reveals how.

### Typical Funnel (Weak School)

```

   ↓
Applications:     30 (30% conversion)
   ↓
Interviews:       20 (67% progress)
   ↓
Offers:           15 (75% offer rate)
   ↓
Enrolled:         10 (67% acceptance)

Result: 100 inquiries → 10 enrollments = 10% conversion

```
Inquiries:        500 (500%)
   ↓

   ↓

## How to Get 500+ Inquiries

### Channel 1: School Website (35% of inquiries)

**Optimization:**
- Clear admissions form on homepage (not buried)
- Auto-confirmation SMS/email ("Your inquiry has been received")
- Mobile-responsive form (60% of inquiries come via phone)
- Auto-routing to admissions officer inbox

**Result:** 175 inquiries/year from website

### Channel 2: Word of Mouth (30% of inquiries)

**Strategy:**
- Parent referral program (small incentive for successful referral)
- Alumni network engaged ("Tell your friends")
- Social proof (publish parent testimonials)
- Visible quality (happy students = happy parents = referrals)

**Result:** 150 inquiries/year from word of mouth

### Channel 3: Google & Local Search (20% of inquiries)

**Optimization:**
- Google My Business listing (updated, verified)
- Local SEO ("best school near me")
- Positive reviews on Google, Justdial, IndiaStack
- Local online directories

**Result:** 100 inquiries/year from search

### Channel 4: Social Media (10% of inquiries)

**Channels:**
- Instagram (student life, achievements)
- Facebook (parent community, announcements)
- YouTube (campus tour, testimonials)

**Result:** 50 inquiries/year from social media

### Channel 5: Direct Visits (5% of inquiries)

**Strategy:**
- "Walk-in" facility (receptionist trained to warmly receive)
- Campus tours (conducted daily)
- Information pack available
- Receptionist captures contact info for follow-up

**Result:** 25 inquiries/year from walk-ins

## Converting Inquiries to Applications (30–40% conversion)

### Critical Step: First Response (Within 4 Hours)

**What:** Immediate SMS/email/call within 4 hours of inquiry

**Example SMS:**
"Thank you for inquiring about XYZ School! We're excited to learn about your child. Our admissions team will call you within 24 hours. Best regards, XYZ School"

**Why:** First response converts 40% of inquiries that would otherwise fade.

### Second Response: Information Package (Within 24 Hours)

**What:** Brochure + fee structure + admissions timeline + next steps

**Delivery:** Email + WhatsApp (accessible immediately)

**Goal:** Parent has all info to decide "Should we apply?"

### Third Response: Personal Call (Within 48 Hours)

**What:** Admissions officer calls parent

**Conversation:**
- "Did you receive the information package?"
- "Do you have questions?"
- "What grade is your child?"
- "Timeline: Admissions open Jan 1, close Mar 31. Entrance test Feb 15."
- "Would you like to apply?"

**Conversion result:** 40% of inquiries → Applications

## Converting Applications to Interviews (75% of apps)

### Intake Process (Day 1–3)

**Coordinator reviews application:**
- Complete? (All documents present?)
- Eligible? (Grade level, age, etc.)
- Decision:
  - Complete + Eligible → Schedule interview
  - Incomplete → Request missing docs (email + phone)
  - Ineligible → Polite rejection with explanation

**Result:** 75% of applications → Interviews

---

## Converting Interviews to Offers (80–85%)

### Interview Process (Day 5–15)

**Format:**
- Parent + child + principal + academic VP
- 20–30 minutes
- Assess: Academic level, learning style, fit with school culture

**Outcome: Offer vs. Waitlist vs. Rejection**

**Metrics:**
- 83% get offers
- 12% put on waitlist
- 5% rejected

---

## Converting Offers to Enrollment (75–80%)

### Offer Communication (Day 15–20)

**Process:**
- Call parent with offer
- Send formal offer letter
- Explain enrollment process: deposit, parent agreement, onboarding

**Follow-up if parent doesn't respond:**
- Email day 2
- Call day 4
- Email day 7
- Final call day 10

**Conversion:** 75–80% of offers → Enrollment

## Conversion Optimization

### Optimize at Each Stage

**Stage 1: Inquiry → Application**
- Improve first response (make it warm, personal)
- Make application form simple (3 fields, not 20)
- Reduce information package (1 page, not 10)
- Offer flexibility (applications accepted year-round, not just certain months)

**Result:** Increase from 30% to 40% conversion

**Stage 2: Application → Interview**
- Reduce wait time (interview within 1 week, not 3)
- Make interview process transparent (tell parent exactly what happens)
- Reduce interview intimidation (principal is warm, not stern)

**Result:** Increase from 67% to 75% progress

**Stage 3: Interview → Offer**
- Have clear selection criteria (not arbitrary)
- Communicate criteria to parents upfront
- Be generous with offers (83%, not 50%)

**Result:** Increase from 75% to 83%

**Stage 4: Offer → Enrollment**
- Make enrollment simple (3-step process, not 10 forms)
- Explain next steps clearly
- Build excitement (summer camp, orientation, new student community)

**Result:** Increase from 67% to 80%

## FAQ

**Q: How many inquiries should a school get annually?**
A: For a 300-student school, 150–200 is basic. 300–500 is great. 500+ is excellent.

**Q: Which channel is best?**
A: Word of mouth (best quality, converts highest). Then website + Google search.

**Q: How quickly should we respond to inquiries?**
A: Within 4 hours is ideal. 24 hours is minimum. After 48 hours, many inquiries are lost.

**Q: Should we have different follow-up for different sources?**
A: Slightly. Word-of-mouth referrals convert faster (trust is pre-built). Website inquiries need more nurturing.

**Q: How do we measure conversion success?**
A: Track: Inquiry → Application (30%+), Application → Interview (75%+), Offer → Enrollment (75%+). Overall: 500 inquiries → 100 enrollments (20%).
