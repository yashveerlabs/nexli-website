## School ERP Pricing Models: Per Student, Per School, or Module-Based

**School ERP vendors use several different pricing structures. Understanding the model your vendor uses helps you evaluate fairness, predict future costs, and compare vendors who use different approaches. The best model for your school depends on your enrollment size, which modules you need, and how much price predictability matters.**

---

## Per-Student Pricing

**How it works:** A fixed annual price per active student on the platform.

**Example:** ₹300 per student per year. A school with 500 students pays ₹1,50,000 per year.

**Who benefits:**
- Small schools: lower absolute cost
- Schools with stable enrollment: predictable annual expense

**Watch for:**
- How is "active student" defined? Are students who withdraw mid-year still counted?
- Does the price per student change if enrollment grows? (Tiered pricing: first 500 at ₹300, next 500 at ₹250)
- Are all modules included or per-module extras on top?

**Best for:** Schools that want costs to scale proportionally with size.

---

## Flat Per-School Pricing

**How it works:** A fixed annual fee regardless of enrollment (within a defined range).

**Example:** ₹1,20,000 per year for up to 1,000 students. Same price whether you have 300 or 900 students.

**Who benefits:**
- Large schools near the top of the range
- Schools expecting enrollment growth (price does not increase)

**Watch for:**
- What happens if enrollment exceeds the range? Does price jump significantly?
- Are different sizes grouped into different tiers?

**Best for:** Schools with larger enrollments or rapid growth, who do not want cost to scale linearly with students.

---

## Module-Based Pricing

**How it works:** A base subscription covers core modules. Additional modules (transport, hostel, payroll, analytics) are priced separately.

**Example:** Base (academics + attendance + basic fee): ₹80,000/year. Transport module: ₹20,000/year. Hostel module: ₹20,000/year. Payroll module: ₹25,000/year.

**Who benefits:**
- Schools that only need specific modules (no transport, no hostel)
- Schools who want to start small and add modules later

**Watch for:**
- Underestimating the number of modules needed. Schools that need 4-5 modules end up paying more than a bundled subscription.
- Module pricing often escalates faster than base subscription.
- Some "modules" should be standard features (compliance calendar, basic reporting), be skeptical if these cost extra.

**Best for:** Schools with very specific, limited ERP requirements.

---

## Staff-Based Pricing

Some vendors price per staff member (admin, teacher accounts) rather than per student.

**How it works:** ₹X per staff account per year.

**Who benefits:**
- Schools with many students but few staff
- Schools where most staff use the system (every teacher, every admin)

**Watch for:**
- Does "staff" include teaching staff only or also support staff?
- How is "active" counted for seasonal staff?

**Best for:** Less common. Rarely optimal unless staff-to-student ratios are unusually high.

## Comparing Models for Your School

For a 500-student school with 30 teaching staff and 10 admin staff:

| **Model** | **Annual Cost Example** |
|---|---|
| Per-student at ₹300 | ₹1,50,000 |
| Flat school tier (up to 750 students) | ₹1,25,000 |
| Module-based (base + transport + hostel) | ₹1,45,000 |
| Per-staff at ₹3,000 for 40 staff | ₹1,20,000 |

The differences are relatively small at this size. At 2,000 students, per-student pricing at ₹300 would be ₹6,00,000 vs. a flat school fee of ₹2,50,000, a dramatic difference.

## FAQ

**Q: Which pricing model is most common in India?**
A: Per-student pricing is most common. Flat per-school pricing is offered by some vendors at scale. Module-based is common for legacy ERPs that built features incrementally.

**Q: Can we negotiate a custom pricing model?**
A: For large schools and chains with significant bargaining power, custom pricing is often available. Multi-school chains can negotiate a combined per-student rate lower than individual school pricing.

**Q: What if our enrollment fluctuates (boarding school with significant mid-year exits)?**
A: Clarify how the vendor counts students. "Active students at any point during the year" vs. "peak enrollment" vs. "end of year enrollment" can result in significantly different bills.

**Q: Is per-student pricing unfair for schools that add students mid-year?**
A: Most vendors count active students at a specific point (beginning of year enrollment) or at the contract renewal. Mid-year additions are typically not billed until the next renewal.
