## ERP for ICSE Schools: What the Council's Requirements Mean for Your ERP

**ICSE (Indian Certificate of Secondary Education) and ISC (Indian School Certificate) are governed by CISCE, the Council for the Indian School Certificate Examinations. ICSE schools have a wider subject offering than CBSE, a percentage-based grading system, and distinct internal assessment requirements. Schools affiliated with CISCE need an ERP that handles these specifics correctly.**

---

## How ICSE/ISC Differs from CBSE

**Grade Level:** ICSE covers Classes 1-10; ISC covers Classes 11-12. Most ICSE schools run both streams.

**Grading scale:** ICSE uses percentage marks (no grade point conversion system like CBSE 9-point). Grades are letter-based (A through F) derived from percentage ranges, but the primary reporting is percentage.

**Subject structure:** ICSE allows more elective subjects than CBSE. Classes 10 students may choose from a broader set of Group III (elective) subjects. The ERP must handle a wider subject catalog and more flexible student-subject mappings.

**Internal assessment:** ICSE's internal assessment component (20% for most subjects in Class 10) includes project work, practical examinations, and assignments. These must be tracked separately from written examination marks.

**CISCE registration requirement:** CISCE requires annual registration of candidates. Unlike CBSE's LOC process, CISCE has its own registration format. The ERP should support CISCE-compatible data export.

---

## Key ERP Requirements for ICSE Schools

### Percentage-Based Marks Management

ICSE reporting is percentage-based. The ERP must:
- Accept marks as percentages or raw marks from defined totals
- Calculate percentage correctly per subject
- Generate subject-wise percentage breakdown for report cards
- Flag students below 35% pass mark

### Internal Assessment Component Tracking

For ICSE subjects with internal assessment:
- Maintain separate internal assessment marks (project, practical, assignment)
- Track internal assessment submissions per student
- Calculate combined final percentage (80% external + 20% internal)
- Internal assessment records must be maintained for CISCE inspection

### Flexible Subject Grouping

ICSE's subject flexibility means:
- Different students may have different subject combinations in Group III
- The ERP must accommodate student-level subject selection
- Class sections may have students with different subject mixes

### Report Card Format for ICSE/ISC

ICSE report cards show percentage per subject with letter grade. The format is distinct from CBSE grade point report cards. The ERP report card module must be configurable for ICSE format.

### NEP 2020 HPC for ICSE Schools

ICSE schools affiliated with CISCE follow NEP 2020 progressively. The HPC requirements apply to ICSE schools as well.

## FAQ

**Q: Does CISCE have a specific ERP data submission requirement?**
A: CISCE requires annual candidate registration with specific data fields. Check the current CISCE registration requirements and verify that the ERP supports data export in the required format.

**Q: Can the ERP handle ISC subjects differently from ICSE subjects?**
A: Classes 9-10 (ICSE) and Classes 11-12 (ISC) have different subject structures and mark weightings. The ERP should support per-class-group academic configuration.

**Q: Is NEP 2020 mandatory for ICSE schools?**
A: NEP 2020 implementation timeline applies to all school boards in India. CISCE has issued guidance on NEP 2020 compliance. ICSE schools should verify current NEP compliance requirements from CISCE directly.

**Q: How does ICSE's internal assessment grading work for ERP purposes?**
A: Internal assessment (20 marks total) is usually composed of multiple components (project: 10 marks, practical/assignment: 10 marks). Each component is tracked separately and combined for the total internal assessment mark.

**Q: Is ICSE less common than CBSE among ERP vendors?**
A: CBSE is more common than ICSE by school count. Most ERP vendors design primarily for CBSE. Verify that the vendor has deployed for ICSE schools specifically and has reference schools under CISCE affiliation.
