## Cloud-Based vs. On-Premise ERP for Schools: What to Choose

**The choice between cloud and on-premise school ERP affects maintenance cost, data control, offline capability, and compliance. Most Indian schools in 2026 choose cloud for cost and convenience, but on-premise remains valid for specific scenarios. This guide explains the genuine differences so you can decide what fits your school.**

---

## What the Terms Actually Mean

**Cloud-based (SaaS):** The software runs on the vendor's servers (or major cloud providers like Google Firebase or AWS). You access it through a browser or app. The vendor manages backups, updates, and security patches. You pay an annual subscription.

**On-premise:** The software is installed on servers your school owns or rents. You manage the servers, backups, and updates. You pay a one-time license (usually) plus annual maintenance. All data stays on your hardware.

---

## The Practical Comparison

### Cost

**Cloud:**
- No upfront server hardware cost
- Annual subscription fee (typically ₹60,000-₹3,00,000/year depending on size and vendor)
- No IT staff required to maintain the server
- Lower risk: if you stop paying, you stop having the software

**On-Premise:**
- Server hardware: ₹2,00,000-₹5,00,000 upfront
- IT staff or contractor for maintenance: ₹60,000-₹2,00,000/year
- Backup power (UPS, generator connection): ₹50,000+
- One-time license: ₹2,00,000-₹5,00,000
- Annual maintenance: ₹20,000-₹50,000/year

For most schools, cloud is significantly cheaper over 3 years. On-premise makes financial sense mainly for large chains that can amortize infrastructure costs across many campuses.

### Internet Dependency

**Cloud:** Requires internet. If your school's internet connection fails for a day, staff cannot access the system. Most cloud ERPs cache recently accessed data for offline viewing, but submitting new data requires connectivity.

**On-premise:** Works without internet. All data is on your local network. Teachers mark attendance, accounts process fees, and admin manages records even during an internet outage.

This is the primary reason on-premise still exists. Schools in areas with unreliable internet, rural India, remote campuses, need systems that work independently of connectivity.

### Data Control

**Cloud:** Your data lives on the vendor's (or cloud provider's) infrastructure. You can export it, but you do not control the hardware. Under DPDP Act 2023, you need to understand where your data resides and who has access.

**On-premise:** Your data stays on hardware you control. This appeals to schools with strict data governance policies or trustees who are concerned about data leaving the campus.

### Maintenance

**Cloud:** Updates happen automatically. Security patches deploy without your intervention. You always use the latest version. Zero IT burden.

**On-premise:** You manage updates. When the vendor releases a new version, your IT team installs it, tests it, and handles any migration. Security patches require action on your end. Old versions remain in use if you delay updates.

### Backup and Disaster Recovery

**Cloud:** Backups happen automatically (usually daily, sometimes real-time). If data is corrupted or accidentally deleted, the vendor restores it. Google's Firebase (which Nexli uses) maintains multiple redundant copies across data centers.

**On-premise:** You manage backups. If your server fails and the backup is also corrupt, data loss is your problem. Most on-premise deployments should run daily backups to an off-site location, a discipline many schools do not consistently maintain.

## Who Should Choose Cloud

Cloud is the right choice for the majority of Indian schools if:
- You have reliable internet (even 2-5 Mbps is sufficient for most school operations)
- You want to avoid IT infrastructure burden
- You want automatic security updates without delays
- You are a single campus without enterprise-scale data governance requirements
- You want lower total cost of ownership

Modern cloud systems like Nexli also include offline capability for key workflows (attendance, cached data viewing) that reduce the internet-dependency concern.

---

## Who Should Consider On-Premise

On-premise remains valid in specific scenarios:
- Schools in areas with consistent internet outages (rural, remote locations)
- School chains with existing IT infrastructure and dedicated IT staff
- Schools with regulatory requirements that mandate local data storage
- Situations where the school's governing trust has explicit data residency policies

Fedena is the most commonly cited ERP with a robust on-premise option. If on-premise is a hard requirement, Fedena should be on your shortlist.

## DPDP Act Implications

The DPDP Act 2023 requires schools to know where student data is processed and stored. Cloud providers like Google (Firebase) publish their data processing agreements and infrastructure locations. Nexli's data lives on Firebase, which operates data centers in multiple regions, clarify with the vendor which region your data is in.

For on-premise, data residency is not a concern because data never leaves your servers. However, your responsibility to secure, back up, and properly delete data increases significantly.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a cloud system work if our internet goes down?**
A: Modern cloud ERPs cache recently accessed data for offline viewing. Teachers can view attendance lists and timetables. However, new submissions (marking attendance, entering marks, processing fees) require internet. For schools with frequent outages, on-premise or a hybrid model is safer.

**Q: Is cloud data safe from hackers?**
A: Major cloud providers (Google, AWS) invest more in security than any individual school server. Firebase uses encryption in transit and at rest. The security risk with cloud is the vendor's security practices, not the hosting environment itself. Ask vendors about their security architecture and test results.

**Q: What happens to our data if we switch vendors?**
A: With cloud systems, ask about data export. Nexli supports CSV/Excel export and Firestore backup. With on-premise, you own the database directly. In both cases, clarify export rights in your contract before signing.

**Q: How do we decide if our internet is reliable enough for cloud?**
A: Track outages for 2-4 weeks. Count how many times per week the connection fails for more than 30 minutes. If it is more than twice per week, on-premise is safer. If it is once per month or less, cloud works fine.

**Q: Is on-premise going away?**
A: It is declining. Most new school ERP deployments in India are cloud-based. But on-premise will remain relevant for schools with specific connectivity or data governance requirements. The market continues to support both models.
