## Ongoing School ERP Support After Go-Live: What Good Looks Like

**Most vendors are attentive during sales and during implementation. The quality of vendor relationship after go-live — 6 months, 12 months, 2 years in — is what actually determines whether the ERP investment delivers sustained value. Understanding what good ongoing support looks like helps schools set expectations, hold vendors accountable, and recognize when a relationship has deteriorated.**

---

## The Support Relationship Lifecycle

**Weeks 1-4 post-go-live:** Intensive. Daily check-ins with the vendor are normal. Issues discovered in real usage are addressed quickly. Vendor is highly responsive because they need to close the implementation successfully.

**Months 2-3:** Active. Weekly check-ins. Issues take a day or two to resolve. Vendor is still engaged.

**Months 4-6:** Routine. Bi-weekly or monthly check-ins. Issues resolved within SLA. The relationship becomes more transactional.

**Months 7-12:** Standard SLA service. Issues submitted, resolved within agreed timelines. Vendor reaches out for annual review or renewal discussion.

**Year 2+:** Maintenance mode. Proactive support for major regulatory changes (DPDP enforcement, CBSE LOC format update). Reactive support for issues. Annual review tied to renewal.

The relationship naturally evolves from intensive to routine. The risk is when it deteriorates from routine to neglectful — slow responses, no proactive communication, feature requests ignored, known bugs unaddressed.

---

## What Good Post-Go-Live Support Includes

### Responsive Issue Resolution

- Critical issues (system down, data loss): response within 2 hours, resolution within 8 hours
- High priority issues (significant module failure): response within 8 hours, resolution within 48 hours
- Standard issues (configuration question, minor bug): response within 24-48 hours, resolution within 5 business days

These should be documented in the SLA, not just stated verbally.

### Proactive Regulatory Updates

When CBSE changes the LOC format, the vendor should notify you in advance and release the update before submission season. When DPDP Act enforcement rules are published, the vendor should communicate what changes in the system (if any) and what the school needs to do.

You should not discover a LOC format change by receiving a rejection from CBSE. Good vendors track regulatory changes that affect their customers and communicate proactively.

### Annual Review Meeting

A structured annual review before contract renewal covering:
- What worked well in the past year
- What did not (and what was or was not resolved)
- What the school's needs are for the coming year (new modules, more campuses, staff changes)
- Pricing and contract terms for renewal

This review gives the school an opportunity to raise concerns before renewal pressure creates awkwardness.

### Product Updates Without Disruption

Regular product updates (bug fixes, new features, regulatory compliance updates) should happen without disrupting school operations. Schools should be notified before major updates:
- What is changing
- When (ideally weekend or non-school-hours)
- What action (if any) the school needs to take

Updates that break existing functionality without notice erode trust rapidly.

## Signs That Post-Go-Live Support Is Deteriorating

- Support tickets take more than 48 hours to receive a first response
- The same bug is reported multiple times without resolution
- Regulatory updates (LOC format, NEP compliance) are late or absent
- The account manager changes without introduction of the replacement
- The vendor is unresponsive during renewal discussions
- Known issues are put on "roadmap" without specific timelines

These signs indicate vendor capacity problems, financial difficulty, or strategic deprioritization of your school's segment.

## FAQ

**Q: What should we do if support quality deteriorates significantly?**
A: Escalate formally in writing: document the specific issues, response times, and unresolved problems. Request a senior meeting with the vendor's operations leadership. If unresolved, evaluate alternatives and exercise contract termination rights per the agreement.

**Q: Should we lock in multi-year contracts for cost savings?**
A: Multi-year contracts typically offer price stability in exchange for reduced flexibility. A 2-year lock-in is reasonable if support quality has been good. A 3-year lock-in without performance clauses is risky.

**Q: How do we stay informed about ERP updates without overwhelming staff?**
A: Designate one ERP coordinator who reviews vendor release notes. Major changes affecting teacher workflows are communicated in advance; minor bug fixes are not.

**Q: Can we negotiate support quality into the contract?**
A: Yes. Response time commitments, proactive regulatory update obligations, and annual review meetings can all be included in the contract. Penalties for SLA breach (credit toward next renewal) are negotiable.

**Q: What is the best indicator of long-term vendor quality?**
A: Ask reference schools how the vendor behaved during a crisis or a difficult problem — not during a smooth period. Crisis behavior reveals vendor character more clearly than normal operations.
