## Teacher Dashboard: Essential Features in a School ERP

**Teacher adoption determines whether an ERP succeeds or fails. If teachers find the dashboard difficult, slow, or irrelevant, they stop using it, and fall back to paper. The teacher dashboard must show teachers exactly what they need for their day's work, in a format that works on a phone, with minimal clicks. This guide explains what an effective teacher dashboard must include.**

---

## Why Teacher Dashboards Often Fail

The most common failure pattern: an ERP that gives teachers access to too much information. Teachers open the dashboard and see a sea of data, school-wide statistics, admin reports, governance tools, none of which is relevant to their role. They leave the app and use WhatsApp instead.

The second common failure: an ERP that requires too many steps to complete a simple task. Marking attendance should take under 60 seconds. Entering marks for a student should take under 30 seconds. If the interface requires 5-7 clicks for each action, teachers will not sustain use beyond the first month.

The right design: teachers see their classes, their pending tasks, and their students, nothing else by default.

---

## What a Teacher Dashboard Must Show

### Today's Teaching Schedule

The first thing a teacher needs every morning: which classes are they teaching today, in which periods, and where.

The timetable view should show:
- Period number and time
- Subject being taught
- Class and section
- Room number

If there is a substitution (another teacher covering for an absent teacher), that should appear prominently.

### Pending Attendance for Today's Classes

Teachers should see clearly which of their classes need attendance marked today and which have already been marked. A single-tap to open the class attendance sheet.

### Homework to Assign or Review

- Pending homework to assign for upcoming periods
- Homework submitted by students that needs review or grading
- Overdue homework with the count of students who have not submitted

### At-Risk Student Alerts

The teacher's dashboard should flag:
- Students in their class with attendance below 80%
- Students who have not submitted the last 3+ homework assignments
- Students with significant drops in marks from last term

This information lets teachers intervene early rather than discovering problems at the end of the term.

### Marks Entry Shortcuts

Quick access to:
- Marks entry sheet for their subject (filtered to their classes only)
- Assessment review and approval status
- Report card review (for class teachers)

### Lesson Plan Status

- Today's lesson plan (what they are supposed to teach)
- Lesson plan submission status (submitted vs. pending for HOD review)
- Curriculum coverage tracker (how far through the syllabus they are vs. planned pace)

### Announcements

School-wide and department-specific announcements visible to the teacher. Not parent-facing announcements, not student-facing announcements, only those relevant to their role.

## Class Teacher vs. Subject Teacher View

Class teachers and subject teachers need different dashboards:

**Class teacher:**
- Full class roster with each student's attendance percentage
- Section-level marks summary (which students need academic support)
- Report card completion status (which students' report cards are pending teacher remarks)
- Student welfare flags (counselling referrals, at-risk indicators)

**Subject teacher:**
- Classes they teach with today's periods
- Marks entry status per class per assessment
- Homework assignment and submission tracking
- Curriculum coverage per class

A well-designed ERP combines these based on the teacher's assigned roles, class teacher for Section 7A, subject teacher for Mathematics in Sections 7A, 7B, and 8A.

---

## Mobile Optimization for Teachers

Teachers often need to use the ERP during or between classes, not at a desktop computer. The teacher dashboard must:
- Load the current-period class attendance in under 3 seconds on 3G
- Allow bulk attendance marking (all Present, mark exceptions) in under 60 seconds
- Work offline with sync when internet returns
- Show marks entry in a finger-friendly form factor

## FAQ

**Q: Can a teacher see students from other classes by accident?**
A: No, in a properly role-scoped system. Teachers see only the classes they are assigned to teach. Cross-class visibility requires explicit permission from the Principal or Admin.

**Q: How do teachers receive HOD feedback on lesson plans?**
A: The lesson plan workflow sends a notification to the teacher when the HOD approves or returns their plan with comments. Teachers see this on their dashboard.

**Q: Can teachers message parents from their dashboard?**
A: Most ERPs support structured teacher-parent communication (announcements, circulars) rather than free-form messaging. This protects both teachers and parents while maintaining appropriate communication channels.

**Q: Can the teacher see the student's complete academic history?**
A: Class teachers should have access to the student's profile including academic history, attendance, and medical flags (where role-permitted). Subject teachers see only their subject's data.

**Q: What happens when a teacher is on leave?**
A: Their classes appear in the substitution system. A substitute teacher sees the original teacher's lesson plan for the day, marked clearly as substitution.
