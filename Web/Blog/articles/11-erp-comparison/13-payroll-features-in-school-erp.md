## Payroll Features in School ERP: What Indian Schools Need

**School payroll is more complex than many ERPs acknowledge. Between EPF, ESI, TDS, professional tax, leave deductions, and varying salary structures across teaching and non-teaching staff, getting payroll wrong is expensive. This guide explains what to look for in a school ERP's payroll module.**

---

## Why School Payroll Needs Specialized Features

Schools manage two distinct staff populations:
- **Teaching staff:** Salary typically linked to qualifications (B.Ed., M.Ed., experience), often with grade pay scales if affiliated with government salary structures
- **Non-teaching staff:** Admin, support, transport, housekeeping, often hourly or fixed salary without grade pay

Both groups have different statutory deductions, different attendance patterns, and often different payment modes. An ERP that handles only one type will force manual adjustments for the other.

---

## Core Payroll Features

### Salary Structure Configuration

The system must support:
- Basic salary, HRA, TA, DA, special allowances per staff member or staff category
- Multiple salary templates (teaching staff, administrative staff, support staff)
- Grade-based increments (annual increment on appraisal or seniority)
- Arrears calculation when a salary revision applies retroactively

### Statutory Deductions

Indian schools must comply with:

**EPF (Employees' Provident Fund):**
- Employer contribution: 12% of basic salary
- Employee contribution: 12% of basic salary
- Monthly remittance to EPFO

**ESI (Employees' State Insurance):**
- Applicable for employees earning up to ₹21,000/month
- Employee contribution: 0.75% of gross salary
- Employer contribution: 3.25% of gross salary

**TDS (Tax Deducted at Source):**
- Applied to salaries above income tax exemption limit
- TDS calculation must account for HRA exemption, 80C deductions declared by staff
- Monthly deduction with quarterly TDS return filing (Form 24Q)

**Professional Tax:**
- State-specific rates (Maharashtra, Karnataka, etc.)
- Must be configurable per state

The ERP must calculate all of these correctly and generate the required reports for statutory filings.

### Staff Attendance Integration

Payroll must link to the staff attendance module:
- Late arrivals and early departures flagged and potentially deducted
- Leave days (earned, casual, medical, unpaid) deducted from salary
- Overtime recorded and paid at configured rates
- Attendance-linked payroll prevents paying full salary to staff on unauthorized leave

### Leave Management Integration

The leave management module must feed into payroll:
- Approved leave is automatically deducted from salary (or not, if it is earned leave)
- Leave encashment at year-end calculated correctly
- Unpaid leave triggers salary deduction

### Pay Slip Generation

Monthly pay slips must:
- Show gross salary breakdown (basic, HRA, allowances)
- Show all deductions (EPF, ESI, TDS, professional tax, absence deductions)
- Show net salary payable
- Be shareable with staff via email or app
- Be archiveable per month per staff member

### Disbursement Record

After payroll is processed:
- Bank transfer records (account number, IFSC, amount per staff member)
- Cash payment records for staff without bank accounts
- Disbursement confirmation from the accounts team

## Access Control for Payroll

Payroll data is sensitive. The ERP must enforce:
- HR Manager and Principal: Full access to salary structures and pay slips
- HODs: Can see their team's attendance but not salary
- Staff members: Can see their own pay slip only, not colleagues'
- Accounts team: Sees disbursement records, not individual salary structures

This role-based access is critical for compliance and internal control.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the ERP replace accounting software for payroll?**
A: School ERPs handle payroll calculation, pay slip generation, and compliance reports. Accounting software (Tally, Busy) handles journal entries and financial reporting. Ideally, the ERP exports payroll data to the accounting system. Ask vendors about this integration.

**Q: How do we handle contract staff who are not on regular payroll?**
A: Contract staff should be managed as a separate staff category with configurable payment terms (per hour, per day, per month). Statutory deductions may differ for contract staff.

**Q: What reports does the payroll module generate?**
A: Minimum required: monthly payroll register, EPF challan, ESI challan, TDS computation (Form 16), professional tax workings, bank transfer list.

**Q: How does payroll handle salary revisions mid-year?**
A: The system should apply the new salary structure from the revision date and calculate arrears for the gap between the effective date and the implementation date.

**Q: Is staff payroll data protected from other staff?**
A: It must be. Role-based access should prevent staff from seeing colleagues' salaries. This is a critical HR control that vendors must demonstrate, not just claim.
