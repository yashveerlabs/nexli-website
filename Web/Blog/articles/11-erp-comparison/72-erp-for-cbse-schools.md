## ERP for CBSE Schools: Board-Specific Requirements and Features

**CBSE is India's most common school board by affiliation count and student enrollment. CBSE schools have specific compliance requirements that a school ERP must support, List of Candidates submission, 9-point grading scale, NEP 2020 Holistic Progress Card, and affiliation-related reporting. This guide covers what CBSE schools must verify in ERP evaluation.**

---

## CBSE-Specific ERP Requirements

### List of Candidates (LOC) Export

LOC submission is a critical annual compliance task for CBSE-affiliated schools with Classes 10 and 12. Schools must submit each candidate's:
- Roll number
- Name (as it should appear on the certificate)
- Date of birth
- Subjects opted
- Scheme of Studies
- Internal assessment marks (for applicable subjects)
- Special needs and accommodation requirements

The ERP must export this data in the exact CBSE LOC format. The format changes occasionally; the ERP vendor must update the export template when CBSE revises it.

**Ask vendors:** "Do you support CBSE LOC export? In what format? When was the last time you updated the format to match CBSE changes?"

### 9-Point Grading Scale (Class 10 Board)

CBSE's scholastic grading uses a 9-point scale:
- A1 (91-100): Grade Point 10
- A2 (81-90): Grade Point 9
- B1 (71-80): Grade Point 8
- B2 (61-70): Grade Point 7
- C1 (51-60): Grade Point 6
- C2 (41-50): Grade Point 5
- D (33-40): Grade Point 4
- E1 (21-32): Grade Point 3 (Grade Point Fail)
- E2 (00-20): Grade Point 2 (Grade Point Fail)

The ERP must support this grade calculation scheme for Classes 9 and 10. Internal assessment (20 marks) and board marks (80 marks) combine for the final calculation.

For Class 12, letter grades (A, B, C, D, E) are assigned by the board; the school provides internal assessment marks.

### NEP 2020 Holistic Progress Card (HPC)

NEP 2020 requires tracking student development beyond academic marks. The Holistic Progress Card includes:
- Cognitive skills (subject knowledge and understanding)
- Social and emotional development
- Physical development
- Creative and artistic skills
- Co-curricular participation
- Community engagement

For CBSE schools implementing NEP 2020 progressively, the ERP must support HPC tracking alongside traditional marks.

### Affiliation Compliance Records

CBSE affiliation requires schools to maintain:
- Fire NOC
- Building safety certificate
- Infrastructure details (classrooms, labs, toilets, library)
- Staff qualification records (B.Ed., NET/SET/M.Ed. as applicable)

The compliance calendar should alert for CBSE affiliation renewal requirements.

### UDISE+ Data Export

CBSE schools submit annual UDISE+ data. The ERP should pre-populate UDISE+ fields from existing student, staff, and infrastructure records.

## FAQ

**Q: Can the ERP handle both CBSE secondary (9-10) and senior secondary (11-12) grading in the same school?**
A: Yes, in a properly designed ERP. Classes 9-10 use the 9-point scheme; Classes 11-12 use percentage and letter grade hybrid. The ERP should support different grading configurations by class group.

**Q: Does CBSE require schools to maintain digital attendance records?**
A: CBSE requires 75% minimum attendance. Digital records in an ERP satisfy this requirement and are more reliably maintained than paper registers.

**Q: How often does CBSE change the LOC format?**
A: CBSE revises formats periodically. The ERP vendor is responsible for maintaining up-to-date export formats. Ask vendors how quickly they update when CBSE revises the format.

**Q: What happens if the LOC export from the ERP has errors?**
A: LOC errors discovered during CBSE processing can delay exam registrations. The ERP should generate an error report before submission, allowing the school to verify and correct before filing.

**Q: Can we track CBSE internal assessment marks separately from board marks?**
A: Yes, this is a standard requirement. Internal assessment (school-conducted) and board marks are separate fields that combine for final grade calculation.
