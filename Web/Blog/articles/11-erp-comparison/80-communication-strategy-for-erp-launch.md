## Communication Strategy for School ERP Launch

**How you communicate the ERP launch shapes the school community's reception. A launch that is poorly announced creates anxiety ("Are they tracking us?"), confusion ("Is this mandatory?"), and resistance ("More technology to learn"). A well-planned communication strategy creates understanding, sets correct expectations, and builds adoption momentum before the system even goes live.**

---

## Communication Sequence

### Phase 1: Leadership Alignment (Before Any Public Announcement)

Before any staff member hears about the ERP, ensure the Principal and senior leadership are aligned and confident:
- What is being implemented and why
- The timeline and what each phase involves
- What is expected of each role
- How the Principal will reference ERP data in their daily work

Leadership ambivalence communicates itself. A Principal who is visibly confident in the decision enables staff adoption; uncertainty at the top creates doubt throughout.

---

### Phase 2: Staff Announcement (4 Weeks Before Go-Live)

**Who:** All teaching and non-teaching staff at a school meeting.

**What to communicate:**
- The school is implementing Nexli (or your chosen ERP) starting [date]
- Why: [specific problems it solves — attendance compilation, parent queries, DPDP compliance]
- What each role will do differently: "Class teachers will mark attendance digitally. Accounts team will record payments in the system. Admin will use the system for circulars."
- What is not changing: "Report cards will still be reviewed by class teachers. Teaching methods are unchanged. Your relationship with your students is unchanged."
- Training schedule: "Your role-specific training is on [date]. Attendance is required."

**What to avoid:**
- Vague promises ("This will make everything easier") without specifics
- Implying surveillance without acknowledging it ("The Principal can see your attendance from anywhere")
- Minimizing the change ("It's very simple, just like your phone")

---

### Phase 3: Parent Communication (2 Weeks Before Parent Portal Opens)

**Timing:** After Phase 1 (student information, attendance, fees) is stable — usually 4-6 weeks after go-live. Do not open the parent portal until you are confident the data is accurate.

**What to communicate:**
- The school has implemented a parent portal that provides real-time access to your child's attendance and fee balance
- How to access it: [link] or [app name]
- What you will see: attendance by day, fee balance and payment history, school circulars
- What you will receive automatically: attendance alerts if your child falls below 80%, fee reminders 7 days before due date
- How to contact the school for questions: [contact details]

**Format:** A one-page printed guide (sent in student bag), a WhatsApp message with the same information, and a demonstration at the next parent meeting.

**What to avoid:**
- Technical language ("we've implemented a cloud-based ERP") — parents do not need or want this
- Promising features not yet live in the system
- Overwhelming parents with everything the portal can do — focus on the 3 things they will use immediately

---

### Phase 4: Student Communication (When Age-Appropriate)

For secondary school students (Classes 8-12), direct communication is appropriate:
- They can see their own attendance and marks in the student portal
- Homework is published in the system
- Report cards are available digitally

For primary school students: communication is parent-only. Students benefit through parents.

## Handling Concerns From Staff

**"Is the Principal tracking how long I take for each task?"**
Address directly: "The ERP records attendance and marks submission. It does not track time-on-task or monitor your activity beyond what is relevant to school records."

**"What if there is an internet problem and I cannot mark attendance?"**
Address directly: "There is a fallback plan: [specific plan]. Occasional connectivity issues are expected and accommodated."

**"Will our data be shared with anyone outside the school?"**
Address directly: "Student and staff data is stored securely. The vendor does not have access to your individual records. DPDP consent governs how parent and student data is handled."

## FAQ

**Q: Should we announce the ERP to parents before or after go-live?**
A: After go-live and after verifying data accuracy. Announcing before go-live creates expectations the school may not immediately meet. Announcing after (when the portal is ready and accurate) manages expectations correctly.

**Q: How do we handle parents who distrust digital systems?**
A: Acknowledge the concern directly: "We understand some parents prefer paper records. The digital system is the school's official record; physical printouts are available from the school office on request." Do not force digital-only access for parents who genuinely cannot use it.

**Q: How do we communicate ERP-related downtime to parents?**
A: For planned maintenance, send advance notice (24 hours). For unexpected downtime, send a short message acknowledging the issue and estimated restoration time. Silence during downtime creates unnecessary anxiety.

**Q: What language should we use for parent communication?**
A: Use the language most parents read fluently — English, Hindi, or the regional language. For schools with multilingual parent populations, a brief translation of key information is worthwhile.

**Q: How do we measure whether our communication worked?**
A: Parent portal activation rate (target: 50%+ within first month), staff attendance compliance rate (target: 85%+ digital within 4 weeks), and inbound query volume (should decrease as parents self-serve in the portal).
