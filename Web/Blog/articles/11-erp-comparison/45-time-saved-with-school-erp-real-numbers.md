## Time Saved with School ERP: Where Hours Go Back to People

**The time savings from school ERP are real but specific. Not every task becomes faster. Some tasks become dramatically faster. Understanding where the hours go back to people — and where they do not — helps set honest expectations and identify which workflows to prioritize in implementation.**

---

## Monthly Attendance Report

**Before ERP:** For a school with 30 sections of 40 students each (1,200 students), monthly attendance compilation requires:
- Class teachers hand-count their registers: 30 minutes per section
- Total across 30 sections: 15 hours of teacher time
- Clerk consolidates into a school-level summary: 2-4 hours
- Total monthly: approximately 17-19 hours across multiple people

**After ERP:** The system calculates attendance percentages continuously. The monthly report is generated in 2-3 minutes.

**Time saved: approximately 17+ hours per month, recovered across teachers and admin staff**

This is the single largest time saving most schools experience. Over a 10-month academic year, it is 170+ hours of staff time.

---

## Report Card Generation

**Before ERP:** For a school with 30 sections producing 2-3 report cards per year:
- Marks collection from teachers: 2-3 days
- Manual calculation and consolidation: 3-5 days
- Data entry for printing: 2-3 days
- Error correction after printing: 1-2 days
- Total per report card cycle: approximately 8-13 working days

**After ERP:** Teachers enter marks directly into the system. Calculation is automatic. Report cards are generated in batch. Review and correction happens before printing, not after.
- Data entry by teachers: concurrent with marking (no separate compilation step)
- Report card generation: 30 minutes to batch-generate all 1,200 cards
- Review and correction: 1-2 days (far fewer errors since there is no transcription)
- Total per cycle: approximately 2-3 working days

**Time saved: 6-10 working days per exam cycle**

---

## Defaulter List Generation

**Before ERP:** Generating a precise defaulter list requires the accountant to review 1,200 individual fee ledger records, identify outstanding balances, and compile a list sorted by class and amount. A thorough defaulter report takes 3-5 hours.

**After ERP:** The defaulter report runs in seconds. Sorted by class, by amount, by days overdue, or any combination.

**Time saved: 3-5 hours per report, which can now be generated as frequently as needed rather than once a month**

---

## CBSE List of Candidates (LOC)

**Before ERP:** LOC submission for Classes 10 and 12 requires:
- Collecting roll numbers, names, subjects, internal marks from multiple sources
- Compiling into the CBSE format
- Checking for errors
- Total: 3-5 full working days for the admission and exam teams

**After ERP:** LOC export pulls from student records already in the system. Manual review of the export takes 2-4 hours for error checking.

**Time saved: 2-4 working days per LOC submission cycle**

---

## UDISE+ Data Preparation

**Before ERP:** UDISE+ annual data requires compiling enrollment counts by grade, gender, and category; teacher counts by qualification; infrastructure details. This compilation from registers and files takes 3-5 days.

**After ERP:** Enrollment and teacher data is already in the system. Infrastructure details are a one-time data entry. UDISE+ export pre-populates most fields. Total effort: 4-6 hours.

**Time saved: 2-4 working days per annual submission**

---

## Parent Information Queries

**Before ERP:** Parents call to ask fee balance, attendance percentage, exam results. Each call requires staff to find the record, check the data, and respond. 20 calls per day at 5 minutes each = approximately 100 minutes of office staff time daily.

**After ERP:** Parents check their balance and attendance in the parent portal. Calls for routine information queries decrease substantially. The remaining calls are substantive (dispute, concern, appointment).

**Time saved: highly variable, depends on parent portal adoption rate (40-80% of information queries redirected)**

---

## Where ERP Does Not Save Time

**Complex parent concerns:** A parent calling to dispute a fee charge, raise a safety concern, or schedule a meeting requires human engagement. ERP does not save time here.

**Decision-making:** Reviewing attendance data and deciding which students need counseling takes the same human judgment. ERP provides the data faster; it does not make the decision.

**Initial implementation period:** During the first 2-3 months, staff are learning. Net time may actually increase before savings begin.

## FAQ

**Q: How do we quantify the time savings to justify the ERP cost?**
A: Calculate current staff time on the tasks above. Multiply by the staff member's hourly cost. Compare to the ERP subscription. Add fee collection improvement as a second benefit category.

**Q: Who captures the time savings — teachers or admin?**
A: Attendance compilation time returns to class teachers and clerks. Report card time returns to the exam team. Defaulter report time returns to accounts. Each role benefits differently.

**Q: Can we track actual time before and after to verify savings?**
A: Yes. Record current time spent on these tasks for one cycle before go-live. Measure again in the first cycle after go-live. The comparison provides concrete evidence.

**Q: What if time savings are lower than expected?**
A: Common cause: staff continuing to maintain paper records alongside the ERP. Address through clear direction that ERP is the only official record.

**Q: Are time savings immediate or does it take time to accumulate?**
A: Attendance savings begin in week 1 (first month-end report is the clearest). Report card savings are visible in the first exam cycle. Fee savings build as parent portal adoption grows.
