## Communication Module Comparison in School ERP

**School communication is one of the most visible ways an ERP succeeds or fails. If parents cannot reliably receive attendance alerts, fee reminders, or event announcements through the system, they revert to WhatsApp groups, which have no accountability, no audit trail, and no role-based targeting. This guide compares what a communication module in a school ERP must do.**

---

## What School Communication Must Solve

Schools need to communicate with multiple audiences simultaneously, with different messages for each:
- **Parents:** Attendance alerts, fee reminders, event announcements, circulars
- **Teachers:** Timetable changes, lesson plan submissions due, HOD notices
- **Students:** Homework assignments, exam schedules, library notices
- **All staff:** Holiday notifications, meeting invitations, policy updates

A system that sends the same message to everyone creates noise and reduces trust. A system that targets by role, class, section, or student group becomes a reliable communication channel.

---

## Circulars and Announcements

The core communication tool. Key features:

**Targeted audience:** A circular must be targeted to a specific audience, Class 7 parents, All Teaching Staff, Students in Section 9A, Transport-using parents. Untargeted mass messages are noise.

**Multiple channels:** The system should deliver through app notifications, SMS, email, and where integration is available, WhatsApp. Parents typically open app notifications within minutes; email may take hours.

**Acknowledgement tracking:** For critical circulars (fee hike notice, school closure, exam schedule), the system should track who has read the circular and send follow-up reminders to those who have not.

**Publish approval workflow:** Circulars from teachers should require HOD or admin review before going to parents. A teacher should not be able to send school-wide messages unilaterally.

---

## Parent-Teacher Communication

Free-form messaging between parents and individual teachers creates problems: teachers receive direct messages at all hours, messages are personal (not institutional), and there is no official record.

Better design: structured communication through the ERP.
- Teachers publish announcements, homework, and feedback through the system
- Parents see updates in the parent portal
- Parent queries go to the school office, not directly to the teacher
- Class teacher announces formally for class-level matters

This protects both teachers and parents while creating an institutional communication record.

---

## Visitor Management

A school's gate is also a communication point. Visitor management:
- Visitor pre-registration (expected visitor list for the day)
- OTP-verified check-in (visitor receives OTP, guard confirms identity)
- Visitor log (who visited whom, for what purpose, timing)
- Blacklist management (unwanted visitors flagged at gate)

A visitor log is also a safety record. In a POCSO or safeguarding context, being able to answer "Who visited this student on this date?" can be critical.

---

## Alumni Communication

For schools with an active alumni program:
- Alumni directory (opt-in)
- Event announcements to alumni
- Mentorship matching notifications

This is distinct from student-facing communication and should be managed separately.

## FAQ

**Q: Can parents respond to school announcements?**
A: Most school ERPs do not implement free-form reply to announcements, this creates unmanageable message volumes. Acknowledgement tracking (read/not read) is the appropriate response mechanism.

**Q: How does the system prevent a teacher from messaging all parents?**
A: Role-based publishing permissions. A class teacher can publish to their class. School-wide announcements require admin or Principal authorization. The system enforces this through the role permission matrix.

**Q: What if a parent does not have a smartphone?**
A: SMS fallback is important for parents with basic phones. Critical alerts (absence notification, fee overdue) should be delivered via SMS in addition to app notifications.

**Q: Can we archive old circulars?**
A: Yes. Historical circulars should be searchable and accessible for reference. This is useful for compliance (proving a fee-hike notice was communicated on a specific date) and for parents who missed an announcement.

**Q: Is there a way to verify parents have received urgent messages?**
A: Acknowledgement tracking shows who has opened a circular. Unread alerts after 48 hours can trigger an SMS reminder. This is particularly useful for critical safety or regulatory communications.
