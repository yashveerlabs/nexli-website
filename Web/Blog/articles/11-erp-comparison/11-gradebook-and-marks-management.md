## Gradebook and Marks Management: What to Evaluate in a School ERP

**A school ERP's gradebook is where academic data lives or dies. Poor marks management, incorrect aggregation, grade calculation errors, missed board-format requirements, creates report card crises at the end of every term. This guide explains what to evaluate before committing to any system.**

---

## The Problem with Marks Management in School ERPs

Many schools have experienced some version of this: teachers enter marks in the ERP, the system calculates grades, and the report cards come out wrong. Sometimes all students show zero marks. Sometimes grades do not match the marks entered. Sometimes CBSE's 9-point scale is applied incorrectly.

These failures happen for predictable reasons:
- Marks are aggregated using subject IDs instead of exam paper IDs, causing cross-subject contamination
- Grade calculation formulas are hard-coded for one board and applied incorrectly to another
- Internal and external marks are merged when they should remain separate

Before trusting any ERP's gradebook, test it with your actual exam data.

---

## What a Gradebook Must Support

### Multiple Assessment Types

A single term typically includes periodic tests, half-yearly and annual examinations, practicals, projects, internal assessments, and co-scholastic assessments. The system must let schools define the assessment structure, types, weights, and grade calculation rules, per class and per board.

### Auto-Calculation Per Board Rules

Grade calculation must follow your board's exact rules:

**CBSE (Classes 9-10):** Internal assessment (20 marks) plus board assessment (80 marks). Grade points: A1 (91-100) through E2 (0-20). Grade = cumulative grade point average across subjects.

**CBSE (Classes 1-8):** NEP 2020 aligned, competency-based, no percentages. Holistic Progress Card for multi-domain assessment.

The ERP must let you configure these rules. A system with hard-coded formulas will require vendor intervention whenever board rules change.

### Marks Entry Interface

Efficiency matters. A teacher entering marks for 40 students across 6 subjects should be able to open the marks sheet for their class and subject, enter marks by student, see running totals update in real time, save partial entry and return later, then submit for HOD review when complete.

### Marks Verification Workflow

Before marks are finalized: subject teacher enters marks, HOD reviews and approves, academic coordinator sees approved marks, Principal can view final aggregated marks before report cards are published. This prevents single-teacher errors from appearing on published report cards.

### NEP 2020 Holistic Progress Card (HPC)

For schools implementing NEP 2020, the HPC replaces traditional report cards for foundational and preparatory stages. The ERP must support multi-domain assessment (cognitive, social-emotional, physical, arts, vocational, life skills), multiple input sources (self, peer, teacher, parent), and visual progress output.

---

## What to Test Before Choosing

1. Enter marks for one complete exam cycle. Verify grades calculate correctly per your board's rules.
2. Ask the vendor to show the aggregation formula. Ensure the system uses the correct exam paper per subject.
3. Generate a report card. Check it matches your board's required format exactly.
4. If implementing HPC, ask for a live demo, not a mockup.

## FAQ

**Q: Can we use different grading scales for different classes?**
A: Yes, in well-designed systems. Classes 1-8 might use a competency-based system while Classes 9-12 use CBSE 9-point grades.

**Q: What happens if a teacher makes a mistake after marks are submitted?**
A: The system should allow correction requests with HOD approval, with an audit trail logging both the original entry and the correction.

**Q: Can we see historical marks for a student across multiple years?**
A: Yes, the student profile should show academic history across all terms and years for trend analysis and counselling.

**Q: How does the system handle marks for elective subjects?**
A: Elective subjects should be configured per class with relevant teachers assigned, with correct aggregation even when students take different electives.

**Q: What if our school uses a custom grading scale?**
A: Look for ERPs with fully configurable grading rules, boundaries, labels, and calculation formulas you can set without vendor involvement.
