## Hostel Management Features: What to Look for in a School ERP

**Boarding schools have a layer of operational complexity that day schools do not. Students live on campus around the clock. Wardens are responsible for safety at 2 AM, not just 2 PM. A hostel management module in a school ERP must handle room allocation, roll-call, weekend leave, and welfare tracking, and it must work reliably on a warden's phone at odd hours.**

---

## Why Hostel Management Needs Its Own Module

Day school operations end at dismissal. Boarding school operations are 24/7. The warden is responsible for knowing where every student is at any time, in their room, at study hall, at the infirmary, or on an approved weekend leave.

Without a hostel module, wardens maintain paper registers, WhatsApp groups, and verbal handoffs. These break down. Students slip through gaps. Incidents happen and the school cannot reconstruct where students were.

A hostel module creates a digital record of room assignments, roll-calls, and approved leaves, the foundation for student accountability and parent assurance.

---

## Core Hostel Module Features

### Block and Room Allocation

At the start of each academic year:
- Hostel blocks configured (Block A, Block B, boys, girls)
- Floors and room numbers within each block
- Bed count per room
- Students assigned to specific rooms

Mid-year changes (student moves rooms, new student joins hostel) should be manageable without re-doing the entire setup.

### Warden Assignment

Each block or floor should have an assigned warden with specific access:
- See all students in their block
- Mark roll-call for their block
- Record incidents for students in their block
- Access welfare information for their students

Wardens should not see data for other blocks without explicit permission.

### Roll-Call (Morning and Night)

Twice-daily roll-call is the core operational rhythm of a hostel. The system must support:
- Morning roll-call (before academic day begins)
- Night roll-call (after lights out)
- Status per student: Present, Absent, Sick (in infirmary), On Leave (approved exeat)
- Quick view: who is unaccounted for (neither marked present nor on leave)
- Alert to supervisor if students are unaccounted for after roll-call window

The mobile interface for roll-call must be fast. Wardens marking 60 students at 10 PM do not have patience for slow systems.

### Exeat (Weekend Leave) Management

Students in boarding schools go home for weekends or holidays. The exeat process must be managed formally:

1. Parent submits a leave request (dates, who will pick up the student)
2. Warden reviews and approves
3. Student departs with a signed leave slip (or digital record)
4. Expected return date is logged
5. On return, student checks back in (warden marks return in the system)

Without formal exeat tracking, wardens cannot answer: "Is this student on leave or are they missing?"

### Student Welfare Tracking for Hostel

Hostel students' welfare data links to the broader student profile:
- Medical visits from the hostel (student reported to infirmary)
- Behavioral incidents logged by warden
- Counselling referrals from hostel context
- Parent communication logs

This integration matters for child safeguarding. A student who is frequently ill, repeatedly violating curfew, or showing behavioral changes is visible to the hostel warden, counselor, and Principal as a unified concern rather than fragmented incidents.

### Hostel Fees

Hostel fees (accommodation, meals, laundry) must integrate with the main fee module:
- Separate fee line item for hostel in the student's fee ledger
- Monthly hostel charges billed and collected
- Hostel fee defaults tracked separately from tuition fee defaults

## FAQ

**Q: Can parents request exeat leave through the parent portal?**
A: Yes. Parents fill in the leave request (start date, end date, person collecting the student), and the warden receives it for approval in the ERP.

**Q: How does the system handle a student who does not return from leave?**
A: The system flags students who were on approved leave and have passed their expected return date without checking back in. This triggers a warden alert for follow-up.

**Q: Can different wardens see different blocks?**
A: Yes, with proper role scoping. Block Warden A sees only Block A students. Hostel Head sees all blocks. This is essential for large boarding schools with multiple blocks and wardens.

**Q: Does hostel roll-call integrate with classroom attendance?**
A: It should. A student absent from morning roll-call should trigger a check for classroom attendance. If they are also absent in class, the concern is escalated.

**Q: How do we handle students who are sick and in the infirmary?**
A: The medical/clinic module should communicate with hostel management. A student admitted to the infirmary is marked as "Sick" in the roll-call rather than absent or unaccounted for.
