## ERP for International Schools in India: What Is Different

**International schools in India, those following IB (International Baccalaureate), Cambridge (CAIE), or other international curricula, have specific operational requirements that diverge from mainstream Indian CBSE/ICSE schools. Some of these requirements are met by India-focused ERP; others require international platforms or specialized configuration. This guide explains the key differences.**

---

## How International Schools Differ from CBSE/ICSE Schools

**Curriculum structure:** IB PYP/MYP/DP and Cambridge IGCSE/A-Level have different assessment structures, grading scales (IB 1-7, Cambridge A*/A/B/C/D/E), and subject nomenclature than CBSE/ICSE. A marks management system designed for CBSE 9-point grading needs significant reconfiguration for IB.

**Fee structure:** International schools often charge significantly higher fees, often partially in foreign currencies (USD/GBP for expatriate families). Multi-currency fee handling adds complexity.

**Student composition:** A significant portion of students at many international schools are expatriate children, students who may arrive mid-year, leave mid-year, and whose parents may not have Indian phone numbers or bank accounts.

**Student visa compliance:** Expatriate students on student visas require the school to maintain visa records, notify authorities of enrollment status changes, and maintain attendance records that may be required for visa compliance.

**Parent communication:** Expatriate parents may have non-Indian contact details (international phone numbers, international email domains). Communication must reach parents even if they are overseas during school term.

**Data residency:** If the school serves students from EU countries, GDPR may apply for those students' data. This is a different regulatory layer than DPDP Act.

---

## ERP Requirements for International Schools

### Configurable Curriculum and Grading

The ERP must support:
- IB's 1-7 scale (or DP criteria A-D for higher-level assessments)
- Cambridge's A*/A/B/C/D/E scale
- Percentage and letter grade hybrid systems
- Subject-specific assessment criteria (IB Personal Project, CAS requirements, Extended Essay)

### Multi-Currency Fee Handling

If fees are quoted or charged in USD/GBP for expatriate families:
- Fee amounts in multiple currencies
- Exchange rate management for INR conversion
- Receipts showing both foreign currency amount and INR equivalent

### Student Visa Record Management

For schools with significant expatriate enrollment:
- Student visa type and number
- Visa expiry date with advance alerts
- Enrollment status change notifications

### Mid-Year Enrollment Flexibility

International schools frequently have mid-year student arrivals and departures. The ERP should support:
- Mid-year enrollment without disrupting existing class structures
- Pro-rated fee calculation for partial-year enrollment
- Transfer certificate and leaving certificate workflows for international destinations

### International Contact Format Support

Phone numbers in international format (+44 for UK, +1 for US), international addresses, and non-Indian email domains should not cause system errors.

---

## Where India-Focused ERP May Fall Short

Most India-focused ERPs are designed for CBSE/ICSE grading and Indian fee structures. Using them for international schools requires:
- Configuration workarounds for international grading scales (often possible but not seamless)
- Manual handling of multi-currency fees (or treating all fees in INR)
- No built-in visa compliance tracking

Specialized international school ERP platforms (Managebac for IB, iSAMS for British-curriculum schools) handle curriculum-specific requirements natively but may not integrate with Indian regulatory requirements (DPDP, CBSE affiliations that some international schools maintain, Indian payment gateways).

## FAQ

**Q: Should international schools use India-focused ERP or international ERP?**
A: Depends on the balance of requirements. A school primarily following IB with significant expatriate enrollment benefits from international ERP for curriculum support. A school that uses an international curriculum but primarily serves Indian nationals and maintains CBSE affiliation may find India-focused ERP adequate.

**Q: Can Nexli handle IB grading?**
A: Nexli's grading system is configurable. IB's 1-7 scale can be configured. Subject-specific assessment criteria (like IB's "criterion-referenced" assessment for MYP) require more specialized configuration. Schools with IB curriculum should verify this during the demo.

**Q: How do we handle GDPR for EU national students?**
A: GDPR compliance for EU national students requires additional data processing agreements and careful data handling beyond DPDP Act. Consult legal counsel for international schools with EU enrollment.

**Q: Do international schools in India need DPDP Act compliance?**
A: Yes. DPDP Act 2023 applies to all personal data of Indian residents processed in India. Even if students are expatriate nationals, the data is processed in India, making DPDP applicable.

**Q: Are there ERP vendors specifically for Indian international schools?**
A: The market is small. Most international schools in India use either specialized global ERP (Managebac, iSAMS) or adapt India-focused ERP with configuration. A few niche vendors serve this segment.
