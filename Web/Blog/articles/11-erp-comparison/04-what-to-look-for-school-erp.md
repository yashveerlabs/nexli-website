## What to Look for in a School ERP: Complete Buyer's Guide

**Most school administrators approach ERP evaluation with a generic feature checklist: Does it have Student Information? Attendance? Grades? Fees? This is a mistake. All established ERPs have these core modules. The differences lie in architecture, compliance readiness, role flexibility, mobile optimization, and security verification. This guide teaches you what to actually evaluate, so you invest in a system that solves your school's real problems, not a feature-rich box that sits half-used.**

---

## The Problem: Why Generic Checklists Fail

A typical school administrator's checklist:
- ✅ Student Information System
- ✅ Attendance Module
- ✅ Marks & Gradebook
- ✅ Fee Management
- ✅ Report Cards
- ✅ Transport Module
- ✅ Payroll & HR
- ✅ Parent Portal

**Result?** Every established ERP checks all these boxes. But the evaluation ends there. You miss the critical differences:

- Is the attendance module mobile-optimized or desktop-first?
- Does the fee module solve *your* specific concession workflow or force you to adapt?
- Is the parent portal child-scoped (privacy-first) or a free-for-all data view?
- Does the platform enforce DPDP Act 2023 compliance or require manual configuration?
- Are roles configurable or hard-coded?
- Is security architecture tested and verifiable or claimed without evidence?

This guide teaches you what to actually evaluate.

## Layer 1: Strategic Fit, Does This ERP Serve Your School's Strategy?

Before looking at features, understand your school's strategic priorities over the next 3 years.

### Questions to Ask Yourself

**1. Compliance & Governance**
- Are you serious about DPDP Act 2023 compliance, or is it just a checkbox?
- Do you need native NEP 2020 Holistic Progress Card, or will report cards suffice?
- Do you operate RTE (Right to Education) seats requiring quota tracking?
- Do you need a formal Compliance Calendar reminding about CBSE LOC, UDISE+, fire NOC, POSH, water testing, lab safety?

**If YES to 2+:** Your ERP must have native compliance features. Platforms like Nexli build compliance into the architecture. Platforms like Fedena require manual configuration.

**2. Role Complexity**
- Does your school have specialist roles beyond Principal → HOD → Teacher?
  - DPO (Data Protection Officer)?
  - Academic Coordinator Tier 1 / Tier 2?
  - Counselor with confidential access?
  - Transport Coordinator?
  - Hostel Warden?
  - Sports Coordinator?
  - IT Security Officer?

**If YES to 3+:** Your ERP must support configurable roles without custom code. Nexli's 118+ role matrix handles this. Fedena requires custom development.

**3. Scale & Ambition**
- Are you a single school or a multi-campus chain?
- Do you plan to expand to 2–3 additional campuses in 3 years?
- Do you run different school types (day school + hostel + international)?

**If multi-campus or expanding:** Your ERP must support per-school data isolation with centralized admin oversight. Both Nexli and Fedena support this; evaluate their multi-campus setup.

**4. Operational Focus**
- Is your school primarily a day school or boarding school?
- Do you run complex Transport (GPS, RFID, multiple routes)?
- Do you run Hostel (roll-call, exeat tracking, medical support)?
- Do you operate Canteen (meal planning, PM POSHAN reporting)?

**If YES to 2+:** Your ERP must have mature operational modules. Vyom ERP excels at Transport and Hostel. Nexli and Fedena offer basic versions. Evaluate directly.

**5. Academic Excellence**
- Do you emphasize Exam results, rankings, and academic analytics?
- Do you need sophisticated report card generation (CBSE 9-point, ICSE A/B/C/D, custom scales)?
- Do you track competitive rankings across classes and subjects?

**If YES to 2+:** Consider academic-focused ERPs like Eduwiser alongside generalist platforms. Eduwiser's exam scheduling and rankings are industry-leading.

## Layer 2: Core Features, Does the ERP Actually Work for Your Operations?

### Module Completeness Checklist

**Core (Non-Negotiable):**
- Student Information System: Master profiles, admissions pipeline, document locker, transfer certificates
- Attendance: Period-wise (by subject) + daily consolidated (by class); attendance alerts
- Marks & Gradebook: Multiple assessment types, auto-calculation per board rules
- Report Cards: CBSE 9-point format (or your board equivalent) with ranks
- Fee Management: Class-wise structures, installments, concessions, online payment gateway
- Parent Portal: Child-scoped visibility (attendance, fees, homework, report cards, announcements)

**Advanced (Depends on Your School):**
- NEP 2020 Holistic Progress Card: Multi-domain assessment with self/peer/teacher/parent inputs
- Transfer Certificate Multi-Department Workflow: Automated clearance (Library → Accounts → Hostel → Transport → Principal)
- Transport Module: GPS live-tracking, RFID boarding, driver/conductor management, route optimization
- Hostel Module: Block assignment, roll-call (morning + night), exeat (weekend leave) tracking
- Canteen & Nutrition: Menu planning, meal headcount (PM POSHAN compliance), FSSAI vendor tracking
- Medical/Clinic: Visit logs, immunizations, allergies, chronic conditions, medical supply inventory
- Counselling Workspace: Confidential session notes, behavioral tracking, at-risk flagging
- Payroll & HR: Salary calculation, EPF/ESI/TDS, leave management, staff directory
- Library Management: Catalog, circulation (issue/return/renewal), overdue alerts, reservations

### Evaluation Questions

1. **Ask the vendor:** "Walk me through your Attendance module using my actual data. Can I do period-wise by subject and daily consolidated? Can I track 75% threshold and send alerts? Does it work offline on a mobile app?"

2. **Ask the vendor:** "Show me how your Fee Management handles my actual fee structure: Class 6 pays ₹50K/year in 2 installments; Class 12 scholarship students pay 25% less. Can your system model this without custom code?"

3. **Ask the vendor:** "Demonstrate your NEP 2020 HPC. Can teachers, students, and parents input scores? Does the system calculate composite domain scores? Can I generate a visual progress report?"

4. **Ask the vendor:** "Show me your Transfer Certificate workflow. Can I require clearance from Library, Accounts, Hostel, Transport in sequence? Does the system prevent issuance until all departments approve?"

5. **Ask the vendor:** "If I need a custom report (e.g., subject-wise performance by gender), how easy is it to build? Do I need a developer or can your admin UI do it?"

**Do Not Accept:** Generic demos with sample data. Insist on running the module with your actual numbers and workflows.

## Layer 3: Compliance & Security, Can You Verify It's Actually Secure?

### DPDP Act 2023 Compliance

**What You Need:**
- Parental consent collection (OTP-verified form, no loopholes)
- Data-access audit logs (who accessed what when)
- Encrypted storage for sensitive data (Aadhaar, medical records)
- DPO dashboard (review audit logs without seeing raw data)
- Breach notification templates and workflows
- Consent withdrawal process (parent can revoke consent)

**How to Evaluate:**

1. **Ask the vendor:** "Walk me through your parental consent workflow. How is it OTP-verified? Can I prove to a regulator that consent was collected?"

2. **Ask the vendor:** "Show me a sample audit log. If a teacher accesses a student's medical record, is that logged? Can my DPO see who accessed what?"

3. **Ask the vendor:** "How do you encrypt medical records? Is encryption at-rest and in-transit? Can you show me the encryption specification?"

4. **Red Flag:** If the vendor says "DPDP compliance is your responsibility; we provide privacy features," they're not compliant by default. You'll spend weeks building workflows.

5. **Green Flag:** If the vendor demonstrates parental consent OTP workflow, shows audit logs, and explains encryption, they've built compliance into the system.

---

### CBSE, RTE, NEP 2020 Compliance

**What You Need:**
- CBSE LOC export in required format for Class 10/12 board exams
- UDISE+ annual data export (school profile, enrollment by grade, gender, etc.)
- RTE quota tracking (EWS seat monitoring, reimbursement claims)
- NEP 2020 5+3+3+4 structure support
- Holistic Progress Card (HPC) for multi-domain assessment
- Internal assessment moderation workflows

**How to Evaluate:**

1. **Ask the vendor:** "Can you export my Class 12 data in CBSE LOC format? Can I verify the format matches CBSE requirements?"

2. **Ask the vendor:** "Do you pre-populate UDISE+ reports with my data, or do I manually enter everything?"

3. **Ask the vendor:** "Show me your NEP 2020 structure mapping. Do you support 5+3+3+4 sections?"

4. **Green Flag:** Vendors who show real UDISE+ exports and CBSE LOC examples have been tested. Vendors who hedge or show sample data are likely generic.

---

### Security Architecture Verification

**What You Need:**
- Role-based access control (RBAC) that's actually enforced
- Per-school data isolation (if multi-campus)
- Firestore/database security rules that are tested, not just claimed

**How to Evaluate:**

1. **Ask the vendor:** "Can you show me your security ruleset? Are your access controls tested? What's your test coverage?"

2. **Green Flag:** Nexli publishes its Firestore security ruleset (tested 145/0 tests passing). You can verify the rules directly.

3. **Red Flag:** If the vendor says "Our security is enterprise-grade" but can't show evidence, it's a claim, not a verification.

4. **Ask the vendor:** "If a Principal tries to access another school's data (in a multi-campus setup), does your system block it? Can you demo this?"

5. **Ask the vendor:** "What's your data breach response plan? How do you notify parents? What's your timeline?"

## Layer 4: Technology & Architecture, Is This Built for Today, Not Yesterday?

### Mobile-First vs. Responsive Design

**Mobile-First (Built for phones first, desktop second):**
- Works on 412px screens (most common phone size in India).
- Functions offline (cached data, sync on reconnect).
- Optimized for 2G/3G networks (smaller payloads, progressive enhancement).
- Teachers mark attendance in the field. Parents check fees on the bus.

**Responsive Design (Built for desktop, optimized for mobile):**
- Responsive layout (looks okay on mobile, but not optimized).
- Requires internet (no offline support).
- May timeout on weak networks.
- Best used on desktop or WiFi-connected devices.

**How to Evaluate:**

1. Test each ERP on your actual school internet (often 2–3 Mbps in India). Don't demo on corporate WiFi.
2. Test on a 412px screen (Galaxy S20 or similar).
3. Try logging in offline, marking attendance, and syncing when WiFi returns. Which system works?

**Platform Comparison:**
- **Nexli:** Mobile-first (tested on Galaxy S20).
- **Fedena:** Responsive, not optimized mobile-first.
- **Vyom:** Responsive, not optimized mobile-first.

---

### Cloud vs. On-Premise

**Cloud (SaaS):**
- Pro: Auto-backup, disaster recovery, automatic updates, no IT maintenance.
- Con: Requires internet, data residency concerns, vendor lock-in.

**On-Premise (Self-hosted):**
- Pro: Full data control, offline capability, no vendor lock-in.
- Con: Requires IT infrastructure, you manage backups and updates.

**How to Evaluate:**

1. **Ask the vendor:** "If I go cloud, where is my data stored? Is it India-resident?"
2. **Ask the vendor:** "What's your backup frequency? Can I recover data if there's corruption?"
3. **If on-premise is important:** Only Fedena and AARAMBH offer on-premise options.

---

### Real-Time Sync vs. Polling

**Real-Time Sync (Firebase, WebSockets):**
- When a teacher marks attendance, all admins see it instantly.
- When a fee payment comes in, the ledger updates immediately.
- Pro: Operational transparency, faster decision-making.
- Con: Requires always-on internet.

**Polling (Traditional database, periodic sync):**
- Changes sync every 5–10 minutes (configurable).
- Pro: More control, works with intermittent internet.
- Con: Data lags behind reality.

**Nexli uses real-time sync (Firebase). Fedena and Vyom typically use polling.** Evaluate if real-time transparency is important to your operations.

## Layer 5: Implementation & Support, Can Your School Actually Adopt This?

### Implementation Timeline

**Fast (2–3 weeks):**
- Data import is straightforward (CSV/Excel).
- Staff training is light (1–2 sessions).
- Minimal customization needed.
- Platforms: Nexli, AARAMBH.

**Standard (4–6 weeks):**
- Data migration requires validation and cleanup.
- Multiple staff training sessions.
- Some customization (custom roles, workflows).
- Platforms: Fedena, Vyom, Eduwiser.

**Slow (8–12 weeks):**
- Complex data migration (legacy systems, data quality issues).
- Extensive customization required.
- Multiple implementation waves (rollout by department).

**How to Evaluate:**

1. **Ask the vendor:** "How long does a typical implementation take? Can you show me a timeline for a school my size?"
2. **Ask the vendor:** "What happens during the parallel run? How long do we run old + new systems together?"
3. **Red Flag:** If the vendor promises 1-week implementation, they're overselling.

---

### Support Model

**On-Site Support (Fedena, Vyom):**
- Implementation partner visits your school.
- Staff training is in-person.
- Post-go-live reviews are scheduled.
- Pro: Personal support, higher adoption.
- Con: Expensive, slower response to issues.

**Remote Support (Nexli, AARAMBH):**
- Email, video call, chat support.
- Staff training is video-based.
- Ticketing system for issues.
- Pro: Lower cost, 24/7 availability (in many cases).
- Con: Requires self-service troubleshooting.

**How to Evaluate:**

1. **Ask the vendor:** "What's included in your support plan? Email? Phone? On-site reviews?"
2. **Ask the vendor:** "What's your average response time for critical issues?"
3. **Call a reference school:** "How responsive is the vendor when you have a problem?"

---

### Training & Adoption

**How to Evaluate:**

1. **Ask the vendor:** "How do you train staff? Is it role-based (Principal, HOD, Teacher, Parent gets different training)?"
2. **Ask the vendor:** "Do you provide video training materials or just live sessions?"
3. **Ask the vendor:** "If a teacher quits and a new teacher joins mid-year, can they get trained?"

**Red Flag:** If the vendor says "Staff training is your responsibility; we just provide the software."

## Layer 6: Cost & ROI, Is This a Good Investment?

### Cost Components

1. **Software License:** Annual subscription or one-time purchase.
2. **Implementation:** Data migration, staff training, customization.
3. **Integration:** Payment gateway setup, biometric device integration, payment reconciliation.
4. **Hardware:** Biometric devices, RFID readers, WiFi upgrade (if needed).
5. **Annual Support:** Vendor support, maintenance, updates.

### Calculating 3-Year TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)

**Example: School with 300 students**

| Component | Nexli | Fedena | Vyom | AARAMBH |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Annual License | ₹1,50,000 | ₹3,00,000 (or ₹50K upfront + ₹20K/yr) | ₹1,20,000 | ₹60,000 |
| Implementation | 2 weeks (₹1,00,000) | 4 weeks (₹2,00,000) | 3 weeks (₹1,50,000) | 2 weeks (₹50,000) |
| Integration | Minimal (₹30,000) | ₹50,000 | ₹50,000 | ₹20,000 |
| Customization | Minimal (₹30,000) | ₹1,00,000 | ₹75,000 | Minimal (₹10,000) |
| Annual Support | ₹30,000 | ₹50,000 | ₹30,000 | ₹15,000 |
| **3-Year Total** | **₹5,80,000** | **₹9,00,000** | **₹6,85,000** | **₹2,55,000** |

**Note:** Fedena appears expensive upfront but includes on-site support. AARAMBH is cheapest. Nexli and Vyom are mid-range.

### ROI (Return on Investment)

**Quantifiable Benefits:**

1. **Admin time saved:** If one admin spends 20 hours/week on manual attendance entry, and the ERP automates 15 hours, that's 15 hours/week × 48 weeks = 720 hours/year = ₹3,60,000 saved (at ₹500/hour).

2. **Fee collection improvement:** If fee collection improves from 85% to 95% (10% improvement), and your annual fee is ₹2Cr, that's ₹20 lakhs recovered.

3. **Teacher time saved:** If subject teachers spend 5 hours/week on mark entry, and the ERP automates 3 hours, that's 3 hours × 45 teachers × 48 weeks = 6,480 hours = ₹32,40,000 saved.

**Break-Even Analysis:**
- TCO: ₹5–9 lakhs over 3 years.
- Annual benefits: ₹20–50 lakhs (depending on your metrics).
- **Payback period:** 1–2 months.

**How to Evaluate:**

1. **Ask the vendor:** "Can you show me case studies of schools that improved fee collection or admin efficiency?"
2. **Call reference schools:** "What was your ROI? How quickly did you break even?"

## Layer 7: The Final 20 Questions, Ask Every ERP Vendor

### Feature & Functionality
1. Can you demo your Attendance module with my actual school data (period-wise + daily consolidated)?
2. Does your Fee Management support my concession workflow without custom code?
3. How do you handle multi-campus data isolation if I expand to 2 additional schools?
4. Is your NEP 2020 HPC production-ready, or is it in beta / coming soon?
5. Can you export my data in standard formats (CSV, Excel, JSON)?

### Security & Compliance
6. Show me your DPDP Act 2023 compliance. How do you collect parental consent with OTP?
7. Can you share your security ruleset or architecture documentation?
8. What's your data breach response time? How do you notify parents?
9. Where is my data stored? Is it India-resident?
10. How do you encrypt sensitive data (Aadhaar, medical records)?

### Implementation & Support
11. What's your typical implementation timeline for a school my size?
12. What's included in staff training? Is it role-based?
13. Do you provide on-site support, or is it remote-only?
14. What's your average response time for critical issues?
15. Can you show me a reference school in my state?

### Technology & Architecture
16. Is your system mobile-first or responsive? Can you demo it on a 412px screen?
17. Does your mobile app work offline?
18. Is your system cloud-only or do you offer on-premise?
19. What's your backup frequency and disaster recovery plan?
20. If I decide to switch vendors in 3 years, how easily can I export my data?

## Closing: The ERP Decision Checklist

Before signing with any vendor:

- [ ] **Strategic Fit:** Does this ERP align with my school's 3-year priorities?
- [ ] **Core Features:** Did I test the platform with MY actual data and workflows?
- [ ] **Compliance:** Can I verify DPDP, CBSE, RTE, NEP 2020 compliance directly?
- [ ] **Security:** Did the vendor show me tested security rules or verifiable evidence?
- [ ] **Mobile:** Did I test the app on my school's actual internet (2–3 Mbps)?
- [ ] **Implementation:** Is the timeline realistic for my school size?
- [ ] **Support:** Does the support model match my staff's technical comfort level?
- [ ] **TCO & ROI:** Did I calculate 3-year cost and quantify expected benefits?
- [ ] **References:** Did I call 2–3 reference schools and ask hard questions?
- [ ] **Exit Plan:** Can I export my data and switch vendors if needed?
