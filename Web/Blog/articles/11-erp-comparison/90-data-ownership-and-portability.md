## Data Ownership and Portability in School ERP: What Schools Need to Know

**School ERP systems hold student records, fee history, health data, staff payroll records, and attendance — years of institutional data that belongs to the school and the families whose children are enrolled. Who legally owns this data, what the vendor can do with it, and whether the school can take it if the relationship ends are questions every school should understand before signing a contract.**

---

## Who Owns School ERP Data?

In a well-structured contract, the answer is clear: the school owns all data entered into the system. The vendor provides the software to store and process that data — they do not acquire ownership of the data itself.

In poorly written contracts, this may not be stated, or may include language giving the vendor:
- A license to use "aggregated and anonymized" data for product improvement
- Rights to retain data for a defined period after contract termination
- Rights to use data for "benchmarking" or "analytics"

**What to look for in the contract:**
- Explicit statement: "All data entered into the system by the Customer remains the property of the Customer"
- Restriction on vendor use: "Vendor will not use Customer data for purposes other than providing the contracted service"
- Data deletion commitment: "Vendor will delete all Customer data within 60 days of contract termination upon written request"

---

## DPDP Act 2023 and School Data

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDP) applies to personal data of Indian residents processed by any entity. For schools:
- Student data (name, age, attendance, marks, health records) is personal data
- Parent contact information is personal data
- Staff records are personal data

**What this means for the vendor relationship:**
- The school is a "Data Fiduciary" — responsible for how student and parent data is processed
- The school must have documented consent from parents for data processing
- The school must ensure the vendor (as a "Data Processor") is bound by contractual data protection obligations
- If the vendor processes data in unauthorized ways, the school bears regulatory accountability

Ask vendors: "How does your Data Processing Agreement reflect the DPDP Act 2023 requirements?" If they cannot answer, that is a compliance risk for your school.

## Data Portability: What It Means and Why It Matters

Data portability is the ability to export your data from the ERP in a usable format and import it into another system. Without portability:
- Switching vendors requires rebuilding 5 years of student records from scratch
- A vendor shutdown means permanent data loss (or very expensive recovery)
- Year-on-year historical data for analytics is lost when you switch

**What usable portability looks like:**
- Student records exportable as CSV or Excel
- Fee history with individual transaction detail exportable
- Attendance history by student, by month, by year exportable
- Marks and assessment history exportable per subject per term
- Staff records exportable

**What poor portability looks like:**
- "We can export your data but it will take 8 weeks and cost ₹50,000"
- Data available only as PDF reports (not re-importable)
- Export in proprietary format that no other system accepts

---

## What to Require in the Contract

1. **Self-service export:** The school can export data at any time without vendor involvement (download button in the admin dashboard)
2. **Formats:** CSV, Excel, or standard JSON for all data types
3. **Completeness:** Every module's data is exportable (not just students — also fees, attendance, marks, staff)
4. **Timeline:** Complete export available within 30 days of request
5. **No charge:** Data export is included in the contract, not a billable service

## FAQ

**Q: Can a vendor legally claim ownership of data we entered?**
A: Not if the contract is clear. If the contract does not specify data ownership, it defaults to the vendor's standard terms, which may or may not favor the school. This is why explicit contract language matters.

**Q: What if our vendor is storing data outside India?**
A: The DPDP Act 2023 has provisions regarding cross-border data transfer. For sensitive personal data of Indian children, data localization (storage within India) is the safer choice. Ask your vendor where your data is physically stored.

**Q: Does data portability mean any ERP can import from any other ERP?**
A: No. Portability means your data is in a readable format. Import format compatibility varies by system. When switching vendors, both the export format and the new system's import capability need to be evaluated.

**Q: What happens to student data when a student leaves the school?**
A: The school must retain academic records (marks, attendance, certificates) for a defined period for verification purposes. The DPDP Act requires that personal data not needed for the original purpose be deleted. The school should have a data retention policy that covers both current and departed students.

**Q: How long should we retain historical ERP data?**
A: Academic records (marks, attendance, TC records) should be retained for 7-10 years for alumni verification and regulatory purposes. Personal contact information of departed families can be deleted earlier, following the school's data retention policy.
