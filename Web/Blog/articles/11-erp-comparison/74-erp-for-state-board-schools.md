## ERP for State Board Schools: Regional Compliance and Grading Support

**India has 29 state boards, each with its own grading scales, examination structures, and reporting requirements. Maharashtra SSC/HSC, UP Board, Karnataka SSLC, Tamil Nadu HSC, Rajasthan Board, each has distinct requirements that a school ERP must accommodate. Most national ERP vendors are optimized for CBSE; state board schools must verify specific features carefully.**

---

## What Varies Across State Boards

**Grading scales:** State boards use different grading systems. Maharashtra SSC uses a grade point scale (10-point); UP Board uses percentage; Karnataka SSLC uses marks and grade; Tamil Nadu uses a different letter-grade system. The ERP must support the specific scale for your state board.

**Examination structure:** Some state boards have two exams (November and March main); others have quarterly or semester exams. Term structures vary.

**Government portal submission:** Many states have their own school data portals (Maharashtra: U-DISE; UP: PRERNA portal; Rajasthan: Shala Darpan) that require annual data submission in state-specific formats.

**Regional language in reports:** State board schools often require report cards and official documents in the regional language (Marathi, Hindi, Kannada, Tamil). English-only report cards may not be acceptable.

---

## Critical Questions for State Board ERP Evaluation

### "Can you configure our state board's specific grading scale?"

For Maharashtra SSC (as example):
- 10-point scale with letter grades
- Specific pass marks by subject
- Grace marks policy
- Internal assessment components

Each state has variations. Ask the vendor to configure your state's specific scheme in the demo.

### "Do you support report cards in our regional language?"

If your school requires Marathi, Hindi, Kannada, or Tamil in official documents, the ERP must support the regional language, not just for interface but for generated documents (report cards, fee receipts, circulars).

### "Can you export data for our state government portal?"

Maharashtra's SARAL portal, UP's PRERNA portal, Rajasthan's Shala Darpan, each requires annual submission in specific formats. The ERP should pre-populate these portal fields from existing data.

### "Do you have reference schools in our state board?"

A vendor with 100 CBSE school customers and zero state board customers has not encountered your board's specific requirements. Ask for reference schools under your specific state board.

---

## What Most National ERP Vendors Get Wrong for State Board Schools

**CBSE-centric default configuration:** Many ERP systems default to CBSE's 9-point grading and require significant reconfiguration for state board grading. Ask whether state board configuration is standard or requires vendor support.

**No regional language support:** English-only documents are not suitable for state board schools where regional language is official.

**No state portal export:** The ERP supports UDISE+ (national) but not the state-specific portal, requiring manual re-entry.

**Incorrect pass/fail calculation:** State boards have specific rules about grace marks, compartmental exams, and subject-wise pass requirements. The ERP must implement these rules correctly or report card calculations will be wrong.

## FAQ

**Q: Can the ERP handle a school that is converting from state board to CBSE?**
A: Yes, in most cases. The academic configuration is updated at the beginning of the transition year. Historical records from state board years remain in the system under the old configuration.

**Q: What if the ERP does not support our state's portal format?**
A: State portal formats can often be addressed through custom export configuration or data mapping. Ask vendors specifically what would be involved and whether it costs extra.

**Q: Are there ERP vendors that specialize in specific state boards?**
A: Yes. Regional ERP vendors with local roots often specialize in the dominant board in their state (e.g., Maharashtra-focused vendors are strong on SSC requirements). These may offer better regional compliance support but may have less breadth in other areas.

**Q: How do we handle concurrent affiliation with state board and another board?**
A: Some schools maintain dual affiliation (e.g., state board for Classes 1-9 and CBSE for Classes 10-12). The ERP must support different academic configurations for different class groups.

**Q: Does DPDP Act compliance vary by state?**
A: DPDP Act 2023 is a central law; it applies uniformly across all states. However, some states may have additional data protection or student privacy provisions under state law. Consult legal counsel for state-specific requirements.
