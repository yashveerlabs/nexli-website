## Parent Portal: What Parents Actually Need from a School ERP

**Most school parent portals give parents too much, or too little. Too much means parents see data from other students, creating privacy issues. Too little means parents cannot find the information they actually need, so they call the school instead. The right parent portal shows each parent exactly their child's data: attendance, fees, homework, report cards, and announcements, nothing more, nothing less.**

---

## What Parents Actually Call Schools About

Before designing a parent portal, understand what problems it must solve. The most common reasons parents contact schools:

1. "Did my child attend today?", Answered by real-time attendance visibility
2. "How much fee is due?", Answered by fee ledger access and payment link
3. "What homework does my child have?", Answered by homework module visibility
4. "When is the next exam?", Answered by timetable and exam schedule
5. "What was my child's report card?", Answered by published report card access
6. "Is the school closed tomorrow?", Answered by announcements/circular access

A parent portal that answers these six questions eliminates 80% of parent phone calls to the school office.

---

## Child-Scoped Data: Privacy by Architecture

This is the most important architectural decision in parent portal design.

**Child-scoped:** Each parent sees only their child's data. If Parent A has two children at the school, they see data for Child 1 and Child 2, not data for other students, not staff records, not school finances.

**Non-scoped:** Parents can see class-level data, student directories, or other data beyond their child. This is a privacy violation under DPDP Act 2023.

Under DPDP Act 2023, schools are custodians of student data. Allowing parents to see other students' data (even indirectly through class comparisons or student directories) is a compliance issue.

Test this during vendor evaluation: log in as a parent and check what data is visible. If you can see another student's attendance or report card, the system is not DPDP-compliant.

## Core Parent Portal Features

### Real-Time Attendance Visibility

Parents should see:
- Today's attendance status for their child (Present/Absent/Late/Leave)
- Notification when marked absent (not just visibility, active alert)
- Monthly attendance summary (attendance percentage per subject and overall)
- Alert when attendance drops below 80% threshold (early warning before it hits 75%)

### Fee Ledger and Payment

Parents should see:
- Current fee balance (what is due)
- Payment history (what has been paid, when, how)
- Upcoming installments and due dates
- Ability to pay online (UPI, net banking, cards) from the portal
- Digital receipt after payment

### Homework and Assignments

Parents should see:
- Homework assigned per subject, per day
- Assignment due dates
- Whether their child has submitted or not
- Any feedback from the teacher

This is particularly important for parents of younger students who want to ensure homework is being completed.

### Timetable and Exam Schedule

Parents should see:
- Weekly timetable for their child's class
- Upcoming examination dates
- Exam syllabus or coverage where teachers have published it

### Report Cards

Parents should see:
- Published report cards per term
- Access should be time-gated (only after official publication, not before)
- PDF download for their records

### Announcements and Circulars

Parents should receive:
- School announcements (holiday schedule, parent-teacher meetings, events)
- Class-specific announcements (from their child's class teacher)
- Emergency notices (school closure, schedule changes)

---

## What the Parent Portal Should NOT Have

- Student directory (other students' names, contact information)
- Staff directory (staff contact information)
- School-wide financial data
- Other families' fee status
- Real-time location tracking of students (privacy concern)
- Free-form messaging to teachers (structured communication only)

## How Nexli's Parent Portal Works

Nexli's parent portal is child-scoped by design. Each parent sees only their children's data, attendance, homework, fees, report cards, timetable, and announcements. No student directory, no school finances, no staff records.

Parents receive automatic alerts when their child is marked absent. The fee ledger shows all charges, payments, and concessions with an online payment option (UPI/net banking/cards). Report cards are published to the parent portal after official release, with PDF download.

Announcements are targeted: a parent in Class 6A sees announcements from the school, from Class 6, and from Section 6A. They do not see announcements meant only for Class 12 or other sections.

## FAQ

**Q: Can parents pay fees through the parent portal?**
A: Yes. The portal should integrate with Indian payment gateways (UPI, net banking, cards). Payment updates the fee ledger instantly and generates a digital receipt.

**Q: Can parents update their contact information?**
A: This is useful, but changes should require verification (OTP) and admin review before they are applied to student records.

**Q: What if a parent has children in different schools (in a chain)?**
A: The parent should log in once and see separate sections for each child at each school, with complete data isolation between schools.

**Q: Can parents submit leave requests for their child through the portal?**
A: This is a valuable feature. Parents fill in a leave request (dates, reason), and the class teacher receives it for approval. The approved leave appears in the attendance record.

**Q: Does the parent portal work on a basic Android phone?**
A: It should. A mobile-first parent portal works in any modern browser on any device. Test it on the lowest-spec phone your parent community uses.
