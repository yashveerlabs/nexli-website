## HR and Staff Management Features in School ERP

**Schools are people-intensive organizations. A 500-student school typically has 40-60 staff members across teaching, administrative, operational, and support roles. HR management, tracking qualifications, attendance, leaves, appraisals, and compliance, is a significant operational burden without a system. This guide explains what a school ERP's HR module must do.**

---

## Staff Directory: The Foundation

Every school ERP must maintain a complete staff directory with:

**Personal information:** Full name, date of birth, gender, contact details, emergency contact, Aadhaar number (encrypted)

**Professional information:** Qualifications (B.Ed., M.Ed., degree subjects, year), experience (previous institutions with dates), specializations, teaching subjects and classes assigned

**Employment information:** Date of joining, employment type (permanent, contract, probationary), designation, department, reporting manager, salary grade

**Background verification:** Police verification status, character certificate from previous employer, reference check records, all with dates

**Documents:** Uploaded copies of qualification certificates, experience letters, identity proof, bank account details for salary disbursement

The staff directory is the source record for payroll, attendance, and leave management. It must be complete and accurate.

---

## Staff Attendance

Staff attendance must capture:
- Daily arrival and departure times (biometric or manual entry)
- Late arrivals (against configured start time)
- Early departures
- Work-from-home or remote work days (if applicable)

Staff attendance must feed directly into payroll, late arrivals and unauthorized absences affect salary deductions.

---

## Leave Management

Schools manage multiple leave types:
- Casual Leave (CL): typically 12 days/year
- Earned Leave (EL): accumulated over service
- Medical/Sick Leave (ML): for certified medical conditions
- Maternity/Paternity Leave: as per statutory requirements
- Compensatory Off (CompOff): for working on holidays
- Special Leave: for official duty (conferences, training programs)

The leave management system must:
- Track leave balances per staff member per leave type
- Process leave applications (staff submits → HOD approves → admin records)
- Alert when leave balance is exhausted
- Calculate encashable leave at year-end
- Feed approved leaves to payroll for accurate salary calculation

---

## Recruitment Pipeline

When schools hire new staff, the recruitment pipeline tracks:
- Job opening (position, department, qualifications required)
- Applications received (name, qualifications, experience)
- Interview scheduling
- Interview feedback and scores
- Offer letter generation
- Joining date and onboarding

A recruitment pipeline in the ERP prevents hiring decisions from happening in email threads and WhatsApp chats with no systematic record.

---

## Probation Tracking

New staff typically serve a 6-month probation period before confirmation. The system must:
- Record probation start and expected end date
- Alert the HOD and HR before probation end
- Record confirmation decision (confirmed, extended, terminated)

Automated alerts prevent HR from missing probation end dates, which can create legal complications if not managed formally.

---

## Appraisals and Increments

Annual performance appraisals drive salary increments. The system should support:
- Appraisal form completion by staff member
- HOD rating and remarks
- Principal review
- Salary increment decision recorded

The link between appraisal results and salary changes in payroll should be direct, no re-entry.

## FAQ

**Q: Can staff apply for leave from a mobile app?**
A: Yes. Staff should be able to submit leave applications from their phone. HODs should receive and process these from their phone as well.

**Q: How do we handle contract staff vs. permanent staff differently?**
A: Staff categories should be configurable with different leave entitlements, pay structures, and statutory deduction rules. Contract staff may not be covered by EPF/ESI; permanent staff are.

**Q: What about staff who work across multiple departments?**
A: A staff member can be assigned to multiple departments. Leave approvals may route to their primary department HOD.

**Q: How does the system handle staff who leave the school?**
A: Departing staff should be marked as "Separated" with a separation date and reason. Their historical records remain (for compliance and reference), but they no longer appear in active directories.

**Q: Can we track professional development (training programs, certifications)?**
A: This is a valuable feature. Staff training records, who attended which programs, dates, certificate issued, should be part of the staff profile and visible to HR and management.
