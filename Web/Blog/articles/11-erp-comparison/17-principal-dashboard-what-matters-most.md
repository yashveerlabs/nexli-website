## Principal Dashboard: What Matters Most in a School ERP

**A principal is the operating system of a school, responsible for seeing everything, knowing what needs attention, and acting before problems become crises. The principal dashboard in a school ERP must surface the right information in two minutes without requiring the principal to run reports or navigate deep into modules. This guide explains what matters most.**

---

## The Principal's Information Problem

Most principals in India manage hundreds or thousands of students, dozens of staff, significant finances, regulatory compliance, and student safety, simultaneously. The challenge is not a lack of data; it is that data is fragmented. Attendance is in one system, fees in another, complaints in email, compliance deadlines in a spreadsheet.

When a principal discovers a problem, a student who has been absent for two weeks, a teacher who has not submitted lesson plans for a month, a compliance deadline that passed, they discover it weeks after the fact.

The right principal dashboard surfaces these issues proactively, not reactively.

---

## What the Principal Dashboard Must Show

### Attendance Health (Today and This Week)

- School-wide attendance rate today (% of students present)
- Comparison with yesterday and last week
- Classes with unusually low attendance (below school average)
- List of students absent 5+ consecutive days (potential dropout risk)
- Staff attendance summary (who is present, who is on leave, which classes are covered)

### Fee Collection Status

- Total fees billed this academic year
- Total collected vs. outstanding
- This month's collection vs. last month (trend)
- Number of students with overdue fees beyond 30 days
- Top defaulter classes (which class sections have highest outstanding)

### At-Risk Students

- Students with attendance below 75% (board exam eligibility risk)
- Students with significant marks decline from last assessment
- Students flagged for welfare concerns (counselling, behavioral incidents)

### Compliance Calendar (Next 30 Days)

- Upcoming compliance deadlines (CBSE LOC, UDISE+, fire NOC renewal, FSSAI license, POSH report, staff background verification, EPF/ESI filings)
- Days remaining to each deadline
- Completed vs. pending

### Staffing Gaps

- Teachers on leave today
- Classes without a substitute teacher assigned
- Pending approvals (leave requests, expense claims, purchase orders)

### Academic Progress

- Lesson plan submission rate by department (which HODs are behind)
- Homework completion rates across classes
- Assessment scores trend (are students improving or declining)

---

## What Should NOT Be on the Principal Dashboard

The principal does not need to see:
- Raw attendance registers (that is the class teacher's view)
- Individual pay slips (that is the HR module)
- Detailed fee ledgers per student (that is the accounts team's view)
- Detailed lesson plans (that is the HOD's review function)

The principal needs aggregated signals, not raw data. Clicking into raw data should be possible but not the default view.

---

## Two-Minute Check vs. Deep Dive

The principal dashboard should support two use patterns:

**Two-minute morning check:** Principal opens the app on their phone before morning assembly. They see attendance health (is today's attendance normal?), overnight events (any POCSO alerts, welfare incidents?), and today's compliance deadlines. Five key numbers visible in one screen.

**Deep dive (15-20 minutes):** Principal sits at a desk and reviews trends. Fee collection month-over-month, attendance decline in Class 9B, lesson plan submission rates by department. Drill-down into each KPI from the dashboard.

Both patterns must be supported. A dashboard that only shows summaries without drill-down forces principals to switch tools. A dashboard that only shows raw data requires time to extract meaning.

## FAQ

**Q: Can the principal approve leave requests from the dashboard?**
A: Yes. Pending approvals (leave, expense, concessions) should appear on the dashboard with one-tap approval or referral.

**Q: How quickly does the dashboard update when a teacher marks attendance?**
A: In a real-time cloud system like Firebase, the attendance count updates within seconds. Traditional polling systems update every 5-10 minutes.

**Q: Can the chairman or director see the same dashboard?**
A: Chairman, Director, and Trustee dashboards should show the same school health KPIs as the Principal dashboard, potentially aggregated across multiple campuses for chains.

**Q: What happens when the principal is on leave, who sees the dashboard?**
A: The Vice Principal or designated role should have identical dashboard access. Role delegation during absence is important for continuity.

**Q: Can the principal set up custom alerts?**
A: Configurable alerts (e.g., "Alert me if daily attendance drops below 85%") are a useful feature. Ask vendors if this is configurable or fixed.
