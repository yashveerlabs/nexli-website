## School ERP Product Roadmap: Questions That Reveal the Real Plan

**Every school ERP vendor has a roadmap. Vendors will tell you about planned features to make the product more attractive during sales. The question is not what they plan to build, it is whether they actually build what they commit to, on a timeline that matters to you. Evaluating a vendor's roadmap requires looking backward (at their delivery history) as much as forward (at their stated plans).**

---

## Why Roadmap Evaluation Matters

You are making a 3-5 year commitment. Regulatory requirements will change (DPDP enforcement rules, CBSE policy updates, NEP 2020 implementation). Your school will grow or change. Features you do not need today may become critical.

A vendor who consistently delivers on roadmap commitments is a reliable long-term partner. A vendor whose roadmap promises routinely miss or disappear creates operational risk.

---

## Questions That Reveal Delivery History

**"Can you show me your release history for the past 18 months?"**

A vendor with a healthy development cadence will have a record of releases: bug fixes, feature additions, regulatory compliance updates. Ask for the list. Look for:
- Regular cadence (monthly or quarterly releases, not one release per year)
- Regulatory updates (CBSE LOC format changes, GST updates, DPDP Act compliance)
- Features released vs. features promised 18 months ago

**"What features did you promise on your roadmap 12 months ago that you have now delivered?"**

This is the single most revealing question. A vendor who can walk you through 5-8 delivered items with specifics has a credible roadmap process. A vendor who deflects ("we delivered many improvements") without specifics may not have a disciplined delivery process.

**"Can I speak to a reference school about a specific feature they were waiting for and whether it was delivered on time?"**

Reference schools who waited for a promised feature have direct experience with roadmap reliability. This verifies vendor claims independently.

---

## Questions About Regulatory Update Commitments

Regulatory compliance is not optional and is not a feature request, it is a core obligation of a school ERP vendor. Ask:

**"When CBSE changed the LOC format last time, how quickly did you update the export, and when did you notify schools?"**
The right answer: notified schools 30+ days before submission season, update available before the deadline.

**"What is your process for tracking DPDP Act 2023 enforcement rule releases?"**
The right answer: legal/compliance team monitors, product team updates the system, schools are notified.

**"If RTE reporting requirements change, how do you handle that update?"**
The right answer: assessed as a mandatory update, delivered before the new requirement takes effect.

---

## Questions About Future Plans

**"What are your top 3 product priorities for the next 12 months?"**

Evaluate whether these priorities align with your school's needs. A vendor whose priority is expanding into higher education is potentially deprioritizing K-12 school features.

**"How do you handle feature requests from schools?"**

Good answers: Feature requests are reviewed quarterly, frequently requested features are added to the roadmap, schools are notified when their requested feature is released.

Poor answer: "We take all feedback seriously" without a defined process.

**"What does the product look like in 3 years, and how does that align with NEP 2020 requirements?"**

NEP 2020 requires Holistic Progress Cards (HPC), competency-based assessment, and multi-lingual support. A vendor without a plan for these is falling behind national education policy.

---

## Red Flags in Roadmap Discussions

- Roadmap is presented as "confidential" and cannot be shared in any form
- Every future feature request from you is answered with "yes, that's on our roadmap" without specifics
- Historical delivery record cannot be provided or is very sparse
- Regulatory updates are treated as feature requests rather than mandatory obligations
- The team presenting the roadmap cannot answer questions about how decisions are made

## FAQ

**Q: Should we make contract decisions based on roadmap promises?**
A: Only if those features are contractually committed with a delivery date. Roadmap items without contractual commitment are aspirations, not obligations. Evaluate the vendor on what they have today; use the roadmap to understand direction, not as a purchase justification.

**Q: What if a feature we need is "coming soon"?**
A: "Coming soon" is the most abused phrase in software sales. Ask: "What quarter is it scheduled for, and what is the consequence if it is not ready then?" If there is no specific timeline and no accountability, weight it as zero in your evaluation.

**Q: How often should the vendor share roadmap updates after we sign?**
A: Quarterly roadmap communications are a reasonable expectation. Annual is acceptable for small vendors. Ad hoc communication about major regulatory updates should happen whenever regulatory changes occur, not just quarterly.

**Q: What happens to our feedback and feature requests?**
A: Ask this question directly and verify with reference schools. A vendor with a clear feature request process, who can name features that came from customer feedback, is listening. A vendor who cannot name a single customer-requested feature that made it to production may not have an effective feedback loop.

**Q: Is a vendor's roadmap ever shared publicly?**
A: Some vendors share a public product roadmap. This is generally a positive signal, it creates accountability. A vendor who is confident enough to publish planned features publicly is more likely to be committed to delivering them.
