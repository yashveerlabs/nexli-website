## Contract Negotiation for School ERP: What to Push For

**School ERP vendors provide standard contracts designed to protect vendor interests. Most schools sign without negotiation. The terms that matter most, pricing escalation, data ownership, exit rights, implementation milestones, and support SLAs, are all negotiable. Understanding what to push for and why protects the school's interests throughout a 3-5 year relationship.**

---

## Term 1: Pricing Escalation Caps

**Standard contract risk:** Annual price increases of 10-20% are common in standard contracts, triggered automatically at renewal. Over 3 years, this can represent a 33-73% total price increase.

**What to negotiate:** A fixed price for the contract period (Year 1, 2, and 3 prices specified upfront), or an escalation cap (maximum 5% per year, tied to CPI). Get this in writing in the contract, not as a verbal promise.

**Vendor pushback:** "We can't predict our costs." Fair. Counter: "Then cap at CPI + 2%." A vendor unwilling to cap escalation is signaling aggressive renewal pricing.

---

## Term 2: Scope of Included Implementation

**Standard contract risk:** "Implementation support" may mean one remote training session for all staff. On-site training, data migration, parallel running support, and role-specific sessions may be additional charges.

**What to negotiate:** List of deliverables included in the implementation fee: on-site days, number of training sessions by role, data migration types covered, parallel running support period (minimum 4 weeks), and who is responsible for what.

**Why it matters:** Post-signing implementation scope disputes are common. Written specifics prevent them.

---

## Term 3: Data Ownership and Export Rights

**Standard contract risk:** Some contracts are ambiguous about who owns the data, or state that the vendor retains a license to aggregated data. Exit data export may be undefined.

**What to negotiate:**
- Explicit statement: "All school data remains the property of the school."
- Right to export data at any time (not just on termination) within 30 days of request
- Defined export format (CSV or standard spreadsheet format for all modules)
- Obligation to provide export on termination regardless of reason for termination (including vendor dispute)
- Data deletion confirmation within 60 days of export

**Why it matters:** If you never need to switch vendors, these clauses never activate. If you do, they are the difference between a manageable transition and an impossible one.

---

## Term 4: SLA Commitments in Writing

**Standard contract risk:** "Responsive support" or "best efforts" language with no defined response times.

**What to negotiate:**
- Critical issue (system down): 2-hour response, 8-hour resolution
- High priority (key module not functioning): 8-hour response, 48-hour resolution
- Standard issue (configuration question, minor bug): 24-48 hour response, 5-business-day resolution
- Service credits for SLA breach (e.g., credit toward next renewal period)

**Why it matters:** Without documented SLAs, you have no basis for escalating slow support or claiming remedies.

---

## Term 5: Implementation Milestone-Based Payments

**Standard contract risk:** Full payment due upfront or in large tranches unrelated to implementation progress. The vendor gets paid regardless of whether implementation succeeds.

**What to negotiate:** Payment tied to milestones:
- 40% on contract signing
- 30% on successful go-live (attendance and fees operational, verified by school)
- 30% on completion of Phase 2 (marks, report cards, parent portal)

**Why it matters:** Milestone payments align vendor incentives with implementation success. A vendor who insists on full payment before go-live is reducing their accountability for that go-live.

---

## Term 6: Exit and Termination Rights

**Standard contract risk:** Contracts may include auto-renewal clauses, penalties for early termination, or 90-day notice requirements for termination.

**What to negotiate:**
- 30-day notice period for non-renewal (not 90 days)
- No penalty for termination if vendor has materially breached SLA commitments
- Auto-renewal opt-out: renewal must be explicitly confirmed, not automatic

## FAQ

**Q: Is it realistic to negotiate with school ERP vendors?**
A: For mid-sized vendors, yes. Most vendor contracts are standard starting points, not take-it-or-leave-it terms. The more competitive the selection process, the more negotiating leverage you have.

**Q: What if the vendor refuses to add SLA terms?**
A: This is a red flag. A vendor confident in their support quality welcomes SLA commitments. Refusal indicates they do not want to be held accountable to response time standards.

**Q: Should we use a legal advisor for contract review?**
A: For a contract above ₹5 lakh over the contract period, a legal review of data ownership, exit rights, and liability clauses is worthwhile.

**Q: What are the most important 3 terms to prioritize?**
A: Data ownership and export rights (protects the school in all scenarios), pricing escalation caps (protects long-term budget), and SLA commitments (protects ongoing operations).

**Q: Can we renegotiate an existing contract at renewal?**
A: Yes. Renewal is the leverage point. A vendor who has had your school for 2 years values retention. Renewal discussions are the right time to negotiate for the terms you did not get in the original contract.
