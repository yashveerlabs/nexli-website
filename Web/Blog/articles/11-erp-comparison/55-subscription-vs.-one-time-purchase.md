## School ERP: Subscription vs. One-Time Purchase, Which Makes Sense?

**Some school ERP vendors offer a perpetual license (one-time purchase) alongside or instead of subscription pricing. The perpetual model feels appealing, pay once, own forever. The reality is more complicated. Understanding the true long-term cost and maintenance implications of each model helps schools make a sustainable technology decision.**

---

## What the Perpetual License Model Actually Means

**What you buy:** The right to use the software as it exists at the time of purchase. Typically on-premise installation.

**What is included:**
- Software installation
- Usually 1 year of maintenance/support
- Access to the version you purchased

**What is typically not included (and costs extra):**
- Year 2+ maintenance (annual maintenance contract, typically 15-20% of license price)
- Major version upgrades (moving from v3 to v4 may be a new license fee)
- Cloud hosting (if you want web access rather than local installation)
- Security patches and compliance updates (may be in maintenance contract or may cost extra)

**The hidden ongoing cost:** An ERP with a ₹5 lakh one-time license and 18% annual maintenance costs ₹90,000/year after Year 1, indefinitely.

---

## What the Subscription Model Means

**What you pay:** Annual (or monthly) fee that includes:
- Software access
- Updates and new features
- Security patches
- Hosting (for cloud-based subscription)
- Basic support

**What this means practically:**
- No separate maintenance contract required
- New regulatory updates (DPDP, NEP 2020 compliance, new CBSE formats) are automatically included
- Security patches apply without your IT team managing them
- No hardware or server costs

**The trade-off:** If you stop paying, you lose access. The software does not "belong" to you.

---

## Total Cost Comparison: 5 Years

**Perpetual license, on-premise:**
- License fee: ₹5,00,000 (one-time)
- Server hardware (if not existing): ₹1,00,000 (one-time)
- IT setup and installation: ₹50,000 (one-time)
- Annual maintenance contract (18%): ₹90,000/year × 5 years = ₹4,50,000
- IT staff time for updates and patches (monthly): ongoing
- Major version upgrade (Year 3): ₹1,50,000-₹2,50,000 estimate

**5-year total: ₹12,50,000+**

**Cloud subscription:**
- Subscription: ₹1,20,000-₹2,00,000/year × 5 years = ₹6,00,000-₹10,00,000
- No hardware costs
- No maintenance contract
- Updates included

**5-year total: ₹6,00,000-₹10,00,000**

For most Indian school sizes, cloud subscription is cheaper over 5 years, and requires no internal IT maintenance burden.

## Scenarios Where Perpetual License Makes Sense

**No reliable internet:** A school in an area with genuinely unreliable internet where cloud access is impractical. On-premise with local access is the only workable option.

**Very large school with strong IT team:** A school with 5,000+ students and a dedicated IT team may find the economics of a perpetual license more favorable at very large scale.

**Regulatory restriction:** Government schools in some states may have data sovereignty requirements that preclude cloud storage.

For most private Indian K-12 schools in urban or semi-urban areas, these scenarios do not apply.

---

## The Regulatory Update Problem

Indian school regulation changes. DPDP Act 2023 is new. NEP 2020 is being implemented progressively. CBSE changes LOC formats. UDISE+ requirements evolve.

A cloud subscription vendor has strong incentive to keep their system compliant, all customers benefit, and noncompliance affects the whole user base. Updates are automatic.

A perpetual license owner must:
- Monitor regulatory changes independently
- Evaluate whether the existing version is compliant
- Pay for updates to maintain compliance (or risk noncompliance)

Regulatory compliance risk is a significant argument against perpetual license for Indian schools.

## FAQ

**Q: Can we own our data if we use a cloud subscription?**
A: Data ownership is separate from the licensing model. A cloud subscription should include data export rights, you can export your student and financial records at any time. Verify this in the contract.

**Q: What happens to our subscription if the vendor raises prices significantly?**
A: Contractual price caps or multi-year lock-in protections are negotiable. If prices increase unsustainably, you can export your data and switch vendors.

**Q: Is open-source school ERP a third option?**
A: Open-source ERPs (like certain versions of Fedena) have no license fee but require significant hosting, maintenance, and technical setup. This typically costs more in practice for schools without in-house developers.

**Q: Can we start with a subscription and convert to perpetual later?**
A: Some vendors offer this. Others do not. If important to you, ask at the evaluation stage, do not assume it is possible.

**Q: What does "SaaS" mean for school ERP?**
A: Software as a Service (SaaS) is the cloud subscription model. The vendor hosts, maintains, and updates the software. You access it via browser or mobile app. This is the dominant model for modern school ERP.
