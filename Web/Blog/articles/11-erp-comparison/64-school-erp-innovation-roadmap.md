## School ERP Innovation Roadmap: Questions to Ask About Future Development

**You are not just buying software for today's needs. You are entering a multi-year relationship with a vendor whose product will need to evolve with regulatory changes, new technology, and your school's growing requirements. Evaluating a vendor's commitment to product innovation is as important as evaluating current features.**

---

## Why the Roadmap Matters

Consider what will change in the next 3 years:
- DPDP Act enforcement will begin; regulations will be finalized
- NEP 2020 implementation will progress through more grade levels
- CBSE and other boards may change their reporting formats
- Your school may add campuses, increase enrollment, or change boards
- Parent and teacher technology expectations will continue rising

If your ERP vendor is not actively developing their product, you will face misalignment between your needs and the system's capabilities within 2-3 years.

---

## What to Ask About the Roadmap

### "What are your three biggest product priorities for the next 12 months?"

A vendor with a credible roadmap can answer this specifically:
- "We are implementing full DPDP Act compliance including OTP consent, access logging, and DPO dashboard in Q3 2026."
- "NEP 2020 HPC tracking is in final testing for August release."
- "We are rebuilding the mobile teacher interface for better offline support."

Vague answers ("we are continuously improving the system") indicate no clear product direction.

### "What was the last major feature released and when?"

Ask for specifics: what was released, when, for which customers, what problem it solved. A vendor actively developing releases meaningful features every 3-6 months. A vendor not developing may not have released anything significant in 12+ months.

### "How are customer requests incorporated into the roadmap?"

Good vendors have a feedback loop:
- Customer requests tracked in a system
- Prioritization based on frequency and impact
- Communication to customers when their request is added to roadmap or completed

Vendors without this process take requests but cannot reliably deliver on them.

### "Are there any planned price changes in the next 12 months?"

If a price increase is planned, better to know now. If the vendor is planning to monetize roadmap items that are currently free, this affects your TCO calculation.

### "What compliance requirements are you preparing for that are not yet released?"

A forward-looking vendor anticipates regulatory changes. DPDP Act enforcement rules, potential changes to CBSE affiliation requirements, and state board digital mandates all require advance preparation.

---

## Red Flags in Roadmap Discussions

**Overpromising:** "We can build anything you need." This is not a roadmap, it is sales language. Ask for specific features, specific timelines.

**Features "coming soon" that have been on the roadmap for years:** Ask reference schools whether promised features have been delivered. Chronic delays indicate a team that cannot execute.

**No mechanism for customer feedback:** If customers cannot easily report issues or request features, the product direction is driven by the vendor alone, not by school needs.

**Roadmap dependent on raising funding:** "We are planning to add these features once our next funding round closes." Vendor financial dependency is a risk, not a roadmap.

**"We do not share our roadmap publicly":** While some confidentiality is reasonable, a vendor unwilling to share any roadmap priorities with prospective customers is a yellow flag.

---

## How Roadmap Delivery History Validates Claims

Ask to see the product changelog or release history. If the vendor claims active development, there should be releases documented over the past 12 months. A changelog showing weekly or bi-weekly releases indicates active development. A changelog with the last update 8 months ago tells a different story.

## FAQ

**Q: Should roadmap features influence our selection?**
A: Current features should drive the decision; roadmap features should inform it. Do not select a vendor for features that do not exist yet. Do verify that the vendor has a credible history of roadmap delivery.

**Q: Can we negotiate roadmap commitments into the contract?**
A: Specific features by specific dates are very difficult to negotiate into software contracts. Vendors are appropriately cautious about legal commitments on future software delivery. Focus on verifying current features and past delivery track record.

**Q: What if the vendor promises features that never get built?**
A: If a specific unbuilt feature was material to your purchase decision, document the promise (email confirming "Feature X will be available in Q2 2026"). Contract termination clauses tied to feature delivery are unusual but can be negotiated for critical requirements.

**Q: How often should we review the ERP decision?**
A: Formally at each contract renewal. Informally, monitor whether the vendor is actively developing, whether support quality is stable, and whether the product is keeping pace with regulatory and operational requirements.

**Q: What if we need a feature that is not on the roadmap?**
A: Evaluate whether the feature is a genuine requirement or a preference. If genuinely required, ask about custom development cost and timeline. Also evaluate whether another vendor has this feature built-in.
