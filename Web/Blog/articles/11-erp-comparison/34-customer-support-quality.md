## Customer Support Quality in School ERP: What to Look For

**Schools make ERP selection decisions based on features and demos. They live with vendor support for years. A vendor with excellent features and poor support creates daily frustration. A vendor with good features and responsive support builds a functional long-term relationship. Evaluating support quality before purchase is as important as evaluating the software itself.**

---

## Why Support Matters More Than It Seems

Consider these scenarios:
- It is the day before CBSE LOC submission and the export function is showing an error
- A fee installment just sent the wrong amount to parents and receipts are incorrect
- The ERP goes down during the peak morning check-in period and teachers cannot mark attendance

Each of these requires immediate vendor response. If your vendor's support desk responds in 48 hours, these incidents become crises. If they respond in 2 hours, they become manageable issues.

Schools operate on fixed daily schedules with hard compliance deadlines. Support that is appropriate for a retail business, "we'll respond within 3 business days", is inadequate for a school.

---

## What to Evaluate in Vendor Support

### Response Time Commitments

Ask for specific response time guarantees for different issue types:

**Critical issues** (system down, data loss, compliance deadline risk): Response within 1-2 hours, resolution within 4-8 hours.

**High priority issues** (major module not working, incorrect data in reports): Response within 4-8 hours, resolution within 24 hours.

**Standard issues** (feature confusion, configuration questions): Response within 24-48 hours, resolution within 5 business days.

These should be in writing in the Service Level Agreement (SLA), not just verbal promises during the sales process.

### Support Channels

**Phone support:** Best for critical issues. Can explain problems and get real-time help. Confirm whether this is a call center or someone who knows your school's configuration.

**Email/ticket system:** Good for non-urgent issues. Creates a written record. Confirm average response times.

**Chat support:** Useful for quick questions. Confirm hours of availability.

**Video call (Zoom, Meet):** Valuable for complex issues or training refreshers. Ask if this is available and whether it costs extra.

**On-site support:** Some vendors offer on-site visits for implementation, training, or complex issues. Usually expensive but valuable for schools in complex transitions.

### Timezone Coverage

Most Indian school operations are 7 AM to 5 PM IST. Your vendor's support team should be available during these hours. Verify:
- What hours is support available?
- Is there a weekend/holiday support option for urgent issues?
- Is the support team in India, or overseas (with timezone offset)?

### Technical Competency of Support Staff

First-level support often consists of agents reading from scripts. When issues are complex (data corruption, configuration errors, integration problems), you need access to someone with technical knowledge.

Ask: "What is the escalation path when first-level support cannot resolve an issue? How quickly can I reach an engineer?"

## Red Flags in Vendor Support

**Vague promises:** "We have excellent support" without specifics on response time or channels.

**No SLA:** Support expectations that are verbal and not documented in the contract.

**No escalation path:** First-level support is the only support. Complex issues stall indefinitely.

**Outsourced support:** If support is outsourced to a call center with no knowledge of your school's configuration, resolution quality suffers.

**Support costs extra:** Some vendors charge extra for anything beyond email support. Know what is included before signing.

## FAQ

**Q: How do we evaluate support quality before buying?**
A: Talk to reference schools. Ask: "When you had a critical issue, how quickly did the vendor respond? Was the issue resolved fully?" The reference school's experience is more predictive than vendor claims.

**Q: Should we pay for premium support?**
A: If your school processes significant fees or has hard compliance deadlines, premium support (faster response, dedicated account manager) may be worth the cost. Evaluate based on what a 4-hour downtime during peak fee collection would cost your school.

**Q: What should be in the support SLA?**
A: At minimum: response time commitments by severity level, resolution time targets, support hours and channels, escalation procedures, and penalties for SLA breach (typically credit on next renewal).

**Q: Can we change vendors if support quality deteriorates?**
A: Contract permitting. Some contracts include exit clauses tied to SLA failures. Negotiate these into the contract at signing, not after problems occur.

**Q: How do we report issues effectively to get faster resolution?**
A: Provide specific details: what you were trying to do, what happened instead, any error messages (screenshot), what device and browser you were using. The more detail, the faster the diagnosis.
