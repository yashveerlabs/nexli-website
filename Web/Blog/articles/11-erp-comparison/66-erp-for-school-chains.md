## ERP for School Chains: Managing Multiple Campuses on One Platform

**A school chain running 10 campuses on 10 separate ERP subscriptions has fragmented its data, its compliance visibility, and its operational oversight just as badly as a single school using 10 spreadsheets. The value of a chain-level ERP is centralized visibility without sacrificing per-campus autonomy. This guide covers what chains need from ERP and how to evaluate platforms for chain-scale deployment.**

---

## The Chain's Core Problem

**The chain director needs:**
- Enrollment numbers across all campuses
- Fee collection rates by campus
- Attendance health across campuses
- Compliance calendar status for every campus
- Academic performance comparison across campuses

**The campus principal needs:**
- Full operational control of their campus
- Confidence that other campuses cannot see their data
- Their own compliance calendar for their campus's deadlines
- Their own reports and analytics

**The conflict:** Both views are needed simultaneously. An ERP that only provides chain-level aggregates leaves campus principals without their operational tools. An ERP that only provides per-campus views leaves the chain director without the visibility they need.

The solution is proper multi-tenant architecture — one platform, multiple schools, data isolation between schools, aggregate view at chain level.

---

## What Chain-Scale ERP Must Provide

### Chain-Level Dashboard

A dedicated view for the chain director or trustees showing:
- Enrollment count per campus (and total)
- Fee collection rate per campus (highlighting underperformers)
- Attendance health per campus
- Compliance deadline status per campus
- Staff count per campus

Without drilling into individual student or teacher records of any campus.

### Per-Campus Operational Independence

Each campus principal has full control of their campus — their fee structures, their academic calendar variations, their role assignments, their compliance deadlines. The chain director can see but not interfere with campus operations.

### Standardized Reporting

The chain can define standard report formats used across all campuses. Comparing Class 10 performance across 5 campuses requires consistent report structures. The chain-level platform enforces format consistency while allowing campus-level variation where needed.

### Policy and Curriculum Distribution

The chain can publish:
- Common curriculum frameworks
- Shared policy documents
- Chain-wide circulars and announcements

That are available to all campuses simultaneously.

### Student Transfer Between Campuses

When a student moves from Campus A to Campus B within the chain, the ERP should support a structured transfer — preserving academic history, attendance history, and fee history in the receiving campus.

## Pricing Considerations for Chains

**Volume discounts:** Chains with significant total enrollment should negotiate per-student pricing that reflects the aggregate relationship value, not individual school rates.

**Multi-campus contract:** A single contract covering all campuses is administratively simpler and creates a stronger negotiating position than separate per-campus contracts.

**Centralized billing:** For finance management, a single invoice covering all campuses is preferable to separate invoices requiring separate approvals at each campus.

## FAQ

**Q: Can we standardize the fee structure across all campuses?**
A: You can standardize the structure (same fee heads, same installment schedule) across campuses through chain-level configuration. But fee amounts may vary by campus based on location. The ERP should support both chain-wide templates and campus-specific overrides.

**Q: How do we handle a chain where different campuses are on different boards?**
A: Each campus configures its own academic structure (board, grading scale, exam types). The chain-level view aggregates by enrollment and performance rather than by academic structure.

**Q: Can the chain view track which campuses have complete DPDP compliance?**
A: Yes, in a properly designed compliance dashboard. Consent completion rate, DPO dashboard status, and data access log availability can be tracked per campus.

**Q: What if one campus uses a different ERP than the others?**
A: This is the fragmentation problem. Migrating all campuses to a single platform is the solution. The migration project for each campus is a standard ERP implementation.

**Q: How does chain ERP handle common staff (staff who work across multiple campuses)?**
A: Common staff need role assignments at each campus they serve. The ERP should allow a single staff profile linked to multiple campus roles.
