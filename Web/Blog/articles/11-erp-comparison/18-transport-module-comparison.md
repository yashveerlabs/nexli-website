## Transport Module Comparison: What a School ERP Should Offer

**School transport is a daily safety concern. Parents want to know their child boarded the bus and arrived safely. School management wants to know where every bus is and which students are aboard. The transport module in a school ERP must connect GPS data, RFID boarding, route management, and parent notifications into a single operational picture.**

---

## Why Transport Needs Its Own Module

Transport is not just a logistical convenience, it is a safety responsibility. If a child does not board the bus in the afternoon, someone needs to know immediately. If a bus is late, parents should receive a proactive notification before they start calling the school. If a bus breaks down mid-route, the transport coordinator needs to arrange a replacement.

Schools that manage transport through spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and driver phone calls have no systematic record, no accountability, and no audit trail when something goes wrong.

---

## Core Transport Module Features

### Route Management

The system must support:
- Multiple routes with named stops and sequence
- Pick-up and drop-off times per stop
- Students assigned to each stop
- Bus and driver assigned to each route

Route management gives the transport coordinator a complete picture: which students are on which bus, on which route, stopping where.

### Driver and Conductor Records

Each driver and conductor should have a profile including:
- Personal details and emergency contact
- Driving licence number and expiry date
- Vehicle class (LMV, HMV) and endorsements
- Background verification status
- Medical fitness certificate expiry

Alert when licences or fitness certificates are about to expire.

### Vehicle Maintenance Schedule

Each vehicle should have:
- Registration number, model, seating capacity
- Insurance policy and expiry date
- Fitness certificate from RTO and expiry
- Maintenance log (scheduled service, unscheduled repairs)
- Fuel consumption records

Automated alerts for upcoming insurance renewals and fitness certificate expiry prevent schools from operating non-compliant vehicles.

### GPS Live-Tracking

GPS trackers installed in buses feed real-time location data to the transport coordinator. The system shows:
- Live map of all buses (where they are right now)
- Estimated arrival at each stop
- Deviation from planned route (alert if bus goes off-route)
- Speed alerts (alert if bus exceeds safe speed)

### RFID Boarding Recording

RFID cards or tags for students record who boards and alights from each bus:
- Student scans card when boarding in the morning
- System records boarding time and location
- Parent receives notification: "Your child has boarded Bus 3 at 7:42 AM"
- Student scans when alighting in the afternoon
- Parent receives notification: "Your child has alighted at XYZ stop at 4:15 PM"

This eliminates the most common parent anxiety: "Did my child get on the bus?"

### Bus Attendance

Daily bus attendance is separate from classroom attendance. The conductor marks which students are present on the bus that day. Absentees are flagged (was the student absent from school, or did they miss the bus?).

---

## What to Look for When Comparing Transport Modules

**Look for:** GPS integration, RFID boarding, parent notifications, driver record management, vehicle maintenance alerts, route optimization tools.

**Watch out for:** Transport modules that only handle student-to-route assignment with no GPS or real-time capability. These are administrative databases, not operational tools.

**Ask vendors:** "Can you show me the live map view? How does the system notify parents when a student boards? Can I see the maintenance calendar for a vehicle?"

## FAQ

**Q: Is GPS tracking mandatory for school buses?**
A: Many states have regulations requiring GPS in school buses. Check your state's Motor Vehicle rules. Beyond compliance, GPS provides operational visibility and parent assurance.

**Q: How do parents receive bus alerts?**
A: Through the parent portal app (push notification), SMS, or WhatsApp (where that integration is available). Each school can configure the alert channel based on what parents actually use.

**Q: What happens if a student is not at their stop in the morning?**
A: The bus conductor marks the student absent for that route. The system flags the absence for the transport coordinator. If the student later arrives at school by other means, attendance is updated.

**Q: Can we manage multiple bus types (school buses, hired vehicles)?**
A: Vehicle records should support multiple vehicle types. Hired or contracted vehicles should be managed with the contractor details in the system.

**Q: How does the transport module help with route optimization?**
A: Basic route management records the existing routes. Advanced optimization (shortest path given student locations) is planned in some systems but not widely available in Indian school ERPs currently.
