## Why Annual Budgeting Matters

**Without a Budget:**
- Principal makes ad-hoc spending decisions
- Money runs out before year-end
- Salaries delayed; staff morale suffers
- No visibility into financial health

**With a Budget:**
- Expected revenue and expenses are clear
- Spending is planned and controlled
- Surpluses and deficits are anticipated
- Strategic decisions are informed

**Outcome:** Schools with budgets finish year on target 85%+ of the time. Schools without budgets consistently overspend.

## Budget Building Process

### Step 1: Project Revenue

**Revenue Sources:**

| Source | Current Year | Projected Next Year | Assumptions |
|--------|---|---|---|
| **Student Fees** | ₹2,00,00,000 | ₹2,10,00,000 | 5% fee increase; 95% collection |
| **Scholarships** | ₹15,00,000 | ₹15,00,000 | Same number of scholars |
| **Donations** | ₹5,00,000 | ₹5,00,000 | Conservative (may get more) |
| **Other** | ₹10,00,000 | ₹10,00,000 | Miscellaneous, catering, etc. |
| **Total Revenue** | **₹2,30,00,000** | **₹2,40,00,000** | |

**Key Assumption:** 95% collection rate on fees (not 100%; realistic)

### Step 2: Project Expenses

**Expense Categories:**

| Category | Current Year | Projected | Note |
|----------|---|---|---|
| **Salaries** | ₹1,30,00,000 | ₹1,37,00,000 | 5% annual raise + 1 new hire |
| **Building/Utilities** | ₹20,00,000 | ₹21,00,000 | 5% inflation |
| **Materials/Books** | ₹15,00,000 | ₹16,00,000 | Growing student enrollment |
| **Maintenance** | ₹8,00,000 | ₹8,50,000 | Regular upkeep |
| **Contingency** | ₹10,00,000 | ₹10,00,000 | 4% of budget (emergency fund) |
| **Debt Service** | ₹5,00,000 | ₹5,00,000 | Loan payment |
| **Total Expenses** | **₹1,88,00,000** | **₹1,97,50,000** | |

### Step 3: Forecast Surplus/Deficit

**Calculation:**
- Projected Revenue: ₹2,40,00,000
- Projected Expenses: ₹1,97,50,000
- **Projected Surplus: ₹42,50,000**

**Healthy:** 15%+ surplus or breakeven is good. Deficit is concerning.

### Step 4: Sensitivity Analysis

**Scenario 1: Optimistic (102% collection)**
- Revenue increases to ₹2,44,20,000
- Surplus: ₹46,70,000

**Scenario 2: Pessimistic (88% collection, 2 staff departures)**
- Revenue decreases to ₹2,28,00,000
- Expenses increase to ₹1,99,50,000 (recruiting costs)
- Deficit: ₹-8,50,000 (need contingency fund or emergency fundraising)

**Conclusion:** Budget is healthy under normal conditions but vulnerable to enrollment/collection drops.

### Step 5: Build Contingencies

**Contingency Fund:** 5–10% of budget
- ₹42,50,000 × 5% = ₹2,12,500
- Set aside for emergencies (equipment breakdown, urgent repairs, etc.)

**Triggers for Contingency Use:**
- Unexpected repairs (AC breakdown, roof leak)
- Urgent staffing needs (teacher sudden departure)
- Emergency building safety (electrical, structural)

## Monthly Variance Management

**Track Monthly:**
- Actual revenue vs. budgeted
- Actual expenses vs. budgeted
- Variance: (Actual − Budget) / Budget

**Example:**

| Item | Budgeted | Actual | Variance | Comment |
|------|----------|--------|----------|---------|
| Fees | ₹20,00,000 | ₹18,50,000 | -7.5% | Collection slow this month |
| Salaries | ₹11,00,000 | ₹11,05,000 | +0.5% | On track |
| Utilities | ₹1,80,000 | ₹2,10,000 | +16.7% | High AC usage (summer); expected |
| Materials | ₹1,30,000 | ₹1,25,000 | -3.8% | Slight underspend |

**Action if Variance Exceeds 10%:**
- Investigate (is it temporary or structural?)
- Adjust forecast (revise annual budget if needed)
- Take corrective action (reduce spending, increase collections, etc.)

## Budget Review & Adjustment

**Monthly:** Finance committee reviews variance (15 min)

**Quarterly:** Full budget review (fees on track? expenses under control?)

**Annual:** Build next year's budget using this year's actuals

## Call to Action

Create a realistic annual budget:

2. **Project expenses** (salaries, utilities, maintenance, contingency)
3. **Forecast surplus/deficit**
4. **Stress-test** (what if collection drops? inflation rises?)

6. **Monitor monthly** (variance, actuals vs. budget)
7. **Adjust quarterly** if needed

## FAQ

**Q1: What's a realistic contingency fund?**
A: 5–10% of annual budget. For ₹2Cr budget, ₹10–20L contingency.

**Q2: Should we budget for salary increases?**
A: Yes. Typical: 3–5% annual raise. Build into budget.

**Q3: What if actual fees are lower than projected?**
A: Reduce discretionary spending immediately (books, events). Don't touch salaries/contingency.
