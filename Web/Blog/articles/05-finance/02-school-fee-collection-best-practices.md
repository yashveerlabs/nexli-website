## Core Best Practices

### Practice 1: Clear Policy at Admission

**What to Communicate:**
- Fee amount and payment schedule
- Consequences of late payment
- Concession/scholarship eligibility
- Mode of payment (online, cheque, cash, etc.)

**Delivery:**
- Written policy (given to every parent)
- Signed acknowledgment (parent confirms understanding)
- Annual reminder (start of each academic year)

### Practice 2: First Payment at Admission

**Why:**
- Sets expectation that payment is immediate
- Reduces later delays
- Improves collection rate

**Implementation:**
- Collection at time of admission (not "after classes start")
- 1st installment due before student enters classroom
- Second and third installments on agreed schedule

### Practice 3: Multiple Payment Channels

**Channels to Offer:**
- Online: UPI, net banking, cards, payment gateway
- Offline: Cheque, DD, cash (at school)
- Mobile: Nexli app payment

**Benefits:**
- Removes friction; parents choose convenient method
- Digital payment is traceable; cash creates disputes

### Practice 4: Automated Reminders

**Timing:**
- 10 days before due: Friendly reminder
- On due date: "Pay today"
- 5 days after: "Overdue; please pay"
- 15 days after: "Urgent; principal will call"
- 30 days after: Formal default notice

**Channels:**
- SMS (fastest, highest open rate)
- Email (detailed message)
- App notification

### Practice 5: Personal Follow-Up

**Process:**
- After 30 days overdue: Principal calls parent
- Tone: "Help me understand. Is there a barrier I can remove?"
- Listen, don't lecture
- Create plan if needed

**Impact:**
- Personal touch converts many defaulters
- Identifies genuine issues (income loss, job change, medical emergency)

### Practice 6: Scholarship & Concession Program

**Eligibility Criteria:**
- Income threshold (e.g., annual household income <₹3,00,000)
- Number of wards in school
- Academic performance (merit scholarships)

**Application Process:**
- Form available; simple questions
- Income certificate (if needed)
- Quick turnaround (approval within 2 weeks)

**Benefits:**
- Removes affordability barriers
- Increased enrollment from low-income families (broader reach)
- Better collection (100% of reduced fee > 0% of full fee)

### Practice 7: Payment Plans for Large Defaults

**When to Offer:**
- Parent owes ₹1,20,000; can't pay lump-sum
- Parent shows willingness to pay

**Example Plan:**
- "Pay ₹15,000 now; ₹15,000 by month-end; ₹10,000 by next month, etc."
- Formal agreement signed
- Monthly follow-up

**Success Rate:**
- ~80% of parents on payment plans complete them
- Better than pursuing legal action

### Practice 8: Report Card Linkage

**Policy:**
- "Report cards are issued only after fees are cleared"

**Enforcement:**
- Provisional report card (numbers but not released to parent until payment)
- Clear message: "Fees must be cleared before annual report card is finalized"

**Caution:**
- Check local regulations (some states restrict this)
- Communicate clearly at admission

### Practice 9: Monthly Review & Escalation

**Process:**
- Generate defaulter list: Students >30, >60, >90 days overdue
- Categorize: Inability to pay vs. Negligence
- Action:
  - Inability: Offer concession/payment plan
  - Negligence: Escalate (call, SMS, notice)

**Escalation:**
- 30–60 days: SMS + Email
- 60–90 days: Principal phone call
- 90+ days: Legal notice / police complaint (last resort)

### Practice 10: Documentation & Compliance

**Track:**
- Who paid, when, how much (every transaction)
- Who's owe what (updated daily)
- What action was taken (call, SMS, meeting)
- Result (paid, payment plan, escalated)

**Why:**
- Defense if parent disputes ("You never reminded me")
- Data to improve system ("Where are we losing collections?")

## Red Flags to Watch

| Flag | Action |
|------|--------|
| Repeated small payments (₹1,000 of ₹10,000 due) | Increase pressure; demand full payment |
| Long silence (no communication from parent for 2 months) | Escalate; possible avoidance |
| Cheque bounced | Demand cash; don't accept cheques again |
| Excuse changes (different reason each time) | Likely avoidance; take firmer stance |

## FAQ

**Q1: Is it unethical to withhold report cards for unpaid fees?**
A: It's a common practice and legally defensible if communicated at admission. However, check local regulations. Courts have generally upheld this practice.

**Q2: Should we offer discounts for early payment?**
A: Yes. "Pay by April 30 and get 5% discount" incentivizes early payment, improving cash flow.

**Q3: What's the best payment method for school fee?**
A: UPI (fastest, zero friction). Followed by net banking, cards. Avoid cash (error-prone, hard to track).
