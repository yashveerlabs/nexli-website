## Common Areas of School Waste

### 1. **Utilities (10–15% of budget)**

**Waste Examples:**
- AC running all day, even when classrooms empty
- Lights left on in unused rooms
- Leaky taps wasting water
- Old, inefficient appliances

**Cost Reduction Strategies:**
- Install occupancy sensors (lights on only when room in use)
- Conduct energy audit (identify inefficiencies)
- Fix leaks immediately
- Set AC temperature to 24°C (instead of 20°C) → 10–15% energy saving
- Replace old equipment with energy-efficient models

**Potential Savings:** 20–30% reduction (₹2,00,000 on ₹8,00,000 utilities budget)

### 2. **Supplies & Materials (5–10% of budget)**

**Waste Examples:**
- Bulk stationery orders that expire or spoil
- Duplicate purchases (two departments buy same thing independently)
- Premium brands when generic work fine
- Overstocking (materials sit unused)

**Cost Reduction Strategies:**
- Centralized procurement (one person buys for whole school)
- Negotiate bulk rates with vendors
- Use generic brands where quality is same
- Order just-in-time (not months in advance)
- Inventory tracking (know what you have before ordering)

**Potential Savings:** 15–25% reduction

### 3. **Staffing (50–60% of budget)**

**Waste Examples:**
- Overstaffing (more staff than needed)
- Inefficient scheduling (staff sitting idle)
- Duplicate roles (two people doing same job)
- High turnover (constant hiring/training costs)

**Cost Reduction Strategies:**
- Review staffing needs (is each role necessary?)
- Cross-train staff (one person handles multiple roles)
- Optimize schedules (match staff to demand)
- Reduce turnover (improve work environment; salaries competitive)
- Outsource non-core (facility management, security, canteen)

**Caution:** Don't cut staff in ways that hurt education quality (e.g., removing teachers). Focus on administrative/non-teaching roles.

**Potential Savings:** 5–10% reduction (₹6–12L on ₹1.3Cr salary budget)

### 4. **Transportation & Canteen (5–10% of budget)**

**Waste Examples:**
- Half-empty buses (inefficient routes)
- High food wastage in canteen
- Overstaffing (canteen workers sitting idle)

**Cost Reduction Strategies:**
- Optimize bus routes (reduce number of trips)
- Consolidate canteen staff (seasonal adjustments)
- Reduce food waste (inventory management, demand forecasting)
- Negotiate vendor contracts (competitive bidding)

**Potential Savings:** 10–20% reduction

### 5. **Maintenance & Repairs (3–5% of budget)**

**Waste Examples:**
- Reactive maintenance (wait for breakdown, then repair expensively)
- Overpaying for repairs (no competitive bidding)
- Unnecessary repairs (things that aren't broken)

**Cost Reduction Strategies:**
- Preventive maintenance (regular checks; avoid breakdowns)
- Competitive bidding (3 quotes minimum for big repairs)
- In-house capabilities (train staff to handle minor fixes)
- Warranty management (claim under warranty instead of paying)

**Potential Savings:** 20–30% reduction

## Implementation Approach

### Step 1: Audit Current Spending
- Review last 12 months of expenses
- Identify highest cost categories
- Look for anomalies (unusual expenses)

### Step 2: Set Targets
- Identify top 3 waste areas (focus here first)
- Set reduction targets (e.g., "Reduce utilities by 20%")

### Step 3: Implement Changes
- Start with quick wins (low effort, high impact)
- Then tackle harder changes (e.g., staffing)

### Step 4: Monitor & Adjust
- Track actual savings
- Celebrate wins (motivate team)
- Adjust if changes hurt quality

### Step 5: Sustain
- Make sustainable changes (not one-time cuts)
- Review quarterly (are savings holding?)

## Important: Avoid False Economy

**What NOT to cut:**
- Teacher salaries (hurts morale, retention)
- Essential educational materials
- Safety/maintenance

**These "savings" cost you more in the long run.**

## FAQ

**Q1: How much waste should we expect?**
A: Typical: 10–15% of budget is wasted. With systems, you can reduce this to 5% or less.

**Q2: Won't cost-cutting hurt education quality?**
A: Not if done smartly. Eliminate waste (leaky taps, overstaffing), not essentials (teachers, materials).

**Q3: Can we cut salaries to save money?**
A: As last resort only. Staff morale and retention suffer. Better to cut waste first.
