## Standard Expense Categories

### Personnel (50–60% of budget)
- Teacher salaries
- Staff salaries (non-teaching)
- Bonuses/incentives
- EPF/ESI contributions

### Facilities (15–20% of budget)
- Building rent/lease
- Utilities (electricity, water, gas)
- Maintenance & repairs
- Cleaning & housekeeping

### Educational Materials (5–10% of budget)
- Books & textbooks
- Stationery & supplies
- Laboratory materials
- Teaching aids/resources

### Operations (5–10% of budget)
- Transportation/fuel
- Insurance
- Licensing/compliance
- Office equipment

### Student Services (3–5% of budget)
- Medical/health services
- Counseling
- Extra-curricular activities
- Canteen/food services

### Debt Service (if applicable)
- Loan payments
- Interest

### Contingency (5% of budget)
- Emergency repairs
- Unexpected costs

## Expense Tracking Best Practices

### 1. **Every Expense Gets a Receipt**
- No exceptions
- Even ₹50 coffee purchase needs receipt
- Receipt shows: date, vendor, amount, description

**Why:** Audit trail; prevents fraud; accountable spending

### 2. **Categorize Correctly**
- Expense must go into the right category
- ₹500 for lamp bulbs → "Facilities/Maintenance"
- ₹500 for printer ink → "Operations/Office Equipment"

**Why:** Budget tracking is only useful if categories are correct

### 3. **Approve Before Spending**
- Minor: Accountant approves (<₹5,000)
- Medium: Finance committee approves (₹5,000–₹50,000)
- Major: Principal/Board approves (>₹50,000)

**Why:** Control spending; prevent frivolous purchases

### 4. **Compare Actual to Budget**
- Monthly: Is spending on track?
- "Utilities budgeted: ₹1,80,000. Actual: ₹2,10,000. Why?"

**Why:** Catches problems early; allows mid-course correction

### 5. **Separate Personal from School**
- Don't mix: Personal expenses paid from school account
- Clear policy: Only school expenses paid from school funds

**Why:** Accounting clarity; tax compliance; ethical governance

### 6. **Document Everything**
- Invoice: Vendor name, date, amount, description
- PO (Purchase Order): What was authorized to buy
- Receipt: Proof of payment
- File all together

**Why:** Audit trail; IRS compliance (if needed); dispute resolution

### 7. **Review Quarterly**
- Finance committee reviews expenses vs. budget
- Discuss: Any unusual spikes? Any cost-saving opportunities?

**Why:** Strategic oversight; identify patterns and problems

## FAQ

**Q1: How detailed should expense categories be?**
A: 10–15 categories work best. Too few = lose visibility; too many = administrative burden.

**Q2: Should we approve expenses before or after payment?**
A: Before (Purchase Order). After (audit) is too late if you don't like the purchase.

**Q3: What's a red flag for expense fraud?**
A: Round numbers (₹10,000 exactly?), no receipt, unusual category, frequent same vendor.
