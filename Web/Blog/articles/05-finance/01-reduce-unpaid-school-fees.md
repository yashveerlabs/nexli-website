## The Problem: Mounting Unpaid Fees

A school bills ₹100 lakhs in annual fees. By year-end:
- ₹60 lakhs collected
- ₹40 lakhs outstanding

That's 40% unpaid. Over 5 years, it's ₹2 crore unrecovered. Cash flow suffers. Staff salaries are delayed. School expansion is postponed.

Yet many schools accept this as normal. "Some parents don't pay. What can we do?"

Wrong mindset. With systematic collection strategies, schools regularly achieve 90%+ fee recovery.

The key: treating fee collection as a systematic process, not an afterthought.

## Strategies to Reduce Unpaid Fees

### 1. Online Payment Options

**Why It Matters:**
Parents delay payment because:
- No online option (must visit bank, inconvenient)
- Prefer digital (safer than cash)

**Implementation:**
- Integrate UPI, net banking, cards
- QR code on fee invoice (scan → pay instantly)
- Auto-reminder SMS with payment link

**Impact:**
- Payment latency decreases from 30 days to 5 days
- Improves collection by 10–15%

### 2. Structured Fee Schedule

**Why It Matters:**
If fees are all due at once (April 1), some parents can't pay in bulk. Break it into installments.

**Example: Annual Fee ₹1,20,000**

| Installment | Due Date | Amount | Policy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1st** | April 15 | ₹40,000 | Admission |
| **2nd** | July 31 | ₹40,000 | Mid-year |
| **3rd** | December 1 | ₹40,000 | End-year |

Smaller, frequent payments are easier for parents to manage.

### 3. Pro-Active Reminders

**Strategy:**
Don't wait for parents to pay. Remind them proactively.

**Timeline:**
- **15 Days Before Due:** SMS/email reminder
- **Due Date:** "Payment is due today"
- **5 Days After Due:** "Your payment is overdue. Please pay by [date]"
- **15 Days After Due:** Principal phone call (personal touch)
- **30 Days After Due:** Parent meeting (discuss barriers, create payment plan)
- **60 Days After Due:** If still unpaid, restrictive action (withhold report card, etc.)

### 4. Identify & Support Genuinely Unable Parents

**Reality:**
Not all unpaid fees are due to negligence. Some parents cannot afford.

**Process:**
- Scholarship/concession application form
- Assess financial need (income verification)
- Approve concession for eligible families
- Reduce fee; ensure payment of reduced amount

**Impact:**
- Removes barriers for low-income families
- Paid fees (at reduced rate) > unpaid fees (at full rate)

### 5. Flexible Payment Plans for Extended Defaults

**Strategy:**
If a parent owes ₹1,20,000 and can't pay lump-sum, create a plan.

**Example:**
- Principal discusses with parent: "You owe ₹1,20,000. Can you pay ₹10,000/month for 12 months?"
- If agreed, formalize in writing: "You commit to ₹10,000 on 1st of each month"
- Track & follow-up

**Impact:**
- Parent sees a pathway; more likely to commit
- School recovers money (even if slow)
- Relationship improves (school is helpful, not punitive)

### 6. Clear Default Consequences

**Why It Matters:**
Parents must know there are consequences for non-payment.

**Consequences (Progressive):**
- 30 days overdue: SMS warning
- 60 days overdue: Provisional report card (not final)
- 90 days overdue: Cannot register for next year
- 120+ days overdue: Police complaint filed (last resort)

Make consequences clear in the fee policy (given at admission).

### 7. Defaulter List & Action

**Process:**
- Monthly: Generate defaulter list (unpaid > 60 days)
- Principal reviews: categorize by reason (inability to pay, negligence, etc.)
- Targeted action:
  - Unable to pay: Offer concession/payment plan
  - Negligent/avoiding: Strict follow-up
  - Dispute: Listen and resolve

**Results:**
- Defaulters decrease monthly
- Cash flow improves

### 8. Refer to Collections Agency (Last Resort)

**When:**
- Parent owes >120 days
- All school efforts have failed
- Legal action is necessary

**Process:**
- School issues legal notice
- Refer to external agency or police
- Recover through legal channels

## Best Practices

**1. Start Collections Early**
On Day 1 of admission, collect first payment. Don't wait for month-end.

**2. Make Payment Mandatory Before Re-Enrollment**
No re-enrollment for next year until fees are cleared.

**3. Balance Firmness & Empathy**
Collect aggressively, but listen to barriers. Help parents who need help; hold accountable those who avoid.

**4. Track Everything**
Use Nexli or similar to track:
- Who paid, when, how much
- Who's overdue, by how much
- What action was taken, result

**5. Report to Board**
Monthly board review: Fee collection %, defaulters, trend. Accountability matters.

## FAQ

**Q1: Is it legal to withhold report cards for unpaid fees?**
A: Check your school policy and local laws. Many schools do this; courts have upheld it as reasonable. But communicate this clearly at admission.

**Q2: Should we reduce fees for low-income families?**
A: Yes. Scholarship/concession programs are fair and improve collection (partial payment > no payment).

**Q3: What's a realistic fee collection rate?**
A: 90%+ is excellent. 85–90% is good. Below 80% indicates a problem.
