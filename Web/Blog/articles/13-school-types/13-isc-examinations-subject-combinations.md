## ISC Examinations: Subject Combinations

Different school types face different operational realities. Understanding isc examinations: subject combinations helps schools choose the right ERP features for their context.

### The Reality of School Diversity

India's schools are extraordinarily diverse. A CBSE day school operates completely differently from an ICSE boarding school or a government school. An ERP that ignores these differences creates friction instead of solving problems.

### Consequences of One-Size-Fits-All Systems

- **Compliance Gaps:** Systems missing your board's requirements leave schools exposed.
- **Workflow Mismatch:** Processes designed for day schools create extra work for boarding schools.
- **Feature Waste:** You pay for features your school never uses.
- **Staff Resistance:** When the system doesn't match real workflows, adoption stalls.

### Key Considerations

**Board-Specific Compliance**
- CBSE, ICSE, State Board, International requirements
- Operational model (day vs. boarding vs. day-cum-boarding)
- Student population characteristics
- Geographic context
- School size

### How to Evaluate for Your School Type

**1. Map Your Critical Workflows**
Write down the 10 most important daily/weekly processes your school runs.

**2. Ask Vendor References**
Request contact details of 3-5 schools similar to yours. Ask how well the system handles your specific needs.

**3. Test Board Compliance**
For CBSE: Does it generate LOC correctly?
For ICSE: Does it support internal assessment rules?
For boarding schools: Does it track hostel attendance and exeat?

**4. Verify Integration Depth**
In a boarding school, hostel attendance must feed into overall records. These integrations separate real solutions from generic systems.
