# Building YouTube Presence: Long-Form Video Strategy

**Published:** June 19, 2026 | **Category:** School Marketing | **Read time:** 5 min

---

## YouTube: The Second Largest Search Engine

69% of parents search YouTube for school information. A YouTube channel with 50+ videos positions you as authority.

YouTube visitors have 5x higher inquiry conversion rate than ad traffic.

---

## YouTube Strategy

### 1. **Channel Setup**
- Branded channel (school logo, banner, description)
- Channel description with school mission
- Links to website and admissions
- Playlists organized by topic

### 2. **Content Ideas**
- Campus tour series (by facility)
- Student testimonials
- Day-in-life vlogs
- Teacher interviews
- Parent Q&A
- Event coverage
- Student projects/science fair
- Board exam tips
- College admissions stories
- Sports highlights

### 3. **Upload Strategy**
- 2–4 videos per month (sustainable)
- Consistent upload day/time
- Series approach (e.g., "Tuesdays = Student Stories")
- Playlists organize content

### 4. **SEO Optimization**
- Title includes keyword
- Description 200+ characters
- Tags relevant
- Captions generated/added
- Thumbnail compelling

### 5. **Promotion**
- Social media (teaser + link)
- Email (video link in newsletter)
- Website (embed on admissions page)
- Google Ads (video ads)
- Other videos (suggested videos, end screens, cards)

### 6. **Engagement**
- Reply to comments
- Pin top comment
- Like/heart comments
- Ask questions (drive comments)
- Respond to messages

### 7. **Measurement**
- Watch time (goal: 4,000 hrs/year for monetization)
- Subscribers (goal: 1,000+ within 1 year)
- Click-through rate to website
- Conversion rate (viewers → inquiries)

### 8. **Monetization**
Once 4,000 watch hours + 1,000 subscribers: Enable monetization.
But for schools, value is lead generation, not ad revenue.
