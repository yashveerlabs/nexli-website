# Social Media Content Calendar: Planning and Execution

**Published:** June 19, 2026 | **Category:** School Marketing | **Read time:** 5 min

---

## Content Calendar: Strategic Planning

A content calendar ensures consistent, strategic posting. Without it, social media is reactive and random.

Social media with calendar: 3x better engagement and consistency.

---

## Strategy

### 1. **Planning Timeline**
- Plan 2–3 months in advance
- Flexible for real-time events
- Themes by month

### 2. **Calendar Structure**
- Monday: Student achievement
- Wednesday: Behind-the-scenes  
- Friday: Community/engagement
- Daily: Stories (optional)

### 3. **Themes by Month**
- June: Summer programs
- July-Aug: Sports and activities
- Sept-Oct: Academic excellence
- Nov-Dec: Events and celebrations
- Jan-Feb: New year resolutions
- Mar-Apr: Admissions and college prep

### 4. **Content Mix**
- 40% Educational/tips
- 30% Achievements/success stories
- 20% Community and events
- 10% Calls-to-action

### 5. **Tools**
- Google Sheets (simple, free)
- Asana or Monday.com (project management)
- Buffer or Later (scheduling)
- Nexli (integrated calendar)

### 6. **Batch Creation**
- Create 4 weeks of content in 1 day
- Use templates for consistency
- Repurpose blog posts into social posts
- Create captions in advance

### 7. **Flexibility**
- 70% planned content
- 30% spontaneous/real-time

This balance ensures consistency while staying responsive.

## Implementation

- [ ] Create Google Sheet calendar

- [ ] Design templates
- [ ] Batch create posts

## The Bottom Line

A content calendar is the foundation of consistent social media. Plan quarterly. Adjust monthly. Execute daily.
