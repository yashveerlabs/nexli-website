## School Mission and Vision Statement: Crafting Your School's Purpose

**Mission:** Why does your school exist?  
**Vision:** What do you aspire to become?  
**Values:** What principles guide you?

These three statements form the foundation of your brand and strategic direction.

---

## Mission Statement (Purpose)

**Good mission is:**
- Clear and specific (not "We provide quality education", vague)
- Action-oriented (what do you DO?)
- Inspiring (resonates with staff and parents)
- Achievable (realistic, not fantasy)

**Examples:**
- "To develop confident, critically-thinking global citizens grounded in Indian values."
- "To provide affordable, high-quality education that transforms lives and builds strong communities."
- "To nurture every student's unique potential through innovative, inclusive, holistic education."

**Process:**
1. Reflect: What problems do you solve for students/society?
2. Draft: Write 3–5 candidate statements
3. Test: Ask staff/parents, do they resonate?
4. Refine: Pick the strongest, wordsmith
5. Adopt: Publish, communicate, live it

---

## Vision Statement (Aspiration)

**Good vision is:**
- Inspiring (paints a picture of future)
- Ambitious but achievable
- Time-framed (3–5 years)
- Measurable (how will you know you've succeeded?)

**Examples:**
- "To become the most innovative, inclusive CBSE school in [State] by 2028, known for student outcomes, teacher development, and community impact."
- "By 2027, to build a thriving learning community where every student graduates with academic excellence, character, and career-readiness."

---

## Values (Principles)

**Choose 5–7 core values:**
- Academic Excellence (rigor, high standards)
- Integrity (honesty, fairness, transparency)
- Inclusion (welcome all learners)
- Innovation (embrace change, adapt)
- Community (serve together, build relationships)
- Compassion (care for wellbeing)
- Courage (take intellectual and moral risks)

**For each value, define:** "What does this look like in action?"
- Integrity: We admit mistakes, follow through on promises, grade fairly
- Innovation: We experiment with new teaching methods, encourage student projects

---

## How to Communicate Mission/Vision

**Website:** Prominent homepage placement
**Office:** Poster in all classrooms and staff room
**Admissions:** Share with prospective parents
**Staff Onboarding:** New teachers learn this on day 1
**Annual Goal-Setting:** "How does this year's plan advance our mission?"

## FAQ

**Q1: How long should mission/vision statements be?**  
A: Mission: 1 sentence (15–25 words). Vision: 1–2 sentences. Values: 5–7, each with brief explanation.

**Q2: Should students help draft mission/vision?**  
A: Yes (for older students, Class 9+). Adds perspective, builds ownership.

**Q3: Can we change our mission?**  
A: Yes, but rarely (every 5+ years). Changing often signals lack of direction. Evolve gradually.
