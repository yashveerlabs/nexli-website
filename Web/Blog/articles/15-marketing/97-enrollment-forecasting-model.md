# Enrollment Forecasting: Predicting School Demand and Capacity Planning

**Published:** June 19, 2026 | **Category:** School Marketing | **Read time:** 5 min

---

## Enrollment Forecasting: Plan for Growth

Predict next year's enrollment using data. Plan staffing, facilities, marketing accordingly.

Schools with forecasting are never surprised by enrollment (good or bad).

---

## Model Elements

### Historical Data
- Past 3 years enrollments
- By grade/section
- By source (admissions channel)
- By retention (who stayed, who left)

### Market Factors
- Local population growth
- Competitor activity
- Economic conditions
- School reputation trends

### Lead Data
- Current inquiries
- Conversion rates
- Pipeline health
- Seasonal patterns

### Forecasting Formula
Next Year Enrollment = (Current Enrollment × Retention Rate) + (Inquiries × Conversion Rate)

### Example
- 300 current students
- 85% retention = 255 students
- 100 inquiries expected
- 30% conversion = 30 new students
- Total forecast: 285 students

### Scenarios
- Conservative (low enrollment)
- Realistic (expected enrollment)
- Optimistic (strong growth)

### Planning
- Staffing (hire ahead of time)
- Facilities (plan expansions)
- Marketing (adjust if forecast low)
- Revenue (budget accordingly)

### Monitoring
- Track actual vs. forecast monthly
- Adjust forecast as year progresses
- Use to fine-tune marketing spend
