# Marketing Budget: How to Allocate Spend for Maximum ROI

**Published:** June 19, 2026 | **Category:** School Marketing | **Read time:** 5 min

---

## Budget Allocation: Follow the Data, Not Tradition

Most schools allocate marketing budget by guessing. "Print ads worked 10 years ago. Let's do ₹2L on print."

Schools with data allocate by ROI. "Google Ads return 4x ROI. Increase budget. Email marketing returns 45x ROI. Increase budget even more."

Data-driven allocation 3x improves admissions outcome compared to traditional budget allocation.

---

## Strategy: Data-Driven Budget Allocation

### 1. **Track All Channels**
Record every admissions inquiry source:
- Website visit
- Google Ads call
- Facebook Ads inquiry
- Social media (organic)
- Referral (friend recommendation)
- Event (open house, sponsorship)
- Media mention
- Other

Use form to capture source: "How did you hear about us?"

### 2. **Calculate Channel ROI**
For each channel, track:
- Budget spent: ₹X
- Inquiries generated: Y
- Conversion to enrollment: Z

ROI = (Revenue from enrollments ÷ Budget spent) × 100

Example:
- Google Ads: ₹50,000 spent → 20 inquiries → 4 enrollments → ₹40,00,000 revenue → **8000% ROI**
- Print ads: ₹50,000 spent → 2 inquiries → 0 enrollments → ₹0 revenue → **-100% ROI**

Print ads are losing money. Google Ads are winning. Obvious.

### 3. **Benchmark Against Targets**
Healthy ROI targets:
- Google Ads: 3–5x ROI
- Facebook Ads: 2–4x ROI
- Email/Content: 10–20x ROI
- Referral: 5–10x ROI
- Organic social: 5–10x ROI
- Events: 2–3x ROI

If channel underperforms benchmark, investigate or cut.

### 4. **Annual Marketing Budget Model**
Total budget = (Target # of enrollments) × (Average revenue per enrollment) × (Target ROI %)

Example:
- Target: 50 new enrollments
- Average revenue: ₹1,00,000 (fees × years)
- ROI target: 10x
- Required budget: (50 × 1,00,000) ÷ 10 = ₹5,00,000

Allocate ₹5,00,000 annually to marketing.

### 5. **Allocation By Channel**
Allocate according to ROI potential:

**High ROI channels (40% of budget):**
- Email/content marketing: ₹1,00,000 (10–20x ROI)
- Google Ads: ₹1,00,000 (3–5x ROI)

**Medium ROI channels (40% of budget):**
- Facebook Ads: ₹1,00,000 (2–4x ROI)
- Events/sponsorship: ₹1,00,000 (2–3x ROI)

**Building channels (20% of budget):**
- Social media organic: ₹50,000 (testing)
- PR/media: ₹50,000 (testing)

### 6. **Quarterly Review**
Every 3 months, analyze ROI.

Channels exceeding ROI target → Increase budget
Channels below target → Investigate or cut
New channels testing → Continue if promising

### 7. **Common Budget Mistakes**
- Continuing ads that don't work ("We've always done print")
- Underfunding high-ROI channels ("Email marketing is unsexy")
- Spreading budget too thin (doing 10 channels poorly instead of 5 well)
- Not tracking (no data, just guessing)

Avoid these.

### 8. **Measurement Tools**
- Spreadsheet (simple, free)
- Google Analytics (free, detailed website data)
- CRM system (Nexli, tracks inquiries and source)
- UTM parameters (tag all links to track source)

## The Bottom Line

Every rupee spent on marketing should generate 2–10x return. Track your channels. Measure ROI. Allocate accordingly. Cut what doesn't work. Scale what does.

Data-driven budget allocation is the difference between thriving schools and struggling ones.
