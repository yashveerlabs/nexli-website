# Email A/B Testing: Optimizing Newsletter Performance

**Published:** June 19, 2026 | **Category:** School Marketing | **Read time:** 5 min

---

## A/B Testing: Small Changes, Big Results

Test subject line: "June Newsletter" opens at 25%.
Test subject line: "Your Child's Achievements: June Spotlight" opens at 45%.

Same email. Different subject. 80% increase in open rate.

A/B testing finds these wins automatically.

---

## Strategy

### 1. **What to Test**
- Subject lines (biggest impact)
- Send time (morning vs. evening)
- Email copy (short vs. detailed)
- CTA button text and color
- Email length
- Image placement

### 2. **Testing Process**
- Split audience 50/50
- Send version A to half
- Send version B to other half
- Measure open rate, click rate
- Scale winning version

### 3. **Subject Line Testing**
Test 1: "June Newsletter"
Test 2: "See What [School] Students Accomplished in June"

Measure which has higher open rate. Use winner.

### 4. **Testing Frequency**
- Test one element per email
- Run test for 1 week
- Analyze results
- Apply learnings to next email

### 5. **Measurement**
- Open rate (goal: 40%+)
- Click rate (goal: 5%+)
- Unsubscribe rate (goal: <0.5%)
- Conversion rate (clicks → inquiries)

### 6. **Tools**
Most email platforms include A/B testing:
- Mailchimp (free)
- Brevo
- Nexli (built-in)

A/B testing finds incremental wins. 20% improvement in open rate = 20% more inquiries over time.
