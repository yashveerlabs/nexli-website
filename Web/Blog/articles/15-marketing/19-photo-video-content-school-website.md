# Photo and Video Content for School Website: Best Practices

**Published:** June 19, 2026 | **Category:** School Marketing | **Read time:** 7 min

---

## The Visual Advantage: Why Video and Photos Convert 5x Better Than Text

A text headline reads: "Our campus has state-of-the-art sports facilities."

A video shows: A student scoring a field goal, crowd cheering, coach celebrating. Takes 15 seconds. Parents feel the energy.

**The data:** Pages with video have 4.8x higher engagement. Prospects spend 2.5x longer on pages with photos. Video increases admissions inquiries by 5–10x compared to text-only pages.

Yet most school websites have blurry photos taken on phones. Or worse: no photos at all.

---

## The Problem: Poor Visual Content Loses Families

### 1. **Low-Quality Photos and Videos**
Most schools rely on candid phone photos: blurry, poorly lit, amateur. Prospective parents see it and think: "If the website looks this rough, what's the campus like?"

### 2. **No Video at All**
Schools describe campus tours, events, student life—but have zero video. Families want to see, not read. Video on your website is expected now. Absence signals outdatedness.

### 3. **Irrelevant or Generic Content**
Photos of old events from 3 years ago. Videotaped assembly with bad audio. Nobody cares. You need fresh, diverse content showing real campus life now.

### 4. **Privacy and Consent Paralysis**
Getting photos/video requires parent permission. So many schools do nothing instead of building a systematic consent process.

### 5. **Not Optimized for Web**
10 MB photo files that take 5 seconds to load. Video that buffers. Slow loading kills engagement.

---

## The Solution: Systematic Visual Content Strategy

### 1. **Photo Content: What to Capture**
Create a shot list for different scenarios:

**Campus Infrastructure:**
- Classrooms (students learning, not just empty rooms)
- Science labs (hands-on experiments)
- Sports facilities (students in action)
- Art and music spaces (creativity)
- Cafeteria (food, community)
- Library (reading, studying)
- Common areas (students mingling)

**Events & Activities:**
- Sports matches (winning, celebrating, team spirit)
- Annual day or cultural events
- Science fairs and competitions
- Community service
- Field trips and excursions
- Graduation ceremonies
- Award presentations

**Student Life:**
- Classroom candids (engaged learning, not posed)
- Group projects
- Friendships and collaboration
- Diversity (students of different backgrounds)
- Leadership and responsibility
- Emotional moments (achievement, growth)

**Staff & Culture:**
- Teachers engaging with students
- Staff at events
- Leadership visible in campus
- School traditions

**Key principle:** Show outcomes, not facilities. A photo of a lab isn't powerful. A photo of a student's experiment succeeding is.

### 2. **Video Content: What to Create**
Budget: $2K–5K per video (or use internal/professional student videos).

**Priority videos:**

**Virtual Campus Tour (3–5 min):**
- Start with main entrance and admin building.
- Walk through classrooms, labs, sports, art rooms.
- Include student voice-overs: "This is our lab where we do real experiments."
- Show actual students (not staged), real classes (not empty rooms).
- End with admission CTA: "Ready to visit? Apply now."

**Student Day-in-Life (2–3 min):**
- Follow one student (or diverse mix) through a typical day.
- Morning arrival, classes, sports, clubs, lunch, dismissal.
- Capture authentic moments (not scripted).
- Voice-over from student: "This is my life at [School]."

**Testimonial Videos (1–2 min each):**
- Students or parents answering: "How has this school changed your life?"
- Authentic on-camera. Raw is better than polished.
- Shoot in natural light. Real emotions matter.

**Event Highlights (1 min each):**
- Annual day, sports day, Science Fair
- Fast-paced, energetic music
- Show wins, achievements, community
- Post on social media + website

**Parent Information Sessions (10–15 min):**
- Principal speaking directly to prospective parents
- Tour the campus
- Answer FAQs
- Make it available on-demand, not live

### 3. **Consent: Build a Systematic Process**
Make photography/videography permission frictionless.

**At admission:**
- Include a consent form: "May we photograph/video your child for school website/social media/marketing?"
- Options: "Yes," "Only for internal use," "No photos."
- Store consent digitally (Nexli tracks this).

**Annual refresh:**
- Email all parents: "Reminder: We'll be photographing sports/events this term. Confirm your consent."
- Make opting in/out easy (one-click email reply).

**Result:** You have consent for 90%+ of students without friction.

### 4. **Photo Technical Standards**
- **Resolution:** 2000+ pixels wide for website images.
- **Format:** JPG (smaller file size), optimize for web (compress).
- **Speed:** Load time under 2 seconds per image.
- **Aspect ratio:** Varied (not all the same format).
- **Alt text:** Every photo should have alt text for accessibility and SEO.

**Tool:** Use Squoosh.app (free) to compress photos without quality loss.

### 5. **Video Technical Standards**
- **Resolution:** 1080p minimum (4K if possible).
- **Length:** Homepage hero video 15–30 sec. Tour video 3–5 min. Testimonials 1–2 min.
- **Audio:** Clear, professional. Background music (royalty-free from Pexels or Pixabay).
- **Captions:** Add captions to every video (boosts engagement by 30%).
- **Format:** MP4 (universal), hosted on YouTube/Vimeo (faster loading than self-hosted).

**Tool:** Use CapCut (free) for simple editing. Descript.com (paid) for caption generation.

### 6. **Placement Strategy**
- **Homepage hero section:** 15–30 sec video of campus/student life. Auto-plays (muted). Click to unmute.
- **Admissions page:** Embedded campus tour video (3–5 min). Below the fold.
- **About us page:** Mix of photos showing culture and values.
- **Grade-specific pages:** Photos of that grade's classroom, events, student achievements.
- **Testimonials section:** Student + parent video testimonials side-by-side with photos.
- **Social media:** Repost video clips, behind-the-scenes photos daily.

### 7. **Content Calendar: Staying Fresh**
Create a monthly visual content plan:

**Month 1:** Campus tour video refresh + staff photos.
**Month 2:** Student testimonial videos (3–5).
**Month 3:** Event coverage (sports, annual day).
**Month 4:** Parent day or open house photos.
**Month 5:** Academic achievements (awards, exams, projects).
**Month 6:** Refresh homepage photos (seasonal change).

New content every month keeps your website fresh and improves SEO.

### 8. **Diversity Matters**
Your photos should reflect your school's actual diversity. If 40% of students are girls, 40% of your featured photos should show girls in leadership, sports, academics. If your school includes CWSN students, feature them in inclusive activities.

Diversity in visuals signals: "Every student belongs here."

## The Bottom Line

Families don't enroll in schools based on text descriptions. They enroll based on what they see and feel.

A school website without video and photos is invisible. Blurry, outdated photos signal carelessness. Fresh, diverse, high-quality visuals signal excellence.

Build a systematic photo and video strategy. Capture real moments. Get consent once, use widely. Keep content fresh and optimized. The result: prospective families who see, feel, and choose your school.
