# Press Release: Getting Your School in the News

**Published:** June 19, 2026 | **Category:** School Marketing | **Read time:** 5 min

---

## Media Coverage: Third-Party Credibility

A parent reads: "XYZ School wins 'Best School' award" in local news. They think: "This school must be good. The media recognizes it."

Press releases get your school in the news. News coverage is more credible than advertising.

---

## Strategy: Press Release Marketing

### 1. **Newsworthy Events**
- Major award or recognition
- Significant achievement (student success, board results)
- New program or facility launch
- Partnership announcement
- Community impact story
- Leadership change or milestone

### 2. **Press Release Structure**
- Headline: "XYZ School Launches Scholarship Program for Underprivileged Students"
- Dateline: "Mumbai, June 19, 2026"
- Opening: Who, what, when, where, why
- Quotes: From principal or key stakeholder
- Facts: Evidence, numbers, achievements
- Contact: School contact for media inquiries

### 3. **Distribute**
- Local news outlets (newspapers, online portals)
- Education journalists (LinkedIn, email)
- Press release platforms (Prweb, PIB)
- Social media

### 4. **Timing**
Avoid oversaturation. Issue 4–6 press releases per year for major announcements.

### 5. **Follow-Up**
After distribution, follow up with journalists. Answer questions. Make interviews easy.

---

## Implementation Checklist

- [ ] **Identify newsworthy stories:** Major achievements, programs
- [ ] **Write press release:** Following standard format
- [ ] **Create media list:** Local journalists, outlets
- [ ] **Distribute:** Email or press release platform
- [ ] **Follow up:** Contact journalists directly
- [ ] **Share coverage:** Amplify news on social, email, website

---

## The Bottom Line

Press releases turn achievements into news. News turns news into credibility. Credibility drives admissions.
