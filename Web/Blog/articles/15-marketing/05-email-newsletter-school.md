## Email Newsletter for School Community: Building Engagement

**Email newsletters keep parents informed, build community, and drive admissions inquiries. This guide covers content strategy, design, frequency, and engagement tactics.**

---

## Newsletter Strategy

**Purpose:** Keep parents informed, celebrate achievements, build school brand

**Content Mix:**
- Academics & achievements (40%): Exam results, student accomplishments, curricular updates
- School news & events (30%): Admissions dates, parent meetings, facilities updates
- Student life stories (20%): Student spotlights, testimonials, day-in-the-life features
- Community & values (10%): Social responsibility, celebrations, alumni updates

**Frequency:** Monthly (consistent is better than random)

**Send time:** Tuesday–Thursday, 10 AM (optimal open rate)

---

## Newsletter Content

**Subject Line (Critical for open rate):**
- "June Newsletter: Your Child's Latest Achievements 🎉"
- "New STEM Lab Open House This Saturday!"
- "Parent Alert: Admissions Timeline Updated"

**Structure:**
- Header: School logo, theme/issue (June 2026 Newsletter)
- Top news: 1–2 most important items (big school announcements, achievements)
- Highlights: 3–4 feature stories (student spotlight, event recap, academic update)
- Calendar: Upcoming events and deadlines
- Call-to-action: "Enroll Now", "Register for Open House", "Read Full Story on Website"
- Footer: Contact, social media, unsubscribe link

**Best practices:**
- Keep it short (parents scan, don't read lengthy emails)
- Use images (break text, show school community)
- Personalize greeting ("Dear Mr. & Mrs. Sharma")
- Link to website (drive traffic for SEO)

---

## Growing Your Subscriber List

**On-School Website:** "Subscribe to Newsletter" popup or form
**At Admissions:** "Add me to newsletter" checkbox on application
**At Events:** Paper signup at school tour, open house
**Social Media:** "Subscribe to email newsletter for exclusive updates"

**Target:** 50% of parents on mailing list within 1 year

---

## Tools & Templates

**Email Services:** Mailchimp, ConvertKit (free for <500 subscribers), Google Workspace
**Template libraries:** Canva (free templates), Mailchimp templates
**Cost:** ₹0–2,000/month depending on service

---

## Measure Success

**Open rate:** Target 25%+ (industry avg 15–20%)
**Click-through rate:** Target 5%+ (industry avg 2–3%)
**Unsubscribe rate:** <1% is healthy

**Monthly review:** Which topics get highest opens? Do more of those.

---

## Common Mistakes

**Too sales-focused** ("Enroll now!") → Parents unsubscribe
**Inconsistent sending** (skipped 2 months) → Subscribers forget you exist
**Too long** (5+ pages) → Ignored, delete
**Poorly designed** (hard to read on mobile) → Low engagement
**Broken links** → Professional credibility damaged

## FAQ

**Q1: How many times can we email before parents unsubscribe?**  
A: 2–4 times per month is safe. Once a week can be too much.

**Q2: Should we personalize emails (name, child's name)?**  
A: Yes. "Hi Rahul's Parents" feels more personal than "Dear Parents". Most email tools support this.

**Q3: Can we sell products/services in newsletters?**  
A: Rarely. Keep focus on school updates and achievements. Selling alienates parents.
