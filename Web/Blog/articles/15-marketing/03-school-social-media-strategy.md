## School Social Media Strategy: Facebook, Instagram, and Beyond

**Social media is where parents research schools, share experiences, and recommend to friends. A strategic social media presence builds community, boosts credibility, and drives admissions. This guide covers strategy, content types, platform selection, and engagement best practices.**

---

## Platform Selection: Which to Use

**Facebook:** Best for parent engagement (age demographic 30–50), community building, event promotion
**Instagram:** Best for visual storytelling, student life, appeal to younger parents
**LinkedIn:** Professional community, staff recruitment
**YouTube:** School tour videos, testimonials, educational content
**WhatsApp:** Parent notifications (not marketing, but essential)

**Recommendation:** Start with Facebook + Instagram + YouTube. Master these three before expanding.

---

## Content Strategy: What to Post

**Academic Updates (30%):**
- Exam results, topper achievements
- Subject spotlights ("Did you know: Here's how we teach STEM")
- Student project highlights
- Curriculum updates (NEP 2020, certifications)

**Student Life (40%):**
- Sports events, tournaments, victories
- Cultural programs, assemblies
- Club activities, competitions
- Student testimonials
- Day-in-the-life videos

**School News & Events (20%):**
- Admissions timeline announcements
- School calendar updates
- Parent meetings scheduled
- Infrastructure updates ("New science lab opening!")

**Community & Values (10%):**
- Social responsibility initiatives
- Diversity and inclusion celebrations
- Staff spotlights
- Alumni success stories

---

## Content Calendar & Posting Frequency

**Facebook:** 4–5 posts per week
**Instagram:** 3–4 posts per week
**YouTube:** 1 video per week (school tour, testimonial, educational)

**Plan 1 month ahead:** Use a content calendar (Google Sheets or Asana). Assign responsibility to staff.

---

## Engagement Best Practices

**Respond to Comments:** Within 24 hours. Show you're listening.

**Encourage User-Generated Content:** "Tag us in your school memories" → builds community

**Use Calls-to-Action:** "Apply Now", "Register for Open House", "Share Your Story"

**Go Live:** Instagram/Facebook live broadcasts during events (admissions day, school tour) → real-time engagement

**Hashtags:** Use 5–10 relevant hashtags (#SchoolName, #IndianEducation, #NEP2020, #StudentLife)

**Paid Ads:** Consider small budget (₹5K–10K/month) to reach parents not following you yet

---

## Metrics to Track

**Reach:** How many people saw your post?
**Engagement:** Likes, comments, shares
**Click-through:** How many visited your website from social?
**Conversion:** How many inquiries came from social?

**Goal:** Track over 3 months. If engagement is low, adjust content. Success metrics: +30% followers and +10 inquiries/month by month 3.

---

## Common Mistakes

**Posting too infrequently:** (Once a month won't build community)
**Selling only** ("Enroll now!" every post) Vs. storytelling
**No response to comments:** Engagement dies if you don't reply
**Inconsistent quality:** Post random, low-quality images; looks unprofessional
**Missing deadlines:** "Applications open Friday!" then posts on Saturday; parents miss it

## FAQ

**Q1: Should I hire a social media manager?**  
A: Start with a teacher volunteer (stipend). If scaling, hire freelancer (₹500–1K/post).

**Q2: What if a parent posts negative comment on Facebook?**  
A: Respond professionally. "We'd love to hear more. Can we DM you?" Take it offline.

**Q3: Should we allow student social media accounts?**  
A: Not official. Discourage accounts in school name. Focus on school-managed accounts only.
