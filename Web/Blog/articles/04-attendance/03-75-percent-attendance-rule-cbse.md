## The Rule: What CBSE Requires

**The Requirement:**
CBSE mandates that students in Class 10 and Class 12 must have **at least 75% attendance** for the academic year to be **eligible to sit for board exams**.

**Important Details:**
- Applies to Class 10 (Grades 9–10) and Class 12 (Grades 11–12)
- Calculated across the full academic year
- Medical absences with proper documentation may be condoned (doesn't count toward attendance calculation)
- If a student falls below 75%, they're barred from sitting for board exams

## Calculating 75% Attendance

**Formula:**
Attendance % = (Days Present) / (Total School Days - Condoned Days) × 100

**Example:**
- School days in year: 250
- Medical leave with certificate: 15 days (condoned)
- Eligible school days: 250 - 15 = 235
- Days student attended: 180
- Attendance %: (180 / 235) × 100 = 76.6% ✓ Eligible

If the student had attended only 176 days:
- Attendance %: (176 / 235) × 100 = 74.9% ✗ NOT Eligible (below 75%)

## Exceptions & Condoned Absences

Some absences are **condoned** (don't count against attendance):

**Condoned Absences (typically):**
- **Medical:** With doctor's certificate
- **School Duty:** Sports competitions, exam duty, school-sponsored activities
- **Natural Calamity:** Flood, earthquake (as per school's discretion)
- **Bereavement:** Death of immediate family (typically 2–3 days)

**NOT Condoned:**
- Vacation/travel (parent's choice)
- Illness without certificate
- Unauthorized absence

**School Policy Matters:**
Schools define their own condonation policy (within CBSE guidelines). A school might condone:
- Medical: Up to 15 days per year
- School duty: Unlimited (usually)
- Other: Case-by-case

Document your policy clearly.

## Why This Rule Exists

CBSE's rationale:
1. **Cumulative Learning:** Board curricula are cumulative. Missing 25%+ of school year means missing core content
2. **Fairness:** Prevents students who haven't attended school from sitting exams alongside regular students
3. **Board Integrity:** Protects the value of board certificates

## Compliance Strategies

**1. Track Attendance Religiously**
From Day 1 of Class 10/12, track attendance daily. Don't assume "they'll be fine."

**2. Communicate the 75% Rule**
- Inform parents at start of academic year
- Show impact: "To maintain 75%, Priya can miss only ~5 days per term"
- Send monthly attendance reports with running calculations

**3. Identify At-Risk Students Early**
- In October (after ~20 school days), identify students below 85%
- These students are at risk of falling below 75% by year-end
- Intervene: parent meeting, counselor involvement, barrier removal

**4. Set a School Target Above 75%**
- School target: 88–90% attendance
- This buffer prevents students from inadvertently falling below 75%
- Individual buffer: If school target is 88%, most students will be at 85%+ (safe)

**5. Manage Condoned Absences Carefully**
- Only condone genuinely eligible absences
- Document everything (medical certs, school-duty letters, bereavement docs)
- Audit: Review condonation list before final calculations (prevents disputes)

**6. Create an Attendance Recovery Plan for At-Risk Students**
- For Class 10/12 students at 60–74% by December:
  - Principal + parent meeting
  - Identify barriers (illness, work, bullying, engagement)
  - Problem-solve: "How can we remove barriers?"
  - Monitor weekly: Track improvement

**7. Be Transparent About Ineligibility**
- If a student will fall below 75% by year-end:
  - Inform parent in advance (December, not May)
  - Explain consequences (can't sit board exams)
  - Discuss options (repeat year, transfer, alternative exam)
  - Document the warning

## What Happens If a Student Falls Below 75%?

**Scenario 1: Student Below 75%, Eligible to Appeal**
- Some boards allow appeal if attendance fell due to serious illness
- Student submits medical certificates and appeal to principal
- Principal submits to board with recommendation
- Board decides (rare approval; mostly appeals are denied)

**Scenario 2: Student Below 75%, No Appeal**
- Student is ineligible to sit board exams
- Options:
  - Repeat the year
  - Transfer to another school (if willing to repeat)
  - Private exam board (NIOS, open school) to appear for exams

**Scenario 3: Prevent It**
- Be proactive: Track, intervene, support
- Most students below 75% are there due to identifiable barriers (health, economic, engagement)
- Remove barriers; attendance improves

## FAQ

**Q1: Is the 75% rule mandatory for all classes or just Class 10/12?**
A: CBSE mandates 75% for Class 10/12 (board exams). Lower classes are usually not subject to this rule, though schools may set it anyway for consistency.

**Q2: Does online learning during COVID count as attendance?**
A: If your school conducted online classes and required attendance, yes. Document it clearly. CBSE's 2020–21 guidelines allowed some flexibility.

**Q3: Can a student appeal if they fall below 75% due to chronic illness?**
A: Technically yes, but appeals are rarely approved. Better to plan ahead: get medical certificates; explore condoned absence options.

**Q4: What if a school doesn't enforce the 75% rule and lets a student sit exams anyway?**
A: CBSE can invalidate that student's exam results. It's a serious compliance violation. Always enforce.
