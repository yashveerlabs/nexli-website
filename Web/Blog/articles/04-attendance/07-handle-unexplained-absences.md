## The Problem: Unexplained Absences & Truancy

A student is absent for 3 days. The teacher marks "A (Absent)" but doesn't know why. No parent explanation. By day 4, school still doesn't know where the student is or why.

Unexplained absences are truancy risks. They also signal potential issues: Is the student sick? Bullied? Working? Lost interest?

## When an Absence Is Unexplained

**Definition:**
- Student marked absent
- No medical certificate, leave approval, or parent explanation provided
- By end of school day, no contact from parent

**Immediate Actions (By 2 PM Same Day):**

1. **Office calls parent:** "Arjun was absent today. Is he okay? Any issues?"
2. **If parent explains:** Record reason (illness, appointment, etc.). Mark absence accordingly.
3. **If parent doesn't answer:** Send SMS: "Arjun was absent today. Please confirm he's okay & confirm reason."
4. **If no response:** Flag as unexplained; escalate to principal

**Next Day:**
- If student returns: Teacher collects written explanation from parent
- If student still absent: Principal calls parent for urgent clarification

**Absence Log Entry:**

| Date | Student | Status | Reason | Provided By | Follow-up |

| June 21 | Arjun | Absent | Unexplained | — | Principal calling today |

Keep detailed records. If a pattern of unexplained absences emerges, you have documentation.

## Investigation Steps

**Step 1: Contact Parent**
- Call (same day, if possible)
- Be non-accusatory: "We want to make sure Arjun is okay."
- Listen: "Is there something we can help with?"

**Step 2: Listen for Barriers**
Parent may disclose:
- Health issue (needs medical support)
- Economic issue (can't afford transport)
- Family crisis
- Student anxiety/bullying

**Step 3: Problem-Solve**
- If health: Connect to clinic
- If economic: Scholarship, transport support
- If family: Counselor involvement
- If bullying: Investigate & address

**Step 4: Document & Follow-Up**
- Record conversation (date, what was discussed, agreed action)
- Follow-up: "Let's check in next week. See if things improve."

## Policies to Establish

**Policy 1: Explanation Requirement**
- Any absence without prior approval requires written explanation within 2 days
- If no explanation: Marked as unexplained, flagged for follow-up

**Policy 2: Consecutive Unexplained Absences**
- 1 unexplained: Office follows up
- 2 consecutive: Principal calls parent
- 3 consecutive: In-person parent meeting; investigate root cause
- 5+ in a month: Escalate (consider counselor, home visit, welfare authorities if abuse suspected)

**Policy 3: Truancy Response**
- Clear written policy: "Unexplained absences without parental contact for 3+ days = potential truancy"
- Escalation: Counselor → Principal → Possible police report (in severe cases)

## FAQ

**Q1: At what point do unexplained absences become truancy?**
A: Policy varies. Common: 3+ consecutive unexplained = concern to school. 5+ in a month = escalation trigger. Set your own threshold.

**Q2: Should parents be fined for unexplained absences?**
A: Fines often backfire (increase resentment, don't solve underlying issue). Better to investigate barriers and remove them.

**Q3: Can a school report truancy to authorities?**
A: In severe cases, yes. If welfare is at risk (exploitation, abuse), schools have duty to report. Consult with your principal and legal counsel.
