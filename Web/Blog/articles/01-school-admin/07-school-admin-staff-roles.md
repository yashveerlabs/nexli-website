## Executive Summary

A school with 300+ students needs 3–5 dedicated administrative staff. Without clear role definitions, tasks overlap, overlap falls through cracks, and accountability is unclear. This article defines core administrative roles, responsibilities, skills, and training for each.

---

## The Administrative Team Structure

### For 300-Student School

```
Principal
  └── Office Manager (1)
      ├── Academic Coordinator (1)
      ├── Receptionist (1)
      └── Administrative Assistant (1–2)
```

### For 500+ Student School

```
Principal
  ├── Office Manager (1)
  │   ├── Academic Coordinator (1)
  │   ├── Finance Manager (1)
  │   ├── HR Coordinator (1)
  │   └── Receptionist (1–2)
  └── Administrative Assistants (2–3)
```

## Core Roles

### Role 1: Office Manager / Administrative Head

#### Purpose
Oversee all administrative operations; report to Principal.

#### Responsibilities

**Daily:**
- Morning standup with team (30 min)
- Review pending requests and prioritize
- Troubleshoot immediate issues
- Monitor communication channels (email, WhatsApp)

**Weekly:**
- Staff meeting (1 hour)
- Financial status review with accountant
- Status report to Principal

**Monthly:**
- Process review and improvement recommendations
- Performance feedback for team
- Compliance calendar check

#### Key Skills
- Organization and multitasking
- Leadership and delegation
- Problem-solving
- Communication
- Technology (Excel, email, possibly ERP)

#### Qualifications
- 12th pass minimum; bachelor's degree preferred
- 3–5 years of administrative experience
- Experience with school operations a plus

#### Time Allocation
- 40% team management and meetings
- 30% process improvement
- 20% crisis management
- 10% compliance monitoring

---

### Role 2: Academic Coordinator

#### Purpose
Manage all academic workflows: timetable, attendance, marks, curriculum, HOD coordination.

#### Responsibilities

**Daily:**
- Monitor attendance (review submitted registers)
- Follow up on absent students (contact parents if <75%)
- Coordinate with HODs on teaching progress

**Weekly:**
- Attendance trends review
- Academic performance review (student performance dashboard)
- Curriculum coverage tracking
- HOD meeting to discuss progress, issues

**Monthly:**
- Detailed academic performance analysis
- At-risk student identification
- Intervention program review

#### Key Skills
- Academic background (B.Ed or similar)
- Attention to detail
- Communication with teachers
- Data analysis

#### Qualifications
- Bachelor's degree in education preferred
- 2–3 years of teaching or academic coordination experience

#### Time Allocation
- 30% attendance and academic tracking
- 25% HOD coordination and support
- 20% at-risk student identification and intervention planning
- 15% curriculum coverage monitoring
- 10% reporting and compliance

---

### Role 3: Finance Manager / Accountant

#### Purpose
Manage school finance: fee collection, payroll, vendor payments, financial reporting.

#### Responsibilities

**Daily:**
- Record fee payments (bank transfers, UPI, cash, cheque)
- Log daily financial transactions
- Monitor fee due list (who hasn't paid)

**Weekly:**
- Fee collection status vs. target
- Defaulter list and follow-up
- Check unpaid vendor invoices

**Monthly:**
- Month-end financial reconciliation (bank match)
- Payroll calculation and payment
- Vendor payment processing
- Monthly financial statements (P&L, balance sheet)

#### Key Skills
- Accounting knowledge (Tally, QuickBooks, or similar)
- Accuracy and attention to detail
- GST/TDS/EPF knowledge (Indian compliance)
- Communication with parents on fees

#### Qualifications
- 12th pass minimum; bachelor's degree in commerce or accounting preferred
- 2–3 years of financial experience in school or business

#### Time Allocation
- 35% daily transactions and reconciliation
- 25% fee collection and follow-up
- 20% payroll and vendor payments
- 15% financial reporting and analysis
- 5% compliance filing (GST, TDS, EPF)

---

### Role 4: HR Coordinator

#### Purpose
Manage staff recruitment, attendance, performance, and records.

#### Responsibilities

**Daily:**
- Staff attendance tracking
- Leave approval support
- Respond to HR-related queries

**Weekly:**
- Staff attendance analysis
- Leave balance review

**Monthly:**
- Staff performance feedback collection
- Credential verification (certifications current?)
- Recruitment pipeline (if hiring)

#### Key Skills
- HR operations knowledge
- Communication with staff
- Confidentiality (sensitive pay/performance data)
- Organization

#### Qualifications
- 12th pass minimum; bachelor's degree in HR or management preferred
- 1–2 years of HR experience

#### Time Allocation
- 30% attendance and leave management
- 25% staff records and documentation
- 20% recruitment (when hiring)
- 15% performance feedback and data collection
- 10% compliance (credential verification, background checks)

---

### Role 5: Receptionist

#### Purpose
First point of contact for visitors, phone calls, inquiries, and admissions.

#### Responsibilities

**Daily:**
- Answer phone (school line)
- Greet visitors; manage gate access
- Log admission inquiries
- Respond to common questions
- Direct calls/visitors to appropriate department
- Manage visitor log

**Weekly:**
- Weekly inquiry summary to admission officer
- Visitor feedback (if applicable)

**Monthly:**
- Inquiry source analysis (which channels are working?)
- Receptionist performance review

#### Key Skills
- Communication (phone, in-person)
- Friendly demeanor and patience
- Multitasking
- Basic IT (email, inquiry form entry)

#### Qualifications
- 12th pass
- Communication skills in English and local language
- Prior customer-facing experience a plus

#### Time Allocation
- 40% phone and visitor management
- 25% inquiry logging and follow-up
- 20% documentation and gate pass
- 15% administrative support

---

### Role 6: Administrative Assistant

#### Purpose
Support office operations: filing, data entry, document generation, errands.

#### Responsibilities

**Daily:**
- File documents in correct location
- Data entry (admission forms, fee receipts)
- Generate routine documents (fee receipts, certificates)
- Make copies and organize files
- Run errands (bank, vendor, post office)

**Weekly:**
- Weekly filing catch-up
- Check document retention (what should be archived)

**Monthly:**
- Filing audit (are all documents in correct location?)
- Inventory check (stationery, forms, etc.)

#### Key Skills
- Accuracy and attention to detail
- Basic IT (Excel, Word, email)
- Organization
- Reliability

#### Qualifications
- 12th pass
- Prior administrative or office experience

#### Time Allocation
- 40% filing and document management
- 30% data entry
- 20% document generation
- 10% errands and coordination

## Training Plan for Each Role

### Onboarding (First 2 Weeks)

All staff:
- School policies and procedures
- IT systems overview (email, access to ERP/software)
- Key contacts (principal, department heads, vendors)
- Tour of facilities

Role-specific:
- Shadowing with existing team member
- Introduction to their specific responsibilities
- First practical task with supervision

### In-Role Training (Months 1–3)

**Office Manager:**
- Leadership training (delegation, feedback)
- Conflict resolution
- Process improvement methodology

**Academic Coordinator:**
- School curriculum overview
- Academic assessment and at-risk identification
- HOD coordination dynamics

**Finance Manager:**
- Tally or accounting software training
- Fee collection processes
- Payroll calculation and compliance

**HR Coordinator:**
- Staff management processes
- Leave and attendance rules
- Recruitment and onboarding

**Receptionist:**
- Inquiry tracking process
- Admission workflow
- Professional phone manner

**Administrative Assistant:**
- Filing system and procedures
- Data entry accuracy
- Document generation process

### Ongoing Professional Development

**Annually:**
- Performance review and feedback
- Skill development in areas of weakness
- Role-specific training (e.g., new accounting software update)

**Monthly:**
- Office team meetings (share learnings, identify improvements)
- Cross-training (learn tasks outside primary role for backup)

**Quarterly:**
- Compliance training (DPDP Act, POCSO Act, etc.)
- New software feature training
- Peer learning (visit other schools, attend workshops)

## Common Mistakes in Role Definition

### Mistake 1: Role Overlap
Multiple people do the same task; no one is accountable.

**Solution:** Clear responsibility matrix (see earlier article on office management)

### Mistake 2: Single Point of Failure
Only one person knows how to do a critical task (fee collection, marks entry).

**Solution:** Cross-train at least one backup for each critical task

### Mistake 3: No Clear Authority
Staff doesn't know who can make what decisions.

**Solution:** Define approval authority for each role (leave <2 days → HOD can approve; >2 days → Principal)

### Mistake 4: Unclear Boundaries
Administrative staff get pulled into teaching (supervising classes, filling in for teachers).

**Solution:** Protect administrative time; define what's admin and what's not

### Mistake 5: No Performance Metrics
No way to measure if role is being done well.

**Solution:** Define KPIs for each role (see below)

- Filing accuracy (98%+)
- Data entry error rate (<1%)
- Document generation turnaround (<24 hours)

## FAQ

**Q: How many administrative staff do we actually need?**
A: Rough rule: 1 full-time admin staff per 100–150 students. For 300 students, 2–3 full-time staff. Varies by school complexity.

**Q: Can one person do multiple roles?**
A: In very small schools, yes. But clarity is critical: 40% on academics, 30% on finance, 30% on HR. Prioritize which role takes precedence.

**Q: How do we find good administrative staff?**
A: Look for reliability and honesty first, skills second. Skills can be trained; character is harder to change. References from previous employers are critical.

**Q: What if administrative staff are overwhelmed?**
A: Audit their tasks. What's truly necessary? What's habit? What can be automated? Often, streamlining processes helps more than hiring more staff.

**Q: Should administrative staff be teachers?**
A: Not recommended. Administrative work requires focus. If a person is both teacher and admin, one role suffers. Better to have dedicated admin staff.
