## Standardizing Processes Across School Chains

School chains have 3-10+ campuses. Without standardization, each operates differently: different admission processes, different fee structures, different disciplinary procedures. This creates confusion for parents, inconsistency in quality, and inefficiency in group operations.

### Problem

- **Inconsistent student experience:** Transferring between campuses means relearning rules
- **Quality variation:** One campus excellent, another average
- **Operational inefficiency:** Each campus duplicates work (procurement, HR, finance)
- **Brand confusion:** Different campuses don't feel like the same school brand
- **Compliance risk:** Different campuses apply rules differently; some may violate policies

### Solutions

**Core Standards (Same Across All Campuses):**
- Admission criteria, process, timeline
- Fee structure and payment terms
- Discipline policy and procedures
- Teacher qualifications and hiring standards
- Curriculum coverage and pacing
- Compliance requirements
- Brand communication guidelines

**Flexible Implementation (Local Variations):**
- Specific fees (adjusted for local cost of living)
- Hiring decisions (selecting campus-specific talent)
- Campus-specific programs or emphases
- Local regulatory adaptations

### Best Practices

**1. Document Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)**
- Admissions: "Lead forms are entered in system within 24 hours, contacted within 48 hours, documents collected within 2 weeks"
- Finance: "Invoices issued by 5th of month, payment due by 15th, default notice sent by 20th"
- Academic: "Timetables announced 7 days before term, lesson plans submitted Friday for next week"
- Every process has a written SOP

**2. Create Unified Data Definitions**
- "Student status: active, alumni, deferred, transferred, withdrawn" (not different terms at different campuses)
- "Fee collection: paid-in-full, partial, default-<30-days, default->30-days" (consistent terminology)
- "Class: identified by Grade-Section" (e.g., "09-A", not "Class 9A" or "9-Section-1")

**3. Centralize Critical Decisions**
- Admissions policy: Group decides criteria; all campuses follow
- Fee policy: Group sets structure; campuses apply with adjustments
- Discipline: Group defines serious vs. minor infractions; consequences consistent
- HR: Group sets hiring standards, interview process

**4. Use One System Across All Campuses**
- Same platform for all (Nexli) ensures consistent data, workflows, and reporting
- No campus operating on isolated systems

**5. Quarterly Process Audits**
- Check: Is each campus following standard procedures?
- Identify: Which campuses deviate and why?
- Support: Help campuses align where they're off track
- Celebrate: Recognize campuses excelling at process adherence

### Implementation Steps

**Phase 1: Document Current State (Week 1-2)**
- List all processes at each campus
- Document what varies, what's consistent
- Identify inefficiencies

**Phase 2: Design Standards (Week 3-4)**
- For each critical process, define: What should be standard?
- Involve stakeholders: Principals, coordinators, staff
- Balance standardization with necessary flexibility

**Phase 3: Communicate (Week 5)**
- Share standards with all campuses
- Explain: Why these changes? What's the benefit?
- Gather feedback; make final adjustments

**Phase 4: Implement (Weeks 6-8)**
- All campuses adopt standard processes simultaneously
- Training on new procedures
- Support during transition

**Phase 5: Monitor and Adjust (Ongoing)**
- Regular audits to check compliance
- Quarterly reviews to identify problems
- Annual review for continuous improvement

### Benefits

- **Quality consistency:** All campuses maintain similar standards
- **Operational efficiency:** Standardized processes reduce duplicate work
- **Scalability:** New campus can adopt proven processes from day 1
- **Parent satisfaction:** Consistent experience across all campuses
- **Leadership efficiency:** Group leaders focus on strategy; operational standards are enforced by system

## FAQ

**Q: Don't standards reduce campus autonomy?**
A: No. They establish guardrails for quality and consistency, but campuses still have autonomy in execution and local decisions.

**Q: What if a campus has a better process than the standard?**
A: Adopt it as the new standard if it's better. Continuous improvement is built in.

**Q: How do we handle campuses that resist standardization?**
A: Educate on benefits, involve them in design, support during transition. Resistance typically fades once people experience efficiency gains.
