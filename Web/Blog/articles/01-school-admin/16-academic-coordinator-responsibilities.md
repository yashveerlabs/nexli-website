## Executive Summary

An academic coordinator bridges teaching and administration. They track curriculum progress, monitor student performance, identify at-risk students, and support teachers. This article defines their role, daily responsibilities, skills needed, and KPIs to measure success.

---

## Role Overview

### Purpose

Ensure academic excellence through systematic monitoring: attendance, curriculum coverage, student performance, and intervention.

### Reporting To

VP of Academics (or directly to Principal in smaller schools)

### Team Reporting

None (coordinating role); works across HODs and teachers

### Key Stakeholders

- VP/Principal (reports to)
- HODs (coordinates with)
- Teachers (provides support to)
- Students (monitors)
- Parents (communicates with, if needed)

---

## Daily Responsibilities

### Morning (8:00–9:00 AM)

**1. Review Attendance**
- Check attendance system for previous day
- Identify students <75% attendance
- Flag patterns (chronically absent, suddenly absent)
- Prepare follow-up list for parent contact

**2. Academic Check-In**
- Scan overnight emails from teachers/HODs
- Note any issues (homework not submitted, class disruption, etc.)
- Prepare brief update for Principal

**3. Curriculum Progress**
- Check if timetable was followed yesterday
- Any missed classes or substitutions?
- Note for follow-up with HOD

### Throughout the Day

**4. Attendance Follow-Up**
- Contact parents of absent students (>1 day in last week)
- Understand reason (medical? other?)
- If chronic, document for intervention planning

**5. Teacher Support**
- Be available for teacher questions
- Support with marking, lesson planning issues
- Substitute for teacher absence (if needed)

**6. Student Support**
- Observe classroom dynamics
- Identify struggling students
- Arrange peer support or extra help

### End of Day

**7. Documentation**
- Log attendance issues, follow-ups
- Update at-risk student tracker
- Document curriculum progress
- Note items for HOD meeting

## Weekly Responsibilities

### Weekly Meeting with VP/Principal (1 hour)

**Topics:**
- Attendance trends (which classes are problematic?)
- At-risk students (who needs intervention?)
- Curriculum progress (are we on pace?)
- Teacher concerns or issues

### Weekly Attendance Analysis

- Calculate 7-day attendance by class
- Identify sections trending down
- Plan interventions for chronic absentees

### Weekly HOD Coordination (30 min–1 hour)

**Agenda:**
- Curriculum progress by subject
- Student performance (marks, assessments)
- Any teaching quality concerns
- Professional development needs

### Homework & Assignment Tracking

- Check submission rates by class
- Follow up on incomplete assignments
- Share summary with Principal

---

## Monthly Responsibilities

### Attendance Report

- Month-to-date attendance by class
- Identify students below 75%
- Send letters to parents of chronic absentees
- Plan intervention: counseling, re-engagement

### Academic Performance Analysis

- Marks from internal assessments
- Identify performing and underperforming students
- Share with parents (if performance is poor)
- Plan intervention: remedial classes, peer tutoring

### Teacher Performance Review (Qualitative)

- Feedback from student engagement observations
- Curriculum coverage progress
- Assessment quality
- Professional behavior
- Provide feedback to teachers (supportive, not evaluative)

### At-Risk Student Planning

- Consolidate attendance + marks + behavior data
- Identify students at risk of failure
- Create intervention plan: tutoring, counseling, parent meeting
- Share with Principal for approval

### Communication with Parents

- Send monthly attendance/performance summary
- Invite parents of underperforming students for discussion
- Plan intervention collaboratively

---

## Quarterly Responsibilities

### Academic Performance Review

- Analyze marks across all assessments (month 1, month 2, month 3)
- Identify trends: improving or declining?
- Subject performance: which subjects need support?
- Prepare summary for Principal

### Curriculum Alignment Review

- Verify curriculum is aligned with board standards
- Check pacing (are we on track to complete by exam)?
- Identify bottlenecks (topics taking too long)
- Plan adjustments if needed

### Professional Development Planning

- Identify teacher development needs (based on observations)
- Plan workshops or training for next quarter
- Source materials or external experts if needed

### Board Exam Preparation (If applicable)

- For Classes 10/12, plan mock exam schedule
- Prepare exam centers and invigilation duty
- Create admit cards
- Monitor exam preparation progress

## Key Skills

### 1. Curriculum Knowledge
- Understand board standards (CBSE, ICSE, State Board)
- Know learning outcomes for each grade
- Understand pacing (how fast should we move?)

### 2. Data Analysis
- Interpret attendance, marks, assessment data
- Identify trends and patterns
- Create visualizations (charts, graphs)

### 3. Communication
- Speak to teachers supportively (not judgmentally)
- Communicate with parents professionally
- Present data clearly to Principal

### 4. Problem-Solving
- Identify root causes (why is Class V attendance low?)
- Develop interventions (action plans)
- Follow through and track results

### 5. Organization
- Track multiple classes and subjects
- Prioritize (which issues are critical?)
- Document everything

### 6. Technology
- Comfortable with spreadsheets, data entry
- Use ERP if available
- Create reports and charts

## Success Metrics

### Attendance

- Target: 90%+ overall school attendance
- Chronic absentees (>15% absence): <5% of students
- Intervention success: 70% of at-risk students improve within 30 days

### Academic Performance

- Target: 70%+ of students score >60% in assessments
- Low-performing students (marks <40%): Identified within 2 weeks of exam
- Intervention uptake: 80% of recommended interventions accepted by parents/students

### Curriculum Coverage

- Target: 95%+ of curriculum covered by end of year
- Pacing: On track (not behind schedule by >10%)
- Assessments aligned with curriculum: 100%

### Teacher Satisfaction

- Survey: Do teachers feel supported? (Target: >75% agree)
- Attendance at PD: 90%+ attendance at coordinator-led training
- Issue resolution: Average issue resolved within 5 days

## Common Challenges

### Challenge 1: Low Attendance

**Root causes:** Sickness, transportation, family situation, lack of engagement  
**Solutions:** Parent communication, counseling, scholarship/support, engagement activities

### Challenge 2: Poor Marks

**Root causes:** Weak foundation, learning difficulties, insufficient practice, poor teaching  
**Solutions:** Tutoring, learning assessment, extra practice, teacher support

### Challenge 3: Teacher Accountability

**Root causes:** Unclear expectations, lack of support, performance issues  
**Solutions:** Clear communication, training, mentoring, performance discussion

## FAQ

**Q: How many students can one coordinator manage?**
A: 300–500 students. Beyond that, add a second coordinator.

**Q: Should coordinators teach classes?**
A: Ideally no. Coordination requires focus. If needed (small school), cap at 1–2 classes and reduce coordination load.

**Q: How do coordinators keep teachers from feeling judged?**
A: Frame as support ("I'm here to help"), not inspection. Praise good practices. Address issues privately and constructively.

**Q: What if a teacher doesn't cooperate?**
A: Escalate to VP/Principal. Coordinator shouldn't handle performance issues alone.

**Q: How much time should coordinators spend on intervention?**
A: 30% of their time. 70% should be monitoring and prevention.
