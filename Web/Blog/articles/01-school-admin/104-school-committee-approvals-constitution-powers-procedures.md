## Executive Summary

School committee approvals are the backbone of institutional governance. A well-constituted committee with clear powers and documented procedures ensures transparent decision-making, prevents misuse of authority, and builds stakeholder trust. Schools with formally structured committees report 35% fewer governance disputes and 50% better fee collection. This article outlines committee types, authority limits, and best-practice procedures for functioning.

---

## What is a School Committee?

A school committee is a governing body with defined members, specific authorities, and documented procedures for making institutional decisions.

**Core functions:**
- Oversee school operations and financial management
- Approve budgets, fee structures, and major expenditures
- Review and approve policies
- Evaluate principal and staff performance
- Ensure compliance with regulations
- Bridge school and community (parents, local leaders, etc.)

---

## Types of School Committees

### 1. School Management Committee (SMC)

**Mandated by:** RTE Act 2009 (mandatory in all government and aided schools; recommended for private schools)

**Composition (as per RTE guidelines):**
- Principal (chair or member)
- Teachers (at least 2; 1/3 of total members)
- Parents (proportional to student diversity)
- Local leaders (ward counselor, local authority rep)
- Persons with expertise (education, finance, social welfare)
- At least 50% representation from disadvantaged groups (SC/ST, women, minorities)

**Powers and duties:**
- Approve school development plan
- Monitor academic performance and attendance
- Oversee timely receipt of textbooks and learning materials
- Approve fee (in private schools)
- Monitor use of government grants (in government schools)
- Review grievances and complaints
- Report to parents on school performance

**Quorum:** Majority of members present

**Frequency:** Meetings at least once per quarter; more if needed

### 2. Finance Committee

**Mandated by:** School governance standards (required in most schools)

**Composition:**
- Principal or nominee
- Finance coordinator/manager
- VP or academic head
- 1-2 parents (if SMC exists, can be SMC parent members)
- External auditor (invited, non-voting)

**Powers and duties:**
- Prepare annual budget
- Approve expenditures within limits
- Monitor income and expenses
- Authorize payment of bills and salaries
- Approve purchase orders and vendor selection
- Review financial reports monthly
- Manage grants and donations

**Authority limits:**
- Finance Chair/Principal: Approve expenses under Rs. 50,000
- Finance Committee: Approve expenses Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 5 lakhs
- Full SMC: Approve expenses above Rs. 5 lakhs

**Quorum:** 3 members

**Frequency:** Monthly meetings

### 3. Academic Performance Committee

**Mandated by:** School quality standards (not statutory but best-practice)

**Composition:**
- Principal
- VP or academic head
- Subject experts (HODs)
- 2-3 teachers
- 1-2 parents interested in academics
- Counselor/welfare officer

**Powers and duties:**
- Review student performance data
- Identify at-risk students and intervention programs
- Approve curriculum and lesson plans (at strategic level)
- Monitor teaching quality and teacher development
- Recommend promotions, detentions, and special support
- Review exam results and post-mortem analysis
- Approve enrichment programs (advanced learners)

**Authority limits:**
- Can recommend but not unilaterally decide student promotions
- Can propose curriculum changes but SMC approves
- Can approve teacher professional development budget under Rs. 10K per teacher/year

**Quorum:** 5 members

**Frequency:** Once per term (at minimum after exams)

### 4. Discipline Committee

**Mandated by:** Schools with formal discipline codes (CBSE/ICSE schools must have one)

**Composition:**
- VP or senior teacher (chair)
- 2-3 senior teachers
- Counselor or welfare officer
- 1 parent representative

**Powers and duties:**
- Review alleged student misconduct
- Conduct fair disciplinary hearings
- Recommend minor penalties (warning, detention)
- Forward serious cases (bullying, violence) to Full Committee for suspension/expulsion
- Monitor repeat offenders
- Ensure procedural fairness (student and parent have right to speak)
- Document all decisions

**Authority limits:**
- Can issue warning or detention
- Can recommend suspension (Full SMC approves)
- Cannot award corporal punishment
- Serious cases (violence, POCSO violations) escalated to Principal + SMC

**Quorum:** 3 members

**Frequency:** As needed (typically weekly during school year)

### 5. Staff Relations / HR Committee

**Mandated by:** Schools with over 50 staff (best-practice for all schools)

**Composition:**
- Principal or nominee
- HR head or VP
- 1-2 senior teachers
- 1 parent representative
- External HR consultant (optional)

**Powers and duties:**
- Approve recruitment and hiring
- Review staff performance and conduct issues
- Approve staff transfers and promotions
- Resolve staff grievances (before escalation)
- Ensure compliance with labor laws
- Approve professional development and training
- Review salary and benefits policies

**Authority limits:**
- Can hire/fire staff per delegation
- Can approve salary increments within budget
- Disputes escalated to Principal or Board

**Quorum:** 3 members

**Frequency:** Monthly or as needed

### 6. Special Committees (Ad-Hoc)

Schools may form temporary committees for specific tasks:
- **Admissions Committee:** Review applications, conduct interviews, decide admissions
- **Building & Infrastructure Committee:** Oversee construction, renovations, maintenance
- **Parent-Teacher Association (PTA):** Organized parent engagement and fundraising
- **Exam Committee:** Coordinate examinations, invigilation, result processing

## Constitutional Powers and Authority Limits

### Decision-Making Authority Matrix

| Decision Type | Authority | Limit | Appeal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Academic** | Academic Performance Committee | Curriculum changes, exam schedules | Principal review |
| **Discipline** | Discipline Committee | Warnings, detention | Full SMC, then Board |
| **Suspension/Expulsion** | Full SMC + Board | Serious misconduct | Board, then Civil court |
| **Fee Approval** | SMC | Structure, increases | Board |
| **Budget under Rs. 50K** | Finance Manager | Routine expenses | Finance Committee |
| **Budget Rs. 50K-5L** | Finance Committee | Major purchases | SMC |
| **Budget above Rs. 5L** | Full SMC | Capital expenditure | Board |
| **Staff Hiring** | Principal + HR Committee | Within approved positions | Board if disputes |
| **Staff Discipline** | VP + HR Committee | Warning, suspension | Principal, then SMC |
| **Policy Changes** | Full SMC | School operations policies | Board |

### What Committees CANNOT Do

- **Financial:** Committee members cannot have vendors, contracts, or suppliers (conflict of interest)
- **Favoritism:** Cannot approve exceptions without documented reason
- **Unilateral decisions:** Must follow defined process (proposal, discussion, vote, documentation)
- **Bypass compliance:** Cannot approve decisions that violate laws (RTE, DPDP, POCSO, etc.)
- **Retaliation:** Cannot take action against whistleblowers or complaint filers

## Committee Procedures: Best Practices

### 1. **Meeting Call and Agenda**

**Rule:** Call meetings with at least 7 days notice (5 days for urgent meetings).

**Agenda must include:**
- Date, time, location
- List of items to be discussed
- Required documents (e.g., financial reports, policy drafts)
- Supporting materials (e.g., budget estimates, performance data)

**Send to:** All members + one external copy (for audit trail)

### 2. **Quorum and Attendance**

**Quorum definition:** Majority of members present (e.g., if committee has 7 members, 4 must attend).

**If quorum not met:** Meeting is postponed, not held.

**Attendance tracking:** Maintain attendance log showing:
- Date, time, members present, members absent
- Reason for absences (excused/unexcused)
- Decision if members habitually absent (replacement or special procedure)

### 3. **Voting and Decision-Making**

**Voting rule:** Majority vote wins. Tied votes are typically escalated to Principal or chair's tiebreaker (define in bylaws).

**Documentation:**
- Record each member's vote (name, vote: yes/no/abstain)
- If abstaining, record reason (e.g., "conflict of interest")
- Minority opinion can be noted (e.g., "2 members objected to fee increase; noted in minutes")

### 4. **Minutes and Documentation**

**Minutes must record:**
- Date, time, location, members present/absent
- Agenda items discussed
- Key points raised (for/against)
- Decisions made and voting outcome
- Action items with owner and deadline

**Format:** Standard template (use the same format for all meetings for easy comparison)

**Distribution:** Minutes circulated within 7 days; approved in next meeting

**Retention:** Store permanently (keep indefinitely for financial/compliance decisions)

### 5. **Grievance and Appeal Process**

**Grievance:** If a person disagrees with a committee decision, they can file a grievance.

**Process:**
1. Submitter writes grievance with specific reasons (e.g., "Fee increase violates RTE criteria")
2. Grievance reviewed by Principal or next-level committee
3. Grievance committee meets within 2 weeks
4. Decision communicated within 7 days
5. If still unresolved, escalate to Board

**Frivolous grievances:** A frivolous grievance (e.g., "I don't like the decision") can be dismissed. Document reason.

### 6. **Conflict of Interest Management**

**When a committee member has conflict:**
- Member discloses it (e.g., "My vendor is bidding for this contract")
- Member recuses (leaves the room during discussion and voting)
- Meeting continues without that member

**Recusal is mandatory if:**
- Member has direct financial interest
- Member's family member is affected
- Member previously promised a specific vote
- Member is involved in the dispute being decided

### 7. **Transparency and Communication**

**Public access:**
- Committee decisions (not detailed minutes) are shared with school community
- Annual performance report summarized and shared with parents
- Financial decisions (budget, fee structure, major expenses) posted on notice board or school app

**Confidentiality exceptions:**
- Personnel decisions (hiring, salary, discipline) remain confidential
- Student discipline cases remain confidential
- Medical/counselling information is confidential

### 8. **Annual Review and Reporting**

**End of year:**
- Review committee performance (Did we meet quorum? Did we decide issues fairly? Were SLAs met?)
- Prepare annual report (decisions made, budget spent, issues resolved, pending items)
- Share annual report with parents and stakeholders
- Identify process improvements (e.g., "We need faster budget approval process")
- Reconstitute committee if needed (some members' terms end, new members join)

## Building Strong Committees

### Recruitment of Committee Members

- **Teachers:** 1-2 senior, experienced teachers (not all newest staff)
- **Parents:** Mix of engaged parents + representation from underprivileged families (RTE requirement)
- **External expertise:** Accountant, lawyer, or education expert (invited as needed)
- **Avoid:** Single parent dominance, all alumni parents, or only wealthy parents

### Orientation and Training

New committee members should receive:
- School background and mission
- Committee powers and limitations
- Governance policies and bylaws
- Previous minutes and decisions (to understand history)
- Training on duties (fiduciary responsibility, confidentiality, etc.)

### Term Limits

- **Teachers:** 2-year term (can be re-elected)
- **Parents:** 1-2 year terms; rotate annually to ensure fresh perspectives
- **Experts:** 1-year terms (invitees as needed)

### Conflict Resolution

If committee members disagree on major issue:
- Facilitate discussion (hear all viewpoints)
- Vote (majority decides)
- Document minority opinion
- Move forward unified (even if some members disagreed)

## FAQ: Committee Governance Questions

**Q: How many committees does a school need?**
A: Minimum 2 (SMC + Finance). Add Discipline, Academic, and HR committees based on school size and complexity. Smaller schools can combine committees.

**Q: Can the principal veto a committee decision?**
A: It depends on the decision type and school bylaws. For academic/discipline, Principal can usually escalate to Board if they disagree. For finance, Principal often must approve. Define this clearly in bylaws.

**Q: What if a committee member misses 3 meetings in a row?**
A: Address it directly. Confirm they can continue. If no, replace. Poor attendance equals poor governance.

**Q: Can committee members be paid?**
A: Typically no (they're volunteers). External experts might charge consultation fees (approved by Finance Committee). Teachers on committee are paid salary (not extra).

**Q: How long should committee meetings be?**
A: 90 min for Finance Committee, 2 hours for SMC, 60 min for others. Longer meetings indicate poor preparation or scope creep.
