## Executive Summary

FSSAI license for school canteens is mandatory under the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006. Every school that serves food to students, whether in-house or through a catering arrangement, must obtain and maintain this license. Schools with FSSAI compliance and food safety programs report zero foodborne illness incidents and consistent inspection approval. This article walks through the licensing process, required documentation, and ongoing maintenance requirements.

---

## What is FSSAI License?

FSSAI License is official authorization to operate a food service business (like a school canteen). It certifies the food operation meets food safety and hygiene standards.

**Who needs it:**
- Every school canteen serving food to students
- Hostels providing meals
- School kitchens preparing food

**Why it's required:**
- Legal requirement under Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006
- Protects student health and safety
- Demonstrates school's commitment to hygiene
- Required for compliance with CBSE/board regulations

**Who issues it:**
- FSSAI (Central) or State Health Department (delegates authority to districts)
- Typically processed at district/municipal level

## FSSAI License Types

### 1. Basic License (FSSAI Basic)

**Who needs it:**
- Small food operations
- School canteens with under 10 staff
- Single-location operations

**Eligibility:**
- Annual food production value under Rs. 5 lakhs
- No chemical/additive processing

**Validity:** 5 years

**Cost:** Rs. 500-Rs. 1,500

**Processing time:** 10-15 days

### 2. Central License (FSSAI Central)

**Who needs it:**
- Large food operations
- Multi-location school chains
- Food production/processing units

**Eligibility:**
- Annual food production value above Rs. 5 lakhs
- Import/export of food
- Multi-state operations

**Validity:** 5 years

**Cost:** Rs. 2,000-Rs. 5,000

**Processing time:** 20-30 days

## Step-by-Step: Obtaining FSSAI License

### Step 1: Pre-Application Preparation (2-3 weeks before)

**Actions:**

1. **Assess your canteen:**
   - Food production estimate (annual value)
   - Staff strength
   - Type of foods prepared (vegetarian, non-veg, processed, etc.)
   - Determine: Basic or Central license

2. **Prepare kitchen/canteen:**
   - Ensure food storage is clean and organized
   - Install handwashing station
   - Organize utensils and equipment
   - Remove any pest or contamination
   - Install first-aid kit

3. **Gather required documents:**
   - School registration certificate / affiliation document
   - Proof of premises (lease or ownership)
   - Building plan (canteen area marked)
   - Electricity bill (proof of supply)
   - Water connection certificate
   - Pan card (if applicable)
   - Photo ID of school administrator

4. **Complete food handler training:**
   - Principal, canteen manager, and staff must complete FSSAI-recognized food handler training (online, typically 20-30 minutes)
   - Obtain training certificate
   - Training available at: https://www.foodsafetytraining.fssai.gov.in

### Step 2: Online Application (FSSAI Portal)

**Process:**

1. Visit: https://license.fssai.gov.in

2. Click "Registration" (for new license) or "License" (for upgrade)

3. Fill application form:
   - Business name (School name)
   - Address of food operation (canteen location)
   - Type of food (veg/non-veg/both)
   - Food production estimate (annual value)
   - Staff details
   - Contact information

4. Upload documents:
   - Photo ID
   - Proof of premises
   - Food handler training certificate
   - Building plan
   - Electricity bill

5. Review and submit application

6. Pay application fee online (Rs. 500-Rs. 1,500 for Basic, Rs. 2,000+ for Central)

7. Receive application reference number (track online)

**Processing status:** Check online using reference number

### Step 3: FSSAI Inspection (10-20 days after application)

**Inspector visits canteen to verify:**

**Food Storage:**
- Is raw food stored separately from cooked?
- Are perishables refrigerated?
- Are chemicals stored away from food?
- Is storage clean and rodent-proof?

**Preparation Area:**
- Is kitchen clean and organized?
- Are utensils cleaned and stored properly?
- Is water supply clean and potable?
- Are staff following hygiene practices?

**Equipment:**
- Are cooking appliances functional?
- Is refrigerator working and clean?
- Are stoves/grills maintained?

**Staff Hygiene:**
- Do staff wear aprons, caps, gloves?
- Are staff trained in food safety?
- Do staff understand handwashing?

**Waste Management:**
- Is waste disposed properly?
- Are garbage bins covered?
- Is drain system clean?

**Documentation:**
- Do you have records of food sources (vendors)?
- Do you have cleaning and maintenance logs?
- Do you have staff training certificates?

### Step 4: Follow-Up (If Issues Noted)

**If inspector finds issues, they'll note:**
- Minor issues: Document in inspection report; can be addressed after license issuance
- Major issues: Must be fixed before license issuance (inspector revisits to confirm)

**Common issues noted:**
- Cleanliness (fix immediately)
- Improper storage (reorganize)
- Staff uniforms (provide aprons, caps)
- Pest control (hire pest control service)

**Timeline if fixes needed:** 5-10 days

### Step 5: License Issuance

**After inspection approved:**
- FSSAI issues official license (typically within 5-7 days)
- License is sent digitally + physical copy
- License is valid for 5 years

**What you receive:**
- Official FSSAI License Certificate
- License number (unique ID)
- Validity period (5 years)
- List of conditions/food items covered

### Step 6: License Renewal (Before Expiry)

**Timeline:**
- Apply for renewal 3 months before expiry
- Submit same application + documents
- Inspection again (similar to new license)
- Renewal process: 15-30 days
- Renewed license issued

## Food Safety Compliance After Licensing

### Daily Practices

- **Staff hygiene:** Handwashing before handling food, wearing aprons/caps
- **Food storage:** Raw and cooked foods separated, perishables refrigerated
- **Cleanliness:** Utensils washed, kitchen cleaned after each meal
- **Temperature monitoring:** Record refrigerator temperature daily (should be under 4°C)

### Weekly Practices

- **Deep cleaning:** Thorough cleaning of all surfaces, equipment
- **Equipment maintenance:** Check stoves, refrigerator, grills for proper function
- **Pest control:** Check for pests; schedule pest control if needed
- **Vendor verification:** Verify food sources are safe/certified

### Monthly Practices

- **Training:** Brief staff on food safety (15-30 minutes)
- **Record review:** Check cleaning logs, maintenance records
- **Supplier audit:** Verify vendors continue to meet standards
- **Health checkup:** Staff health screening (no infections)

### Annual Practices

- **License renewal:** Apply 3 months before expiry
- **Training update:** Staff recertification if needed
- **Equipment servicing:** Annual inspection of major equipment
- **Inspection prep:** Prepare documentation for renewal inspection

## Common Violations and Remediation

| Violation | How to Fix |
|---|---|
| **Dirty/cluttered kitchen** | Deep clean, organize utensils, remove clutter |
| **Raw and cooked food mixed** | Install separate storage areas or purchase additional containers |
| **Staff not trained** | Conduct FSSAI food handler training immediately |
| **No handwashing station** | Install handwashing sink with soap and water |
| **Foodborne illness reported** | Investigate incident, enhance cleaning, report to FSSAI |
| **Expired food items** | Discard immediately, implement expiry check system |
| **Pest infestation** | Hire pest control, seal entry points, install screens |
| **No temperature monitoring** | Purchase thermometer, document daily readings |

## FAQ: FSSAI License Questions

**Q: How much does FSSAI license cost?**
A: Rs. 500-Rs. 1,500 for Basic license, Rs. 2,000-Rs. 5,000 for Central license. One-time payment for 5-year validity.

**Q: Do all canteen staff need food handler training?**
A: At minimum, manager and cooks must be trained. Support staff should also be trained. Training is quick (20-30 min online).

**Q: What if canteen closes for summer vacation?**
A: License validity doesn't stop. If reopening after closure, ensure kitchen is cleaned and sanitized before reopening.

**Q: Can a school without a canteen skip FSSAI?**
A: If school provides meals (even from outside), school needs FSSAI license for the food operation (whether in-house or catered).

**Q: What if FSSAI finds violations during inspection?**
A: Minor issues can be fixed after license issuance. Major issues must be fixed before inspection approval. No license is issued until major issues are resolved.
