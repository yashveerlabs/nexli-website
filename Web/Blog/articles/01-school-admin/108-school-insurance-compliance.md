## Executive Summary

School insurance compliance is a critical requirement and risk management tool. Schools with adequate insurance coverage and updated policies avoid financial exposure from claims and report faster claim settlements. This article outlines the insurance types every Indian school needs, coverage requirements, and procedures for maintaining compliance year-round.

## Types of School Insurance

### 1. General Liability Insurance

**What it covers:** Injuries to students, staff, or visitors due to school negligence

**Coverage includes:**
- Medical expenses for injuries on school campus
- Legal defense if lawsuit filed
- Compensation for permanent disability
- Property damage caused by school

**Annual cost:** Rs. 1,000-Rs. 5,000 depending on school size

**Mandatory:** Yes (in most states)

### 2. Property Insurance

**What it covers:** Damage to school buildings, equipment, and assets

**Coverage includes:**
- Fire, theft, vandalism, natural disasters
- Building damage
- Equipment and furnishings replacement
- Loss of income (if school can't operate due to damage)

**Annual cost:** 0.5-1.5% of building/asset value

**Mandatory:** Yes (required by banks if school has loan)

### 3. Employer's Liability / Workers Compensation

**What it covers:** Injuries or illness to staff members

**Coverage includes:**
- Medical treatment
- Wage compensation during recovery
- Disability benefits
- Death benefits (for staff fatalities)

**Annual cost:** Rs. 2,000-Rs. 10,000 depending on staff strength

**Mandatory:** Yes (for all staff)

### 4. Professional Indemnity / Errors & Omissions

**What it covers:** Claims of professional negligence in teaching/counseling

**Coverage includes:**
- Allegations of improper counseling leading to harm
- Academic assessment disputes
- Career guidance leading to poor outcomes
- Sexual misconduct claims (defense costs, not payments)

**Annual cost:** Rs. 3,000-Rs. 8,000

**Mandatory:** Recommended (not legally required but increasingly important)

### 5. Abuse and Molestation Insurance (AMI)

**What it covers:** Allegations of child abuse by staff or third parties

**Coverage includes:**
- Investigation costs
- Legal defense
- Civil settlements (in some policies)
- Psychological counseling for victims

**Annual cost:** Rs. 5,000-Rs. 15,000

**Mandatory:** Recommended (increasingly required by boards)

### 6. School Bus / Vehicle Insurance

**What it covers:** Accidents involving school transport

**Coverage includes:**
- Third-party liability (injuries to other road users)
- Comprehensive (damage to bus itself)
- Passenger protection (injuries to students)
- Driver protection

**Annual cost:** Rs. 10,000-Rs. 30,000 per vehicle

**Mandatory:** Yes (legal requirement for vehicles on road)

### 7. Cyber Insurance

**What it covers:** Losses from data breaches, ransomware, cyber attacks

**Coverage includes:**
- Notification costs (inform affected parties)
- Credit monitoring services
- Forensic investigation
- Regulatory fines (DPDP Act violations)
- Business interruption

**Annual cost:** Rs. 5,000-Rs. 20,000

**Mandatory:** Recommended (DPDP Act compliance)

## Insurance Renewal Timeline

### 90 Days Before Expiry: Planning

**Actions:**
- Check all insurance policy expiry dates
- Create renewal calendar (alert 60 days before)
- Review coverage adequacy (are limits sufficient?)
- Collect quotes from 2-3 insurance providers
- Compare coverage and premium

### 60 Days Before Expiry: Quote Comparison

**Request quotes from insurance brokers/companies:**
- Provide: School details, enrollment, staff strength, assets value
- Ask for: Detailed quote with coverage details, exclusions, claim process

**Comparison checklist:**
| Factor | Broker A | Broker B | Broker C |
|---|---|---|---|
| Premium | | | |
| Liability limit | | | |
| Deductible | | | |
| Claim settlement time | | | |
| Claims processing | | | |
| Additional benefits | | | |

### 30 Days Before Expiry: Policy Selection

**Choose insurance provider based on:**
- Premium cost (not the only factor)
- Adequate coverage limits
- Good claims history (ask for references)
- Fast claim settlement (target: under 30 days)
- Support and customer service

### 15 Days Before Expiry: Application & Renewal

**Submit renewal application:**
- Sign policy documents
- Confirm all details are correct
- Provide updated information (if any changes: building size, staff, vehicles, etc.)
- Pay premium (typically 1-2 weeks before policy start date)

### On or Before Expiry Date: Policy Activation

**New policy becomes active:**
- Receive policy document and certificate
- Store copies (principal's office + digital)
- Share certificate with staff
- Post certificate on notice board
- Update school records

## Claims Process

### When to File a Claim

**Examples:**
- Student injured at school (falls, sports injury, etc.)
- Staff member injured or ill (workplace injury)
- Property damaged (fire, theft, weather)
- Cyber attack or data breach
- Allegation of abuse or misconduct

### Step-by-Step Claim Filing

**1. Immediate (within 24 hours):**
- Report incident to insurance company (phone call to claims line)
- Provide: Policy number, incident date/time, location, people involved
- Document everything (photos, witness statements, medical reports)

**2. Within 3 days:**
- Submit formal claim form (provided by insurance company)
- Include: Incident report, supporting documents (medical bills, police report, etc.)
- Keep copies for school records

**3. Within 5-10 days:**
- Insurance company assigns adjuster to investigate
- Adjuster may visit school, interview people, review documents

**4. Within 2-4 weeks:**
- Adjuster submits report and recommendation to insurance company
- Insurance company makes decision: Approved, Denied, or Partial approval

**5. Claim settlement:**
- Payment issued (typically within 30 days of approval)
- School files receipt/proof of expenses
- Claim closed

## Documentation & Compliance

### Essential Documents to Keep

**Policy documents:**
- Original policy papers
- Policy certificate
- Endorsements (coverage changes)
- Schedule of coverage (itemized list of covered items)

**Claim records:**
- All submitted claims (copies)
- Adjuster reports
- Settlement letters
- Payments received

**Incident reports:**
- Injury/incident documentation
- Police reports (if applicable)
- Medical reports
- Witness statements

**Renewal records:**
- Previous year's policies (last 3 years)
- Premium receipts
- Quote comparisons
- Payment records

### Storage & Accessibility

**Physical storage:**
- Original policies in principal's office (secure file)
- Easy access in case of incident or audit

**Digital storage:**
- Scan all policies and documents
- Upload to school system (Nexli, Google Drive, etc.)
- Backup copies in 2 locations

**Accessibility:**
- Principal knows location of all insurance docs
- Finance manager has copies
- VP knows basic coverage details
- Staff can be shown certificate if asked

## FAQ: School Insurance Questions

**Q: What's the minimum insurance a school must have?**
A: At minimum: General Liability + Property + Vehicle Insurance. Add others based on risk profile and board requirements.

**Q: If insurance lapses for 1 day, are we covered?**
A: No. Insurance only covers during active policy period. If incident occurs during lapse, school is liable. Avoid lapses by tracking renewal dates.

**Q: Can we use insurance proceeds to cover operational costs?**
A: Typically, insurance covers specific insured losses (damage, injury, liability). Using proceeds for general operations is not the intent. Check policy terms.

**Q: What if a student is injured but parents don't file a claim?**
A: School should still notify insurance company within 30 days of awareness. Delaying notification may void coverage.
