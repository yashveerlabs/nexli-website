Each member of the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) has specific functions under the POSH Act 2013. Understanding these roles before a complaint is filed prevents confusion and delays when the ICC is actually needed.

## Presiding Officer

The Presiding Officer is the most senior ICC member and chairs the committee. She:

- Receives the formal complaint from the complainant
- Opens and assigns a case number to the complaint
- Convenes ICC meetings and moderates inquiry proceedings
- Notifies the respondent (accused) of the complaint and their right to respond
- Chairs interviews of the complainant, respondent, and witnesses
- Prepares the inquiry report with findings and recommendations
- Signs and submits the final report to school management
- Submits the annual POSH report to the District Officer

The Presiding Officer must be impartial. If she has a personal relationship with the complainant or respondent, she must recuse herself and the most senior remaining member takes her role.

## Employee Members

Employee members (minimum two; at least one must be a woman) support the inquiry:

- Participate in all ICC meetings and proceedings
- Assist in interviewing witnesses
- Review evidence submitted by the complainant and respondent
- Deliberate on findings and contribute to the inquiry report
- Support the Presiding Officer in maintaining a fair, orderly process
- Maintain confidentiality of all proceedings

Employee members bring organizational knowledge: they understand the school's culture, hierarchy, and context in ways an external member may not.

## External Member

The external member is independent of the school. This independence is the external member's primary value:

- Provides an outside perspective on the complaint and evidence
- Ensures the ICC is not captured by organizational dynamics (e.g., pressure to protect management)
- Contributes expertise in women's rights, legal procedure, or social work
- Signs the inquiry report as a co-member
- Can raise concerns if the process is not being followed correctly

The external member must be actively involved in proceedings, not a nominal signatory. An ICC that treats the external member as a formality undermines the independence the role is designed to provide.

## What All Members Must Maintain

**Confidentiality:** All members are prohibited from disclosing the complaint contents, the identity of parties, or the inquiry proceedings to anyone outside the ICC.

**Impartiality:** Members must not have pre-existing bias toward either party. They must recuse themselves from any case where impartiality is in question.

**Timeliness:** The ICC has 90 days to complete its inquiry from the date of complaint. All members are responsible for attending scheduled meetings and responding to materials within agreed timelines.

## Interim Measures

While the inquiry is ongoing, the ICC can recommend interim measures to the employer:

- Transfer of the complainant or respondent to a different section or branch
- Grant of leave to the complainant (if needed for their well-being)
- Restraining the respondent from reporting on the complainant's work

Interim measures protect the complainant without prejudging the outcome.

## FAQ

**Q: Can an ICC member be from a different campus of the same school?**

A: Yes. For multi-campus schools, ICC members can be drawn from different campuses to ensure sufficient diversity and to reduce conflicts of interest.

**Q: What if an ICC member is related to the complainant or respondent?**

A: That member must recuse themselves from the case. The school management may need to temporarily appoint a replacement for that specific case.

**Q: Does the external member need to physically attend all ICC meetings?**

A: The external member should participate in key proceedings, particularly interviews of parties and evidence review. Video participation may be acceptable for some meetings, but the external member's active involvement is required, not token participation.

**Q: What training do ICC members need?**

A: All ICC members should be trained on POSH Act provisions, investigation procedures, interviewing techniques, and confidentiality requirements. Training should occur when first appointed and refreshed annually.
