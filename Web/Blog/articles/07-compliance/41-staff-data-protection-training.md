Every school staff member who accesses student personal data has obligations under the DPDP Act 2023. Training is not optional: staff who handle data without understanding the rules create compliance risk for the school. A data breach caused by an untrained staff member is the school's legal exposure, not just an individual mistake.

Training must be practical, role-specific, and repeated at least annually.

## Who Needs Training

**All staff with any access to student data:**
- Teachers (academic records, attendance, grades)
- Counselors (medical and psychological records)
- Administrative staff (enrollment, fees, ID records)
- Finance staff (fee payment data)
- IT staff (system access and security)
- Security/reception staff (visitor logs, ID verification)
- Transport coordinators (student transport records)
- Hostel wardens (boarding student records)

## Core Training Modules

### Module 1: What Is Personal Data

Staff must understand what counts as personal data under DPDP:
- Name, address, date of birth, phone number
- Academic records, grades, attendance
- Medical and health information
- Photographs, videos, biometric data
- Financial information (fee records)
- Behavioral or disciplinary records

Sensitive data (medical, biometric) requires extra care and higher-level access controls.

### Module 2: Consent Requirements

Staff must understand:
- The school can only collect data parents have consented to
- Consent must be specific (separate consent for each data category)
- Verbal consent is not sufficient; written or OTP-verified digital consent is required
- Staff cannot collect new categories of student data without first obtaining consent
- If a parent withdraws consent, staff must stop using that data immediately

### Module 3: Need-to-Know Access

Staff must understand:
- They should only access student data relevant to their role
- A teacher's access to medical records should be limited to what is necessary for classroom safety
- Staff must not share student data with colleagues who have no need for it
- Data must not be copied to personal devices (personal laptops, phones, WhatsApp)
- Email is not a secure channel for sensitive data (use the school's ERP messaging system)

### Module 4: Parent Data Requests

Staff must know what to do when a parent asks about their child's data:
- Direct the parent to the designated data request process (usually the principal or DPO)
- Do not provide data verbally without proper verification
- Do not modify records based on verbal requests without proper documentation
- Do not delete records on request without checking legal retention requirements

### Module 5: Breach Reporting

Staff must know what a data breach looks like and what to do:
- Examples: laptop with student data lost or stolen, unauthorized access to ERP, accidental email to wrong parent, system hack
- Action: report immediately to the principal or DPO; do not attempt to fix it quietly
- Timeline: the school has 72 hours to notify the Data Protection Board after becoming aware of a reportable breach
- Staff delay in reporting can mean the school misses this 72-hour window

### Module 6: Third-Party Sharing

Staff must understand:
- Student data can only be shared with third parties that have a data processing agreement with the school
- Common third parties: government portals (UDISE+, CBSE), transport vendors, health providers
- Staff must not share student data with parents of other students, media, or any unauthorized party
- Photos of students posted to social media by staff require proper parental consent

## Training Delivery

**Format:** In-person sessions with Q&A are most effective. E-learning modules can supplement but should not replace live training.

**Frequency:** Annual mandatory training for all staff. Additional training when there is a regulatory change, a new system is introduced, or a breach occurs.

**Duration:** 2-3 hours for comprehensive initial training; 1 hour for annual refresher.

**Assessment:** A short quiz at the end of training verifies understanding. Staff who score below 70% should repeat the training.

**Records:** Maintain training attendance records with dates, topics covered, and assessment scores.

## FAQ

**Q: Is data protection training legally mandatory for school staff?**

A: The DPDP Act 2023 places obligations on data fiduciaries (schools) to implement appropriate safeguards. Staff training is a recognized safeguard. While specific training mandates will be in implementing rules, untrained staff creating a breach would reflect on the school's due diligence.

**Q: How should schools train contract staff (security guards, canteen staff) who may see student information?**

A: Contract staff who have any access to student data should receive a shorter, role-focused briefing covering: what they should not share, who to report concerns to, and what counts as a security incident.

**Q: What if a teacher accidentally sends student data to the wrong parent?**

A: This is a reportable breach. The teacher must immediately inform the principal and DPO. The DPO assesses whether notification to the Data Protection Board is required (within 72 hours if it is). Training should cover this exact scenario.
