Every school with 10 or more employees must constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) under the POSH Act 2013. The ICC is the body that receives, investigates, and recommends action on workplace sexual harassment complaints. Without a constituted ICC, the school is in non-compliance, even if no complaint has been filed.

Forming the ICC is a straightforward process, but it must be done correctly or the committee's decisions can be challenged.

## ICC Composition Requirements

The POSH Act specifies mandatory composition:

**Presiding Officer:** A woman employed at a senior level at the workplace. She chairs the ICC and leads investigations. If no senior woman employee is available at the workplace, the Presiding Officer must be nominated from another branch or workplace of the same employer.

**Employee Members (at least 2):** Staff members from among the employees, committed to women's causes or who have experience in social work or legal knowledge. At least one must be a woman.

**External Member (exactly 1):** A person from an NGO or association committed to the cause of women, or a person familiar with issues relating to sexual harassment. This external member provides independence and expertise.

**Minimum size:** 4 members (1 Presiding Officer + 2 employee members + 1 external member).

## Who Cannot Be an ICC Member

- The accused in a pending case
- Anyone with a conflict of interest in a case under review
- The school Principal (conflict of interest as management)

## Term of ICC Members

- ICC members serve for a term not exceeding 3 years
- Members can be re-nominated for further terms
- Members must be replaced when their term expires or if they leave the school

## Steps to Form the ICC

**Step 1: Identify candidates**
- Identify senior women employees willing to serve as Presiding Officer
- Identify employee members (at least one woman, committed to the cause)
- Identify an external member (contact local NGOs working on women's rights)

**Step 2: Issue formal appointment orders**
- Management issues a written order constituting the ICC
- The order names each member, their role, and the term of their appointment
- The order is signed by the Principal or authorized management representative

**Step 3: Display ICC composition**
- Display the names and contacts of ICC members at a conspicuous place in the school
- Include in the school's POSH policy document
- Inform all employees

**Step 4: Orient ICC members**
- All ICC members must understand POSH Act provisions and investigation procedures
- External member should provide orientation if they have POSH experience
- Consider formal training from a POSH consultant for the first committee

## Documentation to Maintain

- Appointment orders for each ICC member
- Contact details for all members
- Training records for ICC members
- Records of any case received and disposed

## Annual Report Obligation

The ICC must submit an Annual Report to the District Officer by January 31 each year. The report covers:
- Number of complaints received during the year
- Number of complaints disposed of
- Number of cases pending for more than 90 days
- Number of workshops or awareness programs conducted

## FAQ

**Q: Can the same person be both CPO (POCSO) and Presiding Officer (POSH ICC)?**

A: Yes, if they are a senior woman employee with appropriate training. The POCSO CPO role and POSH Presiding Officer role have different functions, so dual appointment works best for smaller schools where resources are limited, but the person must be adequately trained for both.

**Q: What if no senior woman employee is available at the school for the Presiding Officer role?**

A: The POSH Act provides that if no senior woman employee is available at the workplace, the Presiding Officer should be nominated from another workplace of the same employer. If still not possible, the school must use the Local Complaints Committee.

**Q: How should the school find an external member?**

A: Contact local women's NGOs, women's legal aid organizations, or law firms with family/women's law practice. External members are typically volunteers or may receive a nominal honorarium.

**Q: What happens if the ICC is not constituted and a complaint is filed?**

A: The employer is liable for a fine and repeated non-compliance can lead to cancellation of licences or registrations under the Act. Constituting the ICC only after a complaint is filed also undermines the complainant's confidence in a fair process.
