Schools providing free education to EWS students under RTE can claim government reimbursement for each enrolled student. The reimbursement rate equals the per-student government school expenditure, lower than private school fees but a critical revenue source.

## What Is Reimbursable?

The reimbursement amount per EWS student per year is set by the state government, usually equal to the average per-student expenditure in government schools in the district. This amount does not cover the full private school fee; schools absorb the gap.

## Documentation Required for Claims

- Class-wise list of EWS students with lottery reference numbers.
- Attendance records showing 75%+ attendance (most states require this).
- Confirmation that no fees were charged, zero-fee ledger printout.
- Books and materials distribution records.
- Bank account details of school (PFMS-linked account preferred).

## Submission Timeline

Most states have an annual submission window, typically April-June for the preceding academic year. Missing the window can mean forfeiting that year's claim.

## Common Rejection Reasons

Fees charged (even activity fees), missing attendance records, students enrolled but not attending regularly, and incorrect lottery reference numbers all result in rejected or reduced claims. Clean records prevent these rejections.

## FAQ

**Q: How long does reimbursement take after submission?**

A: Typically 3-9 months. States with PFMS-linked direct bank transfers are faster. Follow up quarterly if payment is not received.

**Q: Is reimbursement taxable for the school?**

A: Consult your chartered accountant. For trusts registered under Section 12A, it may be exempt. For private schools, it depends on registration status.

**Q: What if the reimbursement rate does not cover actual costs?**

A: Schools must absorb the gap. RTE is a social obligation. The law does not require full cost recovery for private schools.
