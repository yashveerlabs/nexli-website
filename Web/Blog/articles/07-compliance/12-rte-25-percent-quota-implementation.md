The Right to Education Act 2009 requires every private school to reserve 25% of Class I seats for economically weaker section (EWS) students. For a class of 40, that means 10 mandatory EWS seats filled through a government lottery, not school discretion.

## How EWS Seat Calculation Works

Multiply Class I intake by 0.25 and round up. A school with three sections of 40 students each has 120 Class I seats, 30 must be reserved for EWS applicants.

## The Government Lottery Process

Parents of EWS-eligible children apply to the district education authority. The government conducts a lottery and allocates students to specific schools. Schools cannot reject lottery-allocated students based on academic criteria, residence zone, or any other reason.

## School Responsibilities After Lottery

Once a student is allocated:
- Issue an admission letter within 7 days.
- Waive all fees, tuition, admission, books, uniform, meals if provided.
- Admit the student to a mainstream section. No EWS-only sections.
- Track the student in a dedicated RTE register with the lottery reference number.

## Record-Keeping Requirements

Maintain a dedicated EWS register showing student name, lottery reference number, admission date, confirmation that no fees were charged, books and materials provided with dates, and annual progression records through Class VIII.

## Common Mistakes

Charging any amount, even "activity fees" or "ID card fees", violates RTE and can result in fines up to Rs 1,00,000. EWS students must receive everything fee-free. Placing EWS students in separate sections is a constitutional violation.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a school reduce its Class I intake to reduce RTE obligation?**

A: No. Schools cannot artificially lower declared intake. The district authority tracks capacity declarations and will investigate unexplained reductions.

**Q: What happens if no EWS applicants come forward for allocated seats?**

A: Seats remain unfilled for that year. Schools cannot fill them with fee-paying students. Report the situation to the district authority.

**Q: Can schools screen EWS students academically before admission?**

A: No. Zero academic screening is allowed. The government lottery is the only selection mechanism.
