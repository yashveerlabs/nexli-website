School science laboratories are environments where students handle chemicals, biological materials, heat sources, and electrical equipment. Laboratory accidents, chemical burns, fire, glass injuries, are real risks when safety protocols are absent. Schools have a duty of care obligation for laboratory safety, and CBSE affiliation conditions specify minimum laboratory safety standards.

## Legal and Regulatory Basis

**CBSE affiliation:** CBSE requires schools to have proper laboratory facilities with safety equipment as part of affiliation conditions.

**State regulations:** Some states have specific laboratory safety rules for schools under their education and/or workplace safety regulations.

**Duty of care:** The school is responsible for all accidents that occur due to inadequate safety measures or supervision.

## Physical Safety Requirements

### Layout and Ventilation
- Adequate ventilation (windows, exhaust fans) to prevent chemical fume accumulation
- Separate storage area for chemicals (not in the main lab space where students work)
- Adequate spacing between workbenches (students must have room to work without crowding)
- Non-slip flooring

### Emergency Equipment
- Eye-wash station (preferably with running water supply; bottled eyewash as minimum)
- First aid kit accessible in the lab (burns dressing, bandages, eye rinse, antiseptic)
- Fire extinguisher (CO2 type for electrical fires and chemical fires)
- Sand bucket for chemical spills (where appropriate)
- Emergency exit from the lab (door that opens outward)

### Chemical Storage
- Chemicals stored in a separate, locked cabinet or store room
- Chemicals labeled clearly with name and hazard information
- Incompatible chemicals stored separately (acids away from alkalis, oxidizers away from combustibles)
- Flammable chemicals stored in a metal cabinet away from ignition sources
- Inventory of all chemicals maintained
- Chemicals purchased in quantities appropriate for school use (not industrial quantities)
- Expired chemicals disposed of safely

## Safety Protocols

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- Safety goggles mandatory for all students and teachers during any experiment involving chemicals, heat, or glass cutting
- Lab coats or aprons for students working with chemicals
- No open shoes in the laboratory (PPE footwear or closed shoes required)
- Gloves for specific experiments as directed

### Supervision
- Every lab session must have a teacher or qualified lab assistant present
- Students must not be left unsupervised in the laboratory
- Teacher-student ratios must allow the teacher to monitor all students

### Before Each Experiment
- Teacher demonstrates the experiment before students attempt it
- Teacher explains hazards and safety precautions for that specific experiment
- Students read and sign a safety briefing form for more hazardous experiments

### Chemical Handling
- Students follow teacher instructions for handling chemicals
- No unauthorized mixing of chemicals
- All chemical containers returned to designated storage after use
- Chemical spills cleaned up immediately following established protocol

## Training Requirements

**Lab assistant:** Must be trained in chemical safety, first aid, and emergency procedures.

**Teachers:** Must be trained in the specific chemicals and procedures they supervise. Lab safety training should be part of teacher orientation.

**Students:** Annual lab safety briefing at the start of the academic year before first lab session.

## FAQ

**Q: Must a school's chemistry laboratory have running water at each bench?**

A: Running water at benches is best practice for chemical labs. At minimum, an eye-wash station with running water (or a properly maintained bottled eye wash station) must be accessible within seconds of the work area.

**Q: How should schools dispose of expired or unwanted chemicals?**

A: Do not pour chemicals down the drain unless specifically safe to do so for that chemical. Contact local hazardous waste disposal services or the chemical supplier for guidance. Many states have procedures for educational institution chemical disposal. Document all disposal.

**Q: Must students sign a lab safety agreement?**

A: Not universally required, but highly recommended for secondary school students. A signed annual safety agreement confirms that students have received and understood safety briefing. In the event of an accident, it demonstrates the school discharged its safety briefing obligation.

**Q: What is the minimum teacher-student ratio for laboratory sessions?**

A: CBSE recommends maximum 35 students per lab session with one qualified teacher or lab assistant present. More hazardous experiments may require additional supervision.
