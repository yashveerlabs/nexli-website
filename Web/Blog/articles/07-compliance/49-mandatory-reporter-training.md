Under Section 19 of POCSO Act 2012, every person who has knowledge or reasonable suspicion that a child has been sexually abused must report it to the police or Special Juvenile Police Unit. This is mandatory. Failure to report is a criminal offence under Section 21 of POCSO, punishable with imprisonment of up to 6 months and/or a fine.

Every school staff member is a mandatory reporter. This is not limited to the CPO, counselor, or management. Every teacher, support staff member, and administrator who learns of possible abuse has a legal obligation to report.

## What Triggers a Mandatory Report

A report is required when any staff member:
- Receives a disclosure from a student (child says abuse happened)
- Observes physical signs that may indicate abuse (unexplained injuries, fear around specific adults)
- Hears a student make statements that suggest abuse ("my teacher does bad things to me")
- Receives information from a parent, colleague, or third party about suspected abuse

The legal standard is "knowledge or reasonable belief", not certainty. Staff should report suspicion; the police investigate to determine facts.

## Who Reports

**Primary reporters:**
- The staff member who received the disclosure or suspected abuse must inform the CPO immediately (same day, as fast as possible)
- The CPO then files the formal FIR with police

**If the CPO is unavailable:**
- The Principal receives the report and contacts the CPO
- If neither is reachable, the staff member can report directly to the police or call Childline (1098)

Staff must never delay reporting because they are waiting to confirm facts, because they don't want to cause trouble, or because they believe someone else will handle it.

## What Mandatory Report Training Must Cover

Training for all staff must include:

**Recognition:** What signs may indicate a child is being abused (behavioral changes, physical signs, statements)

**Receiving disclosures:** How to listen to a student who is disclosing abuse (without interrogating, without promising secrecy, without showing extreme distress)

**Reporting pathway:** The exact steps: inform CPO, CPO files FIR. Staff must know the CPO's name, contact, and where to find them.

**Legal obligation:** Section 19 and Section 21 of POCSO; the personal criminal liability for failure to report

**Confidentiality:** Who should and should not be told about the complaint

**What not to do:** Investigate yourself, confront the accused, dismiss the complaint, wait and see

## Training Delivery Requirements

- Annual mandatory training for all staff with any contact with students
- New staff must be trained before starting or within the first week
- Training must be documented: attendance record with date, topics, and trainer name
- Brief assessment (quiz) to verify understanding
- Refresher training after any case occurs at the school

## Common Staff Misconceptions

**"I'm not sure, so I shouldn't report."** Section 19 requires reporting on reasonable suspicion or belief, not proof. Let the police determine facts.

**"The CPO will handle it, not me."** Every staff member who receives information is a mandatory reporter. Telling the CPO is not optional, it is part of the mandatory reporting chain.

**"It might be a misunderstanding; I'll wait and see."** Delay creates legal liability and potentially allows ongoing abuse. Report immediately.

**"I don't want to cause trouble for a good teacher."** This cannot factor into the decision. POCSO Section 21 applies without exception.

## FAQ

**Q: What if a student asks a teacher not to tell anyone about what they disclosed?**

A: The teacher must still report. Section 19 is mandatory regardless of the student's wishes. The teacher should explain gently that they must tell the CPO to get the student help, and that the CPO will help protect them.

**Q: Can a staff member be prosecuted even if they reported to the CPO but not directly to police?**

A: If the CPO filed the FIR promptly, the staff member's obligation was met by reporting to the CPO. The problem arises if the staff member told no one, or if the chain of reporting broke down.

**Q: What if a parent asks the teacher not to report because they want to handle it privately?**

A: The mandatory reporting obligation overrides parental instructions. This is non-negotiable under POCSO.

**Q: Who verifies that all staff have completed mandatory reporter training?**

A: The CPO or Principal is responsible for ensuring all staff are trained. CBSE inspections may request training records. Schools should maintain records accessible for inspection.
