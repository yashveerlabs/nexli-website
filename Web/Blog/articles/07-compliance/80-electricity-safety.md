Electrical safety failures in schools cause fires, electrocution, and equipment damage. Schools are high-density occupancy buildings with diverse electrical loads, classrooms, laboratories, computer rooms, kitchen equipment, generators. An annual electrical safety inspection by a qualified electrical contractor or government inspector is the minimum standard.

## Legal Basis

**Indian Electricity Act and Rules:** Schools as electrical consumers must comply with the Indian Electricity Act 2003 and the Central Electricity Authority (Measures Relating to Safety and Electric Supply) Regulations 2010.

**State electrical inspectorate:** Most states have an Electrical Inspectorate that conducts periodic inspections of large installations. Schools above a specified connected load may be subject to mandatory periodic inspection.

**CBSE affiliation:** CBSE inspections check that the school's electrical installation is adequate and safe.

## Annual Electrical Inspection Scope

A qualified licensed electrical contractor or inspectorate officer checks:

**Earthing system:**
- Earth continuity across the installation
- Earth resistance values within specified limits
- Earthing of all metal equipment and fittings

**Overload protection:**
- MCBs (Miniature Circuit Breakers) or fuses of appropriate rating for each circuit
- Main switch and distribution board condition

**Wiring condition:**
- No exposed or damaged cables
- No overloading of sockets (extension boards not used as permanent wiring solution)
- Age of wiring (PVC insulation degrades over 15-20 years; older wiring should be tested carefully)

**Laboratory electrical safety:**
- RCD (Residual Current Device) protection for laboratory circuits where students handle equipment
- Socket condition in lab
- Isolation of equipment not in use

**Generator and UPS:**
- Proper changeover switch between mains and generator
- Generator earthing and exhaust ventilation

**Electric water heaters, air conditioning, kitchen equipment:**
- Earthing and protective devices

## Documentation Required

- Annual inspection report from licensed electrical contractor
- Maintenance log of electrical work done during the year
- Record of any RCD/MCB replacement

For schools subject to mandatory state inspectorate inspection: certificate from the Electrical Inspectorate.

## Common Electrical Safety Issues in Schools

**Overloaded extension boards:** Multiple high-power devices (projectors, computers) plugged into a single 5-amp extension board.

**Old wiring:** Wiring installed 20+ years ago that has not been tested. PVC insulation degrades.

**No earthing:** Equipment metal casings not connected to earth, creating electric shock risk.

**Computer room issues:** High density of equipment, often on extension boards; heat buildup in server/UPS areas.

**Laboratory risks:** No RCD protection for lab circuits where students use electrical equipment.

## Remediation Priority

Schools that have not had an electrical inspection should prioritize:
1. Get an inspection done by a licensed electrical contractor
2. Address any urgent safety deficiencies immediately (exposed wiring, no earthing)
3. Plan a phased remediation of older wiring
4. Install RCDs on lab circuits

## FAQ

**Q: How often must schools have their electrical installation inspected?**

A: Annual inspection by a licensed electrical contractor is a minimum standard. Schools subject to mandatory state inspectorate inspection must comply with the inspectorate's schedule (typically every 3-5 years for the formal inspection, with annual internal reviews in between).

**Q: What is the risk if a school has never had an electrical inspection?**

A: Uninspected installations may have degraded wiring, inadequate earthing, or overloaded circuits, any of which can cause electrical fires or electrocution. Regulatory risk aside, the school bears full liability for any incident in an uninspected installation.

**Q: Can the school's regular maintenance electrician conduct the annual inspection?**

A: The maintenance electrician can identify obvious issues during routine work. The formal annual inspection should be conducted by a licensed electrical contractor (holder of appropriate supervisory licence) who can certify the installation's condition.

**Q: Is there a specific form for the electrical inspection report?**

A: State electrical inspectorates may specify a form for their inspections. For contractor-conducted annual reviews, a detailed inspection report on the contractor's letterhead with the contractor's licence number is the standard.
