Every school with 10 or more employees that has constituted an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) must submit an Annual Report to the designated District Officer under the POSH Act 2013. The deadline is January 31 each year, covering the previous calendar year.

Missing the annual report deadline is a compliance failure. Schools that do not submit the report are non-compliant even if no complaints were received during the year.

## What the Annual Report Must Include

The POSH Act and rules specify the minimum contents of the annual report:

**Number of complaints received:** Total formal complaints received by the ICC during the calendar year. If no complaints were received, this is "0", the report is still required.

**Number of complaints disposed of:** Complaints for which the ICC completed its inquiry and submitted recommendations to management during the year.

**Number of cases pending for more than 90 days:** Cases that are still open beyond the 90-day statutory inquiry period. If any, the report should explain why.

**Number of workshops and awareness programs conducted:** Training and awareness activities carried out during the year on POSH compliance, employee rights, and ICC procedures.

**Nature of action taken by employer:** A summary of what management did in response to ICC recommendations (disciplinary action taken, transfer executed, etc.)

## Format of the Report

The Ministry of Women and Child Development has issued a format for the annual report. Schools should use this format or the one specified by their state government's designated officer.

The report is signed by:
- The ICC Presiding Officer
- The ICC External Member (or all ICC members, depending on state requirements)

## Who Receives the Report

The Annual Report is submitted to the District Officer, the officer designated by the state government to oversee POSH compliance. This may be the District Collector, a designated women's welfare officer, or another officer depending on the state.

Schools should identify who the designated District Officer is for their area before the January 31 deadline.

## Record Keeping

In addition to submitting the report, schools must maintain internal records:
- Copies of submitted annual reports for at least 5 years
- Case files for all complaints (received, investigated, and disposed of) for at least 3 years after case closure
- Training attendance records for POSH awareness programs

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with POSH Act requirements (including annual reporting) can result in:
- Monetary penalty (up to Rs. 50,000 for first offence; higher for repeat offences)
- Cancellation or non-renewal of school operating licences or registrations
- Reputational damage and regulatory scrutiny

## FAQ

**Q: Must schools submit an annual POSH report even if no complaints were received?**

A: Yes. The annual report is mandatory regardless of complaint volume. A "nil" report (0 complaints received) must still be submitted by January 31.

**Q: Where exactly is the annual report submitted?**

A: To the designated District Officer in your area. This officer is notified by the state government. Schools should confirm the correct authority and submission method (physical, email, or online portal) in advance.

**Q: What if the school missed the January 31 deadline?**

A: Submit as soon as possible and maintain a record of the late submission. Repeated non-submission creates compounding regulatory risk. Some districts have online portals where late submissions can be made.

**Q: Does the annual report need to include employee names or case details?**

A: No. The annual report contains aggregate numbers, not individual names or case details. Case files are confidential and maintained separately; only totals are reported to the District Officer.
