NEP 2020 emphasizes mother tongue or home language as the medium of instruction through Class 5, and encourages it through Class 8. For schools, this means rethinking the assumption that English-medium instruction is always superior, and building bridges between home language and school learning.

## The Three-Language Formula

NEP 2020 reinforces the three-language formula with flexibility:
- **Language 1:** Home language or mother tongue (medium of instruction, especially Classes 1-5).
- **Language 2:** Hindi or an Indian regional language (if Language 1 is Hindi, choose another Indian language).
- **Language 3:** English or another Indian or foreign language.

No language should be imposed. Schools should offer flexibility within this framework.

## Mother Tongue Medium Instruction: The Evidence

Research consistently shows children learn best when initial instruction is in their home language. Abstract concepts in mathematics and science are more effectively understood when first introduced in the familiar language, then bridged to English. This is the bridge model NEP endorses.

## What Schools Need to Do

1. **Identify the home languages in your student population.** If 40% of students speak Telugu at home but are in a Hindi-medium school, consider whether a bridge language can help.
2. **Develop or adopt textbooks in regional languages** for Classes 1-5 where student population supports it.
3. **Train teachers in bilingual pedagogy**: teaching concepts in one language and reinforcing in another.
4. **Communicate policy to parents** who may prefer English-medium; explain the bridge model.

## Practical Limitations for Schools

Most private English-medium schools face practical challenges: parents expect English medium, qualified regional-language teachers may be scarce, and board examination materials are primarily in English or Hindi. NEP acknowledges these constraints and does not mandate overnight switches.

## FAQ

**Q: Do English-medium schools have to switch to regional language medium?**

A: NEP strongly recommends mother tongue medium for Classes 1-5 but acknowledges that schools operate in diverse contexts. The direction is clear; the pace is flexible.

**Q: What if a school serves students from multiple language backgrounds?**

A: Schools can offer bridge language instruction and should at minimum use students' home language as a supplementary explanation tool. Full-scale bilingual programs are encouraged but not immediately mandated.

**Q: Will CBSE and ICSE boards change their examination languages?**

A: CBSE already offers examinations in Hindi and English. Board language options are expanding gradually. Schools should monitor board circulars for specific changes.
