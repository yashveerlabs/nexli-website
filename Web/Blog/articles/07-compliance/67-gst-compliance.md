GST treatment of schools is a specialized area with significant exemptions for core educational services but taxable treatment for many ancillary activities. Schools that assume all their services are GST-exempt are likely making compliance errors. Equally, schools that register for GST when not required create unnecessary compliance burden.

## Core Educational Services: Exempt

Services provided by an educational institution to its students, faculty, and staff are exempt from GST under Notification 12/2017 (Central Tax Rate). This exemption covers:
- Tuition fees
- Examination fees
- School transportation fees (where transportation is provided by the institution to its own students)

An "educational institution" for GST purposes is defined as one that provides education as a part of a curriculum for obtaining a qualification recognized by any law for the time being in force.

Recognized schools (affiliated to CBSE, state boards, ICSE) meet this definition. The exemption applies to pre-school through higher secondary levels.

## Exempt Ancillary Services

The exemption extends to services provided by the educational institution to its students including:
- Catering services (mid-day meals or canteen services provided to students by the institution)
- Security services consumed within the educational institution
- Cleaning services consumed within the educational institution
- Admission services

## What Is Taxable

Not all school income is exempt. Taxable supplies include:
- **Commercial property rental:** Renting out school premises for events, commercial purposes
- **Non-educational services sold to third parties:** Printing, catering to external clients
- **Sponsorship receipts:** Sponsorship income received by schools may be taxable
- **Coaching/tutorial classes:** Private coaching centers and tutorial institutes separate from recognized schools are generally not exempt
- **Sale of goods:** School uniforms, books, stationery sold at a profit (unless exempted separately)

## GST Registration Threshold

Schools must register for GST if their aggregate taxable turnover exceeds Rs. 20 lakh (Rs. 10 lakh in certain states) per year. If a school's only supplies are exempt (core education services to students), its taxable turnover is zero and GST registration is not required even if total revenue is high.

If the school has any taxable supplies (e.g., hall rental income) that cross the threshold, registration is required.

## Input Tax Credit (ITC)

Schools making entirely exempt supplies cannot claim Input Tax Credit on their purchases (GST paid on capital goods, services). Schools with a mix of exempt and taxable supplies can claim ITC proportionate to their taxable supplies under Rule 42.

## FAQ

**Q: Are school hostel fees exempt from GST?**

A: Hostel fees charged by educational institutions to students are generally exempt under the educational institution exemption, as they are services to students of the institution. However, hostel fees that are disproportionately high or structured as commercial accommodation may attract scrutiny.

**Q: Must schools collect GST on school uniforms sold to students?**

A: Uniforms are goods, and their GST treatment depends on whether the school is acting as a vendor (selling uniforms for profit) or as an agent of a manufacturer. Schools should consult a tax professional on the specific structure of their uniform sale arrangement.

**Q: If the school earns rental income from hall booking, must it register for GST?**

A: If rental income (taxable) combined with any other taxable supplies crosses Rs. 20 lakh, registration is required. Rental of space by an educational institution to commercial users is a taxable supply under GST.

**Q: Are donations received by school trusts subject to GST?**

A: Genuine donations (without any quid pro quo) are not subject to GST. Donations where the donor receives a commercial benefit in return (sponsorship-type arrangements) may be taxable. Schools should structure donations carefully and consult a CA.
