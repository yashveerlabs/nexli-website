Student attendance is a key indicator of educational engagement and a regulatory compliance requirement. CBSE requires 75% minimum attendance for students to be eligible for board examinations. The RTE Act 2009 has provisions related to continuous attendance and retention. Schools must have clear, written attendance policies that are communicated to parents and consistently enforced.

## Legal and Regulatory Basis

**CBSE Board Exam Eligibility:** A student must have attended at least 75% of working days to be eligible to sit for the Class 10 and Class 12 board examinations. The school must certify attendance compliance on the List of Candidates (LOC) submitted to CBSE.

**RTE Act 2009:** Schools must not detain or expel students in elementary classes (Classes 1-8) solely on grounds of non-attendance. The school must work with parents to address attendance issues through counseling and support.

**UDISE+ Reporting:** Attendance data is reported in UDISE+ annual returns.

## What an Attendance Policy Must Cover

**Minimum attendance requirement:**
- 75% attendance is the minimum for board exam eligibility
- Schools can set a higher standard for internal purposes (e.g., 85% required for participation in certain activities)
- Communicate the minimum clearly in the parent handbook

**Procedure for absences:**
- Parents must inform the school of absences before or on the day of absence (phone, app, or written communication)
- Medical absences require a doctor's certificate for absences exceeding 2-3 consecutive days
- Planned absences (family events, travel) must be applied for in advance; principal approval required for more than a day or two

**Attendance tracking:**
- Daily attendance recorded by class teacher in each period or at minimum once per day
- Attendance register maintained in physical form and/or digitally
- Weekly attendance summaries generated for monitoring

**Communication with parents:**
- Parents notified when attendance falls below 85% (warning stage)
- Parents contacted when attendance falls below 80% (intervention stage)
- Parents summoned for meeting if attendance falls below 75% (critical stage)
- Student at risk of not meeting 75% threshold notified in writing

**Leave procedure:**
- Leave application form (or parent portal submission)
- Approval by class teacher for 1-2 days; Principal approval for longer periods
- Maximum pre-sanctioned leave per year specified

**Unauthorized absence:**
- What happens when a student is absent without informing the school
- Process for following up on unexplained absences (welfare concern for prolonged absences)

## Attendance and Student Welfare

Persistent unexplained absence can indicate a welfare concern: domestic issues, bullying, health problems, or economic factors pulling the student out of school. Schools should treat unexplained absence as a welfare signal:
- Contact parents after 3 consecutive unexplained absences
- Home visit for prolonged unexplained absence (particularly for younger students)
- Connect with school counselor if welfare concern is identified

## Attendance Records

Attendance records are regulatory documents. Maintain:
- Daily attendance register (class-wise, subject-wise for secondary classes)
- Monthly attendance summaries
- Annual attendance record for each student
- Records for minimum 5 years (CBSE requirements for board classes)

## FAQ

**Q: Can a school expel a student for poor attendance in elementary classes?**

A: No. Under the RTE Act, a student in Classes 1-8 cannot be expelled for non-attendance. Schools must work with parents to address attendance issues. Detention in the same class for non-attendance is also prohibited under RTE.

**Q: Can a school waive the 75% requirement for a student with medical issues?**

A: CBSE allows principals to grant a 5% relaxation (to 70%) for students with documented medical conditions or participation in national/international sports or cultural events. This requires principal's certificate and supporting documentation. Further relaxation requires CBSE approval.

**Q: Must attendance be taken in every period for secondary classes?**

A: CBSE requires period-wise attendance for secondary classes (Classes 9-12) for the purpose of calculating the 75% requirement. Each period counts, and attendance is calculated across all periods, not just days present.

**Q: How should the school handle attendance for students who are present but habitually late?**

A: Late arrivals policy should be clearly stated: students arriving after a specified time (e.g., 30 minutes after session start) may be marked absent for that period. Consistent lateness should be addressed with parents.
