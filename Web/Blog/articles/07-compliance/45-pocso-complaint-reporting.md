When a student discloses sexual abuse, harassment, or exploitation, the school's response in the first few hours determines the quality of support the child receives and whether POCSO's mandatory reporting requirements are met. Schools need a documented procedure that staff can follow without having to make judgment calls under pressure.

## Step 1: Receiving the Disclosure

When a student (or anyone else) discloses abuse to a staff member:

**Do:**
- Listen calmly and without interruption
- Thank the student for trusting you
- Assure the student they are not at fault
- Explain that you will help and that you need to involve the Child Protection Officer
- Take brief notes (date, time, what was said) as soon as the conversation ends

**Do not:**
- Ask probing questions ("Are you sure?" "Who was it?" "What exactly happened?")
- Promise to keep the information secret
- Show shock, disbelief, or visible distress
- Tell other staff or students about the disclosure
- Contact the accused person before police are involved

The staff member's role is to receive and refer, not to investigate.

## Step 2: Notify the CPO Immediately

After the disclosure, the staff member must:
1. Bring the student (or ensure their safety) and immediately contact the Child Protection Officer (CPO)
2. Tell the CPO what was disclosed: name of student, who was named as the perpetrator (if mentioned), what happened
3. Hand off responsibility to the CPO

This should happen within the same school day. Do not wait.

## Step 3: CPO Initial Assessment

The CPO:
1. Meets with the student in a safe, private space (not the CPO's office if that is near the accused's classroom)
2. Listens; does not conduct an interrogation
3. Ensures the student's immediate safety (separates the student from the accused if the accused is on campus)
4. Notifies the Principal
5. Opens a case file with a unique case ID, date, and initial information

## Step 4: File the FIR

The CPO files an FIR with the nearest police station or the Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU).

**This step is mandatory under POCSO Section 19.** Filing the FIR does not require management approval. If management attempts to prevent the FIR, the CPO should file anyway, failure to report is a criminal offence.

**What to include in the FIR:**
- Student's name, age, class
- Nature of the complaint (as disclosed)
- Name of the accused (if known)
- Date and location of the alleged offence (if known)
- School's contact information and CPO details

Request a copy of the FIR acknowledgment and file it in the case record.

## Step 5: Parent Notification

If the accused is not a family member, notify the parents immediately. If there is any possibility the parent is involved or would obstruct the investigation, notify police first and seek their guidance on how to inform the family.

## Step 6: Support the Student

Regardless of the investigation outcome, the school must support the affected student:
- Provide access to the school counselor immediately
- Adjust academic schedule if the student needs time
- Ensure the student does not share classes or spaces with the accused
- Regular CPO check-ins (weekly minimum)
- Coordinate with parents on support at home
- Monitor for trauma symptoms: withdrawal, anxiety, behavioral changes

## Step 7: Cooperate with Investigation

Police will conduct the investigation. The school's role is to cooperate:
- Provide investigators access to relevant records
- Make staff available for statements when required
- Do not conduct a parallel internal investigation
- Do not share investigation details with other staff or parents

## Step 8: Documentation

Maintain a confidential case record containing:
- Date and method of initial disclosure
- FIR number and police station
- Support provided to the student
- All follow-up actions and dates
- Case closure information (when provided by police)

## FAQ

**Q: What if a student discloses abuse but says they don't want the school to tell anyone?**

A: POCSO reporting is mandatory regardless of the student's wishes. The CPO must explain this gently: "I have to tell the police to protect you and keep other children safe. I will support you through this process." Honoring a request for secrecy in abuse cases is not an option under POCSO.

**Q: What if the accused is another student?**

A: POCSO applies even when the perpetrator is a minor. File the FIR. The Juvenile Justice system handles the case differently than adult offenders, but the school's reporting obligation is the same.

**Q: How long does the school keep the complaint file?**

A: Keep complaint files for at least 5 years from case closure. Some schools keep them for longer given the sensitivity of the records.

**Q: What if police say they cannot file an FIR?**

A: A police officer cannot legally refuse to register a POCSO complaint. If refused, escalate to the SJPU, Child Welfare Committee, or directly to the District Magistrate. Document every attempt to file.
