## Why Schools Need a Compliance Calendar

Schools must meet dozens of regulatory deadlines throughout the year: CBSE submissions, RTE reporting, fire safety audits, staff verifications, data protection checks, fee audits, FSSAI renewals, and more. Missing a single deadline can result in fines, penalties, or loss of affiliation.

A compliance calendar ensures nothing is missed. It's a master timeline of all regulatory obligations, who is responsible, and when action must be taken.

## Key Compliance Deadlines by Timeline

### April-May (Summer Break Planning)

**May:**
- [ ] Complete annual audit (financial statements for FY ending March 31).
- [ ] File income tax returns for school entity.
- [ ] Renew school registration with district authority (if annually required).
- [ ] Plan summer infrastructure maintenance.
- [ ] Distribute staff leave schedule and leave policy.

**June:**
- [ ] Prepare admission plan for new academic year (seat allocation, RTE seats).
- [ ] Register RTE seats with district education authority.
- [ ] Plan curriculum and academic calendar.
- [ ] Order textbooks and teaching materials (based on enrollment).

### July-August (Admission & Onboarding)

**July:**
- [ ] Launch admissions (Class I onwards).
- [ ] Conduct entrance assessments (if applicable).
- [ ] Conduct background verification for new staff (police check, reference).
- [ ] Collect parental consents (medical, photos, data protection, etc.).

**August:**
- [ ] Complete admissions and seat allocation.
- [ ] Finalize staff appointments.
- [ ] Conduct staff training on DPDP, POCSO, and other compliance topics.
- [ ] Prepare class teachers' lists and timetable.
- [ ] Update student master records (collect all admission documents).

### September-October (Academic Year Starts)

**September:**
- [ ] Academic year begins; classes start.
- [ ] Complete student health screening (eye test, hearing test, weight/height).
- [ ] Verify student enrollment in CBSE system.
- [ ] Begin curriculum tracking and lesson planning.

**October:**
- [ ] First progress report prepared.
- [ ] First PTM held (discuss student progress and policies).
- [ ] Conduct fire drill and document participation.
- [ ] Review and ensure all staff background verifications are complete.
- [ ] Compliance audit: Check DPDP consent registry (all new students have consents).

### November-December (Mid-Year Review)

**November:**
- [ ] Mid-term assessments and internal exams.
- [ ] Prepare term-end report (preliminary).
- [ ] Check CBSE curriculum completion % (track on compliance calendar).

**December:**
- [ ] Term 1 ends; publish report cards.
- [ ] Conduct winter health camp or vaccination drive (if planned).
- [ ] Staff appraisals (mid-year performance review).
- [ ] Renew annual licenses/memberships (sports council, library association, etc.).

### January-February (Board Exam Prep)

**January:**
- [ ] Board Exam registration: Finalize List of Candidates (LOC) for Class 10 & 12.
- [ ] Submit LOC to CBSE (deadline usually late December/early January).
- [ ] Conduct invigilation duty planning for board exams.
- [ ] Prepare admit cards for students.

**February:**
- [ ] Finalize result compilation and tabulation procedures.
- [ ] Ensure answer scripts are secured (if applicable).
- [ ] Begin intensive exam preparation guidance.

### March (Year-End & Board Exams)

**March:**
- [ ] Board exams conducted (Class 10 & 12).
- [ ] Non-board exam results compiled and published.
- [ ] Term-end report cards issued.
- [ ] Academic year-end appraisals for staff.
- [ ] Compliance Audit: Annual data protection review (access logs, breaches, consent status).
- [ ] Audit financial statements and fee ledger (annual reconciliation).

### Quarterly/Periodic Compliance Tasks

**Every Term:**
- [ ] Curriculum completion review (80% minimum required).
- [ ] Staff attendance audit.
- [ ] Compliance checklist: CBSE standards, DPDP practices, RTE status.

**Every 6 Months:**
- [ ] Health & safety audit: Fire exits, electrical safety, water quality testing.
- [ ] Staff training records review (ensure all trained on compliance topics).

**Annually:**
- [ ] Full CBSE compliance audit (inspect all standards before reaffiliation inspection).
- [ ] DPDP annual audit: Consent registry, access logs, data retention.
- [ ] RTE compliance: Verify EWS students enrolled, tracked, and provided free education.
- [ ] Staff background verification renewal (every 5 years, but track first dates).
- [ ] Data Protection Officer compliance report.
- [ ] Child protection incident review (check if all POCSO requirements met).

### As-Needed (Regulatory/Emergency)

**Anytime:**
- [ ] CBSE inspection (may be scheduled with 1-2 weeks notice).
- [ ] Government audit or survey (UDISE+, state inspections).
- [ ] Parent complaints or regulatory inquiries (respond within 7-14 days).
- [ ] Data breach or security incident (notify within 30 days under DPDP).

## Building Your School's Compliance Calendar

### Step 1: List All Regulatory Obligations

Identify every regulation your school must comply with:
- CBSE Affiliation (5-year cycle).
- DPDP Act 2023 (ongoing).
- RTE Act 2009 (ongoing, if applicable).
- POCSO Act 2012 (ongoing).
- Fire Safety NOC (annual or biennial).
- Building Safety Certificate (annual).
- FSSAI for Canteen (annual, if applicable).
- Water Quality Testing (annual).
- Staff Background Verification (5-yearly).
- Financial Audit (annual).
- State School Regulations (varies by state).

### Step 2: Map Deadlines & Responsibilities

For each obligation, create an entry:

| Compliance Task | Frequency | Deadline | Responsible Person | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CBSE Affiliation Inspection | Every 5 years | Notified by CBSE | Principal | Pending (Next: 2028) |
| DPDP Audit | Annual | End of March | DPO | Complete (2024) |
| RTE Report | Annual | End of June | Office Manager | Due June 2025 |
| Fire Safety Drill | Quarterly | Every 3 months | Safety Officer | Last done: March 2025 |
| Staff Background Check | 5-yearly | As per hire date | HR | Updated Jan 2025 |

### Step 3: Set Reminders & Escalation

- Alert responsible person 1 month before deadline.
- Escalate to Principal 2 weeks before if not completed.
- Mark as "At Risk" if 1 week remaining and not started.
- After deadline, mark as "Non-Compliant" and investigate reason.

### Step 4: Track Progress & Documentation

For each compliance task:
- [ ] Task started (date).
- [ ] Task in progress (% complete, challenges).
- [ ] Task submitted/completed (date).
- [ ] Proof/certification received (document attached).

## FAQ

**Q1: How should schools prioritize multiple simultaneous compliance tasks?**
A: Use a risk matrix: High-priority (affiliation, child protection, financial audit) first; then medium-priority (routine reports); then low-priority (policy updates). Delegate to spread workload.

**Q2: What if a school misses a compliance deadline?**
A: Immediately notify the regulatory authority, explain the delay, and submit as soon as possible. Document the reason for delay. For future inspections, this delay may be noted, so compliance is critical going forward.

**Q3: Who should own the compliance calendar?**
A: Ideally, a compliance lead or DPO, with principal as final authority. For smaller schools, the principal or office manager can manage.

**Q4: How often should compliance calendar be reviewed?**
A: Monthly (to track progress), quarterly (to audit current status), and annually (to plan next year).

**Q5: Should the compliance calendar be shared with staff and parents?**
A: Share summary with staff (so they know deadlines). Share broad compliance status with parents/governing body (for transparency). Keep detailed compliance gaps confidential (to avoid alarming stakeholders unnecessarily).

**Q6: What if a new regulation is introduced mid-year?**
A: Update the compliance calendar immediately. Assess impact and timeline. Communicate to staff. Adjust other tasks if needed to accommodate new requirement.
