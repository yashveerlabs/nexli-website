## What is the Right to Education (RTE) Act?

The Right to Education Act, 2009, is India's landmark legislation making free and compulsory elementary education (Classes I-VIII) a fundamental right for all children. For private schools, RTE creates specific obligations: reserve 25% of Class I seats for economically weaker sections (EWS) students and provide free education to those students.

For school leaders, RTE compliance is both a legal requirement and a social responsibility.

## Key RTE Obligations for Private Schools

### 1. **25% EWS Seat Reservation (Class I Only)**

Private schools must reserve 25% of total available seats in Class I for students from economically weaker sections.

**Calculation:**
- If a Class I has 40 seats, 10 seats (25%) must be reserved for EWS.
- These 10 seats are filled through government-conducted lotteries.

**What is "EWS"?**
- Family income: Below ₹3 lakh per annum (or as revised by state government).
- Definitions vary by state; check your state's RTE rules.

### 2. **Free Education for EWS Students**

Schools must provide free education to all EWS students admitted through RTE seats.

**"Free" includes:**
- Tuition fees (zero charge).
- Admission fees.
- Books and stationery (school must provide or reimburse).
- Uniform (if applicable).
- Transportation (if school provides).
- Meals (if school provides).

**"Free" does NOT include:**
- Voluntary donations (cannot be requested).
- Activities beyond core curriculum (unless the school provides them for all students for free).

### 3. **Admission Process**

**Government-Conducted Lottery:**
- Parents of EWS children apply to the district education authority.
- Government conducts a lottery to allocate students to schools.
- Schools must accept allocated students; they cannot reject based on academic criteria, caste, religion, or other factors.

**School Responsibilities:**
- Register with the district authority (submit capacity and available seats).
- Accept students allocated by lottery without delay.
- Issue admission letters and collect minimal documentation.
- Admit students at the start of the academic year (no mid-term cuts).

### 4. **Continuing Education (Progression)**

Once a student is admitted via RTE in Class I:
- The student should continue in the same school up to Class VIII (or as per state rules).
- Fees remain free for as long as the student is in the school.
- The student cannot be asked to leave or "promoted out" due to low performance.

**Exception:** If the student voluntarily leaves, they don't have the right to continue the next year.

### 5. **Documentation & Record Keeping**

Schools must maintain records:
- List of EWS students admitted (with lottery reference numbers).
- Proof of fee-free education (no fee receipts issued).
- Books and materials provided (to prove "free").
- Communication with parents (consent for admission, acknowledgment of free status).
- Student performance records (to show no discrimination in academics).

### 6. **Compliance with Other Laws**

EWS students are still subject to all school policies, including:
- Discipline policies (same as for-fee students).
- Attendance policies (75% requirement applies).
- Uniform and appearance codes.

**But:** Schools cannot use these policies to disguise discrimination against EWS students. Example: "Uniform is ₹1,500" makes education non-free; the school must provide uniform as part of free education for RTE students.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

**Financial Penalties:** ₹10,000 to ₹1,00,000 per violation, depending on state rules.

**Loss of Recognition:** State authorities can cancel school registration.

**Legal Action:** Parents can file complaints with district RTE commission; courts can order compensatory education.

**Reputational Damage:** Public perception of discrimination; reduced admissions from fee-paying families.

## How Schools Can Be RTE-Compliant

### 1. **Set Up Processes Before Admission Season**

- Register with district education authority.
- Declare Class I seat capacity and confirm RTE seats.
- Publish admission process and RTE policy on website and notice board.
- Brief staff on RTE requirements and non-discrimination.

### 2. **Implement Free Education System**

- Create a separate fee ledger for RTE students (showing ₹0 charges).
- No bills or fee reminders sent to RTE parents.
- All materials (books, uniform, supplies) provided by school or fully reimbursed.
- If your school charges for activities (music, sports), either make them free for RTE students or don't charge for-fee students either.

### 3. **Create Inclusive Environment**

- Avoid any differentiation in classroom (seating, assignments, teacher interaction).
- Don't ask RTE students about their "poor background" or why they're in the school.
- Ensure RTE students have access to all facilities (labs, playgrounds, library).
- Monitor for bullying; address immediately.

### 4. **Maintain Transparent Records**

- List of RTE students with lottery documentation.
- Attendance tracking for RTE students (same as others).
- Academic records (grades, progress, behavior).
- Evidence of materials provided.
- Parent communication records.

### 5. **Report to Government**

- File annual reports to district education authority.
- Confirm number of RTE students admitted, continued, and progressed.
- Submit financial statements (no fees collected from RTE students).

## Common Myths & Clarifications

**Myth 1: "RTE students can leave anytime; we can admit new students."**
- False. Once a student is admitted in Class I via RTE, they have the right to continue through Class VIII (or as per state rules).

**Myth 2: "We can ask RTE parents for 'donations' for infrastructure."**
- False. Any form of payment (even voluntary donation) violates RTE. "Free" means free.

**Myth 3: "If an RTE student fails, we can ask them to leave."**
- False (mostly). Schools cannot expel students for academic performance. Discipline-related expulsion is allowed only after due process.

**Myth 4: "We can ask RTE parents to buy books from our list."**
- False. Schools must provide books or reimburse the cost. Parents should not pay.

**Myth 5: "RTE seats are only for SC/ST students."**
- False. RTE seats are for economically weaker sections (all castes/religions eligible if income is below threshold).

## FAQ

**Q1: Can a school refuse EWS students if they don't have space?**
A: No. Schools must reserve exactly 25% of Class I seats for RTE. They cannot reduce capacity to reduce RTE obligations.

**Q2: What if more EWS applicants apply than available seats?**
A: The government conducts a lottery to select 25% of applicants. Schools must accept those selected.

**Q3: How long does free education obligation last?**
A: Through Class VIII (or Class XII in some states). Check your state's RTE rules for the exact duration.

**Q4: Can schools charge a "one-time registration fee" for RTE students?**
A: No. Any fee (including registration, processing, or administrative) is prohibited for RTE students.

**Q5: What if an RTE student's family's income increases after admission?**
A: The student continues to receive free education. Income re-verification is not done annually.

**Q6: Can RTE students be segregated into separate classes?**
A: Absolutely not. Segregation violates RTE and constitutional principles. RTE students must be in mainstream classes with other students.
