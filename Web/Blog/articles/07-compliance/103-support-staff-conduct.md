Support staff, including administrative staff, maintenance workers, security personnel, canteen staff, drivers, and hostel wardens, have direct or indirect contact with students. POCSO Act obligations, DPDP Act data protection requirements, and basic duty of care all apply to support staff interactions with students.

Schools often focus conduct codes on teaching staff. A support staff code of conduct addresses the distinct situations and responsibilities of non-teaching staff.

## Who Is Covered

The support staff code applies to:
- Administrative and office staff
- Maintenance and housekeeping staff
- Security and reception personnel
- Canteen and kitchen staff
- Transport drivers and conductors
- Hostel wardens and matrons
- Laboratory assistants and library staff
- Any other non-teaching staff with access to students or student data

## Student Interaction Standards

**All support staff must:**
- Treat students with respect and professionalism at all times
- Not use offensive language, insults, or intimidation toward students
- Report immediately any student who appears to be in distress, danger, or discomfort
- Not engage in unsupervised one-on-one interactions with students in isolated areas

**Prohibited interactions:**
- Any form of physical contact with students beyond what is strictly necessary for the role (a driver assisting a young child into a bus; a warden checking if a student is ill)
- Discussing students' personal information with other students or external parties
- Photographing students or entering student areas not relevant to their role

**POCSO obligations:**
Support staff are mandatory reporters under POCSO Section 19. If any support staff member becomes aware of suspected child abuse, they must report immediately to the CPO or Principal.

## Confidentiality

Support staff, particularly administrative staff, have access to sensitive student information:
- Student addresses, contact details, financial records
- Attendance and behavioral records
- Medical information (school health staff)

This information must not be:
- Discussed with other students or their parents
- Shared outside the school without authorization
- Accessed beyond what is required for the staff member's specific role

## Specific Role Standards

### Security Personnel
- Verify identity of all visitors before permitting entry
- Not permit students to leave campus without proper authorization
- Report suspicious individuals or vehicles near the school
- Not disclose information about students to callers or visitors without verification and authorization

### Transport Staff
- Ensure no student is left on the vehicle after the route is complete
- Not permit unauthorized individuals on the school vehicle
- Report accidents or student welfare concerns immediately
- Not communicate with students through personal devices

### Hostel Wardens
- Conduct roll calls as required and report discrepancies
- Report any student welfare concerns to the Principal or counselor
- Maintain confidentiality of student personal and family information
- Not impose punishments on students; escalate discipline to school management

### Canteen Staff
- Maintain food hygiene standards
- Report any student who appears unwell or distressed
- Not sell prohibited items to students

## Disciplinary Procedure

Violations of the code follow the school's general disciplinary process:
- Incident reported to the support staff supervisor/Principal
- Investigation: facts gathered, staff member given opportunity to respond
- Action proportionate to violation: warning, suspension, termination
- For POCSO violations: immediate FIR with police; employment action after investigation

## FAQ

**Q: Must support staff also receive POCSO mandatory reporter training?**

A: Yes. Any staff member who may be in a position to observe abuse or receive a disclosure must be trained as mandatory reporters. This includes security guards, hostel wardens, canteen staff, and drivers.

**Q: Can school transport drivers have students' home addresses?**

A: Drivers need route information but should not retain individual student home address data beyond what is necessary for each route. Address information should be managed through the school's transport coordinator, not individually held by drivers.

**Q: What if a canteen staff member receives a disclosure of abuse from a student?**

A: The canteen staff member must immediately bring the student to the CPO or Principal and convey what the student said. They should not investigate, question the student further, or tell other staff. They are a mandatory reporter; telling the CPO initiates the mandatory reporting chain.
