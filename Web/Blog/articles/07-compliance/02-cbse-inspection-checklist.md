## The CBSE Inspection: What to Expect

CBSE inspections are comprehensive evaluations that take 2-3 days and involve visiting classrooms, reviewing documents, and interviewing staff and students. The inspection team consists of education specialists who evaluate your school across four dimensions: infrastructure, staffing, academics, and governance. A passing score (70%+) means your affiliation is renewed for another 5 years.

Preparation matters enormously. Schools that prepare thoroughly pass with higher scores; those that scramble at the last minute often face complications.

## Pre-Inspection Timeline

### 12 Months Before Inspection
- Note the inspection year in your calendar.
- Initiate self-assessment against CBSE inspection rubric.
- Start addressing major gaps (infrastructure, staff qualifications).

### 6 Months Before Inspection
- Confirm inspection date from CBSE.
- Brief senior management and principal.
- Conduct internal mock inspection (identify gaps).
- Create detailed action plan to address gaps.

### 3 Months Before Inspection
- Major facility repairs completed.
- Staff qualifications finalized (all teachers must have B.Ed/D.Ed or equivalent).
- Curriculum plan finalized and documented.
- Documentation organized (in folders/files).

### 1 Month Before Inspection
- Conduct second mock inspection.
- Finalize all records and reports.
- Brief all staff on inspection protocol.
- Organize documentation in digital and physical format.

### 1 Week Before Inspection
- Final walkthrough of campus (check for cleanliness, signage, safety issues).
- Prepare welcome pack for inspectors (school brochure, key documents, contact info).
- Brief reception staff and security on inspection schedule.
- Remind teachers to have classroom records ready.

## CBSE Inspection Preparation Checklist

### A. INFRASTRUCTURE (25 Points)

#### Building & Classrooms
- [ ] Classrooms meet minimum size (1.25 sq. meters per student).
- [ ] All classrooms have adequate lighting and ventilation.
- [ ] Separate classrooms for each class (K-V and above).
- [ ] Building structural stability (get engineer's certificate if needed).
- [ ] Ramps for differently-abled access.
- [ ] Ground floor classrooms for Class I-II.

#### Labs & Specialist Spaces
- [ ] Science Lab: Equipment, chemicals, aprons, safety items present. Inventory record.
- [ ] Math Lab: Manipulatives, geometric instruments, teaching aids.
- [ ] Computer Lab: Computers functional; software licenses current. IT support arrangement in place.
- [ ] Language Lab (if applicable): Audio/visual equipment; software.
- [ ] Library: 1,000+ books; classification system (Dewey Decimal). Catalog and borrowing system.
- [ ] Staff Room: Comfortable space; attendance register; staff meeting minutes.
- [ ] Principal's Office: Well-organized; administrative records accessible.

#### Playground & Sports
- [ ] Playground area: Minimum 2,000 sq. meters or as per norms.
- [ ] Sports equipment available and safe.
- [ ] Sports coaching records (staff qualifications).

#### Sanitation & Health
- [ ] Separate toilet blocks for boys, girls, staff.
- [ ] At least 1 toilet per 50 students.
- [ ] Running water in all toilets.
- [ ] Soap and hand-sanitizer dispensers.
- [ ] Urinals functional and clean.
- [ ] Medical room with first aid kit.
- [ ] Water quality testing report (recent).
- [ ] Pest control inspection certificate (recent).
- [ ] Separate drinking water supply (tested and safe).

#### Safety & Emergency
- [ ] Fire extinguishers installed and regularly serviced.
- [ ] Emergency exits clearly marked.
- [ ] Fire evacuation drill conducted (minimum quarterly). Records documented.
- [ ] Emergency contact numbers posted.
- [ ] Security cameras and recording (if applicable).
- [ ] Visitor log maintained at gate.
- [ ] First aid-trained staff on campus.

#### Electricity & Utilities
- [ ] Electrical safety certificate.
- [ ] Backup power (generator) if in area with frequent outages.
- [ ] Meter reading records.
- [ ] Regular maintenance logs.

#### Cleanliness & Aesthetics
- [ ] Campus clean and well-maintained.
- [ ] Walls painted; no peeling paint or graffiti.
- [ ] Classrooms organized; displays of student work.
- [ ] Dustbins available throughout campus.
- [ ] Garden/green space maintained.

### B. STAFFING & HUMAN RESOURCES (25 Points)

#### Principal
- [ ] Bachelor's degree (copy on file).
- [ ] Teacher's training credential (B.Ed/D.Ed or equivalent).
- [ ] 5+ years teaching experience (certificate from previous employer).
- [ ] State recognition or CBSE exemption (documented).
- [ ] Performance evaluation (annual).

#### Teachers (All Classes)
- [ ] Bachelor's degree in subject.
- [ ] B.Ed/D.Ed or equivalent (Teacher training certificate).
- [ ] State recognition for trained teachers or CBSE exemption.
- [ ] Subject specialization matches classes taught (no subject mismatch).
- [ ] Police verification (background check certificate).
- [ ] Subject-wise break-down: Ensure no single teacher teaches all subjects in one class.

#### Specialist Staff
- [ ] Librarian: Diploma or Bachelor's in Library Science. Certification on file.
- [ ] Counselor: Master's in Psychology or counseling certification. Availability schedule.
- [ ] PE Teacher: Bachelor's in Physical Education or certification. Coaching record.
- [ ] Computer Instructor: Bachelor's in IT/CS or relevant certification.
- [ ] Art/Music Teachers: Qualifications and teaching experience.

#### Staff Records
- [ ] Staff strength meets prescribed ratios (1:30 for Class I-V, etc.).
- [ ] Staff attendance records (maintained and audited).
- [ ] Staff leave records (sick, casual, earned leave documented).
- [ ] Staff conduct and performance appraisals.
- [ ] Staff meeting minutes (documentation of academic discussions).
- [ ] Professional development: Training/workshop attendance records.

#### Staff Conduct
- [ ] No staff member has criminal record or child abuse allegation.
- [ ] Background verification completed for all staff.
- [ ] Police verification up-to-date.

### C. CURRICULUM & ACADEMICS (25 Points)

#### Curriculum Design
- [ ] Official NCERT curriculum adopted (copy on file for each class).
- [ ] Curriculum plan created (chapter breakdown by term and month).
- [ ] Curriculum plan distributed to teachers and parents.
- [ ] Alignment with CBSE syllabuses (especially Classes X and XII).

#### Curriculum Delivery
- [ ] Lesson plans prepared by teachers for each chapter/topic.
- [ ] Curriculum completion tracking (% covered by term).
- [ ] Minimum 80% curriculum covered by end of term.
- [ ] Teaching methodology includes: Collaborative learning, experiential learning, formative assessment.
- [ ] Use of teaching aids and educational technology.

#### Assessment & Evaluation
- [ ] Internal assessment policy documented.
- [ ] Multiple formative assessments (class tests, quizzes, projects) per term.
- [ ] Summative assessment (term-end exams) following CBSE pattern.
- [ ] Evaluation rubrics developed (for subjective subjects).
- [ ] Result analysis and identification of weak areas.
- [ ] Remedial teaching for students scoring below 40%.

#### Result Management
- [ ] Mark sheets prepared and verified.
- [ ] Grade calculation per CBSE norms.
- [ ] Passing criteria clearly defined and communicated.
- [ ] Result compilation and tabulation process documented.
- [ ] Distribution of mark sheets to parents with individual progress feedback.
- [ ] Annual result summary (pass percentage, toppers, subject-wise performance).

#### Board Exams (Class X & XII)
- [ ] List of Candidates (LOC) submitted to CBSE before exam schedule announcement.
- [ ] Admit cards prepared and issued to students.
- [ ] Invigilation duty roster prepared.
- [ ] Answer script handling and storage (secure).
- [ ] Result compilation and moderation.
- [ ] Pass out certificate generation and issue to students.

#### Co-Curricular Activities
- [ ] Sports program: Team participation in inter-school competitions.
- [ ] Cultural activities: Annual function, cultural competitions.
- [ ] Community service: Regular volunteer activities.
- [ ] Club activities: Debate, Science club, Environmental club, etc.
- [ ] Records of participation and achievements.

### D. GOVERNANCE & COMPLIANCE (25 Points)

#### School Policies
- [ ] Admission policy (non-discriminatory; transparent criteria).
- [ ] Anti-bullying and discipline policy (clearly defined punishment).
- [ ] Attendance policy (75% minimum required).
- [ ] Fee and concession policy (transparent; displayed publicly).
- [ ] Child protection and POCSO compliance policy.
- [ ] Data protection and privacy policy (DPDP Act 2023).
- [ ] Grievance redressal mechanism (documented, accessible).

#### RTE Act Compliance
- [ ] EWS seat allocation (25% of Class I).
- [ ] Fee concession provided to EWS students (documented).
- [ ] RTE admissions tracked separately.
- [ ] RTE financial support records (government reimbursement).

#### Child Protection
- [ ] Child Protection Officer (CPO) designated and trained.
- [ ] CPO name and contact displayed (on website, notice board).
- [ ] Complaint register (confidential, secure).
- [ ] Staff trained on POCSO Act and mandatory reporting.
- [ ] Counselor available for student support.
- [ ] Records of complaints and resolutions.

#### Data Protection (DPDP Act 2023)
- [ ] Privacy policy published and available to parents.
- [ ] Parental consent obtained for data collection (digitally or on paper).
- [ ] Consent registry maintained with dates.
- [ ] Data breach response plan documented.
- [ ] Data retention policy (how long data is kept).
- [ ] Staff confidentiality agreements signed.
- [ ] Audit trail maintained (access logs).

#### Financial Management
- [ ] Budget prepared and approved by management.
- [ ] Fee structure transparent (display on website, school gate).
- [ ] Financial records audited annually (external auditor).
- [ ] Audit report filed with school management.
- [ ] Bank statements reconciled monthly.
- [ ] No irregular expenditures or discrepancies.
- [ ] Scholarships and concessions documented.

#### Record Keeping
- [ ] Student information system (manual or digital).
- [ ] Attendance registers (daily updates).
- [ ] Progress cards and report cards (structured format).
- [ ] Permanent records (not lost or damaged).
- [ ] Secure storage (locked cabinets or encrypted digital system).
- [ ] Archival of records per retention policy.

#### Parent & Community Engagement
- [ ] School management committee (SMC) functioning regularly.
- [ ] Parent-teacher meetings (PTM) at least twice per year.
- [ ] Parent feedback mechanism (survey, suggestion box).
- [ ] School website updated regularly (information is current).
- [ ] Parent handbook provided (policies and procedures).
- [ ] Open communication with parents (responsive to inquiries).

#### Health & Hygiene
- [ ] Health check-up camp (annual or twice yearly).
- [ ] Vaccination records maintained.
- [ ] School meals (if applicable) follow nutritional standards and FSSAI compliance.
- [ ] Allergy and medication information recorded for each student.
- [ ] Infectious disease protocol (documented responses to illness outbreaks).

## Documentation Organization for Inspection

Create a master file/folder containing:
1. **School Overview:** History, vision, mission, achievements.
2. **Infrastructure:** Photos and certificates (building, labs, playgrounds, safety audits).
3. **Staff Records:** Qualifications, verifications, performance appraisals.
4. **Curriculum:** Approved curriculum plan, lesson plans, syllabus coverage.
5. **Academics:** Sample mark sheets, result analysis, admission criteria.
6. **Policies:** All school policies (displayed and documented).
7. **Compliance:** RTE records, child protection records, data protection records.
8. **Finance:** Annual audit report, fee structure, financial statements.
9. **Parent Communication:** Sample newsletters, PTM records, feedback.
10. **Student Records:** Few sample student files (admission to report card).

Organize physically in labeled folders and digitally in a searchable format. Inspectors should be able to find any document in <2 minutes.

## FAQ

**Q1: How many days does a CBSE inspection take?**
A: Typically 2-3 days for schools with Classes I-XII. Smaller schools or those with Classes I-VI might finish in 1-2 days.

**Q2: Who conducts the CBSE inspection?**
A: A team of 2-3 CBSE-appointed inspectors, usually experienced educators with subject expertise.

**Q3: Can schools request to postpone an inspection?**
A: Rarely. CBSE sets the inspection schedule; postponement is allowed only for emergencies (natural disaster, major facility repair). Submit a formal request with justification.

**Q4: What happens if a school doesn't pass inspection?**
A: The school receives provisional affiliation (1 year) and must address all gaps. Re-inspection is scheduled. If re-inspection is also failed, affiliation is cancelled.

**Q5: Are parents invited to be present during inspection?**
A: Not typically. Inspectors meet with staff and students, but parent presence is not required or expected.

**Q6: How should schools handle inspector questions about staff misconduct or complaints?**
A: Be honest and transparent. Have documentation of any complaints and actions taken. Inspectors evaluate how fairly and professionally the school handled issues, not whether issues never occurred.
