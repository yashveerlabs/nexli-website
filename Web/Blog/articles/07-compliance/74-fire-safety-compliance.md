Fire safety compliance for schools involves multiple overlapping requirements: a Fire No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the state Fire Services, annual maintenance of fire-fighting equipment, fire drills, and compliance with building fire safety norms. CBSE affiliation requires a current fire safety certificate.

A school fire emergency is an extreme risk due to the number of children in a confined space. Fire safety compliance is not bureaucratic, it is directly connected to student safety.

## Fire NOC: What It Is and Who Issues It

A Fire NOC (No Objection Certificate) is issued by the State Fire Services after verifying that a building meets fire safety norms. For schools, the Fire NOC covers:
- Fire exits: adequate width, clear of obstruction, marked with signage
- Emergency lighting on exit routes
- Fire alarm system (manual call points and automatic detection for larger buildings)
- Fire extinguishers: adequate number, correct type (Class A for general, CO2 for electrical), correct placement
- Hose reel systems (for larger buildings)
- Sprinkler systems (for buildings above a specified height, as per state fire code)
- Assembly point marked outside the building

**Who issues:** State Fire Services or the designated fire authority for the area.

**Validity:** Typically 1-3 years. Schools must renew before expiry.

## Fire Equipment Maintenance

### Fire Extinguishers
- Annual AMC (Annual Maintenance Contract) with a certified fire equipment vendor
- Extinguishers must be physically inspected monthly (check that pin is sealed, pressure is within range, extinguisher is not damaged)
- Refilled and re-certified annually (or after use)
- Maintenance records maintained with date, technician name, and outcome

### Fire Alarm System
- Annual service contract with alarm system vendor
- Quarterly function testing of alarm system
- Immediate repair of any defective detectors or call points

### Emergency Lighting
- Monthly test of emergency lighting (confirm it activates on power failure)
- Annual service by qualified electrician

## Fire Drills

Fire drills are the most neglected fire safety requirement. Schools must conduct fire drills:
- At least twice a year (many fire codes specify this; CBSE expects it)
- Unannounced drills are more effective than announced ones
- Drill procedure: alarm activation, students evacuate in orderly manner via designated routes, assembly at designated assembly point, roll call to confirm all accounted for

**Post-drill review:**
- Time taken for full evacuation
- Any confusion or blockages in exit routes
- Any students who didn't know what to do
- Improvements needed before next drill

**Documentation:** Date, time, evacuation time, number of students, issues identified, and sign-off by Principal.

## Staff Training

All staff must know:
- Location and operation of nearest fire extinguisher
- Which fire exit they and their class should use
- Assembly point location
- Who calls the fire services (one designated person, not everyone)
- First steps on discovering a fire: activate alarm, evacuate, call 101

## CBSE Fire Safety Requirement

CBSE requires submission of a current fire safety NOC as part of affiliation documentation. During inspections, CBSE inspectors check:
- Fire NOC validity
- Fire extinguisher maintenance records
- Evidence of fire drills conducted

## FAQ

**Q: How many fire extinguishers does a school building need?**

A: Fire codes typically specify 1 extinguisher per 200 sq meters of floor area, or one per floor at minimum. The exact requirement depends on the state fire code. The fire safety inspection will specify the required number.

**Q: Can a school conduct fire drills without disrupting teaching significantly?**

A: Drills typically take 10-15 minutes and are most effective when conducted during class time. The disruption is minimal and the benefit significant. Schedule drills at different times of day so students practice evacuating from different locations.

**Q: What if the Fire Services takes a long time to renew the NOC?**

A: Initiate the renewal application 3-6 months before expiry. If the Fire Services is delayed and the NOC expires through no fault of the school, maintain documentation of the timely application. Do not let the NOC lapse without initiating renewal.

**Q: Must the fire drill record be submitted to any authority?**

A: Fire drill records are typically required to be available for inspection by fire authorities and CBSE inspectors. They do not need to be proactively submitted but must be maintained and ready for inspection.
