A female employee at a school who experiences sexual harassment at work has the right to file a complaint with the school's Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) under the POSH Act 2013. The process is designed to be accessible and confidential.

Schools must ensure every female employee knows this process and can access it without fear of retaliation.

## Who Can File

- Any woman employed at the school: teachers, administrative staff, support staff, contract workers
- Interns, trainees, or volunteers working at the school
- In some situations, a relative or friend of the complainant can file if the complainant is physically or mentally incapacitated

## What Constitutes Sexual Harassment Under POSH

The complaint must relate to sexual harassment as defined under POSH:
- Unwelcome physical contact or advances
- Demand or request for sexual favors (explicitly or implicitly)
- Sexually colored remarks, jokes, or innuendo
- Displaying sexually explicit material
- Any other unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature

The conduct must be unwelcome: a mutual relationship is not covered under POSH.

## Filing Timeline

A complaint must be filed within **3 months** of the incident. If there are multiple incidents, the 3-month period runs from the date of the last incident.

The ICC can extend this period by another 3 months if it is satisfied that the complainant had sufficient reason for the delay.

## What to Include in the Complaint

The complaint should be in writing (the ICC provides a form, or the complainant can write a letter). It should include:

- The complainant's name, designation, and contact
- The respondent's name and designation
- Details of the incident(s): dates, locations, description of what happened
- Names of any witnesses (if any)
- Any documentary evidence: messages, emails, screenshots, medical records, etc.
- The remedy being sought (transfer, disciplinary action, compensation)

The ICC must treat the complaint seriously even if it is brief or incomplete. The Presiding Officer can assist the complainant in completing the documentation.

## Confidentiality

The complainant's identity, the contents of the complaint, and the identity of the respondent must remain confidential throughout the process. Breaching confidentiality is a violation under POSH and can result in penalties for the person who discloses.

## What Happens After Filing

1. ICC acknowledges receipt and assigns a case number
2. ICC notifies the respondent and shares the complaint (or a summary)
3. Respondent has the opportunity to submit a written reply
4. ICC conducts inquiry: interviews complainant, respondent, and witnesses
5. ICC issues findings within 90 days
6. If complaint is substantiated: ICC recommends disciplinary action to management
7. Management must implement the recommendation within 60 days

## Concurrent Complaint Options

Filing with the ICC does not prevent the complainant from also filing a complaint with police (if the behavior constitutes a criminal offence under IPC or POCSO). The ICC and criminal proceedings run in parallel.

## Protection from Retaliation

POSH prohibits retaliation against a complainant for having filed a complaint. If the complainant experiences retaliation (transfer, hostile work environment, demotion), this is a separate violation that should be reported to the ICC.

## FAQ

**Q: Can a female employee file a POSH complaint against a colleague who is also a woman?**

A: The POSH Act covers sexual harassment of women by any person. It is not limited to male respondents. A woman can file a complaint against another woman under POSH if the conduct meets the definition.

**Q: What if the complainant does not want to file a formal complaint but wants advice?**

A: The ICC should provide a confidential consultation to employees who want to understand their options. A consultation does not trigger a formal investigation unless the complainant decides to proceed.

**Q: Can the complaint be filed verbally?**

A: The complaint should be in writing. If the complainant is unable to write it, the ICC Presiding Officer can assist in recording it, with the complainant's signature or thumb impression.

**Q: What if the complainant wants to withdraw the complaint after filing?**

A: The complainant can request withdrawal. The ICC must decide whether to continue the inquiry (if there is strong evidence suggesting others may be at risk) or close the case. Withdrawal does not mean dismissal in all circumstances.
