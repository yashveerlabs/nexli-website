A data processing register (also called a data inventory or Record of Processing Activities) documents every type of personal data a school collects, why it is collected, who processes it, how long it is kept, and how it is protected. Under DPDP Act 2023, this register is the foundation of the school's compliance framework.

## What the Data Processing Register Must Include

For each data type, record:
- **Data category:** Student academic, medical, financial, biometric, etc.
- **Purpose:** Admission, attendance tracking, fee management, health monitoring.
- **Data subjects:** Students, parents, staff.
- **Who processes it:** Admission team, teachers, finance, nurse.
- **Third parties with access:** Government agencies, payment gateways, transport operators.
- **Retention period:** Duration of enrollment plus X years for each category.
- **Protection measures:** Encryption, access control, physical lock.
- **Consent status:** Consented, exception (legal requirement), or pending.

## Sample Register Entry

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Data Type | Student Medical Records |
| Purpose | Health emergency response, annual health screening |
| Data Subjects | Students (all enrolled) |
| Processor | School Nurse, Principal |
| Third Party Access | External doctor (if called) |
| Retention | Duration of enrollment + 1 year |
| Protection | Encrypted in ERP, locked cabinet for paper copies |
| Consent | Explicit consent collected at admission |

## How Often to Update the Register

Update the register when:
- A new data type is collected.
- A new system is introduced.
- A third party gains access to school data.
- A data type's retention period changes.
- A purpose for data collection changes.

Annual review is minimum; review after any significant system change.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the data processing register a public document?**

A: No. It is an internal compliance document. The school's Privacy Notice (a summary) is public; the full register is for internal use and regulator inspection.

**Q: How does the register differ from the consent registry?**

A: The consent registry tracks which parents consented to what. The data processing register tracks what data exists, who has it, and why. Both are needed for full compliance.

**Q: What if the school uses multiple third-party systems (ERP, payment gateway, transport app)?**

A: Each third-party system should have its own register entry. Review their data processing agreements to confirm what data each receives and how they protect it.
