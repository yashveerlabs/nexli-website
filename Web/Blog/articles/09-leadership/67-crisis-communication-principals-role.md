# Crisis Communication: Principal's Role

In crisis, communication prevents panic and misinformation. Clear, timely messaging is essential.

**Before Crisis** - Prepare statements, identify spokespersons, media contacts list, communication protocols, roles assigned, chains of command, regular updates planned.

**First Communication** - Immediate notification to stakeholders, accurate information shared, acknowledge situation, reassurance about safety, next steps communicated.

**Ongoing Updates** - Regular updates, new information shared, timeline for recovery, support resources available, next planned communication, accurate information priority.

**Transparency** - Honest about what happened, what you know/don't know, what you're doing, root cause investigation, prevention measures, community trust building.

**Empathy and Support** - Acknowledge impact, show care, counseling resources, support services, community rallies, collective healing, compassion demonstrated.

**Media Management** - Designate spokesperson, controlled messaging, no gossip, accurate quotes, consistent information, media inquiries handled professionally.

**Follow-up Communication** - Updates on investigation, actions taken, policy changes, prevention measures, moving forward, community reassurance.

**Documentation** - Record communications, decisions made, timeline, evidence, lessons learned, future improvement, accountability maintained.

Clear crisis communication maintains trust and enables effective response.
