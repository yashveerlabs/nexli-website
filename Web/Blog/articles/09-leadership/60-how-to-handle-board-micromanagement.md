# How to Handle Board Micromanagement

Board micromanagement undermines principal leadership. Address diplomatically and firmly.

Understand why. Fear? Lack of confidence? Past issues? Too involved? Boundary confusion? Understand root cause first. Don't assume intentional.

Set boundaries gently. "I'm happy to report on that. Those operational decisions are my authority." Professional, not defensive. Clarity on roles. Respectful correction.

Provide transparency. Micromanagement often stems from lack of information. Share more. Monthly reports. Board meetings cover operations. Proactive communication reduces interference.

Educate board. Governance vs. operations. Role clarity. What board should focus on (strategy, policy, oversight). What principal manages (daily operations). Suggest training.

Build trust. Deliver results. Follow through on commitments. Transparent about challenges. Proactive problem-solving. Competence demonstrated.

Don't be defensive. When board questions decisions, welcome discussion. Explain reasoning. Provide data. Open to feedback. Show respect for oversight role.

Stand firm on principles. Some things not negotiable. Student safety. Academic integrity. Professional standards. Firmness with respect. "I appreciate input, but I must..." Clear boundaries.

Request private discussion if public pressure. Don't argue in board meeting. Private conversation about appropriate authority. Professional tone. Problem-solving focused.

Document decisions. Your reasoning. Data considered. How you consulted. Creates record. Shows sound decision-making. Defends against future questions.

Know when to escalate. Persistent micromanagement affecting school. Superintendent involvement. HR or legal if necessary. Last resort. Exhaust other options first.

Balance respect for board with leadership confidence. Appropriate collaboration. Clear boundaries. Professional interaction.
