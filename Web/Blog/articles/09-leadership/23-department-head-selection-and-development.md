# Department Head Selection and Development

Department heads are the backbone of academic leadership. They translate school vision to classroom practice and lead professional growth within their departments.

## Role of Department Head

**Academic Leadership**
- Oversee curriculum in their subject
- Ensure alignment to standards
- Foster instructional improvement
- Assessment and data analysis
- Support for struggling teachers
- Development of department practices

**Administrative**
- Department budget and resources
- Schedule and timetable
- Staffing needs and coverage
- Facility and equipment needs
- Communication with families
- Student progression and course placement

**People Leadership**
- Support colleagues
- Facilitate collaboration
- Professional development
- Performance feedback
- Mentoring emerging leaders
- Building department culture

**School Leadership**
- Participate in leadership team
- Contribute to school-wide initiatives
- Represent department in decision-making
- Cross-department collaboration
- School culture and climate

## Selecting Department Heads

**Qualifications**
- Excellent teacher (5+ years minimum)
- Deep expertise in subject area
- Respected by colleagues
- People skills and emotional intelligence
- Organizational ability
- Strategic thinking (not just tactical)
- Communication skills
- Leadership potential
- Commitment to continuous improvement

**Selection Process**

**Application**
- Interested teachers apply
- Statement of vision for department
- Experience and skills relevant
- Leadership philosophy
- How they'd move department forward

**Interview**
- Leadership questions
- Scenario questions
- Vision for department
- How they'd support colleagues
- How they'd ensure quality
- How they'd grow professionally
- Why they want this role

**Peer Input**
- Other teachers in department provide input
- Often the most important factor
- Will work together daily
- Trust is essential
- Teachers should have voice

**Principal Assessment**
- Final decision rests with principal
- Consider all input
- Best qualified leader
- Right fit with school vision
- Person who will contribute to school leadership

## Onboarding Department Head

**First Meetings**
- Clear role description
- What principal expects
- How they'll be supported
- Introduction to school budget and planning
- Overview of school initiatives
- Key issues in department
- Other department heads

**Early Priorities**
- Meet with each department member
- Understand current state
- Listen to concerns and ideas
- Build relationships
- Learn department culture
- Identify quick wins
- Start building vision

**First Year Focus**
- Build trust with colleagues
- Understand department deeply
- Diagnose key issues
- Build relationships with principal
- Participate in school leadership
- Lead one or two improvements
- Build relationships with other departments

## Developing Department Heads Into Leaders

**Strengthen Academic Leadership**
- Coach on instructional improvement
- Observation and feedback skills
- Data analysis and interpretation
- Difficult conversations about performance
- Budget and resource management
- Strategic curriculum planning

**Build Leadership Skills**
- Emotional intelligence
- Decision-making
- Conflict resolution
- Change leadership
- Communication
- Delegation
- Strategic thinking

**Professional Development**
- Department head training/workshops
- Subject matter expertise development
- Leadership courses or certificates
- Coaching or mentoring
- Reading and study groups
- Conference attendance
- Networking with other department heads

**Formal Feedback**
- Annual evaluation
- Regular check-ins (monthly or quarterly)
- 360-degree feedback from colleagues
- Development goals
- Recognition of progress
- Support for growth
- Honest feedback on challenges

## Effective Department Meetings

**Purpose**
- Collaborative problem-solving
- Professional learning
- Curriculum alignment
- Student support
- Department culture
- Communication and updates

**Frequency**
- Monthly minimum
- Bi-weekly ideal
- More frequent if needed
- Consistent day and time
- Protected time

**Structure**
- Agenda shared in advance
- Mix of updates and decision-making
- Collaborative work time
- Professional learning
- Celebrations and recognition
- Ending time honored

**Distributed Leadership**
- Not all leadership from department head
- Rotate meeting facilitation
- Different teachers take leadership roles
- Share responsibility for culture
- Develop emerging leaders
- Celebrate diverse contributions

## Department Head-Principal Partnership

**Regular Communication**
- Monthly or quarterly formal meetings
- Casual check-ins
- Open door policy
- Advance notice of issues
- Collaborative problem-solving

**Principal Support**
- Backup on difficult decisions
- Support for teacher performance issues
- Resources for improvement work
- Protection from excessive demands
- Career development support
- Mentoring and coaching

**Clear Expectations**
- What principal needs from department head
- How success is measured
- What decisions they make independently
- What needs principal consultation
- Escalation process
- Evaluation criteria

**Trust and Autonomy**
- Principal trusts their leadership
- Supports their decisions publicly
- Doesn't undermine authority
- Gives them autonomy
- Helps them succeed
- Has their back with teachers

## Measuring Department Head Effectiveness

**Academic Metrics**
- Student learning outcomes in subject
- Achievement growth
- Advanced placement participation
- Student engagement and satisfaction
- College preparation (if relevant)

**Operational Metrics**
- Budget management
- Attendance and participation
- Resource utilization
- Safety and compliance
- Efficiency

**People Metrics**
- Teacher satisfaction
- Teacher retention
- Professional growth
- Collaboration and culture
- New teacher integration
- Teacher leadership development

**School Contribution**
- School-wide initiative participation
- Cross-department collaboration
- School goal contribution
- Leadership presence
- Cultural contribution

## Advancement and Retention

**Principal Track**
- Some department heads want to become principals
- Create pathway for advancement
- Mentoring and development
- Preparation for principal role
- Support for interviews and moves
- Succession planning

**Specialist Track**
- Some prefer deep expertise over administration
- Create teacher specialist roles
- Recognition of expertise
- Leadership opportunities without administration
- Career advancement possible
- Different but valued pathway

**Long-term Department Head**
- May stay in role many years
- Continue growing and developing
- Take on additional school leadership
- Deepen expertise
- Mentor others
- Respected elder statespeople

## Common Challenges

**Not Ready for Role**
- Honest conversation about gaps
- Support and development
- Clear expectations
- May need to release from role
- Different person may be better fit
- No shame in trying and learning

**Resistance to Change**
- Department heads protect status quo
- Need to balance stability and improvement
- Facilitate change thoughtfully
- Help them lead improvement
- Address resistance directly
- School direction requires change

**Overload**
- Too many responsibilities
- Too many school initiatives
- Competing demands on time
- Help them prioritize
- Remove lower priorities
- Clarify what matters most

**Interpersonal Issues**
- Conflict with colleagues
- Can't handle difficult conversations
- Personality clash with principal
- Address directly
- Coaching and support
- May require change

Department heads are multipliers of principal leadership. Invest in developing them well. They'll improve your school far more than you could alone.
