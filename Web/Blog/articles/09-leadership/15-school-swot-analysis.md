# School SWOT Analysis

SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) is a foundational planning tool. It gives you a realistic view of your current position and helps you plan strategically.

## What SWOT Tells You

**Strengths** → What to leverage
**Weaknesses** → What to address
**Opportunities** → What to pursue
**Threats** → What to prevent or mitigate

## Conducting Your SWOT Analysis

**Step 1: Gather Input**
- Leadership team
- Faculty meetings
- Parent focus groups
- Student representatives
- Individual interviews
- External stakeholder feedback

**Step 2: Brainstorm Each Category**
- Generate lots of ideas
- No critique during brainstorming
- Be honest about weaknesses
- Dream big about opportunities
- Be realistic about threats

**Step 3: Organize and Deepen**
- Group similar ideas
- Add specific examples
- Include supporting data
- Prioritize by impact
- Connect to mission and goals

**Step 4: Synthesize**
- Summarize key points in each category
- Identify patterns across categories
- Note interconnections
- Prepare for strategic planning

## Strengths to Identify

Internal capabilities that serve your mission:

**Academic**
- Strong test scores in certain subjects
- Innovative curriculum in an area
- Highly qualified teaching staff
- Student engagement and participation
- Academic culture of excellence

**People**
- Dedicated, collaborative staff
- Strong principal leadership
- Engaged parent community
- Supportive board
- Student body with diversity and talents

**Operational**
- Good facilities and resources
- Strong systems and processes
- Financial stability
- Reputation and brand
- Strong community relationships

**Cultural**
- Positive school climate
- Strong sense of community
- Clear values and culture
- Student leadership opportunities
- Safe, inclusive environment

## Weaknesses to Address

Internal limitations that work against your mission:

**Academic**
- Weak performance in certain subjects
- Limited curriculum diversity
- Inconsistent teaching quality
- Limited support for struggling learners
- Gaps in technology or resources for learning

**People**
- High staff turnover
- Limited teacher development opportunities
- Parent engagement challenges
- Insufficient leadership depth
- Insufficient student leadership opportunities

**Operational**
- Inadequate facilities or maintenance
- Weak financial systems or reserves
- Poor communication systems
- Inefficient processes
- Compliance or regulatory gaps

**Cultural**
- Negative school climate
- Lack of clear values or culture
- Low sense of community
- Safety concerns
- Exclusionary practices

## Opportunities to Pursue

External possibilities you could leverage:

**Market/Growth**
- Growing enrollment in your region
- Underserved student population
- Demand for your particular educational approach
- New facilities or expansion possibility
- Potential partnerships or affiliations

**Academic**
- New curriculum or teaching methods emerging
- Technology offering new learning possibilities
- Research-based practices you could implement
- Staff expertise you could develop
- Student interests you could build on

**Partnerships**
- University partnerships for student experiences
- Business partnerships for career exploration
- Community organizations for enrichment
- Other schools for collaboration
- Government initiatives or funding

**Community**
- Growing community support
- Increasing recognition/reputation
- Alumni engagement possibilities
- Government relationships
- Media coverage opportunities

## Threats to Address

External challenges or obstacles:

**Competitive**
- New school opening in your area
- School closure possibility if enrollment drops
- Competitors with stronger reputation
- Private schools attracting your families
- Online schooling as alternative

**Economic**
- Economic downturn reducing enrollment
- Family financial pressures affecting participation
- Difficulty attracting staff due to salary competition
- Budget cuts or funding challenges
- Increased costs for facilities or services

**Regulatory/Political**
- Education policy changes
- New testing or accountability requirements
- Regulatory compliance costs
- Political instability
- Changing government priorities

**Social/Cultural**
- Changing family needs or preferences
- Technology disrupting traditional schooling
- Demographic shifts
- Cultural changes affecting values
- Health or safety concerns (pandemic, violence, etc.)

## Using Your SWOT

**Leverage Strengths**
- Build on what works
- Use strengths to address weaknesses
- Highlight strengths in marketing
- Develop strengths further

**Address Weaknesses**
- Prioritize based on impact
- Create action plans for key weaknesses
- Use strengths to address weaknesses when possible
- Allocate resources to improvement
- Track progress

**Pursue Opportunities**
- Evaluate which align with mission
- Assess required resources
- Plan entry strategy
- Test before scaling
- Measure impact

**Mitigate Threats**
- Develop contingency plans
- Take preventive action
- Monitor emerging threats
- Build resilience
- Have backup plans

## SWOT Analysis Example: A Growing School

**Strengths**
- Excellent academic reputation
- Dedicated, experienced staff
- Strong parent community
- Good financial position
- Innovative STEM program

**Weaknesses**
- Limited space for growth
- Weak arts and music programs
- Limited support services for students with special needs
- Aging facilities in some areas
- Limited diversity

**Opportunities**
- Growing enrollment demand
- Expansion into arts education
- New multipurpose facility funding possibility
- Partnership with special education center
- Community partnerships for enrichment

**Threats**
- Overcrowding if too many enroll
- New competing school opening nearby
- Economic downturn affecting family affordability
- Teacher turnover due to low salaries vs. competitive schools
- Increased special education obligations

From this SWOT, strategic priorities might be:
1. Address facility limitations through expansion planning
2. Build arts program to round out education
3. Strengthen diversity and inclusion
4. Improve teacher compensation to reduce turnover
5. Develop special education capacity

SWOT analysis isn't a perfect science, but it provides a structured way to think about your school's situation and make strategic choices.
