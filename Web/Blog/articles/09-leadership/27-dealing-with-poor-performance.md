# Dealing with Poor Performance

Addressing poor performance is difficult but necessary. Avoiding it hurts your school and ultimately doesn't help the struggling person either.

## Recognizing Performance Issues

**What Indicates Poor Performance**
- Student achievement lower than expected
- Inconsistent or inadequate lesson planning
- Student behavior problems in their classroom
- Parents complaining about teaching quality
- Attendance or punctuality issues
- Failure to follow school procedures
- Lack of professional growth
- Negative attitude or disengagement
- Not meeting job expectations
- Impact on school culture

**Distinguish From Other Issues**
- Bad day or week vs. pattern
- Personal struggle vs. job performance issue
- Different style vs. inadequate performance
- Need for feedback vs. need for support
- Struggling but trying vs. not trying
- Context matters (new teacher, difficult year)

## Typical Performance Issues

**Instructional Quality**
- Weak teaching practices
- Low student engagement
- Poor classroom management
- Inconsistent assessment
- Not meeting standards

**Student Relationships**
- Cold or distant with students
- Unfair or harsh discipline
- Favorites and unfairness
- Negative environment
- Damaging relationships

**Professional Behavior**
- Unreliability or absence
- Insubordination
- Negativity and complaining
- Not following procedures
- Disrespect to others

**Professional Growth**
- Refusing to learn new approaches
- Not implementing feedback
- Dismissive of professional development
- Stuck in old ways
- Resistant to change

## Initial Steps

**Observe and Document**
- Classroom observations focused on specific concern
- Specific, descriptive notes (not judgmental)
- Collect data over time
- Student work samples
- Parent feedback
- Colleague input if relevant
- Pattern analysis (isolated incident or pattern?)

**Professional Conversation**
- Private, confidential setting
- Specific, kind feedback
- Focus on observable behaviors and impact
- Give them chance to explain
- Listen to their perspective
- Identify obstacles they face
- Collaborative problem-solving tone
- Not accusatory or punitive

**Conversation Script**
"I want to talk with you about [specific situation]. I've observed [specific behaviors] in [your classroom/interactions/work]. I'm concerned because [impact on students/school]. Help me understand what's going on."

**Listen and Understand**
- Do they understand the concern?
- Do they agree it's an issue?
- What's their perspective?
- Are there obstacles or challenges?
- Do they have capacity to improve?
- What support might help?
- Do they want to improve?

## Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Only use if:
- Clear performance deficiency
- Coaching hasn't worked
- More formal intervention needed
- Want to give genuine opportunity to improve

**Elements of PIP**
- Clear performance goal (specific and measurable)
- Timeline (usually 30-60 days initially)
- Specific strategies and actions teacher will take
- Support you will provide (coaching, training, resources)
- How progress will be measured
- Check-in meetings (usually weekly)
- Consequences if not improving
- Written documentation
- Teacher signature (showing understanding, not necessarily agreement)

**PIP Example: Classroom Management**

**Performance Issue**: Classroom environment is chaotic; students are off-task; behavior referrals are high

**Goal**: By [date], teacher will:
- Establish and teach clear classroom procedures
- Give consistent consequences for rule violations
- Maintain engaged, on-task environment minimum 80% of observation time

**Strategies Teacher Will Use**
- Teach classroom procedures explicitly (first week)
- Use behavior tracking sheet
- Implement consistent positive reinforcement
- Attend classroom management professional development
- Observe effective teacher
- Video self and reflect

**Support I Will Provide**
- Coaching on classroom procedures (2 meetings)
- Modeling of procedures and expectations
- Observation and feedback weekly
- Video clips of effective strategies
- Professional development attendance support
- Flexibility on other obligations

**How We'll Measure Progress**
- Classroom observations (weekly)
- Behavior referral data
- Student engagement surveys
- Teacher reflection notes
- Student feedback

**Timeline**
- Week 1-2: Training and setup
- Week 3-6: Implementation with weekly feedback
- Week 7: Assessment of progress
- Decision: Improving toward goal (continue) vs. not improving (escalate to formal discipline)

**Check-In Meetings**
- Weekly, 30 minutes
- Review data and progress
- Problem-solve obstacles
- Provide feedback and support
- Celebrate progress
- Adjust if needed

**Consequences**
- If improving: Continue or move to maintenance phase
- If not improving: Formal documentation, possible reassignment or termination process

## Supporting Improvement

**Provide Real Support**
- Not punishment disguised as support
- Genuine coaching and resources
- Time and space to practice
- Feedback and encouragement
- High expectations AND belief in capacity
- Specific, actionable feedback

**Remove Obstacles**
- If lack of resources: provide them
- If lack of knowledge: train them
- If lack of time: reduce other demands
- If personal issues: offer support
- If struggling: provide coaching
- If not capable: honest conversation

**Celebrate Progress**
- Acknowledge improvement
- Recognize effort
- Point out specific progress
- Build confidence
- Encourage momentum
- Create positive momentum

## If Not Improving

**Honest Assessment**
- Is real effort happening?
- Is there genuine improvement?
- Is progress sufficient?
- Are they approaching goal?
- Will they likely reach it?
- Is this sustainable?
- What's realistic?

**Decision Time**
- Extend PIP if close to goal
- Move to formal discipline process
- Reassign to different role
- Suggest leaving/resignation
- Termination process (with HR and legal)
- Acceptance that they're not right fit

**If Choosing to Move On**
- May offer resignation opportunity (graceful exit)
- Severance package negotiation
- Reference discussions
- Professional conduct through transition
- Get replacement
- Announce transition to school

**If Formal Discipline/Termination**
- Significant documentation required
- HR involvement essential
- Legal requirements vary by location
- Due process important
- Written notice required
- Specific expectations
- Formal meeting and documentation
- Possible progressive discipline

## Legal and HR Considerations

**Consult HR/Legal**
- Before PIP if possible
- Before termination definitely
- Documentation requirements
- Due process requirements
- Union agreements if applicable
- At-will vs. contract employment
- Grounds for termination

**Documentation**
- Keep detailed records
- Specific examples with dates
- Observations and feedback given
- Support provided
- Progress (or lack thereof)
- All conversations documented
- Fair and consistent process

**Due Process**
- Clear expectations
- Opportunity to improve
- Fair evaluation
- Notice of deficiency
- Support provided
- Opportunity to respond
- Consistency with how others are treated

## The Bigger Picture

**Sometimes It's Hard**
- Confronting performance is uncomfortable
- Fear of conflict
- Doubt about your own judgment
- Guilt or compassion
- Worry about legality
- Fear of retaliation

**But It's Necessary**
- You owe it to students
- You owe it to school
- You owe it to staff who work hard
- You owe it to the struggling person (staying in role they're not good at helps no one)
- School culture depends on accountability
- Results improve when expectations are clear

**Approach with Compassion**
- This is difficult for them
- Failure is painful
- Loss of job is significant
- Treat with dignity and respect
- Honest conversation is kinder than slow fade
- Support them through process
- Help them find better fit

Poor performance affects everyone. Addressing it promptly, fairly, and with support is one of your most important leadership responsibilities. It's also one of the hardest.
