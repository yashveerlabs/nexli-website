# How to Navigate Educational Politics

Politics are part of education. Navigate skillfully to advance your mission.

**Understand Power Dynamics** - Who has influence? Who opposes? What's at stake? Factions present? Alliances? Interests driving decisions? Map the landscape.

**Build Relationships** - Individual connections matter, personal trust, mutual respect, find common ground, relationships before needing help, genuine connection.

**Choose Battles** - Not every fight is yours, select high-impact battles, concede on lower priorities, strategy not emotion-driven, know when to let go.

**Use Formal Processes** - Work through established channels, honor procedures, document everything, professionalism, fair process, appropriate escalation.

**Frame Arguments** - Appeal to shared values, connect to mission, data-driven logic, research-based, evidence compelling, speak their language.

**Build Coalitions** - Find allies, others share your goals, collective voice stronger, united front, build consensus, power in numbers.

**Don't Gossip** - Maintain professionalism, don't participate in rumor-mill, discretion, integrity, reputation for trustworthiness, respect confidences.

**Play Long Game** - Think about long-term relationships, don't burn bridges, future collaboration possible, maintain respect even in disagreement, professional always.

**Know Limits** - Some battles unwinnable, some hills not worth dying on, focus on what you can control, accept what you can't, wisdom in discernment.

**Maintain Integrity** - Don't compromise principles for politics, ethical leadership, example for others, consistency, values-driven decisions.

Political skill is necessary for effective leadership. Use it ethically.
