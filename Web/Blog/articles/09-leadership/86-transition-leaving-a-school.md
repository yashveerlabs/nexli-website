# Transition: Leaving a School

When you leave a school, manage transition professionally to protect school and legacy.

**Timing** - Appropriate notice (90 days minimum recommended), allows time to recruit replacement, proper transitions, staff adjustment, early communication better.

**Documentation** - Leave clear documentation, procedures documented, key contacts listed, ongoing projects status, financial information, successor knows systems.

**Transition Planning** - Work with board/superintendent on successor, mentor if possible, introduce new principal to key people, ensure continuity.

**Communication** - Inform board first, then staff, then parents, then community, consistent messaging, gratitude expressed, continuity assured, celebration of time together.

**Knowledge Transfer** - Spend time with successor, explain school culture, introduce to key relationships, share what's working, lessons learned, set them up for success.

**Completion** - Finish high-priority projects, don't leave land mines, clean handoff, professional conduct throughout, positive departure.

**Emotional Processing** - Process leaving, it may be emotional, relationships developed, impact on people, gratitude for learning, healthy goodbye.

**Avoid Criticism** - Don't criticize successor or school, maintain professionalism, leave on positive note, reputation matters, future relationships possible.

**Alumni Status** - Stay connected if appropriate, alumni network, periodic contact, available for questions if needed, legacy continues.

**Celebration** - Celebrate time together, mark transition, gratitude expressed, closure for all, community ritual around ending.

**Successor Support** - Be available to new principal if needed, answer questions, provide context, support their success, smooth transition, school benefit.

Leave well. Your reputation and legacy depend on how you exit.
