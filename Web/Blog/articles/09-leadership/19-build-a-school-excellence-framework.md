# Build a School Excellence Framework

An excellence framework defines what great looks like at your school. It guides hiring, evaluation, professional development, and decision-making. Without it, "excellence" is subjective and inconsistently applied.

## What Is an Excellence Framework?

A document describing:
- What excellent teaching looks like at your school
- What excellent leadership looks like
- What excellent operations look like
- What excellent student outcomes look like
- How you recognize and develop excellence
- How you address lack of excellence

## Why Build an Excellence Framework

- **Clarity**: Everyone knows what to aim for
- **Consistency**: Excellence is defined the same way for everyone
- **Development**: Clear standards enable targeted improvement
- **Hiring**: You can assess fit with your definition of excellence
- **Evaluation**: You have clear criteria for performance review
- **Culture**: Excellence becomes visible and celebrated
- **Accountability**: Clear standards enable fair accountability

## Building Your Framework

**Step 1: Define Core Competencies**

What capabilities matter most at your school?

**For Teaching**
- Subject matter expertise
- Instructional skill
- Student relationships
- Assessment and data use
- Collaboration
- Professional growth

**For Leadership**
- Vision and strategic thinking
- People development
- Decision-making
- Financial management
- Community building
- Change leadership

**For Operations**
- Systems thinking
- Attention to detail
- Problem-solving
- Communication
- Financial responsibility
- Continuous improvement

**Step 2: Define Excellence for Each Competency**

For each competency, describe what excellence looks like:

**Excellent Teaching Example: Student Relationships**
- Knows each student's interests, strengths, and learning needs
- Creates safe environment where students feel valued and respected
- Responds to student concerns with empathy and attention
- Sets high expectations and supports students to meet them
- Celebrates student successes
- Communicates regularly with families about student progress
- Advocates for student needs within school

**Excellent Leadership Example: People Development**
- Hires talented people aligned with school values
- Creates conditions where people can do their best work
- Provides regular feedback and coaching
- Invests in professional development aligned to growth
- Recognizes and celebrates good work
- Addresses performance issues directly and supportively
- Creates pathways for advancement
- Retains high performers
- Develops next generation of leaders

**Step 3: Define Proficiency Levels**

For each competency, describe different performance levels:

**Developing** (Building skill in this area)
- Specific behaviors showing early development
- Needs support and coaching
- Making progress with effort

**Proficient** (Meets expectations)
- Consistent demonstration of core skills
- Can work independently
- Meets performance standards

**Accomplished** (Exceeds expectations)
- Consistent excellent performance
- Develops others in this area
- Creates innovation or improvement

**Exemplary** (Exceptional performance)
- Outstanding performance
- Models practice for others
- Leadership in this area
- Continuous evolution and learning

**Step 4: Create Descriptors**

For each level, provide specific behavioral descriptors:

**Teaching: Instructional Skill - Proficient Level**
- Plans lessons with clear learning objectives aligned to standards
- Uses variety of instructional strategies suited to learning objectives
- Checks for understanding throughout lesson
- Uses pacing that allows adequate time for learning
- Provides meaningful practice opportunities
- Integrates student interests and real-world applications
- Differentiates instruction for diverse learners

**Step 5: Build Assessment Tools**

Create tools to evaluate each competency:

**Observation Rubric**
- Observable behaviors for each level
- Multiple measures of competency
- Clear evidence requirements
- Consistent rating approach

**Self-Assessment**
- Teachers/leaders rate themselves
- Reflection on growth
- Identification of development areas
- Goal-setting for improvement

**Feedback from Others**
- Student feedback on teaching (secondary level)
- Peer feedback
- Parent feedback (teachers)
- 360-degree feedback (leaders)

**Results/Outcomes**
- Student achievement data
- Student satisfaction
- Retention rates
- Financial performance
- Community impact

**Step 6: Create Development Pathways**

How do people grow from Developing to Proficient to Accomplished?

**For Each Competency**
- What skills need to build first?
- What experiences help development?
- What professional learning helps?
- What coaching or mentoring helps?
- What timeline is reasonable?
- What are milestones?

**Development Activities**
- Targeted professional development workshops
- Peer observation and coaching
- Instructional coaching
- Reading and study groups
- Conference attendance
- Online learning
- Collaborative practice

**Step 7: Build Recognition and Incentive Systems**

How do you recognize and incentivize excellence?

**Recognition**
- Awards and celebration
- Public acknowledgment
- Special responsibilities
- Leadership opportunities
- Compensation increases
- Sabbaticals or special projects

**Incentives**
- Clear path for advancement
- Roles of greater responsibility
- Opportunities for expertise recognition
- Professional development opportunities
- Autonomy and choice

## Excellence Framework Principles

**Aligned with Mission**
- Framework reflects what matters most
- Emphasizes mission-critical competencies
- Lived values embedded in competencies

**Aspirational But Achievable**
- Challenges people to grow
- Realistic with effort and support
- Not so high that it discourages, not so low that it bores

**Developmental**
- Designed to help people grow
- Provides clear pathways to excellence
- Supportive coaching and feedback
- Growth mindset orientation

**Fair and Transparent**
- Same standards for all
- Clear expectations
- Multiple measures of competency
- Opportunity to demonstrate excellence

**Observable and Measurable**
- Behavioral descriptors
- Specific examples
- Consistent rating
- Evidence-based

## Using Your Framework

**Hiring**
- Assess candidates against proficiency standards
- Hire people at Developing to Proficient level
- Clear onboarding to expectations

**Evaluation**
- Annual evaluation against framework
- Multiple measures of competency
- Development plan based on gaps
- Recognition of excellence

**Professional Development**
- Targeted based on competency gaps
- Designed to move people from one level to next
- Differentiated by current level
- Focused on priority areas

**Decision-Making**
- Use excellence definition in choices
- Hire for fit with values
- Reward excellence
- Address lack of excellence

**Succession Planning**
- Identify high-potential people
- Develop in key competencies
- Create leadership pipelines
- Prepare for transitions

## Maintaining Your Framework

**Annual Review**
- Is framework still relevant?
- Do competencies still matter?
- Are proficiency levels realistic?
- What feedback from staff?
- What adjustments needed?

**Continuous Improvement**
- Learn from assessment data
- Adjust framework as needed
- Share learning across school
- Evolve framework with school

An excellence framework doesn't guarantee excellence, but it dramatically increases your odds. It ensures everyone knows what to aim for, can measure progress, and can access support to get there.
