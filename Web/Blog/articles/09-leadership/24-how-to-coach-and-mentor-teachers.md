# How to Coach and Mentor Teachers

Coaching and mentoring are your most powerful tools for improving teaching and supporting teacher growth. They're also among the highest-leverage uses of your time.

## Coaching vs. Mentoring

**Coaching**
- Focused on specific skill or performance
- Time-limited (usually 3-6 months)
- Goal-oriented (improve X)
- Observation and feedback
- Structured process
- Often around current challenge

**Mentoring**
- Broader professional development
- Ongoing (year or longer)
- Relationship-focused
- Guidance and support
- Less structured
- Often for early-career teachers or new roles

## Coaching Process

**Step 1: Build Trust**
- Establish confidential relationship
- Make it safe to be vulnerable
- Show genuine care for their success
- Clarify this isn't evaluation (if possible)
- Listen without judgment

**Step 2: Identify Focus Area**
- What do they want to improve?
- What does the data show?
- What's the gap between current and desired?
- What's realistic to address?
- What will have most impact?

**Step 3: Establish Goals**
- Specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound
- Connected to larger purpose
- Teacher has input and ownership
- Clear success criteria
- Regular check-in points

**Step 4: Develop Strategy**
- What's the strategy for improvement?
- What will they do differently?
- What support or resources needed?
- What professional learning helps?
- What obstacles might arise?
- Contingency planning

**Step 5: Observe and Collect Data**
- Classroom observation focused on goal
- Specific note-taking (not judgmental)
- Video recording (if available and comfortable)
- Student work samples
- Student feedback
- Multiple observations over time

**Step 6: Provide Feedback**
- Describe what you observed (not interpretation)
- Ask reflective questions
- Listen to teacher's perspective
- Celebrate progress and effort
- Identify next steps
- Support and encouragement
- Not criticism, but growth-oriented

**Step 7: Adjust and Iterate**
- Progress toward goal?
- What's working?
- What needs adjustment?
- Next focus area
- Continued support
- Incremental progress

**Step 8: Sustain and Celebrate**
- Recognize achievement
- Help integrate into practice
- Transition to independence
- Maintenance plan
- Plan for next goal

## Effective Coaching Techniques

**Active Listening**
- Listen without planning response
- Hear what they're saying and not saying
- Ask clarifying questions
- Reflect back what you hear
- Show genuine interest

**Powerful Questions**
- Open-ended questions that prompt reflection
- "What do you notice about...?"
- "How might you...?"
- "What would happen if...?"
- "What does the data tell you?"
- "How do you know that?"

**Non-Judgmental Observation Notes**
- Describe behaviors, not evaluations
- "Teacher called on boys 18 times and girls 6 times"
- Not: "Teacher didn't balance gender participation"
- Specific, concrete, observable
- Data-based feedback

**Celebration of Progress**
- Recognize effort and improvement
- Specific praise (not just "good job")
- "I noticed you waited 5 seconds after asking questions, which gave more students time to think"
- Connect to goals and values
- Encouragement and support

**Support and Resources**
- Model the practice
- Provide articles or resources
- Facilitate peer observation
- Video clips of good examples
- Professional development workshop
- Study groups with peers

## Mentoring Program Structure

**Mentor Selection**
- Excellent teacher
- Strong people skills
- Respected by peers
- Committed to mentee growth
- Willing to invest time
- Good listener and coach
- Patient and supportive

**Mentee Selection**
- Early-career teachers (first 3 years)
- Teachers new to school
- Teachers in new role or grade
- Teachers identified for growth
- Teachers needing support
- Voluntary and opt-in

**Matching**
- Grade level or subject compatibility (if possible)
- Personality and communication style
- Mentor's capacity and interests
- Mentee's needs
- Timeline and goals
- Chemistry and rapport

**Mentor Training**
- Coaching skills workshop
- Confidentiality and professionalism
- How to provide feedback
- How to listen and ask questions
- How to support without directing
- How to set boundaries

**Structure and Expectations**
- Frequency: Weekly or bi-weekly minimum
- Duration: Year or longer
- Focus areas: First classroom, curriculum, relationships, professional growth
- Communication outside meetings
- Observation of each other
- Regular reflection
- Open communication

**Support from Principal**
- Provide time for meetings
- Remove barriers
- Protect confidentiality
- Express appreciation
- Mentor compensation if possible
- Professional development for mentors

## Coaching for Performance Issues

**When Coaching Is Appropriate**
- Teacher has capacity but needs support
- Specific skill gap
- Willing to improve
- Early in performance issue

**When More Formal Action Is Needed**
- Repeated non-improvement
- Unwilling to engage
- Serious issue (safety, ethics)
- Performance affecting students significantly

**Performance Coaching Process**
- Clear expectation setting
- Specific focus area
- Regular observations and feedback
- Support and resources
- Clear timeline
- Documentation of progress
- Honest assessment of whether improving

## Peer Coaching Models

**Peer Observation Partnerships**
- Teachers observe each other's classes
- Provide feedback
- Mutual learning
- Reduces power differential
- Builds collaboration
- Professional learning community

**Collaborative Coaching**
- Multiple teachers around specific goal
- Study group format
- Shared resources and strategies
- Collective problem-solving
- Peer accountability
- Community of practice

**Grade Level or Department Coaching**
- Collaborative focus on shared students
- Data analysis together
- Problem-solving together
- Shared accountability
- Collective responsibility for results

## Making Coaching a School Practice

**Normalize Coaching**
- Everyone gets coaching at times
- Not remediation, but growth
- Valued and expected
- Part of professional culture
- Time and resources allocated
- Recognition for learning

**Distribute Coaching**
- Principal coaches some
- Instructional coach if you have one
- Mentor teachers coach
- Department heads coach
- Peer coaching
- Multiple people playing this role

**Ongoing Professional Learning**
- Coaching skills for leaders
- How to observe and provide feedback
- Building feedback culture
- Difficult conversations
- Coaching certifications
- Continuous improvement

## Measuring Coaching Impact

**Teacher Measures**
- Growth in targeted skill
- Implementation of new practices
- Teacher satisfaction and morale
- Retention and engagement
- Professional growth

**Student Measures**
- Changes in student learning
- Improved classroom climate
- Student engagement
- Student achievement
- Student satisfaction

**School Measures**
- Improved instructional quality overall
- Collaborative culture
- Professional learning environment
- Achievement gains
- Retention of good teachers

Coaching and mentoring are not luxuries—they're necessities for school excellence. Teachers improve through coaching more than any other intervention. Make this a cornerstone of your leadership practice.
