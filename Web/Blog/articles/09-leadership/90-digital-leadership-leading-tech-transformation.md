# Digital Leadership: Leading Tech Transformation

Technology is reshaping education. Digital leaders navigate transformation strategically.

**Vision** - Clear vision for technology in school, how it improves learning, future-ready graduates, transformative not just additive, strategic direction.

**Learning** - Stay current on tech trends, understand capabilities, limitations, ethical issues, continuous learning, enough knowledge to lead.

**Infrastructure** - Adequate technology access, reliable internet, devices available, technical support, systems secure, infrastructure adequate for implementation.

**Teaching Integration** - Technology enhances learning not replaces, professional development on integration, pedagogy first not technology first, meaningful use.

**Access and Equity** - All students have access, not creating digital divide, devices for all, internet access, support for those struggling with technology.

**Cyber Security** - Data protection priority, student privacy, secure systems, staff training on security, incident response, legal compliance.

**Digital Citizenship** - Students learn responsible technology use, ethics, online safety, digital literacy, character in digital world, citizenship focus.

**Vendor Partnerships** - Careful vendor selection, quality products, good support, affordability, long-term partnerships, ethical companies, value for money.

**Implementation** - Phased rollout, training adequate, ongoing support, feedback collected, adjustment based on use, sustainable implementation.

**Assessment** - Technology actually improving learning?, Results measured?, Student engagement?, Data on effectiveness?, Adjust if not working.

**Balance** - Technology tool not end goal, human connection still essential, face-to-face learning important, balance analog and digital.

Digital leadership ensures technology serves school mission and benefits all students.
