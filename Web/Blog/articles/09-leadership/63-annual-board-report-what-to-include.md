# Annual Board Report: What to Include

Annual reports demonstrate school's progress and principal's effectiveness. Include key information clearly.

**Student Achievement** - Standardized test results, grade distributions, progress toward goals, achievement gaps, trends over time, comparison to peers, college acceptance rates.

**Financial Summary** - Revenue and expenses, budget vs. actual, reserves, financial health, major capital projects, resource allocation to priorities.

**Strategic Goals** - Progress on multi-year goals, annual milestones achieved, adjustments made, obstacles overcome, outcomes measured, next year targets.

**School Culture** - Attendance rates, discipline trends, staff retention, parent satisfaction, student engagement, community partnerships, school climate.

**Staffing Changes** - New teachers hired, retirements/departures, professional development attended, leadership development, succession planning progress.

**Initiatives and Innovation** - New programs launched, technology implemented, research projects, community engagement, special projects, external recognition.

**Challenges and Solutions** - Issues faced, responses taken, lessons learned, improvements implemented, resilience demonstrated, continuous improvement.

**Community Engagement** - Parent involvement, community partnerships, service and volunteer hours, fundraising, family events, communication frequency.

**Looking Forward** - Vision for next year, strategic priorities, resource needs, anticipated challenges, opportunities, exciting developments ahead.

**Financial Stewardship** - Responsible use of funds, efficiency improvements, risk management, compliance with regulations, transparency about spending.

Present clearly with data, visuals, stories. Balance celebrating achievements with acknowledging challenges. Inspire confidence in school's direction.
