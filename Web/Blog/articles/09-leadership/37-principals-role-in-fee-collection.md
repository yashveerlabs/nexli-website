# Principal's Role in Fee Collection

Fee collection is critical for school cash flow. As principal, you're ultimately responsible for successful collection, though operations staff may manage day-to-day.

## Collection Infrastructure

**Clear Fee Structure**
- Published fees
- Payment schedule
- Due dates
- Penalties for late payment
- Payment methods accepted
- Financial aid availability
- Transparent communication

**Payment Systems**
- Online payment portal (if possible)
- Automatic payment option
- Payment plan options
- Multiple payment methods
- Receipt and confirmation
- Easy access for families
- Reliable and secure

**Tracking and Monitoring**
- Daily tracking of payments
- Monthly reports of collection
- Delinquent accounts identified
- Trends and patterns
- Cash flow forecasting
- Impact on budget
- Early warning of problems

## Collection Strategy

**Communication**
- Clear, early communication of fees
- Payment reminders
- Follow-up on late payments
- Friendly, professional tone
- Multiple communication channels
- Consistency
- Regular updates

**Incentives for Payment**
- Discount for early payment
- Automatic payment option
- Simplified process
- Recognition
- Rewards for on-time payment
- Make paying easy
- Remove barriers

**Accommodations**
- Payment plans for hardship
- Financial aid/scholarships
- Flexible payment dates
- Multiple payment options
- Work-study programs
- Reduced fees for qualifying families
- Case-by-case assessment

**Delinquency Process**
- Clear escalation process
- Friendly reminder first
- Payment plan if needed
- Stronger follow-up if needed
- Payment before continued enrollment
- Collection agency as last resort
- Clear consequences

## Balancing Access and Sustainability

**Inclusion Focus**
- Financial barriers to enrollment
- Scholarship program
- Sliding scale fees
- No student turned away for inability to pay
- Accessible education
- Community focus
- Mission alignment

**Financial Sustainability**
- Fees support operations
- Collection necessary
- Can't run school on promises
- Need to pay teachers
- Maintain facilities
- Provide services
- Financial viability

**Communication of Trade-Offs**
- "We want you here"
- "We also need to be sustainable"
- "Working together on this"
- Options available
- Transparency about situation
- Partnership approach
- Respect and dignity

## Staff Training

**Business Office Training**
- Empathetic collection
- Professional communication
- Problem-solving approach
- Legal requirements
- Data privacy
- Customer service
- Flexibility within policy

**Teacher and Staff Training**
- Not teachers' job to collect
- Understanding fees
- Referring to office
- Empathy for families
- No judgment or shaming
- Referral process
- Supporting business office

## Financial Aid Programs

**Scholarship Program**
- Funded by school reserves
- Grants or discounts
- Need-based
- Merit-based
- Income-based sliding scale
- Transparent criteria
- Application process

**Payment Plans**
- Monthly payment options
- Extended payment periods
- Flexible scheduling
- Accessible to most families
- No penalty
- Automatic payment option
- Manageable for families

**Work-Study**
- Families work for tuition credit
- School tasks or projects
- Fair exchange
- Maintains dignity
- Family contribution
- Benefit to school
- Meaningful work

**Fundraising for Aid**
- School fundraising
- Funds available for scholarships
- Parent fundraising
- Donor-funded aid
- Community contributions
- Fund for scholarships
- Accessible to qualified families

## Addressing Collection Issues

**Low Collection Rate**
- Why are families not paying?
- Barriers to payment?
- Financial hardship genuine?
- Payment systems accessible?
- Communication clear?
- Support available?
- Adjustment needed?

**Cash Flow Problems**
- If collection low, what's impact?
- Can school operate?
- Need contingency?
- Expense reduction?
- Need to communicate situation?
- Emergency funding?
- Long-term solution?

**Communication with Board**
- Collection rates
- Delinquent accounts
- Financial impact
- Corrective actions
- Forecast and outlook
- Plans for improvement
- Support needed

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

**Collection Practices**
- Professional and respectful
- No harassment
- No threats
- No public shaming
- Privacy respected
- Dignity maintained
- Legal compliance

**Debt Collection**
- Only as last resort
- Know legal requirements
- Professional collection agency
- Legal team guidance
- Appropriate communication
- Rights and protections respected
- Financial recovery only

**Tax and Reporting**
- Scholarship and aid reporting
- Tax implications
- Accurate record-keeping
- Audit trail
- Compliance with regulations
- Professional guidance
- Transparency

Effective collection balances inclusion with sustainability. Families who can pay should, enabling scholarships for those who can't.
