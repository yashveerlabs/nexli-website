# Building Resilience as School Leader

Leadership is hard. Resilience enables you to weather challenges and bounce back.

**Adversity Orientation** - Challenges are normal, not personal failures, opportunity for growth, problems to solve not tragedies, perspective matters.

**Purpose and Meaning** - Connected to mission, why you do this work, impact on students, purpose-driven not just job, meaning sustains through difficulties.

**Support Systems** - Relationships matter, family, friends, mentors, peers, professional help if needed, not carrying burden alone, community essential.

**Physical Health** - Exercise regular, sleep adequate, nutrition good, stress management practices, body resilience supports mental resilience, self-care essential.

**Emotional Processing** - Process emotions, talk through challenges, don't bottle up, therapy or coaching helpful, feeling validated, emotions normal.

**Problem-Solving** - Focus on what you can control, take action, try solutions, don't stay stuck in problem, agency matters, sense of efficacy.

**Reframing** - View setbacks as temporary, specific to situation, learned something, growth opportunity, not reflection of you, resilient thinking.

**Celebration** - Notice progress, celebrate wins, acknowledge effort, positivity matters, momentum building, joy and appreciation.

**Growth Mindset** - Believe challenges can strengthen you, learning from difficulty, adversity as teacher, continuous improvement orientation, growth possible.

**Perspective** - Long view, not just today's crisis, this too shall pass, bigger purpose, remember past challenges overcome, perspective restores hope.

Resilient leaders weather storms and model strength for their school communities.
