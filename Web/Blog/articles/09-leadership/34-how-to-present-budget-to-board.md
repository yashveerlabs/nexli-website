# How to Present Budget to Board

Presenting budget to the board is about building trust and getting approval for your priorities. A well-prepared presentation shows competence and confidence.

## Budget Presentation Preparation

**Know Your Numbers**
- Know every line item
- Understand variances from last year
- Have explanations ready
- Know assumptions made
- Understand contingencies
- Prepare for questions
- Anticipate concerns

**Organize Your Presentation**
- Executive summary
- Strategic context
- Revenue projections
- Expense breakdown
- Comparison to previous years
- Key decisions and rationale
- Q&A format

**Visual Aids**
- Charts showing budget allocation
- Trend lines for multi-year comparison
- Key financial metrics
- Impact on strategic goals
- Don't overwhelm with data
- Clear and easy to understand
- Professional appearance

**Anticipate Questions**
- Why this amount for this area?
- How does it compare to peer schools?
- What about cost increases?
- How will you address cuts?
- What if revenue is lower?
- How does this serve mission?
- Contingency planning?

## During the Presentation

**Set the Context**
- Mission and strategic priorities
- Financial health of school
- External environment (economic, regulatory, market)
- How budget serves mission
- Key decisions and their rationale
- Financial assumptions

**Present Numbers Clearly**
- Walk through revenue and expenses
- Explain major categories
- Show comparison to prior year
- Highlight significant changes
- Explain variances
- Link to strategy
- Show bottom line (surplus or deficit)

**Highlight Key Decisions**
- Where are you investing?
- Why are these priorities?
- What won't be funded?
- Strategic reasoning
- Impact on school
- Connection to goals
- Alternative considered

**Show Financial Health**
- Revenue projections
- Expense management
- Cash position
- Reserves
- Sustainability
- Risks and contingencies
- Multi-year outlook

**Connect to Mission**
- How does budget serve mission?
- What will students experience from these investments?
- How will teaching improve?
- How will culture strengthen?
- What outcomes expected?
- Impact on students
- School excellence

## Handling Board Questions

**Listen Fully**
- Understand what they're asking
- Don't interrupt
- Take notes
- Make sure you understand
- Ask clarifying questions

**Answer Directly**
- Address their specific question
- Don't go on tangents
- Be concise
- Provide data if asked for it
- Admit if you don't know
- Offer to get information

**Show Confidence**
- You've done your homework
- You understand finances
- You're making deliberate choices
- You can defend decisions
- You're looking long-term
- You're managing responsibly

**Be Transparent**
- Admit challenges
- Share concerns
- Discuss contingencies
- Acknowledge uncertainties
- Explain constraints
- Be honest about trade-offs
- Build trust through honesty

## Common Board Questions

**On Revenue**
- "How confident are you in enrollment projections?"
- "What if we don't get the grant?"
- "How dependent are we on that donor?"
- "What's backup plan if revenue is lower?"

**On Expenses**
- "Why is this area up?"
- "Can we negotiate better rates?"
- "Do we need this expense?"
- "Have you explored alternatives?"

**On Investment Decisions**
- "Why are we investing here?"
- "What's the return?"
- "How does this serve students?"
- "When will we see results?"

**On Sustainability**
- "Can we sustain this long-term?"
- "What are the risks?"
- "How are reserves?"
- "What if economy slows?"

## Building Board Trust Through Budget

**Competence and Care**
- You know your numbers
- You care about responsible stewardship
- You're strategic, not reactive
- You're forward-thinking
- You've considered implications
- You're protecting school assets

**Transparency and Honesty**
- Clear explanations
- Honest about challenges
- Not hiding problems
- Showing contingencies
- Admitting uncertainty
- Asking for input

**Accountability and Follow-Through**
- You deliver on promises
- You manage to budget
- You report accurately
- You address problems
- You follow up on decisions
- You're reliable

**Strategic Alignment**
- Budget serves strategy
- Clear mission connection
- Resources following priorities
- Long-term thinking
- Excellence focus
- Student impact

## After Budget Approval

**Communicate Approval**
- Share with staff
- Explain priorities
- Explain constraints
- Show how budget serves mission
- Invite input on implementation
- Financial literacy for all

**Manage to Budget**
- Monitor spending closely
- Stay within approved budget
- Report to board monthly
- Address variances
- Follow board guidance
- Deliver on promises

**Report Results**
- Show impact of investments
- Share outcome data
- Celebrate successes
- Address challenges
- Learn and adjust
- Plan improvements

A well-presented budget builds board confidence, ensures board buy-in, and sets up successful execution. Treat budget presentation as important opportunity to demonstrate competence and build trust.
