# Media Relations for School Principals

Media coverage shapes school perception. Smart media relations build positive reputation.

**Proactive Communication** - Share good news stories, achievements, student success, staff excellence, community partnerships, positive messaging, build brand.

**Press Releases** - Announce major news, new programs, achievements, awards, events, formatted professionally, timely distribution, newsworthy content.

**Media Interviews** - Prepare for interviews, clear talking points, confident communication, stay on message, answer directly, media training if needed.

**Social Media** - Regular posting, student achievements, community news, achievements shared, professional tone, positive messaging, community engagement.

**Crisis Communication** - Prepare statements in advance, quick response, accurate information, transparency, empathy, protection of privacy, consistent messaging.

**Relationships** - Know local reporters, personal connections, returned calls, professional respect, provide access, build trust, community relationships.

**Accuracy** - Verify information before sharing, correct mistakes quickly, factual reporting, no exaggeration, professional standards, credibility protection.

Strong media relations create positive school image and build community confidence.
