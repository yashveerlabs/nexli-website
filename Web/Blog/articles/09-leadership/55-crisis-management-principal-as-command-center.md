# Crisis Management: Principal as Command Center

When crisis strikes, principal leads. Preparation and clear command center structure enable effective response.

**Crisis Plan** - Procedures for major incidents, communication protocol, command structure, roles assigned, response sequence, regular review, updates.

**Emergency Operations Center** - Designated location, communication tools, supplies, staff trained, quick activation, coordination center, information hub.

**Communication Protocol** - Who communicates what to whom, message consistency, internal and external, social media response, family notifications, press relations.

**First Response** - Immediate safety priority, secure scene, summon help, medical if needed, containment, prevent spread, protect others.

**Investigation** - What happened? Fact-finding, incident analysis, documentation, interviews, secure evidence, root cause analysis.

**Support** - Counseling for affected, trauma response, peer support, hotlines, resources, follow-up care, long-term support.

**Recovery** - Return to normalcy, grief counseling, community rebuilding, security improvements, policy changes, prevent recurrence.

**Communication** - Transparency with stakeholders, regular updates, empathy, action taken, prevention measures, rebuilding confidence.

**Learning** - What could have been better? Improvements for next time, training updates, plan revisions, shared learning.

**Documentation** - Complete incident record, response actions, decisions made, lessons learned, compliance, insurance reporting.

Crises are inevitable. Preparation makes difference between chaos and coordinated response. Lead with confidence.
