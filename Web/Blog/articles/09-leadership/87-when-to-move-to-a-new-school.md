# When to Move to a New School

Career progression may mean leaving your school. Know when to move on.

**Stable School** - School thriving, culture strong, leadership team capable, succession planning in place, you've accomplished goals, ready to move on.

**New Opportunity** - Different role, larger school, different community, challenge that excites you, growth opportunity, new learning possible, career progression.

**Burnout** - You're burned out, school contributes, can't recover, need change, fresh start, different environment, time to go.

**Conflict** - Irreconcilable conflict with board, superintendent, staff, toxic situation, no path forward, better to leave, fresh start elsewhere.

**Personal** - Family reasons, spouse job opportunity, care for aging parent, relocation desired, personal life circumstances, life balance needed, legitimate reasons.

**Red Flags** - School declining, problems mounting, board unstable, community changing negatively, support eroding, harder to lead, difficult context.

**Stay If** - Unfinished important work, school has potential, you can make difference, conditions improving, challenge appeals to you, strategic reason to stay.

**Interview Questions** - Understand new school, culture, challenges, expectations, why position open, what they want accomplished, realistic assessment.

**Decision Process** - Consult mentors, peers, spouse, family, pros/cons list, gut feeling matters, timing right, ready for next chapter.

**Transition** - Give proper notice, complete well, leave legacy positive, help successor, maintain relationships, professional departure.

Move when right opportunity appears and/or current situation unsustainable. Trust your instincts.
