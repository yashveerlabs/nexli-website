# Financial Risk Management for Schools

Identifying and managing financial risks protects your school's viability. Proactive risk management prevents crises.

## Common Financial Risks

**Revenue Risks**
- Enrollment decline
- Government funding changes
- Donor funding changes
- Tuition uncollected
- Market downturn
- Competition from other schools
- Economic recession

**Expense Risks**
- Unexpected major repairs
- Staff turnover (recruiting costs)
- Inflation (utilities, supplies)
- Regulatory compliance costs
- Insurance claims
- Technology failures
- Facility damage

**Operational Risks**
- Loss of key person
- Fraud or mismanagement
- Accounting errors
- Technology failure
- Vendor failure
- Compliance violations
- Poor financial controls

**External Risks**
- Economic downturn
- Natural disaster
- Pandemic
- Regulatory changes
- Market shifts
- Competition
- Demographics

## Risk Assessment

**Probability**
- How likely is risk?
- Has it happened before?
- Industry trends?
- Local conditions?
- High, medium, or low probability?

**Impact**
- If occurs, how serious?
- Financial impact?
- Operational impact?
- Reputational impact?
- Student impact?
- High, medium, or low impact?

**Ranking**
- High probability + high impact = immediate action
- High probability + low impact = monitor and prevent
- Low probability + high impact = contingency planning
- Low probability + low impact = accept risk

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

**Prevention**
- Reduce probability
- Strong financial controls
- Regular maintenance
- Staff development
- Diversified revenue
- Conservative budgeting
- Proactive management

**Mitigation**
- Reduce impact if occurs
- Insurance coverage
- Financial reserves
- Redundancy/backup systems
- Contingency planning
- Documentation
- Clear procedures

**Transfer**
- Move risk to someone else
- Insurance
- Contracts and guarantees
- Outsource risky functions
- Vendor insurance
- Professional advisors
- Risk sharing

**Acceptance**
- Accept some risks
- Monitor them
- Have contingency plan
- Not all risks manageable
- Strategic decision
- Accepted and monitored
- Mitigation where possible

## Financial Controls and Safeguards

**Budget Controls**

- Variance analysis
- Approval process
- Spending limits
- Forecast review
- Board oversight
- Early warning system

**Cash Management**

- Secure depository
- Audit trail
- Segregation of duties
- Fraud prevention
- Appropriate banking
- Controls on spending

**Insurance**
- Liability insurance
- Property insurance
- Workers' compensation
- Directors and officers insurance
- Cyber insurance
- Appropriate coverage
- Regular review

**Audit and Review**
- External audit annually
- Internal controls review
- Financial statements review
- Fraud and mismanagement check
- Compliance verification
- Third-party oversight
- Independent assessment

## Planning for Cash Flow Challenges

**Revenue Fluctuation**
- Timing of payments
- Enrollment changes
- Government funding timing
- Donor giving patterns
- Forecast cash flow
- Manage timing
- Contingency plan

**Unexpected Expenses**
- Emergency fund/reserves
- Emergency response plan
- Prioritization of needs
- Options for funding
- Lines of credit if needed
- Insurance coverage
- Prevention where possible

**Economic Downturn**
- Cost reduction plan
- Revenue alternatives
- Financial reserves
- Conservative planning

- Contingency funding
- Long-term perspective

## Contingency Planning

**Develop Scenarios**
- What if enrollment drops 20%?
- What if major donor leaves?
- What if facility damage?
- What if staff shortages?
- What if economic downturn?
- What if regulatory change?
- Response plan for each

**Financial Contingency**
- Adequate reserves
- Line of credit available
- Emergency funding sources
- Cost reduction options
- Revenue alternatives
- Timeline to implement
- Stakeholder communication

**Operational Contingency**
- Backup systems
- Staff cross-training
- Documentation of critical processes
- Emergency procedures
- Communication plan
- Leadership succession
- Disaster recovery

## Building Financial Resilience

**Reserves**
- Build emergency fund
- 3-6 months operating expenses
- Drawn only for emergencies
- Replenished when used
- Protects against crisis
- Enables opportunity taking
- Financial stability

**Diversified Revenue**
- Don't depend on one source
- Multiple funding streams
- Tuition, grants, donations, government
- Reduce vulnerability
- Flexibility for downturn
- Sustainability
- Growth opportunity

**Efficiency and Sustainability**
- Lean operations
- Waste reduction
- Efficiency improvement
- Process optimization
- Cost management
- Long-term sustainability
- Continuous improvement

**Board and Leader Awareness**
- Risk communication
- Understanding by governance
- Strategic focus on risks
- Regular review
- Adjustment as needed
- Transparency
- Collective responsibility

Financial risk management is proactive leadership. Identify risks, assess them, and implement mitigation strategies before crisis strikes.
