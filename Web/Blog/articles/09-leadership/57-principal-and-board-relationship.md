# Principal and Board Relationship

Strong principal-board partnership is essential. Clear roles, communication, and trust enable effective governance.

**Roles Clarity** - Board governs, principal operates. Board sets policy, principal implements. Board approves budget, principal manages. Board hires/evaluates principal, principal manages school.

**Regular Communication** - Monthly meetings, regular reports, transparent communication, no surprises, advance notification of issues, collaborative problem-solving.

**Evaluation** - Annual principal evaluation, clear criteria, goal-setting, feedback, performance discussion, compensation discussion, development opportunities.

**Financial Partnership** - Budget development together, board approves, principal manages, monthly reports, variance explanations, planning for future.

**Strategic Alignment** - Board sets direction, principal implements strategy, regular review of progress, adjustments as needed, shared accountability.

**Conflict Resolution** - Disagreements happen, discuss privately first, clarity on roles and authority, respect for other's perspective, resolution focused.

**Advocacy** - Board supports principal publicly, backs decisions publicly, private concerns discussed privately, united front essential.

**Accountability** - Principal accountable for school performance, board accountable for governance, regular reporting, results measured, continuous improvement.

**Board Education** - Keep board informed about education, share research and trends, explain instructional decisions, context for policies, informed decisions.

**Boundaries** - Board doesn't micromanage, principal doesn't exclude board from appropriate decisions, appropriate authority at each level, mutual respect.

Healthy principal-board relationship enables school excellence. Invest in communication and trust.
