# Principal Peer Learning Groups

Peer learning groups provide community, support, and professional growth for principals.

**Group Formation** - 4-6 principals, similar school sizes/contexts, regular meeting schedule, commitment to attendance, confidentiality, trust building.

**Meeting Structure** - Rotating facilitator, agenda set by group, equal participation, problem-solving focus, celebration of wins, mutual support.

**Topics Covered** - Leadership challenges, difficult decisions, staff management, budget, strategy, family relationships, personal wellbeing, current trends.

**Discussion Format** - One person shares challenge, group asks questions, collaborative problem-solving, advice and perspective offered, reflection and learning.

**Accountability** - Each principal has goals, group tracks progress, celebrates achievement, supports challenges, mutual accountability, responsibility to group.

**Resource Sharing** - Share policies, procedures, strategies, resources, best practices, external learning, conferences attended, books read, community building.

**Emotional Support** - Challenges and concerns shared, peer support, validation, normalization of difficulties, camaraderie, combat isolation, mutual caring.

**Sustainability** - Long-term commitment, regular meeting schedule, protected time, compensation if possible, group evolution, adaptation to needs.

Peer learning groups transform principal isolation into community and accelerate professional growth.
