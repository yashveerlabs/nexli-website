# How to Present to the Board

Board presentations demonstrate competence and build confidence. Preparation is key.

Prepare thoroughly. Know your material. Anticipate questions. Have data ready. Clear talking points. Professional slides. Organized materials.

Structure presentations. Opening context. Main content. Key findings. Recommendations. Call to action. Close. Q&A. Time for discussion.

Visual materials help. Clear slides. No clutter. Charts over tables. Professional appearance. Readable fonts. Data visualization. Storytelling.

Practice delivery. Confident presentation. Clear speaking. Pacing. Eye contact. Avoid reading. Engagement. Comfortable with material. Handle interruptions gracefully.

Answer questions. Listen fully. Answer directly. Admit uncertainty when true. Offer to get information later. Don't be defensive. Stay calm. Respectful tone.

Engage board members. Make it interesting. Relevance to their concerns. Give them roles (questions, discussion). Value their input. Build buy-in. Partnership approach.

Follow up. Board materials shared. Minutes recorded. Actions assigned. Follow-up on decisions. Implementation reported. Accountability maintained.

Boards respect competent leaders. Show you know your school and can communicate clearly.
