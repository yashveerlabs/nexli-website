# Business Continuity Planning for Schools

When operations are disrupted, a school must continue essential functions. A business continuity plan ensures resilience.

**Critical Functions** - What's essential? Teaching, student safety, communication, finances, payroll, data access, health services, facility operations.

**Risk Assessment** - What could disrupt? Natural disaster, pandemic, loss of key person, technology failure, facility damage, power outage, loss of utilities.

**Continuity Strategies** - Remote learning capability, backup power, data backup, alternative facilities, cross-training staff, documented procedures, supplies stockpiled.

**Communication** - How to reach everyone? Multiple channels, family notification procedures, staff notification, community communication, accurate timely information.

**Remote Operations** - Learning can continue online, staff can work remotely, communication maintained, assignments accessible, regular check-ins, student engagement.

**Facilities Recovery** - Alternate location options, temporary classroom solutions, essential building systems restoration, timeline for return, contingency funding.

**Staffing** - Who's essential? Cross-training, backup plans, payroll continuation, family needs support, psychological support, recovery timeline.

**Financial Impact** - Budget adjustment for disruption, cash reserves adequate, insurance coverage, expense reduction, revenue projections, recovery plan.

**Testing and Updates** - Regular plan review, test procedures, staff training, updates based on testing, annual review, continuous improvement.

**Compliance** - Insurance requirements, regulatory requirements, stakeholder communication, recovery planning, resource availability.

Continuity planning is an insurance policy for school operations. Hope for best, plan for worst.
