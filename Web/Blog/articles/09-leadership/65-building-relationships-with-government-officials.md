# Building Relationships with Government Officials

Government officials impact schools. Relationships enable advocacy and partnerships.

**Identify Key Officials** - Superintendent, school board members, city officials, state legislators, education department staff, influential decision-makers.

**Regular Contact** - Meetings, invitations to school events, communication, updates on school, relationship building, mutual respect, partnership approach.

**Advocacy** - Education priorities, funding needs, policy positions, community education, factual information, respectful persuasion, evidence-based recommendations.

**Transparency** - Share school information, data, challenges, achievements, honest communication, no surprises, mutual trust building.

**Collaboration** - Joint initiatives, problem-solving together, community improvement, mutual benefit, partnership approach, shared goals.

**Respect Role** - Understand their constraints, priorities, pressures, work within system, respect authority, professional interaction, appropriate boundaries.

**School Tours** - Invite officials to visit, show your school, introduce staff and students, pride in accomplishments, building connections, community education.

**Public Engagement** - Attend civic meetings, participate in community forums, engage in public dialogue, thought leadership, community presence, voice for education.

Relationship building with government leaders creates allies for school advocacy and enables better policies.
