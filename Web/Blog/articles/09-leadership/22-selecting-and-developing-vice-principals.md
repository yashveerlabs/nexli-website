# Selecting and Developing Vice Principals

Your assistant/vice principal is your most important hire. They need to complement your strengths, share your vision, and be capable of eventually leading their own school.

## Why This Role Matters

- Your right hand in daily operations
- Key point of contact for staff, students, parents
- Often manages discipline and operations
- Potential next principal
- Sets tone for school culture
- Handles crises when you're unavailable
- Your feedback and mirror
- Often more interaction with daily school than principal

## Job Description Essentials

**Primary Responsibilities**
- Daily operations and problem-solving
- Student discipline and conduct issues
- Attendance and punctuality
- School schedule and logistics
- Staff coverage and absences
- Parent contact and concerns
- Crisis response
- Building management and safety

**Leadership and Development**
- Co-lead professional development
- Support teacher evaluation and feedback
- Mentor emerging leaders
- Project leadership
- Committee participation
- Substitute decision-making when principal unavailable

**Shared Strategic Work**
- Partnership on strategic initiatives
- Curriculum and academic improvement
- School culture and climate
- Staff recruitment and hiring
- Problem-solving and planning

**Growth Trajectory**
- Learning to be principal
- Developing strategic thinking
- Building leadership skills
- Understanding full school operations
- Increasing responsibility over time

## Selecting the Right Person

**Experience**
- Minimum 3-5 years excellent teaching experience
- Track record of success with students
- Some leadership experience (department head, committee, etc.)
- Ideally: working at your school (knows culture and context)
- Experience in challenging situations

**Competencies**
- Strong relationship building
- Problem-solving skills
- Calm under pressure
- Decision-making ability
- Communication skills
- Organizational skills
- Conflict resolution
- Understanding of instruction and curriculum
- Financial literacy
- Technology competency

**Personal Characteristics**
- Shares school mission and values
- Respected by teachers and students
- Trustworthy and ethical
- Growth mindset
- Collaborative (not territorial)
- Resilient and emotionally intelligent
- Good sense of humor
- Energy and stamina
- Integrity

**Assessment Process**

**Application and References**
- Review credentials and experience
- Contact references
- Look for evidence of competencies
- Red flags: Instability, conflicts, negative references

**Interview**
- Behavioral questions about past situations
- Scenario questions about school situations
- Questions about their leadership philosophy
- Understanding of school's mission and approach
- Why they want this role
- What they want to learn
- Their career aspirations

**Observation**
- Observe them teaching (if internal candidate)
- Observe how they interact with colleagues
- See them handle challenging student situations
- Note their leadership presence

**Panel Input**
- Have teachers and staff interview candidates
- Get their perspective on fit
- Staff will work with this person daily
- Teachers' voices matter in selection

## Hiring Process

**Internal vs. External**

**Internal Advantages**
- Knows school culture and context
- Already established relationships
- Quicker onboarding
- Shows advancement opportunity
- Clear fit with mission
- Can mentor into role gradually

**Internal Considerations**
- May need to shift colleague relationships
- Former peer now supervisor
- Different dynamics may be needed
- May disappoint other internal candidates

**External Advantages**
- Fresh perspective
- No baggage from prior role
- Can bring new expertise
- No internal conflict
- Can hire for specific gaps

**External Considerations**
- Longer ramp-up period
- Need to learn culture
- Build relationships
- May not understand context
- More expensive/competitive market

## Onboarding New Vice Principal

**First 30 Days**
- Clear role orientation
- Introduction to all staff, families, key community
- Tour of all facilities
- Overview of current issues and priorities
- Shadowing of principal
- Key meetings and processes
- First assignment: something successful to build confidence

**First 90 Days**
- Deep learning of school operations
- Build relationships with key staff and families
- Participate in key decisions
- Take on assigned responsibilities
- Regular feedback on transition
- Check-in on needs and concerns
- Adjustment period: open to questions

**First Year**
- Gradually increasing responsibility
- Leadership of specific initiatives
- Professional development to build skills
- Regular mentoring and feedback
- Evaluation of progress
- Adjustment to role as needed
- Building into leadership team

## Developing Vice Principals Into Leaders

**Create Partnership**
- Share decision-making
- Consult them on major decisions
- Show your thinking
- Ask for their perspective
- Listen and consider their input
- Explain your decisions
- Model leadership

**Provide Developmental Opportunities**
- Lead professional development workshops
- Present at conferences or meetings
- Write articles or share expertise
- Mentor teachers
- Lead committees or initiatives
- Build budget
- Plan strategic initiatives
- Make presentations to board

**Formal Development**
- Coaching or mentoring
- Leadership training workshops
- Reading and study groups
- Administrative credential programs
- Master's degree in educational leadership
- Professional development conferences
- Peer observation of other principals

**Feedback and Coaching**
- Regular feedback (weekly or bi-weekly)
- Coaching on specific situations
- Debriefing after key decisions
- Observations and feedback
- What went well, what to develop
- Support for growth
- Honest conversation about career path

**Succession Planning**
- Explicit conversation about principal path
- Timeline and expectations
- Development plan to prepare
- Opportunities to learn key skills
- Networking with other principals
- Preparation for principal interviews
- Support for getting principal role at another school

## Ongoing Management

**Regular Check-Ins**
- Weekly or bi-weekly meetings
- Discuss current issues
- Give feedback
- Provide support
- Address concerns early

**Performance Evaluation**
- Annual formal evaluation
- Clear expectations
- Assessment of competencies
- Development goals
- Recognition of progress
- Honest feedback

**Retention**
- Competitive compensation
- Clear advancement path
- Development opportunities
- Appreciation and recognition
- Work-life balance
- Autonomy in role
- Growth opportunities

## Challenges

**They Want More Autonomy**
- Gradually increase autonomy as competency builds
- Clear decision-making authority
- Support them to make decisions
- Back them publicly
- Provide coaching in private

**They're Not Ready for Principal Role**
- Honest conversation about limitations
- Continued development opportunities
- Perhaps right person for assistant role long-term
- May not have principal aspirations
- Different career paths are okay

**They Want Different Focus**
- They may not want discipline-focused role
- May prefer academic leadership or operations
- Adjust role to fit strengths and interests
- Create different leadership track
- People are different; honor that

**You're Too Busy to Mentor**
- This IS your job as leader
- Protecting time for this is critical
- Mentoring happens in real time while working
- Reflection on work is development
- Won't regret time spent on this

Your vice principal is your legacy. Invest in developing them well. If they eventually lead their own school and do great things there, that's your impact multiplied. If they stay and strengthen your school, that's success too.
