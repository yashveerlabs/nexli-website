# School Curriculum: Principal's Oversight Role

Your role isn't to teach every subject, but to ensure excellent, coherent curriculum across school.

## Curriculum Standards and Alignment

**Alignment to Standards** - Curriculum aligns to national/state/local standards, grade-level expectations, college/career readiness standards, skill progressions across grades.

**Coherence K-12** - Intentional sequence across grades, building on prior learning, avoiding gaps and duplication, smooth transitions, vertical alignment.

**Horizontal Alignment** - Grade-level consistency, multiple sections teaching similarly, fair assessment, equitable rigor, common pacing.

**Balance and Rigor** - Academic rigor, critical thinking, application and relevance, breadth and depth, student engagement, challenging work.

## Curriculum Development and Review

**Regular Review** - Annual or cyclical review of curriculum, alignment check, effectiveness assessment, updates based on new research, stakeholder input.

**Development Process** - Collaborative curriculum work, teacher expertise, research-based practices, piloting and feedback, implementation with support, evaluation and refinement.

**Instructional Materials** - Quality textbooks/programs, alignment to curriculum, multiple resources, age-appropriate, culturally responsive, accessibility considerations.

**Assessment Aligned** - Assessments match curriculum, formative assessments for feedback, summative assessments for evaluation, rubrics and standards, student understanding of criteria.

## Supporting Excellent Teaching

**Professional Development** - Teachers understand curriculum, training in implementation, collaborative planning time, access to resources, peer observation and sharing.

**Instructional Coherence** - Teachers teaching similarly in same grade/subject, pacing guides provided, common assessments, shared strategies, consistency across sections.

**Instructional Materials** - Adequate resources, budget for materials, technology access, supplements as needed, upgrades over time, teacher input on materials.

**Monitoring Implementation** - Classroom observations, lesson plan review, assessment results, student work samples, teacher feedback, adjustments as needed.

## Curriculum Priorities

**Strategic Focus** - What's most important to teach? Align to mission, engage students, foundational skills, social-emotional learning, values and character, life skills.

**Grade-Level Emphasis** - What should every student know/be able to do by grade end? Core competencies, benchmarks, assessment targets, graduation requirements, readiness skills.

**Subject-Specific Priorities** - ELA priorities, math priorities, science priorities, social studies priorities, arts, physical education, electives - what matters most?

## Responsive Curriculum

**Assessment and Adjustment** - Data informs curriculum choices, achievement gaps identified, intervention strategies, enrichment for advanced, progress monitoring, continuous improvement.

**Student Engagement** - Relevance to student lives, choice and voice in learning, real-world connections, project-based learning, authentic assessments, student ownership.

**Diverse Learners** - Differentiation and personalization, accommodations and modifications, support for English learners, advanced learner opportunities, special needs support, universal design for learning.

## Special Topics Integration

**Social-Emotional Learning** - Woven throughout curriculum, explicit instruction, school climate alignment, skill development, emotional safety, character development.

**Technology Integration** - Enhances learning (not distraction), digital literacy, coding/computer science, research skills, collaboration tools, productivity.

**Civic Engagement** - Service learning, community partnerships, activism, leadership opportunities, real-world relevance, student voice in decisions.

**Intercultural Competence** - Culturally responsive curriculum, diverse perspectives, inclusive materials, celebration of diversity, equity lens, bias awareness.

## Accountability and Results

**Goal Setting** - What learning outcomes do we want? Specific and measurable, ambitious but achievable, aligned to standards, progress toward goals.

**Assessment and Data** - Regular assessment, data analysis, progress monitoring, multiple measures, disaggregation by subgroups, honest assessment.

**Adjustments** - What's working? What's not? Curriculum adjustments based on data, support for teachers, resource reallocation, continuous improvement.

**Communication** - Share progress with stakeholders, celebrate achievements, address concerns, transparently about challenges, community involvement.

Your curriculum should be intentional, coherent, engaging, rigorous, and equitable. This is foundational work that affects every student.
