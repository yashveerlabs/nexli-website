# Student Performance Data: How Principals Use It

Data on student performance is essential for school improvement. As principal, you use data to identify needs, allocate resources, and drive improvement.

## Types of Performance Data

**Assessment Results** - Standardized test scores, benchmark assessments, classroom assessments, unit tests, performance-based assessments, formative assessments, multiple measures.

**Academic Indicators** - Grade distributions, course pass rates, college entrance exam scores, AP/honors enrollment, completion rates, progression, advancement.

**Engagement Indicators** - Attendance rates, behavior referrals, suspensions/expulsions, participation rates, late arrivals, homework completion, class engagement.

**Demographic Breakdown** - By grade, by subject, by teacher, by subgroup (race, ethnicity, gender, SES, ELL, special needs), comparison to school/district/state.

**Growth Data** - Progress over time, comparison to starting points, value-added measures, closing of gaps, trajectory analysis, intervention effectiveness.

## Data Analysis

**Regular Review** - Monthly or quarterly data review meetings, leadership team analysis, grade-level analysis, subject analysis, teacher-level analysis, trend identification.

**Disaggregation** - Break down data by category, identify patterns, subgroup analysis, equity gaps, bright spots and concerns, root cause analysis.

**Trend Analysis** - Over multiple years, improving or declining, seasonal patterns, external factor impact, comparison to benchmarks, prediction of outcomes.

**Comparative Analysis** - Compared to prior year, compared to benchmark, compared to peer schools, state/national comparison, relative performance, context matters.

## Using Data for Improvement

**Identify Priorities** - Where are biggest gaps? What's most important to fix? Where is opportunity? What's achievable? Strategic prioritization based on data and impact.

**Set Goals** - Based on current data, what's the target? Ambitious but achievable, multiple-year goal with annual milestones, progress monitoring, accountability for reaching.

**Allocate Resources** - Where to invest? Highest-need areas first, research-based solutions, professional development targeted, support for struggling, enrichment for advanced.

**Intervention Planning** - For students significantly below grade level, targeted, intensive, progress-monitored, adjustment if needed, combination of supports.

**Professional Development** - Based on data needs, teachers in low-performing areas, coaching in needed skills, resources and time, collaborative learning.

## Communicating Data

**To Staff** - Regular sharing, interpretation support, celebration of progress, honest about challenges, collaborative problem-solving, actionable information.

**To Families** - Progress reports, student achievement, growth, areas for focus, how to support at home, strengths and growth areas, positive framing.

**To Board** - Monthly or quarterly reports, trends, progress toward goals, resource needs, actions taken, results expected, transparent about challenges.

**To Community** - School report card, aggregate data, trends, performance compared to peers, progress on strategic goals, community communication.

## Equity and Data

**Disaggregate by Subgroup** - Are there achievement gaps by race, ethnicity, gender, SES, ELL status, disability? Data reveals inequities, action required, monitoring for progress.

**Address Root Causes** - Why gaps exist, stereotypes and biases addressed, equitable teaching practices, support systems for underserved, intentional gap-closing.

**Resource Allocation** - More resources to higher-need students, skilled teachers in high-need areas, not exacerbating existing inequities, equity-focused decisions.

**Progress Monitoring** - Regular check on gap-closing progress, celebrations when gaps close, adjustments if progress insufficient, accountability for equitable outcomes.

## Data Pitfalls to Avoid

**Single Data Point** - Multiple measures needed, context matters, one test doesn't define student or teacher, look at multiple indicators, bigger picture.

**Misinterpretation** - Data literacy important, understand measurement error, causation vs. correlation, understand context, seek expert interpretation, avoid false conclusions.

**Over-Testing** - Too much assessment wastes time, formative assessment is enough for daily feedback, summative assessment periodic, balance assessment and learning.

**Punitive Use** - Data for improvement, not punishment, support when struggling, collaborative problem-solving, development focus, not blame.

## Building Data Culture

**Data Literacy** - Train staff on data interpretation, understanding assessments, reading reports, context and limitations, collaborative analysis, interpretation skills.

**Regular Review Meetings** - Leadership team meets regularly, grade-level meetings include data, department meetings analyze subject data, teacher conversations about data, data-driven decisions.

**Celebration of Progress** - Acknowledge improvement, small wins matter, progress toward goals, building momentum, community of success, intrinsic motivation.

**Continuous Learning** - Best practices in data use, research on assessment, improvement science, peer learning, external expertise, continuous growth.

Data is a tool for improvement. Used well, it reveals opportunities and drives better decisions. Used poorly, it creates blame and defensiveness. Choose wisely.
