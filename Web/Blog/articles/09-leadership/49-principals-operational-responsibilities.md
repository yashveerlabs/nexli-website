# Principal's Operational Responsibilities

Operations keep school running. Daily management, facilities, systems, and logistics fall under your responsibility.

## Daily Operations Management

**Schedule and Calendar** - Develop and manage school schedule, master schedule for classes, special events, professional development days, calendar shared with all, conflicts resolved, efficiency optimized.

**Attendance and Discipline** - Monitor student attendance, follow-up on absences, student discipline procedures, behavior management system, consistency, documentation.

**Substitute Coverage** - Ensure substitute teachers when needed, adequate sub pool, clear procedures, lesson plan availability, communication to students.

**Safety and Security** - Emergency procedures in place, fire drill and lockdown practice, visitor screening, access control, incident response, regular review.

**Communications** - Daily announcements, staff communications, parent communications, community updates, multiple channels, consistency and clarity.

**Administrative Staff** - Manage administrative team, clear responsibilities, professional development, performance management, succession planning, collaborative culture.

## Facilities Management

**Building Maintenance** - Regular maintenance schedule, preventive maintenance, repairs addressed quickly, capital projects planned, budget management, contractor oversight.

**Cleanliness and Safety** - Clean facilities daily, safety standards met, hazard removal, OSHA compliance, work environment quality, pride in facilities.

**Utilities and Systems** - Efficient use of utilities, equipment maintained, technology systems working, HVAC, electrical, plumbing, regular checks.

**Security and Access** - Locks and keys managed, visitor procedures, after-hours access, camera systems, incident response, safety protocols.

**Grounds and Landscaping** - Outdoor areas maintained, sports fields available, parking lots managed, trash and recycling, seasonal maintenance.

## Administrative Systems

**Student Information System** - Accurate student data, grades and progress tracking, attendance records, family information, security and privacy, regular updates.

**Financial Systems** - Budget tracking, accounts payable/receivable, payroll processing, financial reporting, compliance, accuracy and timeliness.

**Personnel Records** - Staff files and documentation, evaluations maintained, HR compliance, confidentiality, access controlled, policies followed.

**Inventory and Purchasing** - Materials and supplies ordered and tracked, purchasing procedures followed, vendor management, budget adherence, efficient processes.

**Technology and Infrastructure** - Computer networks functioning, email systems working, printer and device support, internet access, technology updates, troubleshooting.

## Compliance and Reporting

**Government Reporting** - School and district reports submitted on time, attendance reporting, financial reporting if required, assessment data, compliance documented.

**Accreditation** - Maintain accreditation if applicable, self-study process, visitor preparation, improvement plans, documentation, compliance.

**Legal Compliance** - Laws and regulations followed, policies updated, procedures aligned to requirements, legal consultation when needed, documentation.

**Insurance and Liability** - Adequate insurance coverage, claims management, incident documentation, safety to reduce liability, regular review.

## Problem-Solving

**Address Issues** - Problems brought to your attention, analysis, solution development, implementation, follow-up, continuous improvement.

**Feedback** - Seek input on operations, suggestions for improvement, survey or inquiry, response to feedback, visible changes, continuous enhancement.

**Troubleshooting** - When systems break down, quick response, minimal disruption, temporary solutions if needed, permanent fixes, prevention planning.

## Delegation and Efficiency

**Identify What Must Stay with Principal** - Student discipline significant issues, hiring, budget final approval, major decisions, board communication, some tasks.

**Delegate Appropriately** - Business manager, assistant principal, administrative assistant, office staff, department heads, teachers for committees.

**Empower and Support** - Clear authority, resources provided, training and support, autonomy within guidelines, trust and respect, accountability.

**Efficiency Systems** - Standard procedures, templates, checklists, reduce duplicate work, automation where possible, streamlined processes, time management.

## Monitoring and Improvement

**Daily Rounds** - Walk building regularly, observe operations, notice issues, interact with students/staff, presence visible, responsive to concerns.

**Monthly Review** - Review operations, identify challenges, celebrate successes, look for improvements, data on operations, adjustments as needed.

**Feedback** - Staff input on operations, student feedback, family feedback, external feedback, action on suggestions, continuous improvement culture.

**Prevention** - Look for potential problems, prevent before occurring, systems to catch issues early, proactive management, efficiency and prevention.

Operations are invisible when done well but glaring when poor. Efficient, well-managed operations enable excellent teaching and learning.
