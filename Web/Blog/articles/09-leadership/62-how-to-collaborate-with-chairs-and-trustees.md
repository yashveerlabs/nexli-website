# How to Collaborate with Chairs and Trustees

Strong relationships with board leadership are essential. Chair, vice-chair, and key trustees influence decisions.

**Individual Relationships** - Regular one-on-one meetings, personal connection, understand priorities, find common ground, show respect, listen more than talk, collaboration focused.

**Clear Communication** - Regular updates, advance notice of issues, explain decisions, transparent about challenges, ask for input, value their perspective, partnership approach.

**Respect Their Role** - Board provides governance, you provide operations, leadership shared appropriately, don't exclude them from decisions that matter, involve them appropriately.

**Support Chair** - Help chair succeed in role, provide information needed, make their job easier, respect their authority, public support, private counsel if needed.

**Problem-Solving Together** - When issues arise, discuss options, collaborative solutions, respect their judgment, incorporate feedback, show how their input matters, consensus when possible.

**Keep Confidentiality** - Don't share board discussions publicly, professional discretion, respect privacy, maintain trust, appropriate information sharing only.

**Celebrate Success Together** - Acknowledge board contributions, public recognition, highlight their impact, build community, shared success, team approach.

**Navigate Disagreement** - Respectful discussion, don't take personally, focus on best for school, willing to reconsider, may need to agree to disagree, professional always.

Strong leadership means building trust with those who govern and those who work with you. Invest in relationships.
